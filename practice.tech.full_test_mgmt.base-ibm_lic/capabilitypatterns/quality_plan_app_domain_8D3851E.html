Main Description

The Quality Plan for Application Domain describes the organization, activities, and project factors that have been put in place to achieve the planned level of quality for all work products in the application domain.  It provides a framework for continuous improvement through quality reviews.  Through the use of the project quality plan, if one exists, it defines the approach to be taken when planning and tracking the quality of the application development work products to ensure conformance to specified requirements and that expectations are being met.  However, the scope does not include the quality assurance of the development process.

The Quality Plan document includes the following information:

  • Quality criteria: The characteristics that must be considered as the deliverables are being built.
  • Quality contributors: Project factors that, when applied, will assist in achieving the quality objectives.
  • Quality strategy:
    • Static testing: testing before any code is written.
    • Dynamic testing: testing by exercising the code.
  • Quality roles and responsibilities: Definition of roles and organizations responsible for performing those roles.
  • Quality standards and methods: Mapping of quality contributors and project procedures to required quality standards.

The Quality Plan is developed at project startup and is updated on an ongoing basis.

Key Considerations

The Quality Plan for Application Domain should be kept simple, realistic, and achievable. It is likely that the sponsor will want to approve the project quality plan. The project quality plan should be used as the main driver for the Quality Plan for Application Domain. Discussing the content of the Quality Plan with the sponsor would help to increase his confidence in the project.

The objective of the Quality Plan for AD is to ensure that the deliverables meet the acceptance criteria specified in the Agreement. If too little quality activity is performed, the deliverables will be unacceptable. If too much quality activity is performed, unnecessary and unplanned costs will be incurred.

Generally speaking, test focus areas referenced in the test strategy and master test plan are the quality criteria for testing. These are driven by the business and technical requirements and are based on the risks and costs as defined in the brief outline section. The test objectives are based on the test focus areas (quality criteria). Quality contributors are project factors that, when applied, will assist in achieving the quality objectives for testing. Testing types are logical tests that are used as tools during the physical levels of testing to ensure that the test objectives are met.

Quality planning for testing must consider the cost/benefit trade-off. The primary benefit of performing quality activities is less re-work, which means higher productivity, lower costs, and increased satisfaction. There is a cost associated with performing those quality/testing activities, however. Therefore, the test manager must continually weigh the trade-off between the benefits realized and the costs incurred.

The testing procedures are documented through the various testing work products. The Quality Plan for Application Domain also shows how Quality Strategies for testing are implemented.

The detailed plans for testing document the activities relating to each detailed level of testing.

Where applicable, the quality-plans must conform to ISO9000 and any other relevant standards that have been adopted by the delivery organization or requested by the sponsor.
