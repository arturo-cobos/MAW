Guideline: Supplier Management
Use this guideline to identify, select, acquire and manage products and services required to successfully complete the requirements of the project, from suppliers, external and internal.
Relationships
Related Elements
  • Release Resources (IMSP240 & IMSP711)
  • Select supplier (IMSP711)
Main Description

Purpose


Use this guideline to:
Identify, select, acquire and manage products and services required to successfully complete the requirements of the project, from suppliers, external and internal.

Scope


This guideline applies to:
The activities required by an organization to acquire products and services required by a project.
This document provides guidance for acquiring products, such as the Commercial Off the Self (COTS) products.
Refer to the Supplier Management Procedure (IMSP711) for activities associated with the procurement of services from subcontractors.

This guideline does not apply to:
The steps involved in the use of the appropriate procurement ordering tools.

Overview


This guideline includes:
  • Acquiring Products from Suppliers
  • Procuring Supplier Services

Details

1. Acquiring Products from Suppliers

1.1 Defining Business Needs


The Project Manager:
Step Action
1 Documents the need(s) to be fulfilled by the acquisition of the product under consideration.
Refer to the Requirements Development and Management Procedure (IMSP576) for assistance in defining and documenting the requirements to be addressed by this product.
The defined requirements will be used as the base for analyzing the suitability of potential products being considered.
2 Obtains stakeholder approval of the defined requirements.

1.2 Determining product and source for acquisition


The Project Manager:
Step Action
1
Establishes contact with assigned Procurement Advisor to get assistance in identifying potential products to fulfill defined business requirements. When an account is first established, a Procurement Advisor is assigned to that account.
2 Refer to the Key Decisions Procedure (IMSP445) for assistance in determining the proper product and supplier to address the needs identified in Section 1.1.
Documents the elements of the decision process. In making this decision, the following items should be considered regarding each product / supplier:
  • Trade reports on the product and supplier
  • Product performance reports

Following the key decision procedure and working with the account's Procurement Advisor, identifies the product and supplier best able to address the project's identified needs
3 Reviews the selection decision with the appropriate stakeholders and Account Management in order to obtain approval to place the order.
4 Working with the Procurement Advisor, enters the order for the selected product.

1.3 Transition and Management of acquired products


The Project Manager:
Step Action
1 Establishes a plan for the product being acquired. This plan is to address product:
  • Acceptance
  • Facilities required for storage and use of the product
  • Testing
  • Training
  • Integration
  • Maintenance
2 Establishes and maintains records of the:
  • Date of Acquisition
  • Cost
  • Warranty
3 Completes the acceptance testing of the product
4 Trains personnel responsible for the use of the product. Maintain a record of all training conducted on the product in accordance with the Organization Training Program Procedure (IMSP480).
5 Registers software products in accordance with the Configuration Management Procedure (IMSP585).
6 Ensures that access to and use of the product is in accordance with the agreement with the supplier.

2. Procuring Supplier Services

2.1 Planning Procurement of Services

Step Action
1 The information on what project work should be procured from suppliers, may be derived from the following:
  • Project definition report
  • Project requirements
  • Project software development plan
2 The Project Manager obtains funding and resource commitment for the life of the project by:
  • Requesting and justifying resource commitment for the supplier.
  • Obtaining the Manager's review and approval of the supplier funding and resource request.

2.2 Identifying Candidate Suppliers

Step Action
1 If the service has to be requested from:
  • Internal suppliers, proceeds to section 2.4.
  • External suppliers, continues to the next step.
2 Obtains information about the possible suppliers using a request for information (RFI) and prepares a set of criteria against which the responses to the RFI will be evaluated.
Captures information provided from suppliers' responses to the RFI in pre-qualification vendor analysis.
3 Evaluates the suppliers based on pre-defined selection criteria and identifies a list of candidate suppliers. Selection may also be based on:
  • Client preference
  • Internal recommendations
  • Subcontractor status or experience
  • Stakeholder recommendations (For this purpose, stakeholders may include client and project architect recommendations)
  • Trade shows
  • Internet information

Consider the use of Key Decisions Procedure (IMSP445) to assist in the selection of subcontractors.
4 Prepares a draft supplier selection report, which can include, for example, a simple list of candidate suppliers or a detailed record both of the RFI procedure and the results of using it.

2.3 Finalizing Supplier Agreement

Step Action
1 The subcontractor contract should be based upon:
  • request for proposal
  • subcontractor's proposal
  • subsequent negotiations (if conducted)

The contract should contain the statement of work at a minimum, plus the following information:
  • Scope of work
  • Key assumptions & dependencies
  • Description of deliverables
  • Schedule
  • Acceptance criteria and procedure
  • Costs
  • Change management
  • Measurements
  • Subcontractor performance evaluation criteria and procedure
  • Post-development support or warranty
  • Intellectual capital rights
  • Termination clause
2 Supplier Agreement is finalized by:
  • Adjusting the supplier plans with supplier's concurrence to improve the match between the objectives of the project and the capabilities and commitments of the supplier.
  • Assessing whether the supplier deliverables and delivery conditions are clearly defined.

2.4 Procuring Services from Internal Suppliers


The Project Manager:
Step Action
1 Creates a Service Request and a set of Requirements for the work requested from the identified provider, which in this case, is another group within your organization.
2 Meets with providing group within your organization, as needed, to discuss and clarify the request. Verifies that the Service Request and Requirements are reviewed with the provider, to ensure completeness.
Reaches agreement with providing group within your organization that the request and requirements are mutually understood.
3 Reviews the draft Document of Understanding (DOU) prepared by the provider and returns it to them for any rework needed. Repeats each time rework is performed.
4 Approves the completed and final DOU.

2.5 Starting Subcontractor


The Project Manager:
Step Action
1 Prepares supplier orientation material for the project orientation meeting. This meeting should address the following objectives:
  • Build relationship between all parties
  • Confirm the project objectives
  • Ensure all parties understand their roles and responsibilities
  • Obtain the commitment of all parties to the project
  • Set realistic expectations
  • Communicate the standards and procedures to be followed
  • Ensure open communications
  • Set up administrative processes
2 Fulfills the obligations towards the subcontractor as per the agreement in the statement of work, such as, providing the required working environment, equipment and office materials to the subcontractor when the subcontractor is located at your organization's location.
3 Produces the following subcontractor plans, as part of the SOW:
  • Development Plan
  • Quality Plan
  • Configuration Management Plan
4 The procedure to monitor the subcontractor's Quality activities should be designed to ensure that the subcontractor plans include:
  • Peer reviews and inspections
  • Full life cycle deliverables testing
  • User acceptance testing
5 The feedback to the subcontractor on how well the contract requirements are being met, should not include an appraisal of the performance of the subcontractor.
Formal evaluation provides an opportunity for the subcontractor to obtain feedback on whether or not its contractual commitments with your organization are being met.
Both strengths and weaknesses in the following areas should be included in the evaluation:
  • Quality of deliverables, including documentation
  • Timeliness
  • Personnel skills and experience
  • Flexibility and responsiveness
  • Communications
  • Project management
  • Administration

2.6 Auditing or Reviewing Subcontractors


The Project Manager:
Step Action
1 Periodically reviews or audits the project and discusses an initial set of review findings with subcontractor personnel to reach an agreement on the findings.
The reviews are held with the subcontractor's management and should include:
  • Technical reviews
  • Milestone reviews
  • Issues reviews
  • Quality reviews
  • Overall status reviews
  • Configuration Management Baseline Audit
  • Project Change Management Status Review

If, as a result of the periodic reviews listed above, it is determined that the subcontractor is not fulfilling the terms of the subcontractor plan for the development and delivery of committed work products, the Project Manager has to identify these issues and resolve them in accordance with the Issue Management Procedure (IMSP620).
2 Collects and records any information that will contribute to estimating the cost and determining responsibilities for the compliance incident.

2.7 Assessing Supplier Readiness to Deliver


The Project Manager:
Step Action
1 Raises issues, change requests and actions, in issue management documentation, change management documentation and action management documentation respectively, if the delivery (or part of it) does not appear to be on track.
2 Adjusts project schedules, as necessary, if reassignments of validation or other activities have been identified. Updates other work plans and quality documents, if required (for example, to fine tune acceptance procedures).

2.8 Releasing Subcontractors


The Project Manager:
Step Action
1 The following should be performed while assessing completion of mutual commitments against the terms of the contract defined by the parties involved:
  • Review the contract with the subcontractor to ensure that all commitments have been met
  • Verify that all deliverables have been received and that formal acceptance has been confirmed
  • Verify that the subcontractor has set up resources and processes required to meet the provisions of the warranty clause
  • Ensure that there are no open issues or action items

2 Collects any project assets in the possession of the subcontractor.
De-installs any technical environment provided to the subcontractor during the project.
Reclaims facilities, documents and material provided to individuals working for the subcontractor.

Appendices


There are none.

  • Americas, Asia Pacific and Europe Middle East Africa (EMEA) Geographies Support

  • Global Services Procurement (GSP) Client Resource Guide that provides toll-free number for Geography Procurement Information Centers

For more information or aid in evaluating the products, refer to the IBM Global Supply site. Establishing weights and scores for each product being considered will be more useful in evaluating products.
For IBM managed organizations, the Project Manager can select candidate suppliers informally, without using an RFI, by identifying candidate subcontractors from the IBM Global procurement list of preferred suppliers or subcontractors.
Refer to IBM Global Supply website for further details of purchasing subcontractor service via the IBM Global Procurement Process and releasing subcontractor service via the IBM Global Procurement Process.
Finalizing can also be done by reviewing the proposed contract with IBM Legal and IBM Global Procurement representatives.
