Guideline: Organizational Process Assurance
Use this guideline to obtain guidance on how to perform the organizational and project specific activities of the Project Quality Analyst (PQA).
Main Description

Purpose

Use this guideline to:
Obtain guidance on how to perform the organizational and project specific activities of the Project Quality Analyst (PQA).

Scope

This guideline applies to:
  • The performance of process assurance activities for a project or organizational unit/team
  • The activities performed by the PQA Team for an organization

Overview

This guideline includes:
  • PQA's involvement in project and Quality Planning
  • Guidance for the PQA on determining when to schedule a project's quality activities
  • Guidance for the PQA and Project Manager on analyzing measurements at the project and organization levels
  • Guidance for the Project Manager on reporting measurements to the organization

Details

1 Project Quality Analyst Functions

1.1 Quality Activity Planning Guidance

Quality-related reviews are planned during Project Planning by the project manager with the input and assistance of the PQA and by others on the Project team.
Quality Review activities may include:
  • Work Product Inspections, also called Peer Reviews
  • Process Assurance Reviews, performed by the project's PQA
  • Quality Assurance Reviews, performed in accordance with the organization's quality assurance business rules, as appropriate.
  • System Engineering and Architecture Reviews
  • Other Reviews such as technical reviews for compliance to design and coding standards. This may be a special case where tools are used to verify compliance and the review does not fit the mold of a normal Work Product Inspection.
For projects, all quality-related reviews are planned and documented in the project's project quality plan.
For the PQA Group, all PQA activities, including Process Assurance Reviews across projects and organizational teams/units, are planned, monitored and controlled via the project quality analyst group plan (PQA group plan).
The PQA focus is on performing Process Assurance Reviews; however, as part of reviewing Work Product Inspection Procedure compliance, it is recommended that the PQA attends and/or checks a representative sample of the project's Work Product Inspections.

1.2 Process Assurance Reviews

All Process Assurance Reviews are performed as scheduled in the PQA group plan and in accordance with the Process Assurance Review Procedure (IMSP232) and apply to:
  • Projects (via the process assurance review - project)
  • Organizational unit/team (via the process assurance review - organization)
It is important to understand that Process Assurance Reviews must include assessment of both the process and the associated product usage, in accordance with the requirements of the relevant documented procedures, policies and standards. This is essential to satisfy the Specific Goal requirements of the CMMI Process Area - Process and Product Quality Assurance.

1.2.1 Process Assurance Reviews - Projects

The project Process Assurance Reviews are performed in accordance with the Process Assurance Review Procedure (IMSP232) and using the process assurance review report - project templates.
The PM and the PQA use the project's work type(s) and work classification(s) and consider the project's characteristics such as effort, risk, and experience of the PM and project team, to decide upon the appropriate type and frequency of Process Assurance Reviews for the project.
For example:
  • Effort and duration of the project - a very short, low-risk project may have only one Process Assurance Review.
  • If the organization has shown institutionalization of the procedures, then the review schedule could be altered to be less frequent for projects that are identified as low risk or low effort. This should be agreed between the Project Manager, the organization's Engineering Process Group (EPG) and the organization's Senior Management. Organizations that have small work efforts bundled into releases should be reviewed as projects.
The coverage of Process Assurance Reviews will vary depending on the project's current point in the lifecycle, the work classification and complexity of the project and the maturity of the project team.
Each review examines different procedural compliance steps based on what should have occurred by the time of the review.
The different review types are:
1. Initial Review: Completed after planning is complete
2. Interim Review: On-going during project lifecycle, either at key milestones (for example, project phase end) or at predetermined intervals (for example, every 3 or 6 months; this is common for PM Structure or Service Contract projects) as defined in the project quality plan
3. Completion Review: As the project approaches completion (typically during performance of Project Completion activities)
4. Close Review: Shortly after the project has completed all activities

1.2.2 Process Assurance Reviews - Organizational

The organizational Process Assurance Reviews are also performed in accordance with the Process Assurance Review Procedure (IMSP232) and using the process assurance review report - organization templates.
The scope of each review depends on the organizational set-up for the organization-level processes and activities, for example - metrics management, organizational training, Engineering Process Group (EPG), conduct of Critical Thread Reviews (CTRs). As different individuals/teams are typically responsible for these organization all activities, each set of processes (and the associated procedures and products) may be expected to be reviewed separately.

The frequency of organizational Process Assurance Reviews is typically less than that for discrete projects or PM Structure projects, for example every three, six or twelve months and is also dependent upon the extent of institutionalization (sustained process usage and sustained high-levels of process compliance). As a minimum, they should be reviewed annually.
The PQA Group and the Process Assurance Review activities also need to be "objectively evaluated". These reviews should be performed by a PQA subject matter expert, independent from the organizational PQA Group being reviewed.from the organizational PQA Group being reviewed.

1.2.3 Process Assurance Review Reports - Findings Rating


The purpose of this section is to outline the recommended rating scale for process assurance review report findings.
Each review question, which has actually been reviewed, should be rated as one of the following:
  • Comply: Fully compliant with the procedure and product requirements;
  • Partial: Compliant with most, but not all of the procedure and product requirements, but no major shortfall;
  • Fail: Fails to comply adequately with the procedure and product requirements, either completely or with major shortfalls;
  • Not Applicable: The team is not required to perform the activity that is the focus of the question, or has an approved policy or process exception request relating to the procedure and product requirements.

2 Other PQA Functions

The PQAs are trained in all project management activities, and are expected to act as mentors for Project Managers as part of their function as a project team member and stakeholder. The PQA should make every effort to establish that relationship upon being assigned to a project, helping the PM understand how to perform the required project activities throughout the life of the project. The PQA should be perceived as a help to the project by providing advice and direction, and not as an auditor.
The PQA is required to track deviation and non-compliance items that arise from Process Assurance reviews, by logging them in the organizational PQA non-compliance log template, and managing to closure those items they have logged.
It is the Project Manager's responsibility to ensure that the related non-compliant action items and associated action management documentation are closed, and to notify the PQA when they are complete. The PQA must monitor and report their status by updating the organizational PQA non-compliance log template upon notification of status changes. The PQA should discuss the initial finding and subsequent results with the Project Manager prior to any distribution.
The PQA collects additional measurements from each project and provides them to the Project Manager for collection at the organization level in the process assurance review report -organization.

3. PQA and Project Manager Functions

3.1 Creating the PQA Team

  • The PQA must demonstrate an understanding of Project Management and all procedures in use within the organization
  • There must be an individual designated to manage the PQA Team (the Project manager role) whose responsibilities include:
    • Managing PQA resources, by tracking their availability and assigning them to projects
    • Leading the PQA team activities like collection and analysis of quality measurements from the projects and reporting of results to the organization
  • The PQA may not be full time resources performing this function. The practitioners, such as Project Managers, Subject Matter Experts, Functional Experts, or any member qualified to perform this role, can perform this function as part time. The Senior Management must ensure that the Project Quality Analysts are appropriately skilled and trained to perform this function.
  • The organization must determine its approach on assembling the PQA team based on the size, type, and configuration of the organization. Whatever way the organization chooses must support all Process Assurance requirements covering procedures, policies, and standards, as well as the following concepts:
    • Objective evaluation of project and organizational team, policy, standards, process, procedure and product adherence.
    • Objective evaluation of project work products (note this is discrete from other Validation and Verification activities, such as Work Product Inspections and Testing).
    • Support for and collaboration with the PMs during the project, particularly the planning phases.
  • There is no prescribed way to create the PQA team. Each organization must assess its needs, skills, and resource availability, but some proven ways are:
    • Assign the appropriate number of full-time PQAs to the team, and establish a reporting line for them that is independent of all projects. This option is usually viable only for large organizations.
    • Enlist the use of part-time PQAs to the team, this may include the use of managers, and assign them to projects that are outside their current reporting lines.

3.2 Managing PQA resources

The Project Manager needs to maintain a schedule of each PQA's commitments, compiled from the project schedules the PQAs collected during project planning. The PQA resources should be managed in much the same way as project resources. It is suggested that the PQA Team develop a schedule of PQA activity that is maintained for a period of time - a year, quarter, month, or whatever seems reasonable based on the organization's characteristics. The project quality analyst group plan (PQA group plan) is specifically intended to support planning and control of PQA Group activities, especially Process Assurance Reviews.
In addition, the Project Manager should have information about upcoming projects for some future period of time at the organization level, and should use that information in conjunction with the project quality analyst group plan (PQA group plan) to predict needs and availability of PQA resources.

3.3 PQA Team Tracking, Measuring, and Reporting Guidance

3.3.1 PQA Reporting Guidance

The PQA reports and collects measurements for each project in accordance with the Process Assurance Review Procedure (IMSP232) and Process Assurance Management Procedure (IMSP237).
The organizational PQA measurement report is provided for use in reporting PQA activities. If the organization's PQA team decides to use that report, it should tailor the report form to support the measurements being reported by the organization. The content of the organizational PQA measurement report is sourced from a consolidation of data from the -
  • project quality analyst PQA group plan;
  • process assurance review report - project
  • process assurance review report - organization
  • organizational PQA non-compliance log template
In addition to the organizational PQA measurement report, the Project Manager may use a range of additional spreadsheets or presentations to summarize key findings and trends, tailored to the needs of the recipients.

3.3.2 PQA Team Reporting Guidance

The PQA Team should work with senior management to establish the quality measurements to be collected for the organization and the frequency and manner for reporting them. The PQA team may decide to collect additional measurements for the purpose of analyzing effectiveness of the organization's processes, and/or the success of the projects' use of the processes. Below is a list of measurements from which the organization may choose, but there may be others created by the organization.
The format of the measurement may be:
  • Raw numbers (that is 4 reviews were scheduled and 2 were completed)
  • Percentage of scheduled activities that were completed (that is 50% of scheduled reviews were completed)
  • Any other method that makes sense to management and is specific
The measurements MAY be reported at any of the following levels:
  • Project
  • Department
  • Application area
  • Manager area
  • Any other combination that makes sense for the organization
The measurements MUST be reported at the organization level, and it is recommended that they be collected monthly, with year-to-date totals and comparisons to prior years showing trends.
The organization's Engineering Process Group (EPG) should use compliance data to identify areas of weakness in the procedures or training needs in the organization.

3.3.3 Recommended Measurements

The Measurements should be defined together with the Measurements Group of the related organization.
  • Hours planned and spent for the following categories for each project (should be recorded in the project's schedule), and for PQA Team activities (should be recorded by the PQA Team):
    • PQA planning activities
    • PQA reporting/communications activities (preparing and delivering PQA reports)
      PQA related training
    • PQA consulting (for all mentoring and for the initial review of the project plan and reviews of the re-planning documents)
    • Process Assurance Reviews (executing the review and tracking the non-conformances)
    • PQA work product audits (executing and documenting)
  • Non-conformances opened, to be resolved and closed, by Procedure and by Project.
  • Non-conformances by process area, for the purpose of process analysis
  • Coverage of Process Assurance Reviews by process area; this is recommended when process assurance activities are scaled.
