Guideline: Sponsor Agreement Management
Use this guideline to manage sponsor agreements, which include activities to develop, review, negotiate, maintain and close the agreement with the sponsor.

Main Description

Purpose


Use this guideline to:
Manage sponsor agreements, which include activities to develop, review, negotiate, maintain and close the agreement with the sponsor.

Scope


This guideline applies to:
All projects and activities with external sponsors or clients.

Overview


This guideline includes:
  • Developing the sponsor agreement
  • Reviewing and maintaining the sponsor agreement
  • Closing the sponsor agreement

Refer to the following procedures for details on developing, reviewing and closing an agreement:
  • Project Planning Procedure (IMSP491)
  • Response Management Procedure (IMSP390)
  • Project Close Procedure (IMSP240)

Details

1. Develop the sponsor agreement


The Project Manager:
  • Initiates development of the sponsor agreement at the start of the project, when the target solution, project scope, deliverables or delivery of services, costs, and time scales are defined in sufficient detail for a binding agreement to be negotiated.
  • Provides the pricing department or an equivalent with the pricing estimates and any other information required to determine a project price and payment stream.
  • Reviews any terms and conditions proposed or required by the sponsor that may have been specified in the request for proposal or similar document. If the sponsor requires any nonstandard terms and conditions, seeks advice from the legal department and reconciles any contradictions with the base terms and conditions.
  • Builds the sponsor agreement, integrating the components from initial project plans and project definition. Documents the sponsor agreement using the agreement.
  • Verifies that the agreement is not open to misinterpretation, and reviews it with any appropriate stakeholders.
  • Provides the documents for the processes that cover business approval and formal signing of the agreement.
  • Develops the project decision structure to document the decision-making processes that involve the project manager and the sponsor.
  • Obtains any internal approvals on the agreement that are required by the organization's policies.
  • Negotiates the agreement with the sponsor and, if appropriate, changes the proposed agreement to reflect them.
  • Obtains internal approval for any revisions to the agreement. Repeats the previous and current steps, until both parties are satisfied with the proposed agreement.
  • Submits the agreement for formal approval by both parties.
    Files the original signed copy of the approved agreement in accordance with the organization's business policy.

2. Review and maintain the sponsor agreement

2.1 Review the agreement


The Project Manager:
  • Periodically reviews the agreement and any amendments, to verify that:
    • It remains an accurate reflection of the project.
    • All project management activity takes place against a background of a thorough understanding of the content of the agreement.
  • Reviews the agreement and any discrepancies with the sponsor to obtain the other party's perspective and approval in principle to any proposed changes.
  • Takes the action required to get the project back in line with the agreement, as necessary.
  • Creates Change requests or Issue documents as necessary to handle any discrepancies that will impact any element of the project that is subject to change control.
    Refer to the Change Management Procedure (IMSP610) for information about raising and managing a change request.
    Refer to the Issue Management Procedure (IMSP620) for information on identifying issues and resolving them.

2.2 Revise the agreement


The Project Manager:
  • Obtains any necessary approvals inside the organization for any required amendments to the agreement as per the Change requests or Issue documents.
  • Negotiates the amendments with the sponsor and obtains formal approvals on the agreement from the organization and the sponsor. Files the original signed copy of the approved agreement in accordance with the organization's business policy.
  • Updates the project's baseline and plans as necessary.

2.3 Authorize sponsor billing


The Project Manager:
  • Reviews the agreement to determine if any milestones that trigger payments have been achieved, or if any scheduled payments are due.
  • Checks the agreement to verify that the exact conditions for payment have been met. If there is any reason for doubt, verifies with the sponsor that it is agreed that all the criteria for billing have been met.
  • Uses the organization's business process to authorize the billing department to invoice the sponsor.

2.4 Assess project performance


The Project Manager:
  • Identifies any gaps between the project's accomplishments and the objectives in the agreement.
  • Considers any changes that occurred during the period that will affect the business needs that were originally part of the project scope as stated in the agreement.
  • Updates the business needs and priorities. Determines joint objectives with the sponsor and updates the agreement, as required.

3. Close the sponsor agreement


The Project Manager:
  • Verifies that all work has been completed in accordance with the agreement, and ensures that the following have been properly completed and closed:
    • Actions
    • Change requests
    • Compliance incidents
    • Defects
    • Issues
  • Takes action to address any remaining concerns and to ensure that all outstanding items are completed.
  • Writes a formal project closure letter to the sponsor and closes the sponsor agreement.
  • Gathers sponsor satisfaction feedback and uses it to evaluate the overall project.
