Guideline: P3 - Process, Performance and People Critical Thread Reviews
Use this guideline to ensure the objectives of the P3 - Process, Performance and People Critical Thread Reviews (CTRs) are obtained by further defining the procedure tasks and the agenda items for each meeting.

Main Description

Purpose


Use this guideline to:
Ensure the objectives of the P3 - Process, Performance and People Critical Thread Reviews (CTRs) are obtained by further defining the procedure tasks and the agenda items for each meeting.

Scope


This guideline applies to:
An organization describing how to conduct the P3 - Process, Performance and People Critical Thread Reviews (CTRs) meetings, including defining the communications management plan (organizational) for P3 CTRs, enabling the project portfolio tracking system and conducting the reviews using the expanded description of the agenda items.

Note: The gray shaded parts of this asset support a procedure or other asset that is required for attainment of SEI Capability Maturity Model Integration (CMMI) level 4 or 5. These parts are optional for organizations not using CMMI as a basis for process improvement or organizations targeting a lower maturity level. However, such organizations may find it helpful to implement these aspects of this asset.

Overview


The P3 - Process, Performance and People Critical Thread Reviews (CTRs) are an established communication vehicle used by the various levels of management throughout the organization, to ensure that activities are managed consistently. These CTRs include a standard agenda, with regularly discussed topics, which:
  • Ensures management activities are proceeding as per the plans and schedules.
  • Identifies issues that need to be escalated to a higher level of management.
  • Identifies items to be added to the Process Improvement Plans.
  • Determines any required corrective actions.

Details

1. Communications management plan for P3 CTR (organization level)


The communication management plan (organizational) for P3 CTR provides the basis for defining the organization level plan. However, the organization context may lead to a need to tailor this template. Some typical situations and their tailoring approach includes:
(a) Matrix organization structure with several lines of management providing oversight of the project.
One approach is to managing all lines of management needs in one communications management plan (organizational) for P3 CTR, by identifying separately the CTRs for each line of management. In a very large and fragmented organization this may be a challenge. An alternative is for each line of management to manage their own communications management plan (organizational) for P3 CTR and require the project manager to select and integrate those relevant to the project.
(b) Integrated P3 Critical Thread Reviews
The organization size and allocation of responsibility for process, performance and people functions in an organization may lead to one person having responsibility across several pillars of review. In this case it may be more efficient to merge the P3 reviews at a single level. The agenda templates may be tailored to achieve that intent.
The first level review is described using a merged agenda, as the information from the project manager feeds into each pillar of the P3 CTR. A merged agenda is an effective use of the project managers time. The allocation of responsibilities in a organization may lead to the splitting of this integrated agenda into the component pillars of P3.

2. Project portfolio tracking system


The complexity of collecting, and reporting information across a range of projects in a portfolio typically leads to the need for this system to be automated with an IT system.
(a) A major challenge in establishing a project portfolio tracking system is the efficient collection of information which may be in other project level tools. For example, the solution development technical environment of a project may track defects in dedicated tools. If a lot of information has to be re-entered into another tool for portfolio reporting purposes only, the value of that effort may be questioned. The same concern arises with project management tools contain schedule or financial information which may need to be re-entered. There are benefits in tool integration or data exchange between the tools which contain the information and the project portfolio tracking system which reports on that information.
(b) A challenge with portfolio information being distributed across several tools is how this information is merged in portfolio reports , For example, the financial information may be in one tool and quality metrics in another,. It is essential that identification of the entity called 'the project' is consistent across all tools if there is an expectation of building portfolio reports of merged information.
(c) To ensure complete portfolio reports information about every project must be available, and it must be current to the reporting period.

3. P3 Level 1 CTR Agenda Items


The purpose of the P3 level 1 CTR is to review project status report for insight into the project. Validate appropriate project management discipline is being applied. Verify and validate all key information is reported, and hence validate the project health assessment.
Refer to the P3 Level 1 CTR Agenda attached to the meeting documentation.
First line manager review responsibilities may include:
  • Respond to escalation of issues, either by accepting and hence owning them, or re-directing the escalation to other stakeholders.
  • Respond to and manage procedures requiring higher levels of approval than the project manager is authorized. Examples include:
    • Change requests above clip levels
    • Re-baselining project plans, ensure sponsor agreement documented

Review and update portfolio tracking tool records to ensure information is valid for current reporting period.
The following steps includes standard agenda items to include in the P3 Level 1 Critical Thread Review:

Step
Action

1
Action Items Review:
Review actions from the previous meeting recorded in the meeting documentation - P3 Level 1 CTR Agenda.

2
Next Line Management Level Performance Meeting - Debrief Report:
Communicate highlights and actions passed down from the next higher management level P3 CTRs.

3
Project health, status review:
Review the health of each project, defined within the project status report, which is created in accordance with the Project Monitoring and Control Procedure (IMSP421), as follows:
  • All projects and their current status are reviewed at first line level reviews
  • All projects with a project health review risk assessment of high or medium, or with a project health classification of 'B', 'C' or 'D' (when using letter grades) or 'red' or 'yellow' (when using color grades) are reviewed at second level reviews
  • All projects with a project health review risk assessment of high, or with a project health classification of 'C' or 'D' (when using letter grades) or 'red' (when using color grades) are reviewed at third line and organizational level reviews

For details regarding the risk assessment, refer to the Continuous Risk Management Procedure (IMSP600).
Review all project status reports, created in accordance with the Project Monitoring and Control Procedure (IMSP421), as follows:
  • At first line and second line level CTRs, review the status of each project. Each project review includes and is not limited to the following:
    • Status
    • Significant risks
    • Significant open issues
    • Dependencies (both on other projects and to other projects)
    • Significant changes or significant number of changes
  • Significant variances from:
    • Plans/estimates in actual or projected effort
    • Cost or schedule
    • Any other items of concern or significance, as appropriate

At third line and organizational level CTRs, review by exception or rolled up as appropriate

4
Process Assurance Review Report - Project Review:
Review the process assurance review report - project, received during the review period.
All reports are reviewed at first line level review, where only a sample of reports are reviewed at second line and third line level reviews.

5
Deliverables Sign-off Review:
Review the verification of process completion, deliverables sign-off, and release activities occurring during the review period, using the documentation created in accordance with the Deliverables Release and Acceptance Procedure (IMSP540), at first line, second line, and third line level CTRs.

6
Quality and Testing Activities Review:
Review the status of quality and testing activities as reported in accordance with the Project Monitoring and Control Procedure (IMSP421).

7
Defects Review:
Review magnitude and trends in numbers of defects found and defects fixed in work products, using the details extracted in accordance with the Defect Management Procedure (IMSP520).

8
Root Cause Analysis Results:
Review the results of the Root Cause Analysis by project and the associated status of any related action items as reported in accordance with the Project Monitoring and Control Procedure (IMSP421).
Analyze the Root Cause Analysis results and status for RCA measures and for possible improvement information, if any, which can be derived from planning and performing RCA.
Examples of RCA measures are:
· Number of root causes removed
· Cost and Effort involved in the root cause analysis and resolution activities
For details on Root Cause Analysis, refer to the Root Cause Analysis Procedure (IMSP525).

9
Financial Metrics Review:
Review financial metrics appropriate to your organization. Refer to the Organizational Measurement and Analysis Procedure (IMSP473).

10
Contractual Metrics Review:
Review contractual metrics appropriate to your organization. Refer to the Organizational Measurement and Analysis Procedure (IMSP473).

11
Time Recording Review:
Review time recording appropriate to the organization. Refer to the Organizational Measurement and Analysis Procedure (IMSP473).

12
Key Client Contacts:
Review significant contact with key clients during the review period, including who, when, purpose, outcome and follow-up actions.

13
General Business:
Provide an opportunity for CTR attendees to raise other business matters, which have not already been addressed.
Record items for future meetings


4. Process CTR Agenda Items


The purpose of the Process CTR is to assess the implementation of the organization's processes across the portfolio of projects, identify opportunities or needs for improvement in the processes or their deployment.
Refer to the Process CTR Agenda attached to the meeting documentation.
The following steps includes standard agenda items to include in the Process Critical Thread Review:
Step Action

1
Action Items Review:
Review actions from the previous meeting recorded in the meeting documentation - Process CTR Agenda.

2
Next Line Management Level Process CTR:
Debrief Report Communicate highlights and actions passed down from the next higher management level Process CTR.

3
Process Assurance Review Report - Project Review:
Review the process assurance review report - project, received during the review period.
All reports are reviewed at first line level review, where only a sample of reports are reviewed at second line and third line level reviews.

4
Process Assurance Review Report - Organization Review:
Review the process assurance review report - organization, created in accordance with the Process Assurance Procedure (IMSP232).
All reports are reviewed at first line level review, where only a sample of reports are reviewed at second line and third line level reviews.

5
Project Health Reports Review:
Review project health reports received during the review period, as follows:
  • All project health reports are reviewed at first line level reviews
  • All project health reports with a risk assessment of high or medium, or with a project health classification of 'B', 'C' or 'D' (when using letter grades) or 'red' or 'yellow' (when using color grades) are reviewed at second level reviews
  • All project health reports with a risk assessment of high, or with a project health classification of 'C' or 'D' (when using letter grades) or 'red' (when using color grades) are reviewed at third line and organizational level reviews
  • Activities for software quality management and quantitative process management are reviewed at the first line level reviews on a periodic and event driven basis
  • Activities for software quality management and quantitative process management are reviewed at the fourth line level reviews on a periodic basis

For details regarding the risk assessment, refer to the Continuous Risk Management Procedure (IMSP600).

Verification Process Review:
Review the verification process and resolve any identified issues at first line, second line, and third line level CTRs.

7
Testing Process Review:
Review the testing process and resolve any identified issues, at first line, second line, and third line level CTRs.

8
Business Area Review:
Review Business Area Review (BAR) results received during the review period.
It is essential that all managers review all reports.

9
Project Risks, Issues and Defect Prevention activities.

10
Deliverables Sign-off Review:
Review the verification of process completion, deliverables sign-off, and release activities occurring during the review period, using the documentation created in accordance with the Deliverables Release and Acceptance Procedure (IMSP540), at first line, second line, and third line level CTRs.
This agenda item is not applicable for organizational level CTRs.

11
Process Improvement Plan Review:
Review the status of process improvement activities and Engineering Process Group (EPG) activities, as follows:
  • At the first line, second line and third line level CTRs; the EPG representative presents the Process Improvement Plan for the review period
  • At the organization level CTRs; the EPG representative presents the Integrated Process Improvement Plan

12
Process Improvement Review:
Review the organizational unit's process improvement opportunities, in accordance with the Process and Technology Improvement Procedure (IMSP470).
General Business Provide an opportunity for CTR attendees to raise other business matters, which have not already been addressed.

13
Organizational process management activities review (senior management on a periodic basis; project manager on both a periodic and event-driven basis).

14
Quantitative project management activities review (senior management on a periodic basis; project manager on both a periodic and event-driven basis).

15
General business:
Provide an opportunity for attendees to raise other appropriate matters not already addressed.

5. Performance CTR Agenda Items


The purpose of the Performance CTR is to assess the status and health of the specified portfolio of projects. The portfolio status and health is assessed by considering the status and health of each project, as well as the portfolio aggregated and trending information.
Refer to the Performance CTR Agenda attached to the meeting documentation.
Recommended techniques for assessing many projects is to be selective in the information reported, and focus on troubled projects or watch list projects which have been assessed in the first level project review as potentially facing challenges to be successful.

6. People CTR Agenda Items


The purpose of the People CTR is to monitor the resource assignments across the portfolio of projects, identify opportunities or needs for improvement in the people management of the organization.
Refer to the People CTR Agenda attached to the meeting documentation.
The following steps includes standard agenda items to include in the People Critical Thread Review:
Step Action

1
Action Items Review:
Review actions from the previous meeting recorded in the meeting documentation - People CTR Agenda.

2
Next Line Management Level People CTR - Debrief Report:
Communicate highlights and actions passed down from the next higher management level People CTR.

3
Key People Management Metrics Review: Review key people management metrics appropriate to your organization, such as resignations, separations, or sick leave.
Refer to the Organizational Measurement and Analysis Procedure (IMSP473).

4
Resource Allocation Review and Resource Balancing Review:
Review resource allocation and balancing by:
  • Maintaining a rolling three month resource allocation plan
  • Considering any impact on utilization and Skills Development Plans

For more information, refer to the Organizational Measurement and Analysis Procedure (IMSP473).

5
Calendar Review:
Review upcoming events and identify items to be added to the organizational calendar.
All organizations should maintain a nine-month rolling calendar, which rolls up into the Organizational Calendar.

6
Invite Guest Speakers to talk on Special Topics:
Provide an opportunity to invite guest speakers to enhance organizational knowledge on special topics.

7
Achievements and Awards Review:
Identify personal achievements and recommend awards.

8
Skills Development Plan Review:
Review the skills development plan as follows:
  • At the organizational level CTRs, review the Organizational Skills Development Summary
  • At second line and third line level CTRs, review the rolled up view of skills requirements from the projects
  • At all line level CTRs, review the following:
    • Skills Development Plans created in accordance with the Project Planning Procedure (IMSP491)
    • Status of Individual Development Plans (IDPs)

9
Annual Leave Plan Review:
Review the Annual Leave Plan.

10
Organizational Structure Review:
Review the organization structure and identify any updates required, including updates to employee administration forms.

11
Special Projects Review:
Review special projects, which are opportunities to mentor the best people in the organization and gain value added contributions to the business.

12
Succession Plan Review:
Review the accuracy and currency of succession plans, considering any needed updates.

13
General Business:
Provide an opportunity for CTR attendees to raise other business matters, which have not already been addressed.
