Role: Project Portfolio Manager
This role is responsible for obtaining the business results expected by the portfolio sponsor from all projects, programs (or both) included in the portfolio.
Role Sets: Management, Manager
Relationships
Additionally Performs
  • Report to delivery organization management, escalate issues (IMSP421)
Main Description
This role will determine the set of plans by which the portfolio business objectives will be met and the controls to enable adjustment before failure. There will be regularly scheduled portfolio reviews facilitated by this role with the portfolio sponsor and portfolio stakeholders, to provide management visibility of the status of the project portfolio. This role will establish the Project Portfolio Management System to create the environment to manage the project portfolio and will exercise this responsibility within the business constraints that their organization has established. This role will use documented processes and procedures to collect, assess, and manage information about the projects and programs in the project portfolio. This role will work directly with the project managers and program managers to determine the procedure used to gather the information needed to manage the portfolio.
Staffing
Skills
  • Expertise in project portfolio management disciplines
  • Knowledge of project management and program management disciplines
  • Capability of monitoring, tracking and reporting projects
  • Analytical capability of reviewing reports and interviewing to assess the underlying project health and performance
  • Business value management skills, tracking how business value can be, and is, delivered through the project portfolio
  • Communications skills to a wide range of stakeholders, including executives, clients, project managers, program managers and team members
Assignment ApproachesThis role's assignments are often associated with project office or program office teams in larger projects or programs, or in the project office of a business executive responsible for a portfolio of projects within their organizational, departmental, functional, or geographical responsibility.

The size, diversity or complexity of projects within the project portfolio will determine the level of skills or experience warranted for the assignment.
