Artifact: Communications Management Plan (Organizational) for P3 CTR (ART 0577)
A document to describe how the process, performance and people critical thread reviews are to be conducted by the organization to achieve the communication transfer up and down the management levels in the organization, concerning the health and status of the portfolio of projects being managed.

Purpose
The purpose is to enable project portfolio managers, organizations or programs to describe how projects within their jurisdiction shall be required to contribute to the process for reviewing projects and communicating information about the health and status of projects up the organization levels.

Description
Main Description

This plan describes the activities by the organization in reviewing the health and status of the individual projects and a portfolio of projects, by identifying at organization level:

  • Which critical thread reviews which will occur, the agenda, who attends, and the reporting cycle schedule
  • The criteria for including projects in the critical thread reviews
  • The technical environment support for portfolio management including the portfolio tracking system, reports to be used, and how it is maintained.

A project manager uses this information as input to defining the project level communications management plan, as it represents one of the projects communication pathways. Depending on either business environment, projects may be subject to several portfolio reporting requests to support differing matrix management reporting chains.

Brief Outline

This plan contains the following components:

  • Plan overview
    • P3 CTR portfolio grouping and selection criteria
    • Overview of CTR activity table
  • Media
    • Identify the tools or technology for project portfolio tracking system
  • Scheduled meetings
    • Separate entries for each type and level of critical thread review, including attendees, agenda and reports submitted
  • Reporting
    • Descriptions of the portfolio reports, and how they are used
  • Other communications
  • Schedule
  • Assumptions, dependencies, risks
  • Costs

Key Considerations

This plan is defined by the project portfolio manager when the project portfolio tracking and system is being established. This could occur when a program is started, or when an existing delivery organization or service line decides to implement a portfolio tracking and reporting system. 

This plan contributes to a more complete project portfolio management system which may be defined at the same time, depending on the scope of activities required at the portfolio level.

It is recommended that the plan be defined and distributed to project managers prior to projects being started, as there may be implications for the project start up and planning activities from the directions in this plan.

Tailoring
Impact of not having

The lack of a plan to describe the process for reviewing a portfolio of projects can:

  • Undermine the value of management reviews of a project, leaving the project activities isolated and exposed to considerable risk
  • Enable in-appropriate responses to escalated issues, or other first level review responsibilities which may expose the project manager to delegated authority violations
  • Cause poor communications up and down the management levels with oversight responsibility for the project, resulting in missing business benefits and increasing project risk
Reasons for not needingAn organization, or a program with only one project may not require this plan. Project portfolio management is a discipline which applies across multiple projects.
