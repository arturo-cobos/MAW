Task: Define Service Operations
Service hierarchy contains services and operations. Defines nodes in a service hierarchy that will become operations.
Disciplines: Technical Architecture
Purpose

    Main Description

    Services must have operations in order to be invoked by a service requester. These may be evolved from services identified during process decomposition and aggregated during service specification and augmented by operations identified from detailed analysis of existing assets such as fine-grained mapping.

    Once you have made service exposure decisions, you look at the service hierarchy in the service model and decide which nodes will become operations on the service you have identified. Typically, the service hierarchy will have a decomposition of imperative verbs like Submit Order with a larger number of child nodes. Once you decide what your services are and their granularity, you can now designate which other elements of the service hierarchy are operations on a given service.
    Key Considerations
    Service Hierarchy will contain Services and Operations. At this point the architect needs to make a design decision and designate which nodes in the service hierarchy are operations. Also, when determining service composition and flow, the dynamic aspect of service interaction. Note that this is an iterative process.
