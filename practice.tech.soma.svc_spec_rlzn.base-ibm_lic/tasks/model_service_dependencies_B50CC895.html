      Main Description
      Describe the relationship between the service and the services and/or components it depends on. This may be considered similar to component modeling where we use a UML diagram to show dependency relationships between components. Here we show the same with services. This activity depicts how a given service is related or associated to other services or functional components. Service dependency can be either a processing dependency where a service is dependent on other services in the context of a business process or can be a functional dependency where a service is dependent on other services or functional components in order to provide the functionality required of it when it was invoked.

      E.g. Transfer Balance service relies on check balance, withdraw balance, deposit balance, notify customer. In most cases service dependencies will transfer back into service composition and flows.

      Detailed review of the service will often expose a set of dependencies among services and between services and underlying service components: the applications they rely on for realization of their functionality. Although most dependencies might be on other (exposed) services, some may be on functionality that has not been chosen to be exposed as a service. In the ideal world of SOA, everything is a service; in reality we might be starting out with a smaller number of services that depend on underlying components and this combination will be capable of delivering the required quality of services such as performance and availability.
