Task: Associate Use Cases with Service Operations
Associate (system) use-cases and show how they map to services and operations. The use-cases will be used to describe the internal functioning of the service/operations on the service.
Disciplines: Technical Architecture
Purpose

To identify system level use-cases to support operations on services. To identify the detailed behavior of the operation on the service through creation or refinement of system level use-cases.   Note: business use-cases correspond to services and system use-cases typically correspond to operations on that service.

      Main Description

      In general, services correspond to business use-cases or business processes, sub-processes or activities.

      In the SOA Solution Stack, we see a layer for processes and another for services. Note that these are practically, two sides of the same coin, so to speak. If the wiring of the business process is to be available to the service consumer, then we map that entity to the business process layer. If the service consumer is not going to be changing the wiring or composition (includes a sequencing of services) of the underlying services, and the service provider is the only party who will have control over changing the sequence of the composition (composed service), then we allocate that entity, in this case a composite service to the service layer.

      If the use cases are not available then the use cases and or the functional requirements needs to be created. If however the use cases exist then a thorough analysis of them is required. A service operation is usually a coarse grained business function that is implemented through the use of one or more system level use cases.

      In order to ensure proper granularity we need to take into account the following considerations:

      1. The business use-case should be mapped to a service

      2. A business use-case can consist of other business and/or system use-cases

      3. A system use-case will typically be mapped to a service operation.


      Key Considerations

      If use-case analysis has been done at the business level, then the use-cases corresponding to services should have been already identified. If this has not been done, then it should be done at this stage. Service operations need to have some level of detail on what is going on inside of them. If system level use-cases have already been identified, then we associate or map those system use-cases to the appropriate operations.
