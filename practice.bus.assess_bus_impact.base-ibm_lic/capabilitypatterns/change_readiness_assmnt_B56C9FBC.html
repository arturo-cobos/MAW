Work Product Descriptor (Artifact): Change Readiness Assessment
Change Readiness Assessment is a report that assesses the organization's commitment, readiness, and ability to accept and sustain the changes that will be required by an initiative.

Main Description

The report is based upon an initial scoping of the changes likely to occur with the project and their probable impact. The report highlights issues that will impede change relative to:

  • Sponsorship of the change
  • Attitudes to change formed by past experiences 
  • Culture of the organization

Key Considerations

One of the outputs of the change management workshop should be the identification of some key individuals who will drive the change in the target organization.  After the initial contact with these individuals, do not leave a gap in your communication with them until the assessment is complete.  These leaders should meet with project management early in the project to review the charter, project overview, guiding principles, and project team organization.  Ideally they should come to identify themselves as members (core or extended) of the project team and should work side by side with the team for the duration of the project.  The success or failure of the program will depend on the ability of all the project management team to:

  • Encourage effective sponsorship of the changes
  • Encourage all individuals to own the changes
  • Manage the resistance to the change by effective issues management

Be prepared before any meetings with sponsors and key stakeholders to speak comfortably about the role that they ideally should play.  Every conversation with a sponsor is an opportunity to encourage and coach them.  Sponsors are individuals who sanction the project and they should be actively involved in its progress.  Change sponsors must provide leadership to ensure that the process of change takes place successfully.  They must legitimize the change, provide a clear vision of what must occur, and be willing to demonstrate the organizational commitment necessary to execute and complete the project successfully.  Significant and successful change within a target population will not occur without effective and sustained sponsorship.

Key client sponsors need to understand the conclusions and evaluate and agree to the recommendations before they are carried forward into the subsequent work product, Transition Management Strategy.  Closely involve broad representation from management throughout the process, and seek input from multiple client sources.  Most of the content for this work product will be collected through discussions and events with clients at different levels of the organization.  Prepare for the reviews by anticipating their reactions to the conclusions and increasing your knowledge of and comfort with the supporting data.  Client team members should be involved in both the development and review of the report.

Be careful to consolidate data collection needs so as not to have multiple interviews with client employees, especially with executives.
