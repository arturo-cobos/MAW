Task: Participate in audit or review (IMSP421 and IMSP232)
This task prepares for and participates in audits or reviews, such as project health or compliance reviews, and addresses any issues or corrective actions.
Purpose
To ensure that planned reviews are conducted, and all reviews are supported appropriately, including addressing issues and corrective actions.

        Main Description

        This task is based on the WWPMM work pattern M5: Participate in audit or review.

        This task is launched when a decision has been made that the project should be audited. It covers preparing for and participating in compliance or health reviews, and responding to findings from reviews.

        Compliance reviews are typically conducted by the business unit and/or competencies responsible for the project. These reviews focus on ensuring that project management, technical, and quality assurance procedures are being followed. Sponsor compliance reviews focus on ensuring procedures specified in the sponsor Agreement are being followed.

        Health reviews are intended to proactively look for warning signs of unexpected events and issues that might impact the success of the project and to anticipate or react to them. Health reviews are also an excellent learning experience for all involved, leading to higher maturity levels in project management.

        The reviewer might be:

        • Functional management
        • Representative of the sponsor
        • Head of a program that wants to audit one project
        • Another organization such as a certification body

        Project management must prepare for the audit or review, and then take part in it.

        The compliance reviews can focus on the compliance of the project with procedures or on the health of the project, or both. The health reviews can deal with technical, management or business aspects of the project.

        For compliance reviews, project management will:

        • Brief the compliance review team.
        • Participate in interviews and review meeting(s).
        • Provide evidence as requested of adherence to the defined procedures.
        • Ensure that the review is proceeding as planned
        • Review preliminary findings with the review team before the review team finalizes its review report.

        For formal health reviews, project management will:

        • Identify the framework of health dimensions that will be used for assessing the project's health.
        • Recall early warning signs of project failure and associated root causes.
        • For each health concern identified, evaluate the level of threat and the root causes of the concern.
        • Assess the possible actions that can be taken to address the concern.
        • Record issues and actions using appropriate documents.

        The key outputs of this task are the Quality Review Documentation; and the Issue Log, Issue Documents, Action Log, Action Control Documents or Event Log, as needed.

        This task includes the following:

        • Prepare for review
          • Understand the type of review
          • Collaborate on development of agenda
          • Provide adequate documentation to reviewers
          • Ensure correction of known deficiencies
        • Participate in review
          • Participate in the audit or review as required
          • Brief the review team as needed
          • Evaluate any findings and concerns, assessing possible actions
          • Review preliminary findings with the review team before the review team finalizes its review report
        • Discuss and agree on corrective actions
        • Update work plans and Action Log
        • Monitor completion of corrective actions
