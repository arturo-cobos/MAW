Task: Understand objectives and plan defining activities (IMSP395)
This provides an understanding of the objectives and client expectations for the project being considered, and will create the initial project definition, initial project control book, and the operational schedule that will address the work required to complete the defining and planning activities.
Purpose
To understand the project well enough to define it, so an appropriate approach and target solution can be developed, and to plan for the remaining defining and planning activities.

        Main Description
        • Gain understanding and reformulate as necessary:
          • The business context within which the project being defined is being set up.
          • The business problem that must be solved and any constraints that restrict potential solutions.
          • The sponsor's view of the target solution and of the overall approach to implement it.
        1. Identify the project's stakeholders as appropriate.
        2. Review the Project Charter or the service request (or other documents such as requests for service if in an outsourcing environment) and then clarifies the client's expectations in terms of:
          • The time requirements
          • The estimate or quote due date
          • The required delivery and implementation dates for the deliverables
          • Any risks that the client perceives
          • Any questions and issues that the client has
        3. Evaluate potential mismatches and severe risks in the sponsor's expectations. Confirm the sponsor's charter if the expected project seems safe, or imagine possible project scope options and project shaping directions that suit the delivery organization capabilities. Document accordingly the set of objectives that the project being considered should achieve.
        4. Establish a Project Control Book to store the artifacts produced during the proposal effort.
        5. Capture client expectations by documenting a first draft of the Conditions of Satisfaction (COS).
          • The purpose of COS is to identify and manage the client's expectations across the life-cycle of an engagement.  Documenting and periodically reviewing the client's expectations across the life-cycle enables changes in priorities and new expectations to be easily identified and provides objective criteria for the client to use when gauging IBM's success.  COS is an easy way to solidify customer "buy-in" and to help identify and special expectations surrounding engagement delivery.  Finally, COS gives you the opportunity to renegotiate expectations that are out of scope or cannot be met.
        6. Clarify business controls, including Sarbanes-Oxley (SOX) compliance and data security & privacy needs.
        7. Create or update the Project Definition. 
        8. Document the initial information regarding:
          • Project identification
          • Project objectives
          • Project background

          9.   Determine the way to come to an agreement on the project being considered with the sponsor, and what further work must be accomplished in Defining and Planning activities to reach this agreement.

        Note that, at this point, the delivery organization may consider that the project being envisioned cannot be achieved with an acceptable level of risk, and decide not to proceed.

        Determine the technical skills that are appropriate to contribute to shaping the project, and document plans for undertaking the rest of the Defining and Planning activities.  Build the Operational Schedule which documents the list of work items each member of the proposal team has to achieve, along with the effort or timeline.
