Task: Release Resources (IMSP240 & IMSP711)
This task outlines the release of project resources (staff, supplier and technical environment) at the end of the project or when the resource contribution is coming to an end.
Purpose
This task is performed to ensure that the project resources are released in a reasonable sequence with appropriate business controls.

        Main Description

        This task focuses on releasing project resources.  It is always done at the end of the project, but can also be done at other points of the project lifecycle.  Activities include:

        • Determine a reasonable sequence in which people should leave and plan accordingly.
        • Release the suppliers in accordance with the Supplier Management Procedure (IMSP711).  Validate that all project related confidential and sensitive information is irretrievably erased from supplier assets.
        • Debrief and release staff.  Activities may include:
          • Plan final meeting with departing team member(s) to discuss:  outstanding work; handover of responsibilities, solicit lessons learned and ensure reusable assets are submitted to KnowledgeView.
          • Provide report of individual performance, if requested by management.
          • Complete the Project Assessments for each team member as they are released from the project.  For IBM GBS, the project manager is required to complete Project Assessments for all GBS team members who have >100 hours on the project.
          • Ensure all relevant files are moved to project libraries, Personal Information, Sensitive Personal Information and Business Sensitive Information has been removed from the workstation, peripheral equipment and portable media, and that all access to the technical environment, including networks, systems and applications, is revoked.
        • Release the technical environment.  De-install workstations and the common technical environment, as applicable.

        Note: 

        • The Staff Schedule is documented in the Event Log (in the GBS PM Minimal (WWPMM) delivery process.
        • All plans (i.e. the Client Data Security & Privacy Plan) in the GBS PM Minimal (WWPMM) delivery process are documented within the Project Management System Summary (PMSS).
        Steps
        Determine a reasonable sequence in which people should leave
        Determine a reasonable sequence in which people should leave and plans accordingly. Plan an orderly closure of work focused on minimizing project costs without losing team member expertise that could still be needed on project.
        • Review project management schedule, operational schedule and human resource plan to determine which assignments are coming to an end
        • Adjust the operational schedule to reflect the work to be completed, as well as necessary staff reassignments. Adjust the staff schedule
        • Notify the team member's organization (internal or external staff provider) of the pending release. Consider any contractual limitations pertaining to reassignment of staff and make sure team member's organization is aware of those limitations. Consider any contractual limitations pertaining to reassignment of staff and make sure team member's organization is aware of those limitations.  If required, provide the staff provider concerned with the required notice (often one month). It may be desirable to negotiate a change in the planned finish date.
        • Validate that client contractual requirements for redeploying staff are met.
        Release suppliers
        Release the suppliers in accordance with the Supplier Management Procedure (IMSP711). Validate that all project related confidential and sensitive information is irretrievably erased from supplier assets

        Refer to the Supplier Management guideline for details on releasing suppliers

        Note: This activity may be performed prior to the end of the project if appropriate.

        Debrief and release staff

        Release staff, including:

        Final Team Meeting

        • Plan final meeting with departing team member(s) to discuss following:
          • Any outstanding work
          • Modalities of any incomplete work and any handover of responsibilities
          • Opportunities for reassignment within the project that might be open to the individual
          • Any additional lessons learned by soliciting team member's personal opinion about what was done well and what could have been improved. This will be input for the project evaluation report
          • Ensure any reusable intellectual capital has been submitted to the appropriate repositories
        • Hold the meeting

        Skills Related

        • Provide report of individual performance if requested by management
        • Complete the Project Assessments for each team member as they are released from the project.  For IBM GBS, the project manager is required to complete Project Assessments for all GBS team members who have > 100 hours on the project.
          • Team members update functional skills profile and CV with new skills and project/experience details.
        • Ensure all off- boarding activities are carried out per the project's off-boarding checklist.

        Data Security & Privacy

        • Ensure all relevant files are moved to project libraries, Personal Information, Sensitive Personal Information and Business Sensitive Information, obtain and retain written confirmation that all has been removed from the workstation, and that all access to the technical environment, including networks, systems and applications, is revoked in accordance with the project's access control process.
        • Ensure all passwords are revoked.
        • Ensure all physical access to floors, buildings are removed and ID badges are returned as appropriate.
        • Ensure all other off-boarding and project specific off-boading requirements are met.
        • Update access logs to show removal of system, environment, application and network access has been removed.
        • Update Separation of Duties matrix to show change in staff assigned role and access
        • Update the asset inventory to show that the office and material has been returned or reallocated
        • Update the staff schedule and human resource plan to show that the individual is no longer a member of the project team

        Note: This task may be performed prior to the end of the project if appropriate.

        Release technical environment
        De-install workstations and the common technical environment, as applicable. This includes:
        • Review asset inventory to identify components that have been installed on workstations
        • Review agreement to understand disposition requirements
        • Collect reusable office suite development and utilization ideas for submission to intellectual capital repository
        • Save or archive project files or transferring files to other team members, as appropriate
        • Ensure that departing person(s) cleans common storage areas that were used
        • Ensure all data has been deleted from workstations, peripheral equipment and portable media used
        • Ensure all contractual commitments for removing or retaining data are met
        • Uninstall software following manufacturer's guidelines and returning to sponsor, as required
        • De-install hardware following manufacturer's guidelines and returning to sponsor or other owner
        • Collect software and hardware components that have been allocated for the duration of the project
        • Either keep components, peripheral equipment and portable media used for redeployment, or return to sponsor
        • Record official return to sponsor or owner
        • Revoke access authorizations to technical environment
        • Update Asset Inventory
