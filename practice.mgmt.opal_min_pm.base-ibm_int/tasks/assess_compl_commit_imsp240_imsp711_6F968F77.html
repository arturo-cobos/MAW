Task: Assess completion of mutual commitments (IMSP240 and IMSP711)
Outlines the assessment performed to evaluate the completion of all commitments by the project stakeholders.
Purpose
This task is performed to review and assess the completion of all commitments between stakeholders either at the end of the project or at the designated milestone identified by the project.

        Main Description
        This task involves assessment of commitments made by all stakeholders, which include:
        • Agreement (i.e. SOW) between the sponsor and the delivery organization, here the sponsor can be internal or external to the organization
        • Agreement between the delivery organization and the supplier

        Typically such an assessment involves:

        • Reviewing the agreement (i.e SOW) to confirm the completeness of commitments
        • Addressing any outstanding issues
        • Recovering or disposing any hardware and/or software
        • Ensuring that ongoing support needs are addressed, including any contractual addenda or agreements that are required
        • Reviewing the client satisfaction based on the their expectations

        Note:  The Delivery Control Documentation is documented in the Event Log (in the GBS PM Minimal (WWPMM) delivery process.

        Reference IGSDF - Using GBS CRM on Engagements for links to your local GBS CRM Playbook for specific guidance on CRM Step 9. Close Project for tools used to store Conditions of Satisfaction (COS).

        Steps
        Assess completion of mutual commitments
        Assess completion of mutual commitments. This includes but is not limited to:
        • Reviewing the agreement (i.e. SOW) to identify any areas where terms may not have been met
        • Checking the extent to which stakeholder commitments have been met
        • Addressing any concerns and ensuring that any outstanding items are completed
        • Reviewing the agreement (i.e. SOW) with the sponsor to ensure that it is agreed that all of the delivery organization's commitments have been met
        • Recovering any delivery organization property from the sponsor or the supplier
        • As agreed with the sponsor, carefully returning or destroying any of the sponsor's confidential material and sensitive information
        • Ensuring that ongoing support needs are addressed, including any contractual addenda or agreements that are required
        Conduct closeout assessment of client satisfaction

        Conduct closeout assessment of client satisfaction on expectations established throughout the project lifecycle.

        This includes: 

        • Conducting the Conditions of Satisfaction closeout review
        • Understanding how / if GBS has met client expectations
        • Informing the client of potential upcoming IBM Client Value Assessment - Transaction Survey process
        Key Considerations

        Reference OPAL Procedures:

        • Project Close Procedure (IMSP240)
        • Supplier Management Procedure (IMSP711)

        At the end of the project meet the client to determine whether their expectations have been met or exceeded covering aspects like performance, communication and any outstanding issues. This opportunity can be used to discuss with the client the potential future business for IBM. Record the results of this meeting and follow through for any outstanding issue.

        Remind the client that IBM surveys their clients at the end of a project because we value their feedback. Inform them that they may be contacted to participate in a survey. Verify the client contact information for the survey and update it in relevant repository.
