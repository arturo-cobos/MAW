Task: Launch project, understand scope, risk, build startup plan (P391)
This task will conduct the planning for project launch, ensure that the agreement and related information is understood, refine or prepare the initial risk management plan, confirm the solution engineering methodology that was selected, and establish the plans for start-up.
Purpose
To ensure that work done in previous phases of the project is used as the basis for this phase, providing an appropriate understanding of the project.

        Main Description

        Reference IGSDF - Using GBS CRM on Engagements for links to your local GBS CRM Playbook for business rules on whether a formal Project Launch needs to be conducted.

        This task is based on the WWPMM work pattern S1: Initiate phase.

        This task is part of activity that is the first to be launched when starting the execution of a project (or of a new major technical phase or sub-project), and needs a first set of project team members (at least the project manager) who will plan the activities necessary to get the project up and running.

        The key outputs of this task are the outcome of the seven keys based assessment, performed at the time of project launch, Milestone List, Operational Schedule, and the Project Management System Summary plans, which may include the Risk Management Plan, Human Resource Plan, Financial Plan, Technical Environment Plan, Project Quality Plan, Communications Management Plan, and others.  Output may also include the Risk Definition, which may be included in the Event Log.

        This task includes the following:

        • Review the notification related to the project launch - if one exists (depends on the business rules for the IOT/GMU.  This letter marks the move from solution design to solution delivery. Note:  See the appropriate GBS CRM business process for the businesss rules for your community.
        • Understand scope and risk, confirm method used, and develop startup plans
        • Understand agreement and other definition and planning elements
          • Review the outcome of the seven keys based assessment, performed at the time of project launch list of questions aimed at identifying project risks and concerns within the first 10 days.  Discuss any issues identified with stakeholders or the engagement partner.
        • Refine or prepare initial risk management plan
        • Confirm the solution engineering methodology selected or conduct a Method Adoption Workshop (MAW)
        • Develop the startup plan
        • Document the process, methods and tools described in the proposal for planned usage and used in delivery on the project across all delivery sites in the Process, Methods and Tools (PM&T) Profile
