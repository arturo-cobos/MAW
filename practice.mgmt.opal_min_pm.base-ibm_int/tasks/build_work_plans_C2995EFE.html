Task: Build work plans
This task refines the work units identified in Defining, determines stakeholder involvement and separation of duties, clarifies deliverables, organizes a team, builds a work breakdown structure, estimates, understands risks and builds a high-level schedule for the project or program.
Purpose

To expand the work done during project definition, so the project can be planned and started with an understanding of the deliverables, the schedule the risks and the staffing requirements.

      Main Description

      This task is launched when dates, products and services targeted for a given project organizational unit (POU) are known.  Note that the goal here is to let each project organizational unit (POU) verify that what is expected from them, as described in the Project Definition, is realistic. In other terms, this activity consists of refining, in terms of activities, tasks, and intermediate work products, the work units that are identified in the Project Definition, to such a level that reliable estimates can be obtained, and to make sure that the work load and time frame are consistent, and that the resources will be available.

      During a selling process, as described in the Client Value Method (CVM), this PM process interacts with "Closing the sale" with the customer within the bid phase.

      This task includes the following steps:

      • Determine relevant stakeholder involvement, ensuring that all the components of the proposal will be properly defined and integrated.
      • Clarify deliverable definitions and quality characteristics
      • Define deliverables components and conduct Method Adoption Workshop (MAW)
      • Organize work breakdown structure (WBS) and determine team structure

      The key outputs of this task are plans at the POU level: the Deliverable Definitions, the Product Breakdown Structure, the Work Product List, the team structure in the Organizational Breakdown Structure, the Human Resource Plan, POU estimates in the Financial Plan (will be consolidated later in Finalize sponsor agreement task) the Work Breakdown Structure, high-level Project Management Schedule for the POU and the Project Management System Summary.

      Steps
      Determine relevant stakeholder involvement

      Determine how relevant stakeholders, including the response team, will be involved in establishing the POU plans and committing to it. 

      • Using the planning framework in the project definition as input, determine how relevant stakeholders, including the response team, will be involved in establishing the Project Organizational Unit (POU) plan and committing to it.
      • Document their participation in the Communications Management Plan or the Stakeholder Participation Management Plan, or both.

      The Stakeholder Participation Management Plan should be used in addition to the Communications Management Plan to supplement information about stakeholders that is not captured in the Communications Management Plan for complex projects or projects without strong support for organizational change.

      In small organizations where control is centralized and there is strong support for organizational change, or the client is experienced with this type of change and has a defined strategy, the stakeholders' information, requirements, and their involvement can be documented in the Communications Management Plan.

      The key outputs of this task are the Communications Management Plan or the Stakeholder Participation Management Plan, or both, noting that both of these may be part of the Project Management System Summary.

      Clarify deliverable definitions and quality characteristics

      Ensure that the deliverables and their quality characteristics for each project organizational unit (POU) are identified and documented, so the project can be planned and started with a clear understanding of these items.

      • Review project scope and identify the work products that each project organizational unit (POU) plans to deliver to the sponsor or another POU.
      • Clarify the acceptance or completion criteria for the work products, and determine the quality characteristics and quality criteria that will contribute in meeting the acceptance criteria.
      • Update the Deliverable Definition accordingly

      The key outputs of this task are the Deliverable Definition and the Project Quality Plan.

      Define deliverables components and conduct Method Adoption Workshop (MAW)

      Have each of the performing organizations that are proposed to be part of the project, develop the plans describing the means that will be necessary to carry out their part of the project (or a project phase, or sub-project).

      Break down the operational deliverables to a level that allows you to recognize:

      • An existing component/asset from which the product breakdown structure element will easily be derived.
      • A component that can be bought off the shelf or subcontracted.
      • A component for which a technical delivery process and related estimating technique are known.

      A Method Adoption Workshop (MAW) is conducted, when appropriate. During the MAW, a review of the business requirements is done to aid in the method selection process.  Method content is available on MethodWeb for review and download to the tailoring tool (i.e. RMC). A basic work breakdown structure (WBS) is created during this tailoring process and is used as a basis to build the project plan.

      Enhance the Work breakdown structure (WBS) by describing the activities that are necessary to build, integrate, and rework the work products to be delivered by the project organizational unit.

      Note that the goal here is to let each POU verify that what is expected from them, as described in the Project Definition, is realistic. In other terms, this activity consists of refining, in terms of activities, tasks, and intermediate work products, the work units that are identified in the Project Definition, to such a level that reliable estimates can be obtained, and to make sure that the work load and time frame are consistent, and that the resources will be available.

      This steps includes the following actions:

      • Identify the components, breakdown of components, or intermediate work products, for each work product to be delivered to the sponsor or to another project organizational unit. This component breakdown should reach a level that the components are recognized as belonging to a technical work pattern and related estimating technique are known, or the components are identifiable as reusable assets.
      • Document the components in the Product Breakdown Structure (PBS).
      • Determine the layout of activities necessary to create those components or intermediate work products in an orderly manner, and make a crude estimate of the workload and duration for performing those activities. A method adoption workshop (MAW) can assist for some technical work products, but likely will not provide all the information required.
      • Document the Work Product List and the initial Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) for the POU.
      • Document the process, methods and tools described in the proposal for planned usage and used in delivery on the project across all delivery sites in the PM&T Profile.

      The key outputs of this task are the Deliverable Definition, Work Breakdown Structure, Product Breakdown Structure and Work Product List.  In some situations, a Project Defined Process will also be built.

      Organize work breakdown structure (WBS) and determine team structure

      Determine and document the resources that can undertake the project organizational activities.  Define a team structure that best fits the activities to be performed.  Structure the activities so that responsibilities can be clearly assigned to components of the team structure.

      • Determine the nature and number of resources that will be necessary to undertake the project organizational unit activities and, referring to relevant team structure models, design the team structure that best fits the activities to be performed.
      • Document in the Human Resource Plan and in the team structure of the Organizational Breakdown Structure (OBS) for the project organizational unit (POU).  This can be done in the Project Management System Summary.
      • For the activities that were identified in the initial project organizational unit Work Breakdown Structure (WBS), bundle into groups and structure so that responsibilities can be clearly assigned to components of the team structure.
      • Determine the activities and resources to manage the POU work as well as those for providing support when appropriate.
      • Document in the WBS for the POU being considered.
      • Plan for a self-assessment / peer review of the solution (unless the solution becomes BTT 3 or greater and requires Risk Management review).

      The key outputs of this task are the Work Breakdown Structure, Organizational Breakdown Structure; and the Human Resource Plan, which may be part of the Project Management System.
