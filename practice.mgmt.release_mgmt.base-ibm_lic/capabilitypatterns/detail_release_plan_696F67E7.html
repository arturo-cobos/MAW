Main Description
An incremental or evolutionary approach to software development involves a pass through the "Outline Solution" and "Macro Design" phases, then repeated iterations through the "Micro Design", "Implementation" and "Deployment" phases. These approaches divide the development effort into release cycles which incrementally add capability to the application. Release plan development involves assigning required capability as described in the use cases to individual releases and estimating implementation, system test, delivery, and deployment schedules.

In order to assign use cases to iterations, the use cases should be ranked according to criteria of interest to the customer and project management. Some considerations for this ranking are:
  • effective reduction of program risk such as use cases that are high risk, complex, or rely on new technologies.
  • exposure and test of assumptions concerning the project's underlying technical structure.
  • significant impact on the architecture, such as use cases that involve development of large portions of particular architectural layers.
  • demonstrate to the customer that progress is being made.
  • provide the customer with useful capability.
In each release cycle, the phases, activities and tasks must also be estimated. However, it is often difficult to commit to the exact content of a release ahead of time because of the lack of good productivity estimates to apply to the plan. In this case the release plan may initially be created in an incomplete state and be extended and refined during the project.

The Release Plan is developed by the project manager and the sponsors. Together, they ensure that the overall plan delivers an application which meets the requirements within the available time and budget.
