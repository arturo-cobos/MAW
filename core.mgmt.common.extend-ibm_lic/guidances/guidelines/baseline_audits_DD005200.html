Guideline: Baseline Audits
Use this to perform configuration baseline audits including preparing for them, performing them, reporting the results and tracking resulting action items to closure.
Main Description

Purpose

Use this guideline to:

Perform configuration baseline audits including preparing for them, performing them, reporting the results and tracking resulting action items to closure. Configuration baseline audits are independent assessments of project baselines to verify their integrity, their completeness and correctness, their compliance with applicable configuration management (CM) standards and procedures, and to review the structure and facilities of the project library.

Configuration baseline audits are independent assessments of project baselines to verify their integrity, their completeness and correctness, their compliance with applicable configuration management (CM) standards and procedures, and to review the structure and facilities of the project library.

Scope

This guideline applies to:

Requirements, software, and documentation baselines that are audited based on the project management system summary.

Overview


This guideline includes:
  • Planning for baseline audit
  • Preparing for audit
  • Conducting audit
  • Recording results
  • Reporting results
  • Tracking and closing corrective actions

Details

1. Plan baseline audit

The Configuration Manager:
Step Action
1 Identifies which type of baseline audit will be performed:
  • Requirements
  • Software
  • System Documentation
2 For the baseline audit to be performed, identifies the Audit Items that apply:
  • Refer to Appendix A. Requirements Audit for its audit items and additional details
  • Refer to Appendix B. Software Audits for its audit items and additional details
  • Refer to Appendix C. System Documentation Audits for its audit items and additional details
3 Sets the time period and scope for the audit

2. Prepare for audit


The Configuration Manager:
Step Action
1 For the baseline audit to be performed, reviews the Audit Items that apply and gathers the required information, as appropriate:
  • Refer to Appendix A. Requirements Audit for its audit items and additional details
  • Refer to Appendix B. Software Audits for its audit items and additional details
  • Refer to Appendix C. System Documentation Audits for its audit items and additional details

3. Conduct audit

The Configuration Manager:

Step Action
1 Conducts the audit according to the project management system summary and the designated steps in:
  • Appendix A. Requirements Audit
  • Appendix B. Software Audit
  • Appendix C. System Documentation Audit

4. Record results

The Configuration Manager:

Step Action
1 Prepares a configuration audit findings covering the following:
  • Date the audit was performed
  • Effort spent to perform the audit
  • Name of the auditor and participants
  • Description of audit steps performed
  • Summary of results including strengths, weaknesses and findings
2 Records any identified audit failures using configuration audit findings. Initiates an action control document for process issues that are evident.
3 Reviews the report with the Project Manager and revises it when needed.
4 Files the configuration audit findings.

5. Report results to project management


The Configuration Manager:
Step Action
1 Sends the report to the Project Manager.
2 Reports audit results to Project Management in the Project Management Review Meeting.
Refer to the Communications Management Guideline for details on Project Management Review Meeting

6. Track corrective actions to closure


The Configuration Manager:
Step Action
1 Reviews the status of findings that resulted from Baseline Audits and takes actions to have them corrected and closed.
2 Reports overdue actions to Project Management in the Project Management Review Meeting.
Refer to the Communications Management Guideline for details on Project Management Review Meeting.
