Description

Due to the iterative and incremental nature of the Agile with Discipline approach requirement-related tasks are repeated throughout the AWD project.  The level of detail of the outputs varies depending on the level of detail required to support the current phase and/or iteration. In the Open Phase, requirements are expected to be a high-level breadth of coverage useful in planning the first Definition iteration, and a rough estimate of several Development iterations, when the details are elaborated via story development.

Identify Requirements

The Business Analyst takes primary ownership of these activities and deliverables, but works with the other team members to execute workshops, facilitate the story writing, prioritization, and prototyping. The team helps to capture workshop results and translate them into agile requirements and estimates.

Note that this activity closely aligns with the following activities in other processes:  execute, requirements and design.

Refine Requirements

At the start of each iteration, the team collaboratively reviews the charter and stories planned for the iteration, reviews and refines estimates and assumptions. The lead developer works with the team to determine who will work on which tasks.  The team may decide to develop partial Use Cases to expand on stories that have more complex requirements, or if a visual model will help the team understand the story better.
