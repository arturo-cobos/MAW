Activity: Assess estimating basis (IMSP421 and IMSP525)
This activity compares the estimating basis used to initially estimate the workload of the project or its phase, with the trends of the actual workload spent on work units that are completed or in progress. Adjustments are then made to assignments, estimates to complete the work, and project plans.

Description

This activity is based on the WWPMM work pattern M2: Assess estimating basis.

This activity is launched periodically as part of the "Monitor project work" activities.

When creating the work plans, and later expanding them, an estimating basis and assumptions are used to estimate the effort required to perform the planned work items, based upon historical data if available. After the project team members have spent some time performing the work assigned to them, some of the assigned work units will have been completed, others will be in progress, and others will not have begun.

When performing progress tracking, the project manager makes an assessment with the team members of the estimates to complete the work units that are in progress, that may reveal variances between the planned efforts and actual efforts.

Additionally, the estimates to complete the work units that have not yet begun are based upon the initial estimating basis and assumptions that was used when creating and expanding the work plans. If the trends from progress tracking demonstrate that the initial estimating basis and assumptions are not being confirmed by the actuals, then the whole estimate to complete may be totally wrong, and actions must be taken to anticipate a potential future overrun. Actions might include modifying the organization of the project, reconsidering assignments, strengthening the team with higher skills, postponing activities that are not in the critical path, etc. As far as possible, these corrective actions should not affect the key commitments of the project, and thus consist of plan adjustments only. But it may also become obvious that key commitments will not be achievable, and that negotiations with the project sponsor should be launched before the sponsor realizes that commitments will not be met. See the WWPMM process "TC.30.20. Adjust project plans" for actions that may be considered.

An assessment of the estimating basis should be done initially as soon as there are enough measurements for valid analysis, and then periodically until the end of the project.

Note that this assessment will be made easier if the estimating technique used when creating the work plans is based on grouping the work units by categories, such that work units of the same category are assumed to require a comparable amount of effort to be achieved. Thus, the effort to complete work units that are completed or underway is a good indication of the effort that will be needed to complete the work units that have not begun. See example in the introduction of "TC.30.10 Review estimating basis".

The key outputs of this activity are Action Control Documents, Operational and Project Management Schedules adjusted as necessary and Change Requests when plan adjustments will not be sufficient.

Please refer to M2: Assess estimating basis in the WWPMM web site for the original activity steps that decompose this activity.

This activity includes the following:

  • Compare estimates, adjust assignments, reconcile plans
    • Compare productivity measurement with estimating basis
    • Adjust assignments and estimate to complete
    • Reconcile project management plans
