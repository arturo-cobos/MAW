Description
During the (initial) proposal phase (CRM Solution Design), there is often insufficient time to accurately scope the engagement. Because of the risks associated with substantial enhancements to existing applications or with new applications development, the preferred approach is to conduct a "scoping" phase prior to contracting with the client for a multi-phase effort to deliver a solution. This phase is intended as this "scoping" phase and is designed to accurately gather the information needed for the client to make an informed investment decision.

On the other hand, the client is generally not interested in overly investing in an exploratory-type effort. Therefore, Solution Outline is a quick hitting phase designed to get in and out rapidly, collecting and developing only the information absolutely needed by the client to make the investment decision (hence the word "outline" in many the activity or task names of the phase). A typical Solution Outline is conducted in one to three months, depending on the overall size and complexity of the engagement.

The primary objective of Solution Outline is to determine the complexity and scope of the solution. If we understand the complexity and scope, we are able to provide the client with necessary information to make the investment decision. The following major activities in Solution Outline each in turn address complexity land scope:
  • Capture Existing Environment: Before recommending a solution to the client, we must first obtain a basic understanding the client's organization and business. This objective is accomplished through the development of several basic business modeling and organization work products. These work products are not exhaustively completed in this phase; rather, first drafts are produced and then iteratively refined in each of the remaining phases.
  • Outline Business Solution:
    • Understand Business Strategy:
    • Understand Business Architecture (Discovery):
  • Outline User Experience Design:
  • Outline Technical Solution:
    • Identify and Outline Technical Requirements
    • Outline Technical Architecture: This activity produces a quick draft (high level) of the architecture. This provides a feel for the size and complexity of the infrastructure needed to deploy the application. The architecture is iterative refined in subsequent phases.
    • Outline Application Model: This activity produces a quick draft (high level) of the various aspects of the application models which provides a feel for the size and complexity of the application. These work products are iteratively refined in subsequent phases.
  • Conduct Requirements Reviews:
  • Perform Impact Assessment: This activity addresses two primary objectives. First, the work products produced in this activity gauge the level of organization change likely with the new solution and the organization's ability to cope with the change. Second, the As-Is Process Definition and Assessment and Customer View and Requirements work products verify that the business processes to automate are in fact known by the client and when automated will positively contribute to the business goals.
  • Outline Solution Development Plans: Outline Solution Strategy develops several technical plans which taken together comprise an overall strategy in which to approach the engagement.
