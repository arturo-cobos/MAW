Activity: Outline Use Cases
Identify the solution scope, the uses cases lying within this, and the major non-functional (or quality) requirements. Understand the complexity of key use cases.
DescriptionWork Breakdown StructureTeam AllocationWork Product Usage
Purpose
To understand the overall scope of the solution, and identify uses cases lying within this scope and their complexity. To identify major non-functional requirements the solution will have to meet.
Relationships
Parent Activities
  • Outline Business Solution
Description

Identify use cases within the scope of this solution through examination of:

  • Relationship of the solution's net inputs and outputs with the existing IT environment, particularly existing applications
  • The service portfolio of candidate services previously developed, and
  • The definition of processes in-scope for this solution.

The primary scenarios of some key uses cases may also be sketched during this activity, in order to understand their complexity. Key use cases are those which seem to provide the most critical functionality desired by the client, or which would appear to have difficult requirements in terms of the number of business entities involved or possessing challenging non-functional requirements.

Major non-functional requirements are also identified in this activity. These are also sometimes called 'quality of service' requirements and consist of such things as data and transaction volumes, response time, availability etc. In this phase of the life cycle it is important to understand which of these requirements may be particularly challenging, but it is not necessary to pin down the requirements to particular values that can be test against, as this is deferred to the next phase.

Key Considerations
It is important in this phase to constrain the selection of key use cases to a small percentage of the overall solution in order to maintain the time box nature of this phase and not trap the project in 'analysis paralysis'.
