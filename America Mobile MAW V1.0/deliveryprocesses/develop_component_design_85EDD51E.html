Task Descriptor: Develop Detail Component Design
Develop the internal design (realization) of a component.
Based on Method Task: Develop Component Design
Relationships
RolesPrimary:
  • Application Developer
Additional: Assisting:
  • Application Architect
InputsMandatory:
  • [Technical Architecture]
  • [Technical Specification]
Optional:
  • None
External:
  • None
Outputs
  • Component Design
Steps
Detail Internal Structure of Component
  • Identify the component’s internal elements that collaborate together to provide the required behavior.
  • Distribute responsibilities to the participating elements and ensure that they have the relationships required to collaborate.
Detail Internal Behavior of Component
  • Describe how sequences of interactions in the design realize the component’s required external behavior.
  • Identify any new design elements needed to satisfy the component’s behavioral requirements.
Describe Interface Dependencies
Describe dependencies between internal component elements and the interfaces of external components.
Refine Design Decisions
  • Refine the design to an appropriate level of detail to drive (or document) implementation and to ensure that it fits into the architecture.
  • Document any testability issues, such as design elements that are difficult to test or critical performance areas.
  • Evolve the design by examining recent changes in the larger context of the design and determine if refactoring and redesigning techniques will improve the robustness, flexibility, and understandability of the design. See Guideline: Evolve the Design for guidance specific design decisions and on making design improvements just when they're needed.
  • Incorporate Architectural Mechanisms from the architecture. Apply consistent structure of the elements and organization of the behavior as in other areas of the design and use patterns identified in the architecture.
