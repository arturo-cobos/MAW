Iteration: Outline Technical Solution

Purpose

To define an outline architecture model for the proposed system that can meet the business requirements, including non-functional requirements. This will include decisions on:

  • The overall shape and style of the architecture.
  • "Global" (application wide) decisions about various aspects of the architecture, including technology and design constraints.
  • Selection of relevant assets and assessment of the costs and benefits of using them.
  • To enable assessment of viability and risks in implementing and deploying the system.
  • To enable realistic estimation of effort and costs.
Relationships
Parent Activities

Description

The objectives of this activity are to determine the overall complexity and scope of the solution’s qualitative and architectural requirements, predominantly from the application and infrastructure perspectives as captured in the Component and Operational Models respectively.

Given the context of this activity within the Solution Outline Phase, these models will represent a quick, high level draft of the solution’s architecture, providing a feel for the size and complexity of the application’s structure, together with the infrastructure needed to deploy it. Subsequent phases will iteratively refine these models, and so the overall thrust of this activity is to give the project a high degree of comfort that the solution is "doable" from all perspectives (including technical, financial, skills and timescales), and to ensure there are no surprises in store later in the project.
