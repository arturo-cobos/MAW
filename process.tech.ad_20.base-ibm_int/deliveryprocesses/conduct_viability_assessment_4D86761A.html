Task Descriptor: Conduct Viability Assessment
Perform a review of the system being developed to assess its ability to meet critical requirements and the likelihood of it being delivered in time and on budget.

Steps
Plan the Assessment
Planning the assessment involves identifying key personnel to be interviewed, scheduling the meeting or interviews; developing agendas and checklists; and, collecting and distributing materials for assessment participants.  On a large project, participants may include the architecture team, client IT organization representatives and product specialists.  Adequate time should be allocated to accommodate schedules and allow for potential rework.
Prepare for the Assessment
Assessment participants should study the assessment material.  They should document any identified findings such as issues, defects, concerns, and comments.  The review material should be distributed several days prior to the review to give adequate time to prepare.  Review materials would include relevant artifacts to whatever level of completion has been achieved so far.
Conduct Meetings and Interviews
For a formal assessment, conduct a kick-off meeting for participants, presenting the solution, major artifacts and key issues.  Key project team members can also be interviewed to delve deeper into the solution and uncover critical risks and issues.
Assess Viability
Upon completion of data gathering, the architect prepares a Viability Assessment.  For each artifact there may be viability issues to assess. Look at each artifact and compare it against any checklists describing the validation and verification criteria for the artifact.  Examine if the solution is viable in light of the contract and subsequent project change requests.  Will it deliver the architectural qualities that are required?
Present Preliminary Findings and Risks
Risks and issues identified during the review are documented and forwarded to the project manager for inclusion in the projectâ€™s risk and issue management processes.
Develop and Deliver Final Viability Assessment Report
The final report should summarize the recommendations and findings of the viability assessment.  Risk should be stated with appropriate containment actions.  Follow-up items should be stated in terms of owners and completion dates.  The need for additional follow-up solution review by external reviewers needs to be confirmed and tentatively scheduled.
