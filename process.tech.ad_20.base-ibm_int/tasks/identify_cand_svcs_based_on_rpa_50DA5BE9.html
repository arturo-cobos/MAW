Task: Identify Candidate Services based on Rules and Policy Analysis
Identify candidate services that will encapsulate business rules and policies and that need to access from multiple access points and for consistency, need to be handled in a uniform fashion.
Disciplines: Business Modeling
Purpose

To capture and externalize rules and policies that may need to changed with the changes in the business model, product and service offerings in order for the business to remain competitive.

Relationships

      Main Description

      Rules may need to access from various channels, services and components., provide a single point of access for rules and provide a single point of access for policy determination and enforcement. This applies for both business functional capabilities and IT infrastructure services such as security or other non-functional requirements.
      Steps
      Identify encapsulation requirements for rules and policies
      Identify events, conditions and actions
      Decide which conditions or actions need to externalized
      Apply the Rule Pattern language to determine Rule Types and how to realize rules

      Update realization and architectural decisions as well as the business rules catalog based on your decision.

      Use the industry best practices for business rules and policies such as the business rules pattern language for alternative ways of implementing rules and policies.
      Update service portfolio
      Add the service to the service portfolio/service hierarchy in the service model.
      Update service hierarchy
      Add the service to the service portfolio/service hierarchy in the service model. Define service hierarchy by grouping service under categories based on functional areas identified.
      Key Considerations
      Is the rule/policy changing and thus should the rule or policy be externalized? Use the Rule Object Pattern Language for alternatives to implementing Rule Services.
