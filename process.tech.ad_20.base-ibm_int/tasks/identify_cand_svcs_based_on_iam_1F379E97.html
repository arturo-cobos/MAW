Task: Identify Candidate Services based on Data Model
Candidate services are identified that enable data access, update or other data-driven manipulation.
Disciplines: Business Modeling
Purpose
To document candidate services based on information analysis and modeling.
Relationships
RolesPrimary Performer:
  • SOA Architect
Additional Performers:
  • Information Architect
InputsMandatory:
  • Data Model
    		Optional:
    • None
    Outputs
    • Service Portfolio
      Main Description

      Identify the candidate services that might be thought of as accessing, updating or otherwise manipulating information. Information services decouple information from consumers (applicatons, processes, etc.) and leverage IOD patterns and capabilities. The identification of information services helps to specify the scope of services that need to be designed by data architects.

      Steps
      Identiy information services relevant to business significant states
      Identiy information services relevant to business significant states of key business entities.
      Identify information service to disparage data sources and disparity in data
      Identify information services to addresses the information management related issues and challenges such disparage information sources and disparity in data format etc. Use the key business entities identified to find out the information management related issues and challenges
      Explore the process Model for Data-oriented Manipulation

      Categorize the process model's decomposition into process services and information services.
      Separate out the information manipulating candidate services
      Separate out information manipulating candidate services into the service model
      Apply security constraints to services based on data access rights of user roles
      Identify requirements for analytics
      Identify types of analytics
      Identify analytics services
      Update service portfolio
      Add the service to the service portfolio/service hierarchy in the service model.
