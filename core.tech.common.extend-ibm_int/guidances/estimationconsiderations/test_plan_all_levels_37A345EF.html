Main Description

A Detailed Test Plan for all levels, except Unit, typically takes 1 to 2 weeks to develop for a project size between 10,000 and 50,000 hours, and 2 to 4 weeks for projects greater than 50,000 hours.  A Detailed Test Plan may take longer depending on the complexity and availability of skilled resources. This consideration does not include documenting the test specification or the test execution plan, or time to update the plan when more information becomes available.

Allow 1 day per module to document the Unit Test Plan. For projects less than 10,000 hours, it is worth considering one combined comprehensive test plan for all levels of testing.

Allocate resources for testing based on the projected size and the level of risks driven by business and technical requirements.

  • Take into consideration the complexity of key architecture work products.
  • Effort spent by developers in debugging and rework is recorded against the Source Code work product.
  • Effort spent by testers running tests is recorded in this work product.

Allocate resources based on the projected size and the level of business and technical risk. Please see the Notation section for examples of risks. The resources and time needed for testing is directly related to the level of business and technical risk. The following is a very rough guideline based on risk:

  • High Risk - Plan for total effort for all levels of testing to be about 50-60% of overall project effort. This depends on a number of factors. For example, project size, complexity, required delivery date, and technology are some of the major factors that impact risks and costs.
  • Medium Risk - Plan for between 20% to 50% of overall project effort for testing.
  • Low Risk - Plan for up to 10% to 20% of overall project effort for testing.
