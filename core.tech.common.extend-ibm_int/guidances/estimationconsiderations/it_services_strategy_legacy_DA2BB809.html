Main Description

The level of detail required in the strategic portfolio of IT services depend greatly on who the user is of the service portfolio and their intended purpose. The level of granularity required by the CIO or a business executive to make a high level sourcing decision is quite different to that required by an IT functional manager seeking to develop detailed workflows and procedures. In estimating the time required to develop the IT Services Strategy, it is therefore vital that you understand who will use the resulting output and for what purpose.   

The time taken to develop this work product will also be influenced by the degree to which the proposed future IT Services Strategy differs from the client's current strategy. If significant changes are to be proposed then additional time and effort will generally be required at this stage to develop, justify and ensure the necessary "buy in" to the future strategy.   

On a short engagement or one with a limited scope a high level version of this work product can be produced with 4-5 person days effort. On larger engagements, or where high impact strategic decisions are being made, this may extend to 2-3 person weeks of effort. If a very detailed IT service portfolio and an associated sourcing strategy is to be developed, this could extend to 2-3-person months effort.
