Main Description

Developing the Usability Test Plan consists of documenting the specifics of when, where, and how the usability test will be conducted. While developing the Usability Test Plan, three areas where care should be taken are:

  1. documenting the profiles of potential test participants,
  2. writing the usability test cases, and
  3. ensuring the measures correspond to the usability requirements.

Review or Obtain Profiles of Users to Document the Types and Number of Users that will Participate in the Test

  • Identify people with characteristics similar to those in the different end user categories. Examples of these could include:
    - people who will have to train end users on this system
    - people who once used similar systems but no longer do
    - students and members of the general public, recruited by newspaper advertisements
    - workers with specific skills, recruited from temporary employment agencies
  • If written profiles of the users exist, use these profiles to guide your selection.
  • Strive for involving a subset of users that are maximally representative of the categories that have been identified. The subset should include some inexperienced users—if they can be successful with the system, chances are high that other users will do no worse.


Prepare the Usability Test Cases

  • Identify those use cases that will have the biggest impact on usability evaluation. Typically, this information can be identified when validating the use cases with actual end users of the system. Users can provide information regarding which use cases provide the highest satisfaction, are done most frequently, and are most important to their jobs.
  • For this subset of use cases, develop a set of usability test cases containing starting and end states with concrete data. Unlike use cases in general, usability test cases should be very specific and not talk about “a customer,” but rather about “John Smith” and “Mary Jones.” The use case scenarios can serve as the basis for the usability test cases since they also describe particular instances of system-actor interaction.


Identify Appropriate Usability Test Measures and Criteria

Identify usability test measures that correspond to the solution’s usability requirements. Review the usability requirements and identify particular requirements that can serve as objective and subjective measures for the test.
