Purpose

Poor estimates of cost, schedule, and effort have been confirmed as a major contributor to the occurrence of troubled projects. Industry experience over the last twenty years has shown that improving project estimates is not just a matter of finding a better method or tool; the needed improvement stems from recognition that estimation is a business process that requires proper governance and disciplined improvement methods.

This practice explains the four states of the estimate lifecycle and the steps to estimate size, effort, schedule, and cost of a project from an initial indicative estimate through to project closure. Following the principles outlined in this practice will ensure an estimate is based on best practice processes, the best information available, and that the estimate undergoes the appropriate levels of scrutiny. The practice also outlines the content that should be represented in the estimate and how to use that information.
