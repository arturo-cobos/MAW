Main Description

Getting Started

After getting some basic familiarity with the concepts of estimating and estimating best practices, consider the specific lifecycle state for your project. You will be required to select an appropriate approach and technique to generate your first estimate, and then determine whether there is a specific tool that is appropriate to apply, leveraging the experience of knowledgeable subject matter experts in both estimating and the project type being estimated.

As an estimate is based on inputs that have some uncertainty associated with them, you must understand how to assess and present a confidence level in an estimate to represent this inherent variance. Once underway, this variance information can be further used to provide insight as to how the project is performing.

Common Pitfalls

Leverage estimation checklists to ensure you have addressed commonly overlooked omissions in your estimate. Look for existing tools that are recommended by your organization or address your project type prior to creating a new estimating model.
