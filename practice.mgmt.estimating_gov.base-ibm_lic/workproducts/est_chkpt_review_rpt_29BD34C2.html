Purpose

This artifact is itself validating and verifying that the estimate is being tracked and reviewed to the best of our ability and is fit for purpose, that is, is sufficiently detailed and accurate for the estimate to be used by management to adjust project plans if appropriate and as required, following the estimating process. 

It is used to indicate whether the next stage of the governance process can be performed.

Description
Brief Outline

This artifact records:

  • Project name, Estimation Report and Estimation Checkpoint Report (effort, cost and schedule  progress) and Estimation Factors Report (Estimation Factors progress) under review, and review date
  • The responsible parties for the estimating activities 
  • Confirmation of the completion of estimation activities, and associated follow-on actions as necessary
  • Any deviations from the estimating procedure or quality
  • A hardcopy signature by the Project Manager to indicate the approval status of the estimate

Key Considerations

Although the initial estimate (for pricing input) has been completed, estimating activity continues during project delivery to check for instance that assumptions made earlier are still valid, that the estimated rate of progress is confirmed by actual figures and so on. If observations are made that run counter to the estimate, consideration must be given to altering project plans, and changing subsequent estimating practice when new work arises.

Do not confuse the Estimation Checkpoint Review Report (what happened at the regular review of the committed estimate during project delivery) with the Estimation Checkpoint Report (contains details of effort, cost and schedule recorded to date).

Tailoring
Impact of not having

Consequences of not having this work product are:

  • Lack of ability to audit completed estimates leading to the situation where the root cause of troubled projects cannot be clearly defined
  • Lack of clarity about where an estimate currently sits within the estimating process
  • Absence of documented estimating responsibilities that may lead to reduced estimate accuracy and missed estimate deadlines
  • Absence of documented estimating activity that may lead to reduced estimate accuracy
  • Lack of a documented baseline against which the estimate can be developed further
  • Lack of a complete set of Estimating Governance reports against which other estimates may be compared for estimating improvement purposes
  • Lack of ability to identify when generally accepted best practices are not being followed, and the reasons for not following them
Reasons for not needingThis artifact is highly recommended for use in documenting the results of reviewing the estimates during project delivery (i.e., the Committed estimates). Reasons for not using this artifact should be documented and approved by management.
