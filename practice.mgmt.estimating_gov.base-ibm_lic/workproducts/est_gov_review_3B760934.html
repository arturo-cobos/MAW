Artifact: Estimating Governance Review (ART 0536)
This artifact contains the reviews created at each of the governance points in the estimating lifecycle, that together form the complete record of the governance history and approval for each estimate.
Domains: Project Management
Work Product Kinds: Record
Purpose

These filed Governance Reports, along with their associated estimates, enable engagement teams to identify who was involved in each estimate, and confirm the activities that occurred during the estimating process.

This information is very valuable during the creation of new estimates, when the reports can be used to provide information on previous estimates which, alongside previous project actuals, will enable historical comparisons and hence improved future estimation.

Description
Brief Outline

This report is comprised of the full set of 5 Governance Point reports, each of which is produced as a result of the corresponding estimating Governance Point.  These Governance Points, in chronological order, are:

  • Independent Technical review
  • Independent review
  • Price review
  • Estimating Checkpoint review
  • Confirm Project Data is Appropriate review
Key Considerations

Ensure that the independent roles really are filled by people who are independent.  They should not have worked on either this particular estimate OR on the engagement for which this estimate is intended.  This enables them to be truly impartial and give what may sometimes be unwelcome, but necessary, advice.  If it proves not to be possible to find a fully independent person, then someone else from the engagement could fulfill this role but they should NOT have worked on this particular estimate.  The extent of their independence should be noted on the review report.

Prolonged discussions about the estimate do not need to be held during the review.  Actions arising from the review should be documented in the report.

The reviews to create this artifact can be held and documented as part of another review (such as Risk Management) and does not need to be held separately.
