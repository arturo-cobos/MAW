Solution Design to Solution Delivery Transition Verification
This document describes items that need to be verified for effective transition from solution design to solution delivery.
Relationships
Related Elements
  • Ensure new project is registered and material transferred (IMSP391)
  • Obtain client's agreement for proposed agreement and baseline (P491)
Main Description

The delivery project manager can use this guidance document during the transition meeting from the bid/engagement team to the delivery team. It will ensure all of the work products are created during the engagement phase and key information regarding the overall engagement process and the client background is provided and communicated to the delivery project manager.

The objectives are to:
  • To gather key engagement and client information
  • To present a consolidated package to the delivery team
  • To identify the key work products and information created during the engagement phase
  • To identify focus areas, such as assumptions, opportunities, and known issues and risks

The engagement partner should review the information below and identify those work products that were created during the Opportunity and Solution Design phases. These work products should be packaged for handover to the delivery project manager.

The delivery project manager should use this document during the transition meeting with the Engagement Team to ensure all produced work products are identified, located, and in the PM possession and pertinent information regarding the sale, solution design, and the client have been communicated.

Examples of background Information regarding the client, opportunity, and the negotiations that is important to communicate to the Delivery Project Manager:
  • Selection process
  • Why IBM was chosen
  • Underlying assumptions supporting the bid
  • Negotiation issues and/or verbal commitments/statements made during proposal phase that are not in documented in contract
  • Future opportunities and risks with this client
  • Company/Industry background
  • Client's locations and other logistics
  • Client's expectations
  • Current environment (business, technical, political, cultural)
  • Known limitations (holidays, freeze periods, etc)
