Tool Mentor: Risk Management Using Rational Portfolio Manager
This tool mentor provides guidance on how to perform Risk Management when using the OnDemand Process Asset Library (OPAL) and Rational Portfolio Manager (Rational PM).
Tool: Rational Portfolio Manager
Main Description

1. Purpose

This document provides guidance how Rational PM is used to support Risk Management in accordance with the OnDemand Process Asset Library (OPAL). This document may serve as a template for creating a tailored procedure or guideline.
This guidance document contains three sections:
  1. Purpose
  2. How Rational PM supports the OPAL procedure
  3. Usage table for Rational PM data elements (fields)

The intent of this guidance is to provide sufficient direction on how Rational PM is used on a project to support Risk Management, so that there are no ambiguities about the process and the team training can be concise.

The guidance assumes a basic understanding of how to use Rational PM and is not intended to be training material. Detailed training on the use of Rational PM can be found by selecting Education and Training from the GBS Rational Portfolio Management Enablement page.

Step 1 - Prepare for Risk Management

This step is not part of the RPM Risk Management state flow. It determines potential sources of risk and classification criteria, defines risk qualification criteria, and establishes risk management strategy.

Step 2 - Identify and Analyze Risk-performed by Project Manager or Contract Business Office

Please note that Rational PM elements in BOLD text and highlights are considered required. Other fields and portlets are recommended for completeness. Refer to the table in section 3 for a complete description of all fields, standards and practices.

  • Identify Risk - CREATE Risk in Scope Management using the OPAL IMSP600 Risk template and the OPAL IMSP600 layout.
    • Key information that should be captured includes Rank, Description, Target Date, Proposed By, Risk Matrix, and Consequences. A value for Risk Category should be captured in the Attribute portlet. Assign Risk owner in the Staffing view or Resources portlet. State set to IDENTIFIED
    • The OPAL IMSP600 Risk template can be located by selecting Template from the Rational PM Scope Management View and expanding the Scope Templates / OPAL Scope Templates folder


 

  • Analyze Risk - Evaluate the risk in terms of impact, probability, and time frame of possible occurrence. Update Risk Matrix and enter data in the Order of Magnitude Mitigation Cost/Revenue, Background, and Consequences portlets.
    • If the Risk is accepted change State to ASSIGNED. Update Approved By portlet with name of person accepting the risk.
    • If the Risk is invalid update Status portlet with reasons for why the risk was rejected and change State to CLOSED

Step 3 - Plan Risk Response - performed by Risk Owner

  • Determine if Risk is in scope of project

    • If Risk can be transferred to another project or organization

  • Transfer risk and track it as External Dependency

    • RECORD: document decision in Status portlet. Change Stateto CLOSE

    • If Risk is in scope of project

  • Create Risk Mitigation Plan and store results in the Summary of Risk Mitigation Plan portlet

  • Determine Risk containment approach and create Risk contingency plan and Risk reserve plan. Store in the Background portlet or attach as document in Document Management

  • RECORD: document decision in Status portlet. Change State to RESPONSE PLANNED


Step 4 - Continuously Assess Risk - performed by Project Manager

  • Track risk control information and determine risk control actions. Reassess risk factors, examine the risk for indications of the probability of risk increasing, and determine if the risk factors previously identified still apply. Execute Risk Mitigation Plan as needed.
  • Update Status portlet with results of the Risk assessment. Update Risk Matrix, Mitigation/Impact, Mitigation/Factors, Order of Magnitude Mitigation Cost/Benefit, Background, Summary of Risk Mitigation Plan, Consequences portlets based on the assessment.
  • Outcome of assessment
  • If the Risk has occurred Change State to OCCURRED
  • If the Risk has not occurred but is still valid State remains RESPONSE PLANNED
  • If the Risk is no longer valid Change State to CLOSED


 

  • Use the Rational PM Portfolio Viewer for detailed analysis of Risk
    • Utilize the Rational PM Pivot OPAL ENG351 Risk Details for analysis


  • Utilize the Rational PM Reports OPAL ENG351 Risk Details Report and OPAL ENG353 Risk Log to capture point in time reports and for distribution to stakeholders without access to Rational PM. Reports can be generated on a periodic basis and stored in the PCB to capture a point in time view of the Risks.



Step 5 - Launch Risk Control Actions - performed by Project Manager or Contract Business Office

  • React to risk occurrence, execute actions based on the Risk Contingency Plan
    • Update the Status and Actions Taken portlets. Validate the Risk Contingency plan and update the Summary of Risk Mitigation Plan portlet.
  • Outcome
    • If action is not required to react to the Risk occurrence Change State to IMPLEMENTED: CONTINGENCY
    • If action is required State remains OCCURRED

Basic states
Identified: Initial state
Assigned: Risk is being resolved with approved plan
Response Planned: Risk that has been supported with Risk Mitigation and / or Contingency plans
Occurred: Risk that occurred and requires review and possible response
'Implemented: Contingency': Risk that has previously occurred, plans and actions taken to control risk have finished
Closed: Risk has been resolved, validated, and accepted by the Risk Owner
Non OPAL States: These state values are available in RPM but are not used in the OPAL procedure
