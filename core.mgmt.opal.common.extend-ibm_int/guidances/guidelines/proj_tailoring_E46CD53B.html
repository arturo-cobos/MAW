Guideline: Project Tailoring (Internal)
This guideline provides information about how to tailor your organization's process asset library, so that the amount of project management effort applied to any piece of work is commensurate with its work classification and attributes.

Main Description

The classification of work performed by the organization varies considerably, from low-risk work efforts that may be completed in an hour or two to high-risk projects that may take several years to complete.  Typically your organization's organization reference document will capture local work classification type and any associated tailoring recommendation.

The purpose of tailoring is to apply organizational assets to a particular project in a manner that is consistent with project's objectives, constraints and environment without burdening the project with unnecessary activity. 

This guideline supports the Project Tailoring Procedure (IMSP150) and describes techniques for tailoring your organization's process asset library. They are:

  1. Tailoring the Project Plans
  2. Procedure Tailoring
  3. CMMI tailoring
  4. Guidance tailoring
  5. Work Product tailoring

Project Managers may choose to use organization's process asset library to their project as-is or tailor it such that they can benefit from proven practices while maintaining compliance with the intent and purpose of organization's process asset library; however usage of the Project Tailoring Procedure (IMSP150) and production of the project defined process are mandatory. 

The guideline also provides information regarding the Capability Maturity Model Integration (CMMI) and tailoring your organization's process asset library based on the desired level of process maturity.

Tailoring choices are documented in the project management system summary, in the project procedures description.
