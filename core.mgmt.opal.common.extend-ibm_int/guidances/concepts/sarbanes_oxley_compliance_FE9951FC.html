Concept: Sarbanes-Oxley (SOX) Compliance
Use this standard to comply with the Sarbanes-Oxley Act (The Act).

Main Description

Purpose

Use this standard to:
Establish requirements to be met to comply with the Sarbanes-Oxley Act (The Act).

Scope

This standard applies to:
Organization's support of clients that are publicly registered companies under the jurisdiction of the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC), which are those that must comply with the Sarbanes-Oxley Act (The Act). The Act regulates individuals responsible for corporate governance, disclosure and transparent financial reporting, and specifies several requirements ("Sections"), including, but not limited to, the following:

  • Management's quarterly certification of their financial results (Section 302)
  • Management's annual assertion that internal controls over financial reporting are effective, attested to by an independent auditor (Section 404)
  • Record retention policies (Section 802)

For example, Organization may support development or maintenance of financial reporting applications, or the internal controls that must be in place to support them, and must support The Act, as appropriate. Each Organization must establish guidance on how to implement and support The Act when supporting a client who must comply with The Act.

Standards

  • To support a client who must comply with The Act, the following requirements are mandatory:
    • Usage of Sarbanes-Oxley (SOX) Compliance Guideline
  • Compliance is required to all Sections of The Act, as applicable.
  • Each organization must establish guidance on how to implement and support The Act when supporting a client who must comply with The Act.
  • To make an annual assertion on the effectiveness of internal controls, all controls that are deemed significant to the financial reporting process must be documented and evaluated.
  • If processing transactions, hosting data, or performing other significant services, information on the design and operating effectiveness of internal controls is required to gain an understanding of the controls. This may be done through an evaluation of the controls or through a SAS No. 70 service auditor's report.
