Concept: Life sciences organization compliance standard
This standard defines the Life Sciences Organization and provides the list of the assets that are mandatory for a Life Sciences Organization.
Main Description

This standard applies to support of a Life Sciences Organization client.

Life Sciences Organizations are defined as companies that are involved in the manufacturing of the following for human use:

  • Pharmaceutical products (prescription products or over-the-counter products such as aspirin and cough syrups)
  • Biologics (including blood and blood products, vaccines, cellular/gene therapy, tissue regeneration)
  • Medical devices (such as IV syringes, dialysis systems, body implant products such as breast implants, pacemakers or stents used to repair blocked blood vessels, x-ray machines)

Note that this list concentrates on those sectors where GxP regulations apply. GxP is a collective term for good practices where the x stands for M (Manufacturing), C (Clinical) or L (Laboratory) and as the term implies, it concentrates on manufacturing, clinical and laboratory areas of work in these companies. It is also important to note that in addition to GxP regulations, other regulations, such as privacy, may also apply to all of these companies including those that are listed above and those that are excluded from the list.

Life Sciences Organizations are subject to government regulations (laws and guidelines) and directives, such as the United States Food and Drug Administration (FDA), United States Health and Human Services (HHS), the European Union Data Privacy, European Agency for the Evaluation of Medicinal Products, the Japan Ministry of Labor Health and Welfare (MLHW), and are required to comply with the applicable regulations. For an Organization's Life Sciences Organization Client, the compliance with these regulations is the client's responsibility. However, an Organization must ensure that the applicable regulations are complied with when creating, updating and/or managing regulated client applications, infrastructures, networks, COTS (Commercial Off The Shelf) products and/or interfaces. For a mapping of the Life Sciences Organization Requirements and how the Process Asset Library and Unified Method Framework support them, refer to the Life Sciences Organization Compliance Mapping.

Standards

To support a Life Sciences Organization client, the following assets are mandatory:

  • Life Sciences Organization Compliance Guideline
  • Life sciences organization validation plan
  • Life Sciences Method Adoption Workshop (MAW) in the Unified Method Framework.

To support a Life Sciences Organization, the following assets are recommended:

  • Life Sciences Organization Compliance Mapping, which provides a mapping of Life Sciences Organization Requirements, and the Process Asset Library and Unified Method Framework components that support them
  • Life Sciences Trainings, available from the Process Asset Library Training Home page:
    • Introduction to Regulatory Compliance 
    • Regulatory Compliance for Practitioners
  • Life Sciences Glossary, which includes terms and acronyms applicable to Life Sciences Organizations
