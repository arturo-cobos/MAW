Task: Develop Current State Process Maps
Develop process maps for current state business processes. Develop a current state business process map if none is available, or refine an existing one to capture the current state.
Disciplines: Business Modeling
Purpose
To identify the key milestones of business processes and create business process maps.

        Main Description

        Document current state or "as-is" process maps for business processes. If documentation on existing processes exist, validate and refine with business users.

        The high level process map is usually six to ten boxes which represent the high-level milestones in the current process. The process maps usually contain just the high level milestones and have no decision diamonds. It is a map of the current state of the process, not the to-be state or what it might look like in the future. There is no magic to the number six to ten milestones or phases. So it could be as few as four milestones and as many as twelve. More than twelve milestones and we are probably getting too detailed and could combine some to simplify.

        Steps
        Identify start and end points
        Identify start and end points first. This helps set limits right from the start. They may change as you communicate this map with more people but they are the starting point. It’s better if the scope is within the sponsor’s range of authority and responsibility.
        Name the process
        The process name should tell everyone what you are talking about. When you name the process don’t include acronyms or jargon. Keep the words straightforward business words.
        Add activities under milestones
        Add activities that are mentioned under their milestone. Even if you do not ask groups to list any activities when they build the high-level map, some will always come up. So add them, but don’t try to gather more. You will have fewer activities the higher in the organization your content provider is. People doing the work will want to give you more activities. The time for the detail is the process flow/process diagram.
        Capture roles
        Add roles to activities when they are mentioned. But don’t feel you need to get them all. You will be able to add them later.
        Document problems, issues, pain points & root causes

        %EOL% Document problems and inefficiencies as they come up along with their severity and frequency. However, now is not%EOL% the time to discuss them or solve them. Just document them.%EOL%
        Document improvement opportunities and ideas
        Document improvement opportunities and ideas as they come up. Don't discuss them; just capture them and move on. Document opportunities for automation and business transformation.
