Task: Identify Candidate Services based on Process Decomposition
Identify candidate services that are derived from an analysis of the process model.
Disciplines: Business Modeling
Purpose
To identify candidate services to build the service portfolio based on top down analysis of the business.
Relationships

    • Service Portfolio
      Main Description

      Many or most in processes are good candidates for services or for inclusion in the service portfolio as services and/or operations on services. Use future state business process as the basis for identification of services by selecting a subset of the activities and processes presented in the to be process model as candidate services. If an activity is a low-level IT activity, that pertains to or describes an action with an IT system it is probably a candidate for service operation rather than a service itself. Services are described by the service interface which contains a number of service operations.

      Steps
      Identify processes and sub-processes
      Eliminate fine-grained activities that interact with the user interface
      Eliminate a very high level processes that have too much baggage
      Update service portfolio

      Add the service to the service portfolio/service hierarchy in the service model.

      Update service hierarchy

      Add the service to the service portfolio/service hierarchy in the service model. Define service hierarchy by grouping service under categories based on functional areas identified.

      Candidate services organized using a business significant categorization scheme, making evaluation of candidate service more manageable. Domain Decomposition Functional Area Analysis typically provides the classification scheme.

      Key Considerations
      There are various means of service identification. Service and flow identifcation from processes is the focus of consideration in this task. Differentiate between systems and people/manual processes. Considerations of granularity are important. Decompose the processes into activities and sub-activities. Stop when you have arrived at the interactions with backend systems.
