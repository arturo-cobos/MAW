Artifact: Correspondence Document (ART 0563)
This artifact contains information communicated to relevant parties, and records that the notification has taken place.
Purpose
It provides evidence that key communications and transmissions have occurred and identifies the parties involved in the communication. It also provides a means of documenting specifically what was and was not officially communicated as a historical reference if a dispute should occur at some time in the future.  This communication may also act as a notification that the receiving parties may need to take certain actions based on the information provided.
Relationships
Container Artifact
  • Correspondence Log
Description
Brief Outline

The artifact should contain at a minimum the following:

  • Correspondence ID
  • Correspondence name (subject or short name of the correspondence)
  • Correspondence description (Details of information being communicated, including effective date for any notifications)
  • Category (for example, "notification", "approval", "informational")
  • Date sent (for outgoing communications)
  • Date received (for incoming communications
  • Sender’s name
  • Sender’s contact information (including phone, email address, street address)
  • Recipient’s name
  • Recipient’s contact information (including phone, email address, street address)
  • Other stakeholders (may include copied recipients of the communication)
  • Archival location

This artifact may also contain additional information regarding:

  • Impact of communication on recipients or expected actions to be taken
  • Whether formal acknowledgement of the communication is required
Tailoring
Impact of not havingIf the communication of key information is not recorded, it cannot be firmly established that resources were notified of information on which they should act.
Reasons for not needingSmall organizations or engagements with co-located teams may not need to document correspondence. In addition, another artifact such as a log, may be used instead track incoming correspondence.
