Role Descriptor: User Experience Professional
A human factors or usability engineering professional who applies user centered design (UCD) principles to provide guidance and expertise for developing solutions and producing work product deliverables to ensure that they are usable and productive.

Main Description
Usability engineering professionals are architects who normally have graduate degrees (masters or doctorates) in the behavioral sciences with additional training in computer science. In addition to the above qualifications, usability engineering professionals may have subspecialties as follows: User Interface Architect - responsible for producing the user interface design and evaluation plan, designing the overall UI solution design, creating iterative prototypes and design specifications. Visual Architect - responsible for the overall look of the product, including brand image, colors, splash screens, icons and visual resources . User Assistance Architect - responsible for on-line and hardcopy user information, help screens, prompts, and training. Usability Tester - responsible for writing usability test plans, conducting usability evaluations and testing, analysing results, and preparing usability test reports.
