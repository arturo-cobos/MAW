Main Description

The client's value proposition is the acknowledged business value and benefit to be derived by the client by engaging with IBM and implementing an IBM solution. It is is based on the client's need and business challenges and addresses the business urgency, new capabilities and other expectations of the client. Based on how the client defines value and puts us at a competitive advantage regarding the client's buying decision, the value proposition is substantiated when the solution is implemented, used as intended by the client and resulting benefits are acknowledged, contributing to the client's agenda.

Key to creating the client's value proposition are clarity on the clients compelling reason to act (need, issues to be resolved, timeline, expected benefits, consequences if nothing is done, buying behavior and value drivers, budget available), desired new business capabilities, best-fit solution options acknowledged by the client, tangible (expected business payoff, value or ROI) and intangible benefits expected by the client. Client value propositions move from general to specific as the opportunity and picture of the solution matures thru the Client Value Method phases.

In order to deliver value to the client, the client value proposition must be created and refined using a strategy focused on the client’s buying behavior expectations and win themes that differentiate IBM. As the solution is being designed and developed, the IBM team’s strategy is to create a value proposition focused on what the client is expecting and what IBM will deliver.
