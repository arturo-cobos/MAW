Checklist: Estimating Considerations
This checklist should encompass every facet of business problem, eliminating redundancies and speeding the flow of information. Accurate estimating begins with the understanding that every aspect of a project has a cost associated with it.

Main Description

Albert Einstein once said: “Not everything that can be counted counts, and not everything that counts can be counted.”

That’s why estimating involves listening to the client to understand project goals, predicting costs by understanding the requirements, using supplier and subcontractor relationships for pricing and constructability, capturing cost history and communicating this information in a timely and meaningful way to the project team.

There are many sources to assist in performing estimations. Agile with Discipline requires the use of the following:

  • Agile Estimating Checklist and Report

The checklist referenced above is divided into three areas:

  • Considerations for Work Breakdown Structure components
  • Considerations for Project Management
  • Other considerations

This checklist helps ensure that all considerations have been made when performing the estimate, so that sufficient time is allocated to perform the work.

Depending on the size, complexity, or type of project or task, the team member may make detailed considerations in some areas, or skip over others as not being applicable to the project or task being estimated.

The elements in this checklist must be considered when performing estimates, and therefore evidence of making these considerations must be documented and stored in the Project Control Book (PCB). Team members and Proposal Team Leader and Project Team Leaders are also encouraged to use any existing checklists which are currently in use to assist in performing estimates, to ensure that well rounded estimates are developed.

Check Items
Actual results from similar past projects have been identified
This must be verified by the estimator before estimating. If any are found, the estimator should consider using those results to perform or validate the estimate. If none are found, then other techniques must be employed.
Following task overheads verified for each task in the WBS

When estimating effort and duration, other people's (i.e. application specialists, technical specialists, enterprise services, managers and project office) time has been considered, for the following overheads.

Reviews and Assurance

The time for team members to attend meetings for the following plus any preparation and wrap-up time has been considered, i.e.

  • Quality Assurance reviews
  • Team meetings


Function Points

Time for team members to attend meetings as required to complete the Function Point Count and review activities has been considered, i.e.:

  • Function Point counting
  • Function Point Count review(s) and validation(s)
  • Reporting the Function Point Count(s)

Other Work Breakdown Structure considerations

The following other WBS structure considerations have been made: 

  • Configuration Management
  • Team consultation and interaction
  • Documentation
Following role overheads considered for tasks/ongoing activity

These considerations are made when estimating effort and duration. These considerations do not solely relate to management roles, but may also relate to the roles of team members.

Verify if the following roles have been considered:

  • Project Management
  • Change management
  • Reporting, including specialized reporting and feedback to management from team members
  • Budgeting
  • Tracking
  • Recruiting and resource management
  • Time for meetings and project reviews
  • Management of external dependencies
  • Risk assessment and management
  • Issue management
  • General management activities to manage the team
  • Maintenance of PCB
  • Maintenance of Project Management
  • Plan Maintenance of Solutions Engineering
  • Plan Maintenance of Quality Management Plan
  • Correspondence management
  • Revising estimates
  • Allow for learning curves for new technical environments
  • Mentoring by senior team members
  • Configuration management planning
  • Time recording
Following external/internal factor considered

The following factors that may impact the overall project from an internal or external perspective have been considered while estimating:

  • Resource Skill Level
  • Vendor and or subcontractor support
  • Additional testing tools
  • Application training
  • Specialized technical training
  • Post implementation support
  • Documentation
  • New environments and on-going costs to support these environments
  • Hardware capacity
  • Critical computer resources
  • Prototyping
  • On-going maintenance
  • Supplies (extraordinary)
  • Courier costs
  • Any specialized equipment needed for development that should be costed into the project. Ensure that all aspects of the product, not just the Software, are included in the estimate
