Deliverable: Agile Project Definition and Charter (DEL 0515)
This deliverable provides an elaboration of the client's initial charter or vision, and is a formal deliverable of the definition iteration. It includes the definition of the project after initial stories are defined and decomposed and planned for the in-scope work. It enables the team to secure the commitment of the stakeholders and impacted entities to support the agile with discipline approach within the time and budget constraints.
Purpose

It serves as the basis for all work performed within the boundaries for the project, and is used to document and obtain commitment for an “Agile with Discipline” approach.

Main Description

This deliverable is extremely important and serves as the single blueprint for the approach forward and as a unifying contract between stakeholders. It is used to open, manage, implement and close the effort. It is the single point of reference for goals, objectives, deliverables, estimates, resources, and budget commitments.

Ensure all Accelerators/Best Practices are listed within the document.

Obtain approval of the document from key stakeholders to pursue business needs utilizing Accelerators/Best Practices, such as variable scope management, which is typically a major change from their past project experiences.

Develop initial drafts with the Business Customer, and/or via brainstorming sessions with additional stakeholders. Ensure the Business Customer drives the initial sections.

In specifying the project and general approach at a high-level, ensure:

  • Projects are not too large or complex.
  • Each project consists of a small, dedicated core project team of 5 to 10 professionals.
  • Use of dedicated facilities and equipment for the Project.

Use Roles and Responsibilities document for standard roles when specifying the resources.

Refine the deliverable in conjunction with drafting the project plan.

Review with stakeholders and update to reflect their feedback.

Obtain all approvals, including from the business customer, from business and executive I/T Sponsors and from other key stakeholders. To support audits, ensure all approvals are kept in the AWD Control Book.

Ensure the final version is accessible to the team, password-protected and version controlled.
