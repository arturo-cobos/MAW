Guideline: Developing the Deployment Plan
Migrated development approach from existing IBM Global Services Method work product.
Relationships
Related Elements
  • Deployment Plan
Main Description

The following items must be identified and incorporated into your plan document:

  • Compatibility and conversion criteria.
  • Target dates for starting and completing deployment, including the times when the attempted deployment of the new system would be difficult.
  • Any suggestion for implied sequence for the deployment.
  • New roles, responsibilities and/or skills required.  Identify the types of individuals that are responsible for the migration of data resulting from restructuring, modularization, or file conversion related to the new system.
  • Any specifications or limits on the deployment of the system components.
  • Training, education and learning objectives from the end-user perspective.
  • Planned supplier support structure.
  • Support criteria.
  • Transportation, shipping, installation, and integration issues.
  • Issues with existing applications being replaced or enhanced.
  • Gaining the project sponsor’s acceptance.

In support of the deployment, the following must be developed:

  • Deployment objectives.
  • A deployment team organization.
  • Hardware, software, and resource requirements.
  • Deployment schedules.
  • Milestones.
  • Coordination of the Deployment and Education and Training Plans.
  • A tested way to measure the impact on any related projects.
  • A fit with existing systems.
  • A plan to access archived data.
  • A list of critical success factors / a critical path.
  • A list of contractual obligations.
  • A Plan to measure how the conversion, installation and integration will be verified.
  • Site preparation checklists.
  • Site alteration recommendations, which are a list of the recommended modifications or upgrades, required to bring the client’s environment into compliance with the environmental requirements of the new system.
  • The orderly removal of the existing system.
