Artifact: Education and Training Evaluation (ORG 310)
The Education & Training Evaluation work product specifies the management, delivery, and evaluation of the training of management, staff, and other stakeholders.
Domains: Learning
Work Product Kinds: Assessment
Purpose

This work product provides for the delivery and evaluation of training, including training staff to deliver training, process knowledge, skills, job and role and functional training.  The delivery and evaluation of education and training is key to preparing management and staff for their roles in the new organization and to enable them to use and understand new systems, processes, and ways of working. The evaluation should ensure that this training has been successfully delivered and the requisite skills achieved.

Key Considerations
  • Based on the course evaluations and informal feedback, the post production support strategy should be revised as needed.
  • If at all possible, have the client take full responsibility for scheduling the training.
  • If a client chooses to deliver training using their own instructors, refer to the Train the Trainer technique paper.
  • The training instance and data load must be carefully planned and managed.
  • It is recommend that a client representative be in all classrooms to address process related questions and to facilitate knowledge transfer to the client.
  • If a dress rehearsal is conducted, do not underestimate the preparation time needed. It will likely take a full-time coordinator several weeks with a great deal of involvement from the subject matter experts, security, and the training team.
  • To facilitate hands-on end user training, a training database may be created, populated, and maintained.
Tailoring
Impact of not havingWithout the work product, the IBM team may not deliver quality training to the client.
Reasons for not needingThe work product is necessary for a successful implementation where additional skills development is needed. However, if the client delivers the end user training or outsources it to another company, this work product may not be required.
