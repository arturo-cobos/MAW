Main Description

The work product includes the actual training material as well as other information such as a detailed description of the course/s that will be offered, the user roles which will be associated with each course, the type of delivery method/s to be used, the locations of the classes, quick references, help files, and job aids. These materials may be as simple as a presentation, or as complex as an entire classroom or distance learning curriculum.

The Learning Solutions Material document also contains:

  • A mapping of the system transactions to roles followed by a mapping of those roles into courses.
  • A description of the delivery method for each course. If a training instance is being used, then indicate the plan for setup and maintenance of the instance. If using the web, be sure to indicate any plan for establishing the links to existing or new websites.
  • A list of locations available for education and training, and their specifications (classroom, size, number of PCs, available dates, technical setup, etc.). An identification of the instructors for each training module is included as well as the number of participants to attend each training course and how many times each course should run.
  • Based on the roles that are mapped to courses, a curriculum is published and verified with the sponsor.

Key Considerations

The Instructional Designer will need to work closely with the Information Developer(s) and other development team members to ensure that his/her vision is realized, especially in those cases where a detailed design is not available to the development team.

The importance of QA and testing in the production of the Learning Solutions Materials cannot be overstated – even the simplest of errors. If overlooked and put before the client, can affect the client’s evaluation of the overall quality of the solution, and may affect final customer satisfaction and repeat business opportunities.

QA responsibilities are not restricted to the Information Editors and Testers; it is the responsibility of every development team member. Each development team member must perform his/her role with quality in mind, and must accept responsibility for the overall quality of the deliverable. In doing so, the team increases its chances of delivering a learning solution that will meet or exceed the customer’s expectations.

In creating the Learning Solution Materials, communication between team members and between the client and development teams is critical. The PM should ensure that communication systems are in place, that team members are briefed on communication protocol, and that team members make use of the systems in place to inform and remain informed.
