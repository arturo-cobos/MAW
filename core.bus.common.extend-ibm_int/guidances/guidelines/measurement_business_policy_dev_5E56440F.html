Guideline: Developing Measurement Business Policy
Relationships
Related Elements
  • Measurement Business Policy
Main Description

The following steps will lead to a set of policies that are documented, well understood and effectively managed:

  1. The corporate measurement program, if it exists, typically prescribes a number of business policies that each organization should adopt.  This set of policies should be incorporated into a specific business policy to improve standardization across organizations.
  2.  Each of the business processes required to collect measurement data, analyze measurement results, and report measurements should be reviewed to determine what business policies are required to make the process repeatable, accurate, and well managed.  These policies should make up the organization-specific content of the Measurement Business Policy.  Sections are provided in the basic business policy document to provide for capturing policies at the required levels for an organization. Sections describing the implementation plan for the measurement policies and the audit procedures should also be provided.
  3. The Measurement Business Policy should be reviewed by each of the key business process owners to reach agreement on the established processes.  Each of the business process owners should sign off on the business policies.
