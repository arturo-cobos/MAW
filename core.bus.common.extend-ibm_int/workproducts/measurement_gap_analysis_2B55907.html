Artifact: Measurement Gap Analysis (BUS 359)
The Measurement Gap Analysis work product identifies measurements that are critical to the achievement of the client’s stated business goals and that are not currently being adequately collected, analyzed and used.
Domains: Business Value & Financials
Work Product Kinds: Assessment
Purpose
The main purpose of the Measurement Gap Analysis work product is to identify the gaps between the measurements that IBM proposes to a client and the measurements that the client currently collects, analyzes and uses. Additionally, the Measurement Gap Analysis examines the existing organizational structure and processes to determine if they can adequately support the measurement model. Inadequacies are identified and requisite changes are proposed.
Description
Main Description

The Measurement Gap Analysis work product identifies new processes or changes to existing processes that should be instituted to support such measurement data collection, analysis, and use. Furthermore, it proposes changes to the organization or its supporting processes so that it can support the collection, analysis, and use of the selected measurements.

The Measurement Gap Analysis work product articulates the difference between the instance of the measurement model and the customer’s current measurements. It provides recommended refinements to the following:

  • measurement model
  • process definition
  • future organization design

The term “measurement” as used in this document reflects the sorts of entities that must be elicited when defining and developing the measurement program and system for an organization.  Generally, the term “measurement” can refer to either a computed metric, which involves a calculation among primitive metrics, or a primitive metric that reflects a directly observable or countable event or data point.

Brief Outline

The notation for the Measurement Gap Analysis work product should be a document produced by typical word processors.  It should be consistent with the notation used in the measurement model.  Where appropriate, sections of the document should be in simple table format.

The Measurement Gap Analysis document should include, as appropriate, the following sections:

  • Introduction – Briefly describe what the Measurement Gap Analysis document is all about.
  • Purpose – State the purpose(s) of the Measurement Gap Analysis.
  • Scope – Provide a statement limiting the scope of the measurements covered in the gap analysis to organization-level measurements.
  • Description – Outline the steps taken in performing the gap analysis.
  • Gap Analysis Overview – Provide a high-level summary of the overall results of the gap analysis relative to the measurement categories.
  • Mapping of Current Measurements to Measurement Model – Provide a table listing the selected measurements from the measurement model versus the client’s measurements.
  • Identified Gaps Relative to Proposed Measurements - Provide a summary of measurement gaps and data gaps.  A gap may be a selected metric that the client does not currently collect.  A gap may also be a discrepancy in the value of an attribute of a selected metric.  For example, the measurement model may require the collection of metric m monthly, but the client may only be collecting it semi-annually.
  • Current Customer Measurements Not in the Measurement Model – Provide a list of measurements that the client collects, analyzes, and uses, but that are not among the selected measurements in the measurement model.  Those that do not support a business goal are marked as candidates for elimination.
  • Required Changes in Process Definition - Provide a list of necessary changes to (proposed) client processes in the context of the measurement program.
  • Required Changes in Future Organization Design - Provide a list of necessary changes to the (proposed) client organization in the context of the measurement program.
  • Other Requirements Critical to Successful Implementation - Provide a list of other requirements, if any, that are deemed necessary for the client’s measurement program to succeed.
  • Glossary – List terms and their definitions or acronyms and their expansions if these are unique to the client and not otherwise covered in the project’s glossary.

Tailoring
Impact of not having

Without this work product, it would be impossible to see the relationship between the measurement model that IBM is proposing to a client and the client’s current state of affairs with respect to measurement. Not making this relationship explicit could lead to any of the following:

  • A failure of the client to accept the measurement model
  • Significant issues on the client’s part with the measurement model after its implementation has begun
  • A failure of the client to accept the implemented measurement system
Reasons for not needingThis work product is critical to the success of the measurement program. There is no reason for exclusion of this work product. If the client does not currently capture any measurements or does not have any measurement program in place, the entire list of selected measurements in the current instance of the measurement model, by default, represents the measurement gap. All of the selected measurements would be considered unsatisfied.
