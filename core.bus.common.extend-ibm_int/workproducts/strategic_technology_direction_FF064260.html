Artifact: Strategic Technology Direction (ART 0640)
This artifact is a high-level description of the strategic vision, goals and objectives an enterprise intends to achieve with its Information Technology capabilities and the technology transition initiatives required to implement the business vision, goals and objectives.
Domains: Business Strategy
Work Product Kinds: Strategy
Purpose
A clearly described Strategic Technology Direction provides the management of an enterprise or its IT function with a strategic context for direction setting and prioritization as well as guidance for technology strategy formulation and execution. The artifact supports making strategic technology decisions that will result in a closer and more effective alignment of technology capabilities with business strategy requirements.
Description
Main Description

This artifact is a high-level description of the strategic vision, goals and objectives an enterprise intends to achieve with its Information Technology capabilities, how its current IT capabilities align with and support the business goals and objectives, the characteristics and capabilities of the future technology environment that it needs to deploy to achieve those goals and objectives and the technology transition initiatives that are required.

Brief Outline

The Strategic Technology Direction artifact typically contains the following sections:

  • Summary of Business Vision, Mission, Goals and Objectives
  • Summary Description of Current Technology Envronment and Capabilities
  • Assessment of the Alignment of Current Technology Capabilities to Business Strategy
  • Identification of Gaps, Strengths and Weaknesses of the Current Technology Capabilities
  • Identification of Technology Trends and Capabilities in the Industry
  • Vision of a Future Technology Environment and Capabilities Aligned to Business Strategy
  • Identification of Key Transition Initiatives to Achieve the Future Technology Vision
