Main Description

This document describes the objectives, scope and activities of the Service Delivery turnover process. In some organizations Service Delivery is known as Production Support, Operations, or other similar titles. This definition is intended to assist the practitioner in understanding and performing a structured turnover from the application development/ maintenance team to the service delivery organization.

The Service Delivery Turnover Definition serves to define the process of turning over applications or systems for SD activities and the work product describes:

  • The scope of SD turnover from development team/maintenance team to the SD team
  • The activities required for the SD turnover

Service Delivery (SD) comprises of those activities that are involved in running a production system on a daily basis and resolving any run-time/production issues that crop up. This may be a service provided by IBM under contract to a client organization or a group within the client’s organization that is responsible for this kind of activity. IBM Service Delivery involves any or all of the following organizations:

  • IT Optimization.
  • Strategic Outsourcing.
  • Infrastructure and System Management Services.
  • Technical Support Services.
  • Network Services.
  • Application Management Services

Key Considerations

Acceptance criteria should focus on the needs of the SD team to provide its services effectively rather than on the design constraints on the System. Some questions that the SD team might ponder in deriving acceptance criteria:

  • Can SD team members efficiently access the documentation? Is the documentation in the proper and understood format and with proper nomenclature?
  • Are ‘Requirements’ and ‘Specifications addressing those requirements’ clearly documented?
  • Are testing results and resolutions to defects clearly documented and understood?
  • Does the documentation provide a useful picture of the whole application or the whole service and how the sub parts are interrelated?

Determine other operational procedures (e.g., asset change management) and support tools (e.g., problem trace) that could facilitate the SD services and decide how to obtain them.

Consider IT continuity requirements as essential, based on the engagement context.

Identify all software and hardware dependencies and when they are needed (for example, before the first iteration, after the second iteration) to avoid schedule delays. There is often significant lead time required if hardware or software needs to be approved, acquired and installed.

If the development/maintenance team and the SD teams are part of the same organization, the SD turnover is subjected to the guidelines and processes outlined by the development/ maintenance team at the start of the engagement. If the organization does not have an established turnover preparation and takeover process, the guidelines provided in this document can be used to define a new process for this activity. It is important to note that the SD turnover involves a certain effort and as such a cost is associated with it and will have to be accounted for. If the turnover includes the warranty period of application development, then the defects during that period should be tracked separately. Any additional effort involved in the tracking should be factored into the estimates upfront. 

Validation and verification is done with the client sponsor and IT client executives. The requirements should be previously documented by the client prior to the consulting engagement. A good way to validate the requirements would be to receive client approval on them and if they are not acceptable to have the costs available to meet the client’s requirements to ensure that the requirement outweighs the costs.
