Work Product Descriptor (Artifact): Application Maintenance Turnover Definition
The Application Maintenance Turnover Definition work product describes the application maintenance turnover objectives, scope and activities including the identification of assets to be turned over from the application development team to the maintenance team based on the application maintenance engagement.

Main Description

The Application Maintenance Turnover Definition intends to assist the application development team and maintenance team in understanding and performing a structured turnover.

The process of turning over a new body of application functionality and processes to ongoing maintenance and support organizations is accomplished by identifying the appropriate assets to be turned over based on certain acceptance criteria. A well documented turnover definition will ensure that the maintenance team understands the application system in its entirety for a smooth implementation and maintenance of the application in the production environment.

This is the plan for the transition of the application to the accepting maintenance/support team.

Key Considerations
  • It is important to note that the application turnover involves a certain effort and as such a cost is associated with it and may have to be accounted for. Most often this cost is included in the overall costs of the project itself and is part of the initial estimating and budgeting process. When appropriate or required for finance or accounting purposes, this cost can be broken out, budgeted and managed as a unique body of work.
  • The application turnover process can be complicated by the lack of appropriate collaboration between the development and maintenance/enhancement teams, (e.g., a quality turnover requires very high levels of communication between these parties). Sometimes the development and maintenance teams are one. Even in such situations, there are logical needs (e.g., information sources to conduct problem investigations, alteration of existing documents as opposed to creating new ones) which demarcates development from maintenance/enhancements. These needs should act as the focus of application acceptance definition, which extends to the need for a formal turnover packaging process. This need is supported by the production of the customer deliverable, in that those assets required for the successful delivery and maintenance of an application are properly provided. Strictly enforce the procedures supporting the production of the turnover packages.
  • While turnover preparation may start any time before the actual handover, it is best to start turnover preparation soon after business and functional requirements baseline is ready. This ensures that all parties throughout the life cycle of the project have understood any required assets. A good configuration management system will be invaluable in this objective.
  • Acceptance criteria should focus on the needs of the mainteance team to provide their services effectively rather than on the design constraints on the application. Some questions that the maintenance team might ponder in deriving acceptance criteria:
  • Can maintenance team members efficiently access the documentation? Is the documentation in the proper and understood format and of proper nomenclature?
  • Are requirements and specifications addressing those requirements clearly documented?
  • Are testing results and resolutions to defects clearly documented and understood?
  • Does the documentation provide a useful picture of the whole application and how the sub parts are interrelated?
  • Determine other operational procedures (e.g., asset change management) and support tools (e.g., problem trace) that could facilitate the maintenance/enhancement services and decide how to obtain them.

The diagram below illustrates the turnover of an application from the AD team to the AME team. It illustrates the involvement of the AME team in the creation of checklists and approval of assets (or deliverables) created during the turnover lifecycle till the transfer of responsibility to it. This process should not be considered to be a complete definition of all project management processes. 

 
The scope of AME activities involve making changes to the existing application as required by the client and testing those changes in a test environment before implementing them in a production environment. In this context, the AME team is responsible for:

  • Defining all project assets and acceptance criteria it deems necessary for initiating maintenance activities.
  • Creating maintenance assets and turnover assessment checklists by ensuring that all relevant project assets and acceptance criteria are defined. Generic maintenance asset and turnover assessment checklists are included in the customer deliverable.
  • Validating and verifying that the project assets meet the acceptance criteria for a smooth turnover.
