Main Description

This artifact documents the findings, risks and recommendations which are the results of performing a Viability Assessment. A finding (and the associated risks) could be anything that is likely to impact the delivery of the solution such as:

  • The ability to meet the solutions requirements.
  • The availability of the right skills.
  • The stakeholder acceptance of the technical elements of the proposed solution.
  • The ability to meet the schedule and budgetary constraints defined for the solution.
  • Lack of reusable elements in the solution.

Key Considerations

Consider the following when performing a Viability Assessment:

  • A Viability Assessment can be performed either as part of a formal Quality Assurance review or on a more "ad-hoc" basis when stakeholders or project management have raised concerns outside the normal review cycle.
  • Use any available checklists as a starter set of questions to ask.  Modify these before the review to suit the needs of the particular project.
  • Ensure viability issues are clearly stated and there is an action plan in place to address them before presenting the final report.
  • Ensure people who will be interviewed are briefed beforehand as to the purpose of the assessment so that they can prepare the right material.
  • When interviewing people as part of an assessment try to use neutral language as much as possible, do not use language which could be construed as apportioning blame.
  • It is better to raise critical issues early, when they can be resolved, rather than blindly following a schedule that leads the project team down the wrong path.
  • The assessment needs to balance risks against costs, remaining sensitive to the broader issues of project success.
  • Viability Assessments can be difficult and sensitive.  The reviewer needs to be a persuasive communicator who can raise and discuss sensitive issues.
