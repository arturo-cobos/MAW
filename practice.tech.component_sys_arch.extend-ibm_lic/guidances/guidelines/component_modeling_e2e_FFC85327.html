Main Description

The process of modeling components is covered in some detail in a set of UMF Guidelines, this guideline brings several of these together to form a view of Component Modeling from start of finish. The practitioner should make sure they understand the concept of logical and physical component differentiation prior to reading the guidelines describing the major sequential steps in Component Modeling:

  1. Identifying Components: Partitioning a system into subsystems, identifying components and assigning responsibilities to them
  2. Structuring Components: How to structure your Components to work towards the goal of acheiving high cohesion and loose coupling
  3. Specifying Components: How to specify Component interfaces, operations and signatures, as well as pre and post conditions
