Capability Pattern: Defining
Gain a thorough understanding of the sponsor's business needs, and outline what will be delivered to meet those needs, and an approach for how to deliver it. Also includes, defining the context and setting objectives of the project.

Description

This work includes:

  • Gaining an understanding of the objectives of the project and how the relate to the business needs
  • Gaining agreement on the target solution
  • Outlining at a high-level what will be accomplished and a general approach for how it will be achieved
  • Determining the scope and shape of the project

Audience: All persons responsible for defining projects or pre-sales solutions, such as Opportunity owners, Partners (identified as Relationship Owners on MethodWeb), IT Architects, Specialists, Consultants, Project Managers, and Proposal Managers.

These activities and tasks provide a thorough understanding of, and sometimes clarify, how the expected outcome of a project being considered will respond properly to a sponsor's business need. This activity group also covers determining the project approach and organization that are likely to minimize risks and best support IBM's business strategy. The key output of this activity group is a Project Definition work product that gives an initial (high level) description of what and how the project being considered would deliver, as a prerequisite to enter planning activities where these elements will be refined and estimated.

This activity is based on

  • Client Value Method (CVM) Phase 1 - Understand Client's Business and Needs: The purpose of this planning activity (CVM phase 1) is to evaluate the client's business environment and the client/IBM relationship. Building on the client relationship and understanding of the client's issues, the team explores a specific issue and UNDERSTANDS the client's desired approach to solve it. The team confirms WHAT value the client wants (value drivers) and HOW they want it (client expectations) for this opportunity. The progress indicator for this activity is a validated opportunity.
  • Client Value Method (CVM) Phase 2 - Explore Options and Approach: Team IBM begins to explore the options available to satisfy the client need that is gathered and validated in the CVM Phase UNDERSTAND. The various options should be evaluated to determine which best delivers the client value and demonstrates IBM Unique Value, showing how we go beyond the competition to offer the value the client wants.

Usage GuidanceThese activities may be done to define a project, program or long term endeavor.
Key Considerations
Changes to established scope may typically require a formal change request depending on when these activities are timed.

The Defining process applies to all projects where a preliminary plan, proposal or response is needed.  The process starts when a business need is expressed by a client and the solution design phase has commenced. This may be a new piece of work, or a request for work outside the scope of an existing Agreement. 

The result of the Defining process is:

  • A Project Definition, defining the scope of the project and containing a high-level shaping of the project being considered is developed and approved.
  • The preliminary, high-level plans are developed and approved.
  • The project's organizational work units (such as sub-projects or sub-contractor components have been identified and individual plans have been sketched for each.
