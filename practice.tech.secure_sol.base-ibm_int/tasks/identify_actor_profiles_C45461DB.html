      Main Description
      Identifying the various actors and the functions they can perform is one of the key components in determining security zones. Without understanding who needs access to assets and who does not, it is difficult for the team to plan the protections required to mitigate any associated risks. The team needs to identify the roles present in the environment, along with their location, data needs, transactions they can perform, and the environment in which they are performed. Some questions that will provide the overall picture of the individuals, groups and processes in the environment include:

      • Who are the users?
      • What is their role?
      • What environment are they located?
      • What business processes do they perform?
      • What actions can an actor perform?
      • What types of data is accessible to the actor?

      These questions are not exhaustive. The security professionals on the project team should reference ICAP and ISO for question topics to help gain a further understanding.

      The project team should capture this information in the manner most appropriate to them. While it is a good idea to have a spreadsheet or other document that captures all roles and their access rights, further means of documenting the information may be necessary.
