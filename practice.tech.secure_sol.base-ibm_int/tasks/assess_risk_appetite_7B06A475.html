Task: Assess Risk Appetite
Disciplines: Security and Privacy
Purpose
Identifying the Risk Appetite for an organization or business unit helps prioritize required security architecture components according to the impact and likelihood of risks. The Risk Appetite for each organization can be different; some organizations are more risk adverse and others more risk tolerant.

    Main Description

    Risks should be identified in the early planning stages and refined throughout project. As requirements are refined, the resulting affect on risk must be reconsidered and the Risk Analysis updated to reflect those changes. Things that should generally be done when performing a Risk Analysis are:

    • Review the scope of the project, considering the entities that will be involved in the scope of the project
    • Identify the value of information assets to an organization within the scope of the project
    • Understand the potential vulnerabilities to these assets
    • Understand the potential threats against which the information asset must be protected
    • Design countermeasures or safeguards to address the risks
    • Determine the residual risk so the client can then determine its level of risk acceptance
    • Ensure all members/stakeholders have the same understanding of each risk

    All risks should be captured either in a spreadsheet or database and linked to a particular asset. The database should clearly link the risks to the associated assets and the threats, threat agents, vulnerabilities for those assets. In addition, the risks should be linked to applicable architectural decisions and requirements that address them. Each identified risk should be appropriately indexed and prioritized. The customer must sign off on each risk before moving forward with design. This ensures both the customer and the project team understand and agree on what the risks are, how the risks are to be mitigated, and what residual risk remains.
