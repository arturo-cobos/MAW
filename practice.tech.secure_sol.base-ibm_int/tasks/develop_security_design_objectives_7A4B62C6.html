      Main Description
      The Design Objectives are the goals that must be achieved when implementing the security solution. These goals should be based on the Business and IT Drivers, as well as the project requirements. They should also harmonize with the Security Principles.

      The Design Objectives are derived from the requirements that have been harvested throughout the project. They can be thought of as central themes the organization has expressed in its security requirements. An example of a Design Objective would be “Control access to information, consistent with roles, responsibilities and privacy policies”. Design Objectives are generally organized into 7 categories within MASS: ‘Defend Against Attacks’, ‘Correct and Reliable Operation of System Components’, ‘Defend Against Fraud’, ‘Trusted Identities’, ‘Protect Information in Store or Transit’, ‘Access Control for Information’, and ‘Access Control for Systems and Processes’.

      The objectives must be captured in a spreadsheet. The spreadsheet should contain the mapping of each objective to the above 7 categories. The objectives should be regularly referred to during the design and execution of the project, to ensure proper delivery.

      It should be noted by the project team that the Architectural Decisions used to support this task may be represented in more than one DCD (Deliverable Content Description). If the project team wishes to keep the decisions in both documents, they must ensure that both documents contain the same information. Otherwise, the team may wish to pursue only reporting one set of the decisions.
