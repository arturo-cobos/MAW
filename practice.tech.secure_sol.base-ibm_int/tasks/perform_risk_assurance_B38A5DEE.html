Task: Perform Risk Assurance
Disciplines: Security and Privacy
Purpose
Ensure the customer’s risk has been reduced to acceptable levels and requirements have been met.

      Main Description
      During this task, the solution will be evaluated to determine if it aligns the client with appropriate and acceptable levels of risk. As the aim of security is to reduce risk of asset compromise or loss, a review must be conducted to ensure the solution provides the appropriate levels of risk reduction. Although risk can never be eliminated entirely, the solution created should mitigate the risk to an acceptable level.

      If the solution has been created as a result of a prior client Risk Assessment, the project team should use that Risk Assessment as an input. The team should identify each risk presented in the Assessment along with how the solution addresses the risk. The change in likelihood, severity, or other values of determination in the risk rating should also be noted. This allows the client to see how much risk has been mitigated or transferred. A simple table or spreadsheet is an effective way to convey this information.

      Alternatively, if a Risk Assessment has not been performed, the project team should have an idea of the risk posture of the client through the ‘Risk Appetite’ task in the ‘Data Gathering’ activity. From this task, the project team should understand the clients’ current risks and their overall approach to risk. The project team should then take the known client risks as well as any risks that are deemed noteworthy and list them in a table or spreadsheet. The estimated likelihood should be noted, if known. Finally, the team should list how the solution addresses each risk and/or how it supports the risk posture of the client.

      The project team should present the information captured to the client for their review and sign off. This ensures the client agrees that the solution has provided them with the levels of risk reduction needed.
