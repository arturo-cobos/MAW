Generic examples of Security Principles that could apply to any type of project.

Main Description

A sample Principle might look like the following:

Security Design Principle

The most restrictive data protection or privacy policy will be applied globally. 

Motivation:

  • Different countries have different data protection and privacy legal requirements.
  • Applying different policies in each country will make it difficult to manage transfer of personal information and it is likely legal rules will be broken. Therefore, a consistent policy is needed for data protection and privacy across the company.

Implication:

  • A policy needs to be created that is a superset of all legal requirements for data protection. It is likely that extra controls will be implemented in countries where they are not required

Security Guiding Principle

All successful and unsuccessful logon attempts must be logged. 

Motivation:

  • Logging of all login attempts will allow for monitoring of intrusion or unlawful access to the systems. Risk of sensitive information leaks is minimized through production of activity logs

Implication:

  • The amount of logging throughout the network will need to be determined.
  • There are additional storage and log management costs.
  • Policies and processes need to be in place regarding storage and management, use, reporting, escalation of logs.
  • Which systems log the information, logging has to be supported.
