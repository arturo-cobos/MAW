Task: Package proposal components and review with client (IMSP491)
This task will consolidate the proposed plans and commitments, and review them with the client with the goal of obtaining their approval of the proposal.
Purpose

To consolidate the proposed plans and documents into a single repository so that all relevant material can be easily distributed to, or accessed by, people who provide approvals, and to facilitate the understanding of and the approval of, the proposal by the client.

        Main Description

        This task is based on the WWPMM work pattern P4: Finalize sponsor agreement agreement and WW QA "Proposal Baseline Assessment (PBA)" and "Contract Baseline Review (CBR)" processes, if applicable.

        This task is launched when all the information that is required to finalize the proposed Agreement is available. This activity must be executed in compliance with the policy and procedures of the delivery organization that are applicable for legal concerns as well as for pricing and should be customized accordingly.

        This task is launched when the Work Breakdown Structure (WBS), work estimates, and the overall schedule to produce the project deliverables are worked out.

        It consists of identifying other elements of costs than the basic costs to produce the project deliverables. One example could be the cost of implementing specific procedures that the project will need to implement in order to facilitate its management and the communication with all stakeholders. Additional constraints may be identified as well and might lead to additional risk.

        Although a number of tradeoffs have been made between potential options when defining the project target solution, approach, and overall organization, in order to minimize risk factors, it is now time to assess residual risks and define appropriate responses. This may add activities and checkpoints to the project WBS, require specific additional resources, and necessitate the planning of additional features in the management system to address these risks.

        Special care is thus given to the Risk Management Plan and to the final writing of the deliverable completion criteria. Additionally, the main features of the Project Quality Plan, Communication Management Plan, and Technical Environment Plan, are typically reviewed at this stage, as well as the composition of the future project management team and project office.

        For commercial projects, this activity interacts with the pricing process and the specification of the project income statements. The pricing process and income statements are typically dependent on each delivery organizations' business rules, and thus are not part of WWPMM scope.

        Finally, a proposal (statement of work, terms and conditions, offering proposal, plan contract, etc., depending on the delivery organization business rules) is finalized and reviewed before being submitted to the project sponsor.

        The key output of this task is the sponsor Agreement.

        This task includes the following:

        • Package proposal components and review with client
          • Consolidate the plans and commitments into a single repository so that all relevant material can be easily distributed to, or accessed by, those who will provide approval
          • Conduct a proposal review by the client to ensure that all deliverables have been identified and the client understands what will be delivered upon project completion
