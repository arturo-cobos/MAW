Task: Accept deliverables (IMSP540 and IMSP711)
This task will oversee the acceptance of a supplier's deliverables to the project, including acknowledging receipt of the deliverable, making a determination if the deliverable meets the acceptance criteria, and formally notifying the supplier of the acceptance or rejection.
Purpose
To ensure the formal successful acceptance of a deliverable from a supplier.

        Main Description

        This task is based on the WWPMM work pattern HD2: Accept deliverable.

        This task is launched when a subcontractor (or the sponsor) has just released a deliverable to the project (it may be a part of the final sponsor deliverable, a part of the technical environment, or of an intermediate work product). Acceptance work begins. Review the Agreement and Deliverable Definition to verify all clauses of the delivery and acknowledge receipt of the delivery. If delivery requirements are omitted, an issue is raised. Formal acknowledgment of delivery is recorded.

        Track progress of the acceptance work. This is done interactively by negotiating correction with the delivering group. Review the project team's validation findings on the supplier deliverable. If critical acceptance criteria are not being met or progress is slower than expected, corrective actions or issues might be needed. Findings are communicated to the supplier and prioritized. Corrective actions or issues should be raised if necessary.
        When the acceptance plan is complete and corrections verified, formally decide to accept or to reject the deliverable. Provide documentation of formal acceptance, acceptance with limitations, or rejection.

        The key outputs of this task are the Delivery Control Documentation or the Findings Log or the Event Log.
        The task includes the following:

        • Acknowledge receipt of deliverable
        • Initiate and oversee acceptance work
        • Determine acceptance or rejection and formally notify supplier
