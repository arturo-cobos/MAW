Main Description

The Learning Solution Design must describe in detail the "look and feel" of the training solution, as well as how the content is organized and presented. Once approved, the Learning Solution Design provides information and structure for Information developers, who will use the information provided here, as well as information from subject matter experts and existing documentation, to produce the course.

All parties who have a direct interest in this training should review this document. This ensures that a common vision is shared as the engagement progresses from the design to production.

Key Considerations

Ensure that the education detail offers enough detail for clients to visualize the learning solution in its entirety, and for development team members to develop the solution with minimal confusion and back tracking.

Remember that the Learning Solution Design is a contract with your client. The entire development team must be prepared to deliver on the contract, and it is therefore critical to discuss and resolve any potential areas for confusion.

When creating performance objectives, remember to identify those objectives for which learners can demostrate mastery in the intended environment. For example, it is not appropriate to indicate that learners in a classroom setting with no computers will be able to perform a particular function within a given application, or that learners in an e-learning environment will be able to complete a sale.

Stay in scope. If the client or a SME expresses a desire to include content or materials not included in the SOW, disucss the possibility of a PCR with your project manager.

If the learning solution is technology-based, be sure to consult with the appropriate individuals to ensure that the learning materials will be accessible within the client's technical environment. All media and other course elements should adhere to strict technical guidelines, and the Learning Solution Design should recommend events that are possible within these guidelines.

This work product provides the leverage to get mutual understanding of what is required between the development team and client. Involve the client core team intimately in the design so that they feel ownership of it. Having the client team present the new design to the sponsor is especially effective.

Whenever possible, team with Subject Matter Experts (SMEs) so that they feel that they are a part of the development team. As you will rely on them heavily for guidance during the production Phase, getting their buy-in early on is critical. One way to do this is to involve them in the creation of the Learning Solution Design, so that they feel ownership of it.
