The Story
Jane owns a small accounting business with three employees. She has just won a big new account and has hired a new employee to help her company with the additional workload. She has a personalized account at greatname.biz and uses the site to collaborate with her employees for business purchases.
Objective
Add a new employee to her company’s e-profile and get a new Office in a Box for him.
Logging in
Jane goes to greatname.biz and logs in to her personalized Office in a Box. When her personalized space appears, there is a message for her reminding her of the monthly supplies that she usually buys. Included in the message is a notification of the latest release of her tax preparation software. She is given options to add this upgrade to her supply order and to compare this new release to other tax software on the market.
Comparison
She clicks on the comparison button, bringing up a new window with tax preparation software sorted by price. She clicks on the sort by profession, listing the programs by their suitability for professional tax preparers. These comparisons confirm her choice. She closes the comparison window, and clicks on the upgrade button. The site informs her that she will need to upgrade her standard systems with an additional 32 megabytes of RAM to make the new software work efficiently. This is fine with her, so she clicks OK.
PricePad
Jane quickly reviews her supply list, and clicks on buy. The PricePad appears on her screen with the supplies, four upgrades for her company’s tax software, and the additional memory for her computers.
e-Profile
Next, Jane brings up her company’s e-profile, by choosing that button on her personalized page. The profile page contains a list of her most recent purchases at greatname.biz and several configuration options. She clicks on “add new users,” and types in information for her new employee. She assigns privileges and restrictions to the user’s account. When she is finished, the site asks if the new employee will need a new Office in a Box. Jane clicks on Yes.
Configurator
The configurator comes up with Jane’s standard office system. Since each of her employees works at home, they have a comprehensive set of office equipment, including their computer. The standard system includes the upgraded tax software and the additional memory that she has on her price pad. She clicks to add the system to her PricePad, and the ship to address is prepopulated with the new employee’s information.
Volume Incentive
Jane clicks buy on her PricePad, and a detailed invoice appears on her screen. A pop-up window announces that because of the amount of her purchases that day, she is entitled to a 5% discount and free shipping. Jane clicks continue and buys all of the items on her PricePad.
