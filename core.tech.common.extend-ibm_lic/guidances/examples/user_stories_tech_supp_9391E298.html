The Story

John is a partner in a consulting firm, and his firm buys hardware and software from greatname.biz because of their superior service and efficient online commerce applications. John and his associates rely on the Internet for e-commerce, research, and communication with their clients John’s company relies on one of greatname.biz’s partners to provide their Internet connection service. The company issues each employee a PDA for e-mail, calendars, and address books. Their PDAs are equipped with modems so that they can keep connected without having to carry laptop computers around.

Objective

Get his new computer, from greatname.biz connected to the Internet.

Getting it out of the box

John has just received a new computer from greatname.biz. He has taken it out of the box and connected all of the peripherals. His next step is to install the Internet Service Provider’s software on the computer so that he can connect to the Internet and begin some research. However, he is having trouble with the ISP’s software.

Bright Idea

John is a very experienced computer user, and knows that with the Windows Dialer and Internet Explorer already installed on his computer, he won’t need his ISP’s software. He will configure them himself, and be online soon. As he is using the Windows troubleshooting software to set up the dialer, he finds out that he just needs some information about the TCP/IP configuration. Like many confident people with years of experience using computers, John would rather go online to get the information that he needs rather than calling the customer support line.

The PDA Interface

Because John is a big fan of his PDA, he regularly uses it for his e-mail and to connect to the Internet. He also uses his PDA to make online reservations. Often, he has even used it to visit the greatname.biz Web site to make impulse buys. John connects to greatname.biz and uses the online help to get the TCP/IP settings he needs to set up his Windows dialer.

The Solution

John logs on to the site using his personalized settings. His profile contains information about the company’s standard hardware, software and ISP configurations. When he writes “How do I connect to the Internet?” at the natural language search prompt on his PDA, he is quickly taken to an area of the site that will walk him through the process of connecting his computer to the Internet.
Each screen on the PDA interface helps him complete a step in the process, much like the Windows troubleshooter, but the Web site is able to take him a step further than Windows. It knows his hardware configuration, the software that he has pre-installed on his computer and the TCP/IP settings that he should use. If all else fails, the Web site will connect him with a customer service representative. However, that’s not necessary this time, because with the help of the Web site, John is able to get up and running on the Internet in a few minutes.
