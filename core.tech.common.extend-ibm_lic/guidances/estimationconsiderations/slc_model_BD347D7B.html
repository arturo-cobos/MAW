Estimation Considerations: Service Level Characteristic Model
These are general considerations to be used when estimating how long it will take to construct a service level characteristic model.

Main Description

The effort spent analyzing the service level characteristics of the proposed architecture varies by orders of magnitude between projects.  It can vary from being implicit within the development of the architecture models, through one or more short reports based on existing information, to major studies requiring specialist teams, substantial prototypes or simulations and several alternate designs.

To estimate the effort, consider:

  • Whether a separate analysis is required?  (If not, allocate zero effort to this artifact, and allow effort for appropriate analysis while producing the architecture models).
  • What service level characteristics (if any) need a separate analysis and report?  Estimate each study separately â€“ for example, there may be separate requirements to conduct a database sizing study and a continuous availability study.
  • How accurate or precise does any modeling and the resulting analysis have to be?
  • Whether the required information can be inferred from published sources or reference sites, or whether the analysis requires the building of models, technical prototypes, or other explicit supporting activities?
  • What is the required degree of completeness of the architecture models?  The degree of detail to which the service level characteristic model is developed is dependent on the required degree of completeness (precision, accuracy and detail) of the architecture models.

The service level characteristic model brings together the results of multiple other activities, such as the creation of several alternate architecture models and the execution of several service level characteristic models against those architectures with possibly varying inputs.  The time required analyzing these multiple threads of work, and to reach recommendations is probably a small fraction of the time it will take to actually perform the work upon which the service level characteristic modeling is based.
