Main Description
The performance of an IT system usually has three aspects - all of which must be provided for when delivering a new business system to a client:
  • Response time, both on-line and batch
  • Throughput, for example, the number of transactions per second, or records processed per time-period (minute, hour, day, month, etc.).
  • Capacity, for example, processor, memory, network, storage, etc.
The meeting of performance requirements can be of equal priority to meeting the functional requirements of the system. ‘Poor’ performance is a major contributor to customer dissatisfaction. Increasingly performance of systems is apparent to the real end customer, who will often interact directly with the system. Inadequate performance can result in project delays, cost overruns and missed business opportunities.
 
The purpose of Performance Estimation and Modeling is to generate views of both the performance and required capacity of key elements of the system, long before that system is fully developed, and in sufficient time to allow corrective action to be taken if necessary. It is also the basis for further capacity plans once the system is live.

This technique paper provides a subtask by subtask guide to achieving appropriate levels of performance of a system, as stated in the non-functional requirements, throughout the complete Lifecycle of a project or programThe approach discussed in this document assumes that strict, well-defined performance requirements have been specified for the new system. Adherence to these requirements will be a key customer determinant of the success of the overall system once delivered. However, in situations where performance is less of a concern, a more lightweight approach would be more appropriate.
