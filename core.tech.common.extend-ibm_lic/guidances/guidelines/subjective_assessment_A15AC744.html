Guideline: Subjective Assessment
Main Description

Overview

A questionnaire is used as an instrument for the subjective measurement to assess the adherence of a practice. The questionnaire is an inexpensive, fast and easy instrument. For each practice, the questionnaire consists of 4 to 5 closed-ended questions. Each question is designed to gather information on how the practitioners perform a practice. Subjective Assessment can be performed anytime during the project’s lifecycle. However, it is recommended to assess the practice adherence regularly for example monthly or every iteration.

Assessment Process

  1. Preparation
    1. Project manager or the person who is responsible for the practice assessment selects the practices which will be assessed.
    2. Gather the questionnaire for each practice. You can obtain the questionnaire by following the link from the Practice’s table of content or you can use the excel file which are provided here.
    3. Prepare the questionnaire. If you do not use the provided excel file, you can copy the questionnaire from selected practices into any type of document for distribution. However, we recommend to use the excel file since it will be easier when you are perform the analysis.
    4. Select the team members who will participate in the questionnaire. Note: you should only distribute the questionnaire to the team members who use the selected practices.
    5. Decide the mechanism to distribute and gather the data. Typical mechanism are e-mailing. However, if you want the questionnaire to be anonymous, you may not want to use e-mail.
    6. Set the due date for completion.
  2. Inform the team
    • Project manager or the person who is responsible for the practice assessment informs the objective of questionnaire to the team members who will participate in the questionnaire. Make sure they understand that this questionnaire is not for evaluate their performance but rather to collect information on how well the team is doing on specific practices.
  3. Distribute the questionnaire to the team members
    • Follow the mechanism in Step 1(6) to distribute the questionnaire.
  4. Collect data
    1. Collect all the excel files from the participants 
    2. Copy the score from the excel files which you are collecting to the original excel file in the column provided.
  5. Analyze data
    • The excel file provide the results of the questionnaire in average column, overall score chart and the distribution score charts
    • Average score shows how well your team is doing in the specific questions. The goal is to have the score as close as 4 as possible.
    • Pitfall for average score: for each average score, check the deviation. If the deviation is very high, it mean that there is very high
  6. Present the results to the team members 
  7. Indicate improvement 
    • Define the target area that your team needs to work on. you should focus on the practices that have low average score
