Guideline: Defining Non-functional Requirements
The objective of this technique is to assist in defining the Non-Functional requirements for the target business system, captured within the Non-functional Requirements work product.

Main Description

The non-functional requirements for a business system address those aspects of the system that, whilst not directly affecting the functionality of the system, can have a profound effect on how that business system is accepted both by the users and the people responsible for supporting that system.

These aspects cover a broad range of themes, ranging from how easy the system is to maintain once installed, to how quickly the individual transactions within the system can be executed.

The major non-functional themes identified by the Application Enabling & Design Technical Council are listed below:

  • Performance (including Capacity)
  • Scalability
  • Availability (including Recoverability and Reliability)
  • Maintainability (including Flexibility)
  • Security
  • Manageability
  • Environmental (including Safety)
The requirements of the system to be delivered must be understood for each of these non-functional themes. Together with the functional requirements they define the baseline against which the business system must be designed. It is essential that both sets of requirements are captured during the initial stages of a Project.

This technique will help the project team identify, and then capture, the correct level of information for each of the seven non-functional themes listed above.
