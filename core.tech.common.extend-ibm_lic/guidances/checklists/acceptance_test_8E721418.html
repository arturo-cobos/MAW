Checklist: Acceptance Test Plan

Related Elements

Check Items
Acceptance criteria and tests is be clear and unambiguous

Be written so plan recipients can easily understand what will and will not be done.

Sign-off process is explicit and agreed to, in advance, by all stakeholders

In order to be most effective it should include a clause in the contract with the customer stating that the system will be formally accepted if all acceptance tests are successful.

The appropriate static testing has been performed on the acceptance criteria and plan
Is specific to which tests, data and environments are required
Be very specific as to which tests, data, and environments are required to make this determination, and how they will be defined.  Include the detailed data in appendices not the body of the report, this practice will make reading easier and more focused.
Includes built-in joint sessions with developers, testers and clients
Include built-in joint sessions with developers, testers and clients, or their designates, in order to grow consensus and buy-in as early as possible, resolve issues, and dissolve us/them boundaries.
Uses the Use Case Model
Requirements documentation is often lengthy and full of ambiguities.  The Use Case Model is constructed to simplify understanding of what is meant by the requirements and to reduce confusion.  It effectively assists both the development and testing teams to understand what is expected.  It is very helpful to the acceptance test team in establishing clear acceptance criteria.
Uses static testing techniques
This ensures that the document remains clear to those who did not participate in its development.
Determine the test execution process, as much as possible, as part of the plan.

This way, the tests can be incorporated into the previous levels of test, prior to the true acceptance test, with a greater chance of early detection of defects.

Includes all the elements of a good plan
