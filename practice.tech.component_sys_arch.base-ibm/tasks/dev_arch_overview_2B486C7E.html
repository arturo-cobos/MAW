Task: Develop Architecture Overview
Develop a high-level overview of the architecture.
Disciplines: Technical Architecture
Purpose
To create a high-level, shared vision and scope of the proposed architecture, allowing it to be communicated to the sponsor and other stakeholders.

    Steps
    Analyze Input Artifacts
    Review and analyze all existing and relevant artifacts that will help in understanding the requirements. In particular look at the objectives, constraints, and measures of success for by reading relevant customer documents, including artifacts that describe project goals.
    Research Assets and Products
    Review architectural assets, including reference architectures and descriptions of similar engagements, research potential products and packages that could be used as part of the architecture. Evaluate the deltas between requirements and specifications.  Evaluate the impact of the deltas on the total development approach and effort.
    Envision Alternate Architectures
    Using the requirements and list of potential assets and products create one or more potential architectures. Envision possibilities for the architecture by considering the nature of what is being requested and the kind of architecture that will be appropriate. Draw on experience to hypothesize about system concepts to find analogies, and investigate industry models and other model assets.
    Evaluate Alternative Architectures
    Evaluate the alternative architectures and assess the pros and cons of each. Document the rationale for all decisions as Architectural Decisions. This may require experimentation, benchmarking and prototyping. Assess the viability of implementing the proposed architecture.  If appropriate, build risk identification and mitigation prototypes. Review the alternatives with the sponsors and come to a decision.
    Document Selected Architecture
    Depict the selected architecture using an appropriate drawing or modeling tool along with associated documentation of the architectural elements shown.
