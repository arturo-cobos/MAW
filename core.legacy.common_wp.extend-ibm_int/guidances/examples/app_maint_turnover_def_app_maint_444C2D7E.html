Turnover objectives

Turnover scope

Major deliverables by the AD team to the AME team
Outline the major deliverables that will be delivered by the AD team to the AME team.

Service Level Agreement
Document any informal understanding of customer satisfaction issues that should be transferred, if needed, from the AD team to the AME team. Service Level Agreements ( SLA’s) constitute an important part of the satisfaction criteria and must be addressed between IBM AME team and the customer. The SLA is used by the AD team in designing and addressing non-functional and operational issues during development. These same SLA requirements are passed to the AME team to ensure continuity in the production environment. The SLA’s that need consideration are: Application Availability, Problem Responsiveness, Batch Window Availability, Escalation, Estimation Turnaround, Problem Resolution and Software Availability. Once in ongoing maintenance and production mode, the actual elements of the SLA may be the subject of new negotiations with the customer as experience with the application matures.

Planning framework

Brief description of each work unit
Break the turnover down into its major work units. (High-level WBS)

High-level turnover schedule
Provide a high-level schedule for application turnover.

Key assumptions and dependencies
List the key assumptions which, if invalid, would make it necessary to revise the turnover plan. List any key dependencies.

High-level estimates
Provide a high-level estimate of the amount of effort involved in knowledge transfer, assessing the application assets, creating checklists and devising acceptance criteria. The estimate is required to plan for turnover duration and resources before committing to start maintenance activities.

Overall risks and risk mitigation plans
List any risk factors associated with the application turnover. Identify risk mitigation measures.

High-level training and HR plan
Document any necessary skills and resources that will be needed by the AME team.

Team Structure

Customer organization
Provide information about the customer organization in the form of an organization chart. This may be necessary for issue and/or risk escalation to the customer and for continuation of seamless interactions with customer after maintenance activities are initiated.

Application development team organization
Provide information about the AD team structure in the form of an organization chart. This is necessary for issue and/or risk escalation and communication between AME team and the AD team during the turnover process and warranty period.

Maintenance organization
Provide information about the AME team structure in the form of an organization chart. This may be necessary for issue and/or risk escalation by AME team members to AME Project Manager.

Turnover Asset Identification, Acceptance Approach and Turnover Assessment
Depending on the scope of turnover, the following is to be determined:
· Type of asset and asset status
· Acceptance criteria
· Configuration control procedures
· Packaging considerations
The above serve as inputs for the creation of:
· Turnover specific maintenance asset checklist.
· Turnover specific assessment checklist.
