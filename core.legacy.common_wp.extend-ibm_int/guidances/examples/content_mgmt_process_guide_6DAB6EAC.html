Main Description

Large numbers of websites can cause conflicting information and inconsistent presentation, which hinder customers from quickly and easily doing what they want. As part of the effort to enhance the customer web experience by converging websites developed over time by different business units, a common set of content management processes is defined here. The processes provide a structure for clear identification of content owners and responsibilities and for ensuring content quality. This process defines common roles and responsibilities and workflow that can be adopted by anyone developing a new website or anyone wanting to improve his website content management processes through use of documentation and automation.

This Content Management Process Guide provides mid-level documentation. It is intended to be used as a guide supplemented by additional activities as required to meet the unique requirements of a given area.

This guide also provides a definition of roles and responsibilities needed to execute these processes. One of the first steps to getting started is ensuring that a resource is assigned to each role and is prepared to meet the responsibilities associated with a given role.
