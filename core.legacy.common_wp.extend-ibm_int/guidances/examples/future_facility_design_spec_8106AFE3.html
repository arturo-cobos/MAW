Example: Future Facility Design - Specification

The design also included sections on Data Center Space Planning, Fire Protection Systems, Mechanical Systems, Electrical Systems, Data Center Monitoring Systems and Physical Security.

Main Description

Building Structure

The building steel structure would be designed for a three (3) story building, with the third floor as a future option.  The finished floor to ceiling height would be approximately thirteen (13) feet allowing for 9’-0” height ceiling, 2’-0” for structure, and 2’-0” for distribution of mechanical/electrical systems.

The lower floor’s structural slab would be designed for 100 psf live load throughout.  This is to support the raised computer floor area and mechanical systems located on the lower floor.  The lower floor slab would also be depressed at the raised floor so that the computer flooring would be at the same elevation as the balance of the floor which eliminates the need for ramps or steps into the computer room.  The entire concrete floor slab and the concrete walls for the data center should have a sealer applied to prevent dusting and moisture and water vapor penetration.  Any gaps at construction or expansion joints caused by differential settlement of the floor should be cleaned and resealed with new sealant.  The upper floor would be a concrete slab designed for 75 psf live load for primary office space and conference functions.  The roof of the building would be a structural slab designed for 100 psf live load.  The roof slab would be flat with a waterproof membrane roof and tapered insulation to create roof pitched for drainage.  This slab would be used as the third story if long term requirements change.

The foundation walls and exterior walls above grade to be +/- 12” thick reinforced concrete.  A perimeter drainage system would be required to remove water from the building perimeter, design to be based on a geological survey.  The interior walls at the stair, elevator, and computer room would also be reinforced concrete.  The exterior facade would be of metal sandwich panels applied to the concrete structural wall. A ribbon of windows may be included in the office areas on the east facade of the building.  These windows would be 1” laminated glass in fixed steel frames. 

All pedestrian entries into the data center building would be at grade with extra heavy duty steel doors and frames.  Sidewalks sloping away from the building will allow for handicap access.  IBM also recommends that a breezeway be installed to connect the new data center building to the main building.  There would be a separate exterior entrance to the breezeway to allow entrance to the data center building and/or the main building.    

The existing covered windows at the existing computer room on the north facade of the main building should have the temporary sheet metal covers removed and windows restored as required to allow natural light into the former data center and relocated mail room area.

General Construction

Fire-rated resistive and tornado resistive construction, including partition assemblies, door assemblies, hardware sets, and ductwork fire dampers should be used to protect the perimeter of the data center as well as to compartmentalize it internally as currently the practice.  Minimum recommended fire ratings used in most data center designs are as follows:

  • Data Center perimeter (concrete) - 2 hour
  • Office areas - 1 hour

Interior Partitions

The interior of the space would have gypsum board partitions either running slab-to-slab in conference and mechanical room areas or 6” above ceiling in office areas. A return air plenum would be created above the ceiling to allow air to return to the mechanical system air handlers.  Ducted returns would be utilized in full height wall areas

Ceiling System

The finished ceiling should ideally have a minimum of 9 feet of clearance above the raised floor The ceiling tile should be a sound absorbent, vinyl-coated (vapor resistant) tile which will aid in humidity control in the data center space.  Installation of the lay-in tiles should include metal hold-down clips. An airtight seal should be provided at all extended perimeter walls to contain fire suppression gas and minimize leakage of the controlled air-conditioned environment. 

The ceiling above the office area and the remainder of areas in the building should have a minimum height of 9 feet of clearance.  The ceiling should be standard 2x4 lay-in tile.

Raised Floor  (Access Floor)

The raised floor will be installed as a depressed slab. This would eliminate the need for any ramps, steps, and hand railings.  The 3600 square foot size of the depressed slab area should be large enough to provide for future expansion of the data center.  The offices and tape library are located on the raised floor.  These areas could be relocated off the raised floor in the future to provide for more space for future expansion. 

An 18 inch finish floor height will provide adequate space to create an area for computer cabling within the computer room as well as a plenum for computer room cooling.    

The new raised access floor should utilize panels with steel construction with a high-pressure laminate finish and a concentrated point load capacity of 1,250 psi.  The panels in high traffic areas, such as aisles or an equipment staging area, should have a 1,500 psi concentrated point load capacity.  A move-in rolling load of 1,250 psi and a repetitive manual cart rolling load capacity of 1,000 lb. should be designed.  All panels should be color coordinated. 

The understructure should be a system of pedestals and rigid grid of bolted stringers in a 2' x 2’ basket weave pattern.  Accessories as required for a complete system installation include cable cutouts, perforated airflow panels, plenum dividers, vertical closures (fascia), panel lifting devices, perimeter supports and equipment cutouts.  Equipment, furniture, fixtures and on-the-floor partitions should be carefully located for floor panel removal to facilitate access to the plenum area.

Architectural Summary Recommendations

  • A two level building with a concrete roof and expansion to an additional third floor.
  • A breezeway connection between the main building and the data center building.
  • Reinforced concrete exterior walls with minimum window areas.
  • Applied metal panels for the outside exterior walls.
  • Two hour, concrete slab-to-slab perimeter walls, floor, and ceiling around the data center
  • One hour, slab-to-slab perimeter walls for certain offices and conference rooms
  • 18 inch high raised floor system with a depressed slab
  • Suspended ceiling with vinyl-coated ceiling tiles for the data center
  • Building designed in accordance with ADA (Americans with Disabilities Act) requirements 
  • Access for equipment movement in and out of the raised floor area
