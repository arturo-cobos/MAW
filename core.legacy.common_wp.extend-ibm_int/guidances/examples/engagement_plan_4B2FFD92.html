Description and Purpose:
This is a skeleton deliverable for customizing as the output from an ITA Planning Engagement. It was produced during an actual client engagement and documents a proposed architecture definition plan to produce an ITA.
Note: RETCO is a fictitious name.
Introduction
This document summarizes the output from the Information Technology Architecture (ITA) Workshops held during the week of xx/xx/xx
The attendees at these workshops were (not all full-time):

  • IS Director - client
  • IT Strategist - client
  • AD Manager - client
  • DB Administrator - client
  • Telecomms Manager - client
  • Project Manager - client
  • I/T Architecture Consultant(s) - IBM
This document forms the basic terms of reference for the proposed RETCO Technical Architecture (TA) Definition Project.
Agreed Objectives of the RETCO TA Project
The overall objectives were agreed to by the group:
Note: The following is a sample set of objectives - customize as appropriate:
To define a structured and cohesive Information Technology Architecture which encompasses all relevant current and future platforms/networking environments
To provide a responsive technical framework that enables and enhances RETCO business directions and initiatives
To provide an understandable and maintainable statement of the I/T Strategy and how it will be applied within the company.
The sponsor of this RETCO TA project will be ........??
The owner of the TA will be........??

Statement of Benefits

The benefits to RETCO of carrying out this Technical Architecture (TA) Definition Project include:
Note: The following is a sample set of benefits - please customize as appropriate:
  • Known stable base for all future I/T hardware and software procurement and application development
  • Consolidation of existing technical strategies
  • Increased credibility of the Computing Division within the business
  • Identification of any new or changed I/T Strategies, policies and procedures required
  • Productivity benefits:

- Confidence in known stable framework.
- Training and skills can be focused.
- Increased flexibility in the resourcing of development and implementation projects.

  • Increased cost effectiveness
    - for future purchases and developments
    - through known support requirements
  • Increased quality of service through:
    - integration of known platforms
    - longer life-span of solutions

Agreed Scope

The scope of the TA is to include:
1. All Hardware and Software platforms/environments:

- Mainframe
- Midrange - including stores and depots
- Black box
- Personal - including mobile and desktop

2. Networking
3. Interfaces and Protocols (between and within environments)
4. System Services (print, e-mail, decision support, data management, file, security)
5. Systems Management
6. AD Environments (tools, methods, etc)
7. Package evaluation and selection criteria (to be further defined)
8. Outline action plan
9. Recommended processes for the management, control and implementation of the TA
At this point, no specific areas have been excluded from this scope, although it is understood that some may be defined to a greater level of detail than others.
This initial project will define a logical Technical Architecture with current implementation details included as appropriate.

There are three other main I/T initiatives, currently ongoing, which this TA definition project needs to be aware of:
Note: These are examples of initiatives:

  • Continued emphasis on the shortening of supply chains

Timings and Resourcing
Revised target dates and start date:
  • TA planning workshops held during the week of 20th August
  • Main TA Definition Project to commence in September, with the following activities to be included:

- Definition of a logical Technical Architecture with defined functions detailed as necessary. Initial implementations of each of these functions, based on current practice and standards, will be included where appropriate
- A formal QA review of the Technical Architecture
- An agreed set of follow-on activities to ensure the successful implementation and management of the TA

  • The agreed deliverables will be produced by end December.

Resourcing:
Following on from our discussions, we now have a clearer understanding of RETCO requirements. Based on IBM's experience with other, similar sized projects, we believe the resources required for this TA Definition Project will be as follows:
RETCO Resources
TA Owner - half/day week
Team Leader - 3-4 days/week
Team member - 2 days/week
Team member - 2 days/week
Project manager - 1 day/week
Documenter/administrator - 1-2 days/week

Note: The last two roles could be combined dependant on the skills and availability of resources within RETCO. However we would recommend that the person designated as Team leader, is NOT responsible for project management.
Whilst we realize that all the workshop attendees are very busy, their knowledge and participation will be key to the success of this project. We recommend consideration of RETCO's senior professionals for the Team Leader/member roles, whilst still making members of RETCO's I/T management team available for key workshops and review meetings.
IBM Resources
Lead IBM SD Consultant - 2 days/week
Second IBM SD Consultant - 3-4 days/week
IBM Specialists - 1 day/week i.e. 13 days as required
QA Reviewer - 6 days
It is anticipated that the core team identified above will undertake the main activities, supported, as required, by other participants as specific requirements and areas are evaluated in more detail.

Note: Resourcing requirements will be reviewed on a regular basis as the project proceeds.
Access may also be required to RETCO technical specialists in the following disciplines:

- Client/Server

It is estimated that up to 20 days of RETCO's specialists' time may be required during the project. Actual requirements to be defined and agreed with the TA owner prior to commencement of work.
Facilities:
A workroom will be required for the duration of the study, with access to terminals and printers for the production of the deliverables.
Constraints and 'Keys' to Success
The constraints to the production of acceptable TA Definition deliverables, within the stated timescales, were identified as follows:

  • Resource and interviewee availability within the agreed timescales
  • Quality and appropriateness of requirements information available - an initial assessment showed that much of the information needed as input to this project was either documented, or a source (to be interviewed) known.
Note: Unknowns should be documented here too. For example, the PC area was the only one identified where there is no identifiable, single source of information, including the existing inventory, which is two years old.
  • The availability of adequate facilities (working rooms, documentation facilities)
The following were seen as key success criteria for this project:
  • The TA must be able to be communicated to all users, simple to use and update (size and usability)
  • The project must produce deliverables, which are tangible, beneficial and relate to the business.
Deliverables
The following will be the main deliverables from the engagement, to be used as appropriate with senior customers, Systems Managers and members of the Computing Department.
  • TA Definition Report (60-100 pages)
  • Executive Summary (5-10 pages maximum)
  • Presentation materials
  • Online User's Guide (format, style and presentation still to be agreed)
In addition, the Outline Transition Plan section of the TA Definition Report will highlight TA general education requirements and deliverables.
Deliverables will be produced using the Microsoft Office Suite of programs. All source code will belong to RETCO.
Review of the TA Deliverables
It is recognized that a key factor to the subsequent successful implementation of the TA, will be its acceptance by RETCO business consultants and I/T professionals. A final list of reviewers has still to be agreed, but will include:
  • Business Consultants
  • Technical Consultants (including AD)
  • I/T Services.
Those selected should be representative of all potential interested parties. Some will focus more on organizational issues and concerns - in principle, most will be interviewed and receive a copy of the final draft as a matter of courtesy. The others, we believe, will wish to be more actively involved, and will be asked to review draft materials, at agreed intervals, for content, consistency and usability.
The RETCO formal review process will be used for this activity.

Key Business Directions

  • Multimedia will change the way the company does business in the future
  • The business will drive the use of technology - the challenge for the I/T Department is to recognize technology advantages in advance and recommend appropriate uses for the business
  • The business must be responsive and dynamic to customer needs.
  • Expansion will be by acquisition.

Business Systems - perceived drivers and obstacles
Summarized from discussions held prior to and during the planning workshops

  • The business needs to reduce the time taken to get a product from the source of supply, to being available for use. The main technical obstacle here is the current I/T 'end of day processing' (batch)
  • Improve customer service and customer perception of service - moving to a 24/7 operational requirement - everything has to be designed to be capable of 24 hour operation (architectural requirement)

Potential for PC environment and the Internet to be used more effectively to support the business. Whilst the network is not an inhibitor to business growth, the weak link is the differing platform networking facilities.
Key considerations:

- control of data (central business data, vs local data) and potential proliferation of copies
- access to data by users (data currently compatmentalised between systems) - requires a level of data sharing - batch updates and copies of data should be shared between systems
- have some small groups of users who do their own thing.

Current centralized I/T Strategy supports centralized Corporate ethos
