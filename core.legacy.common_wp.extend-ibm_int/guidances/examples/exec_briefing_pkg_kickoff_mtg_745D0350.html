Example: Executive Briefing Package - Kickoff Meeting
Listing of content included in an Executive Briefing Package for a kickoff meeting for e-business. Actual engagement examples can be found in the e-business Strategy & Design Knowledge Network Database.
Relationships
Related Elements
  • Executive Briefing Package
Main Description

Introduction to e-business – What does “e-business” mean (i.e., much more than “e-commerce”)?  How can you tell if a company is an e-business?

  • IBM’s definition of e-business
  • Types of e-business
  • Characteristics of companies above the e-line

Business Strategy in e-business – What makes e-business so much of a business strategy issue, rather than a technical issue?

  • Importance of establishing a clear business strategy
  • How to develop/redefine e-business direction
  • How technology can impact a company’s strategy (e.g. napster changing music industry)

Industry uses of e-business – How is e-business being used within the industry?

  • Industry trends in e-business

Qualifying e-business Initiatives in the Context of Business Strategy – How do you recognize a good e-business initiative from a bad one?  How do you ensure that an initiative will support value creation, value capture and sustainability?  Illustrate the importance of an e-business strategy by showing how a technology-driven approach can lead to business failures (e.g., Ingram music).

  • How to evaluate what is a good e-business strategy
  • Review case studies and examples relevant to the client’s business

Creating and managing an e-business Portfolio – What do companies need to think through in order to be successful with e-business?  How do they cope with the uncertainty inherent with e-business?

  • The different options on which to base an e-business portfolio
  • The major activities in creating a portfolio of initiatives
  • Management System and its importance

Considerations in Planning e-business Initiatives – What is involved with planning e-business initiatives, from developing an e-business strategy all the way through implementation (and operation) of an e-business initiative?  What examples of timelines can we show, based on our experiences with clients?

  • Planning Considerations
  • Define resource requirements and level of management participation 
  • Timing estimates based on our experience
