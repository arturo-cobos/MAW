Main Description

Following is a sample outline for a marketing communications plan:

Situation Analysis

This section should provide an overview of the group/division, as well as a situation analysis; i.e., product/brand strengths, market position, target audience information, competition, challenges, opportunities, etc.  This summary should dovetail with the group/division’s marketing plan (which should be reviewed before this plan is developed).

Objectives

This section should identify the primary objectives of the marketing communications plan; i.e., objective is to launch a new product.

Key Messages

This section should outline the top division/group/product messages. Try to keep to three or four key messages.  As mentioned previously, these should be brief, like a headline in a slide presentation, and supported by proof points that can vary by target audience.

Strategies

This section should address strategies to be employed to support the group/division’s marketing goals.  For the objective to launch a new product, strategies may include an internal communications and operations effort to ensure that systems are in place to effectively support the launch, a partner communications effort to ensure that they understand how they can help the company sell this new product, and an external communications effort to “get the word out” to end-users about the product and its benefits.

Tactics

This section should outline the specific recommended tactics, by target audience, to be undertaken in support of the division/group’s business objectives.  All plans should address how the various tactics will work together to support an integrated communications effort.  Marketing components that can be part of the program mix include:

  • Direct Sales, Telesales
  • Web Site
  • Direct Mail
  • Advertising
  • Promotions
  • Public Relations
  • Packaging
  • Point of Purchase Displays

In the case of our new product launch, we might consider sales training for company representatives, product briefings or Web-based tutorials for resellers, a public relations campaign to secure news articles on the new product as it hits the shelves, and a sweepstakes promotion to encourage product trial.

Roadmap and Resources

This section should provide a timeline for plan execution, as well as financial and other resources needed to complete the project. It should also indicate client, IBM or third-party responsibilities; e.g., does the client maintain a customer database that can be used to help measure program results; can the client provide IBM team with background information on the products; should IBM Learning Services handle employee/customer training; which sales promotion agency will manage the sweepstakes?
