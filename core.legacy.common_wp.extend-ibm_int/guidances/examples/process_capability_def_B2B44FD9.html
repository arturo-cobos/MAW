Main Description
Process Design Points
The Process design points should be documented in one of two ways: by using a decomposition diagram and/or by using a flow diagram. Typically the decomposition diagram is prepared first so that the key process design points can be identified at the appropriate process level.
The hierarchy design view shows the major elements of the process being examined and can be developed at the Process Group, Process, sub-process and activity levels. The purpose is to identify what needs to be examined in more detail when defining the process capabilities.

 

The process design flow template supports the definition of the process capability by indicating the sequence in which the processes, sub-processes and activities take place.


When the design points and process capabilities have been identified, the graphic below may be used to show the relationship between the strategic capability, the process design points and the process capabilities. This graphic will typically be prepared to work with the key stakeholders and the executives to secure their agreement and commitment to the process design points and required capabilities.


The key to reading the chart is:
  • Gray boxes represent the process design points that are needed to meet the strategic capability.
  • The green text represents the supported strategic capability.
  • The blue boxes represent the process capabilities
  • The red text represents the target metrics (You should specify metrics and targets for all process capabilities)

Process Capability Graphics – Process/Sub-Process View
When processes enabler definitions are being developed, the following three examples could be used as templates. The first shows the design points on process group level and the next two contains the definition of the design points and required process capabilities for the processes. The Second shows the details associated with each sub-process. These graphics are typically used by the project team, key subject matter experts and solution architects to ensure that key design points have been completely defined and documented and that the process capability requirements are also completely documented.

Example 1 represents the highest level of the business process group level. The group in this case is the Marketing & Sales of off the shelf products.



The second example shows the details of a major process within the Process Group. In this case, it shows the major sub-process within the major process. For each process within the process group there will be a similar set of charts.


The third example shows the major activities with a given sub-process.
