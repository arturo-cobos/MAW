Overview section:

Proof of Concept: Plan Production, Produce Product

Objective:
 To test the hypothesis that processes relying on cell manufacturing techniques, and the related changes to production planning, will produce superior inventory turns, lower inventory investment, and shorten lead times - while not increasing the cost of manufacturing.  The test will prove that the logistics of the planning process - timing of data, production of the plans, re-layout of the manufacturing floor - are worth the investment and are achievable in a production environment.

Scope:
 The test included a single product, the four-inch gate valve, and a single production facility.  This product has nine parts in its bill of materials, from both inside and outside the company.  The test will simulate the changing of the manufacturing techniques (from large batch to cell), and the associated changes in lot sizes and lead times.  The results will allow us to measure any improvements in inventory carrying costs and direct manufacturing costs.

The product being used has similar attributes to 60% of the products in production at the time of this writing. While each product (or group of products) will require some industrial engineering of their bill of material, routing, lead-time, etc., the results of the simulation can be used to do a preliminary assessment of the potential improvements for these products as well.

Our other products do not match the attributes of the product being simulated.  For these products, we would recommend either an additional simulation activity, or continuing the current manufacturing style.

Techniques:

The simulation was planned and executed using an automated tool, IBM ARIS Toolset. The following are some details from the test.  Detailed parameter settings and data used can be found in appendix A. One product was modeled:

  • Nine parts were set up, all with order point mechanisms
  • Order points were set to trigger when a function of safety stock and daily consumption rate (multiplied by lead time) was hit
  • Work-in-progress queues were set to 100 (cell manufacturing)
  • Finished product lot sizes were set to 1200 (reduced because of shorter lead times)

Results:

  • Inventory turns went from 2.3 to 21
  • Customer orders were always filled from stock (no back-orders)
  • Lot size reduced from 8000 to 1200

Execution Schedule:
See project master plan.

Summary:

Conclusions:

  • The cell manufacturing style can produce the benefits suggested by preliminary analysis,
  • The planning process (order point triggering manufacturing orders based on warehouse inventory levels) can be achieved within the allowable time window,
  • A plant floor re-layout, assumed by the test, will need to be in place before the full benefits of the approach can be accomplished.

Actions:

  • Plan a project to re-engineer the bills, routes and lead times of the products.
  • Plan a project to redesign the plant floor layout.
  • Synchronize these efforts with the new computer system implementation.
