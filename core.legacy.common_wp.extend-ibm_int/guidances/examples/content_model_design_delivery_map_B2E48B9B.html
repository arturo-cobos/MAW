For example, in a scenario where the user is looking for detailed information about a topic or product, what are the requirements, and how do they differ on a web site from traditional environments? In a traditional environment, the user might call and ask for the documentation, and then have to wait while it was found, ordered, and provided. When the information arrived, it would be a traditional publication, and might be somewhat out of date.
Compare that one example as a web scenario, and consider the necessary underlying framework. First, the user must be able to search for and find the content they are seeking. In order to meet that objective, the content must a) exist; b) be organized within the site and other content in a manner consistent with the user’s needs and expectations; c) be indexed in terms the user will use; and d) must be found by the supporting application (search).
Second, they must be able to access the information in a format that is usable for them, and which meets their needs. An “accidental” or “inherited” information architecture simply delivers the content of the paper document to the user’s screen. That, however meets neither the user characteristics, needs, nor expectations, and takes no advantage of the web as a medium with unique capabilities.
Studies have shown that web users do NOT sit and read large documents, especially if their objective is detailed information, on the screen. Source: Web Design and Delivery
