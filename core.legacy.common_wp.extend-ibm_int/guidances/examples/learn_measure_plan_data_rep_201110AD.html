Main Description
The following Measurement Menu is based on a scenario in which a business priority was identified to increase profits through an increase in repeat sales revenue and a reduction in quality-related costs. The Company has experienced reduced sales revenue because of customer dissatisfaction with the disruption in operations caused by on-site computer repairs. The problem was traced back of supplier defects caused by inadequate supplier selection.
The Company believed that the problem was due to poor training in supplier selection. The company was ready to hire a consultant to teach negotiation skills when an IBM consultant intervened to suggest the development of business priorities linked with specific skills and knowledge, and also identify other contributory causes. A recommendation was developed based on a desire to increase profits through increased repeat sales and reduced quality-related costs.
The analysis showed that the problem was not due to a lack of negotiation skills in general, but was due to a combination of factors. As a result, it was clear to the Company that a general negotiation skills course would not solve the problem. As a result of the effectiveness indicators, the focus of the intervention was redefined. The new focus was on accurate calculation of the trade-off between price and quality terms and a better understanding of the “total cost of quality.”
