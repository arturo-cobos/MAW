Summary Knowledge Management Enablers

SAMPLE SUMMARY ASSESSMENT OF KM ENABLERS:

Leadership
· KM is recognized as key to corporate strategy; no understanding of revenue potential of knowledge assets; learning is understood as necessary to stay competitive; little compensation or direct incentive for individual contribution to corporate knowledge assets.
· Corporate mgmt buy-in and allocation of resources; regional management cynicism.

Culture
· Limited sharing of knowledge assets across regions, almost none across practices; little trust among regional managers, competitive culture permeates organization in spite of corporate directives; flexibility and innovation within regions; little emphasis on non-product specific learning opportunities.
· Communities of practice forming along service delivery capabilities.

Technology
· Widespread use of Lotus Notes (e-mail, corporate Deliverables Database); planned implementation of R-5 and the web-enabling of support databases with Domino Server; pilot use of Team Room for project and community collaboration activities; knowledge portal options being explored.
· Comprehensive Knowledge Management and Process Management tools in place; web-enabled, stand-alone and client-server access to Methods and Tools repositories.

Measurement
· No linkage of Knowledge Management activities to financial results; no consistent measures of KM efforts; only anecdotal evidence of KM successes.
