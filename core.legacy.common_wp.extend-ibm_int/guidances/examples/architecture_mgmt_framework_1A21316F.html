Sample 1 - Architecture Management Framework

Introduction

The goal of an Architecture Management plan is to provide a framework for managing the design, deployment and evolution of an Enterprise Architecture (covering both business and IT architectures). Through the involvement of the many technology stakeholders, the process works to ensure conformance to the architecture while allowing for exceptions to meet unique requirements. It also serves to ensure that the architecture evolves to meet new business challenges with appropriate technology solutions.

Usage

Users and I/S professionals making design or product selection decisions should consult the architecture for guidance. If they are unable to find a solution in conformance with the architecture, they should invoke the Architecture Management Process to request a change or variance to the architecture to meet their unique requirement.

The application design and development process interfaces to the architecture management process through formal design reviews. This interface provides a structured approach to assisting design teams in conforming to the architecture and making design tradeoffs in line with enterprise-wide requirements.

Update

The plan should be continuously updated to reflect changes in the organization or communications infrastructure. As the architecture matures, the processes, including such activities as design reviews and publication in a newsletter, will become an integral part of the I/T infrastructure. By keeping the management processes up to date, the architecture will be able to maintain its vitality and maximise return to the enterprise.

Management Processes
The Architecture Management Framework consists of four sub-processes:

  • Architecture Review and Approval Process
  • Architecture Vitality Process
  • Architecture Exception and Appeals Process
  • Architecture Communications Process

To ensure the successful implementation of these processes, the Architecture Management Group (AMG) and the Business Steering Committee (BSC) will need to establish regular and ongoing informational and working meetings.
Each of the processes is detailed in the following pages. As the processes are implemented and begin to mature, they can be further detailed to reflect implementation specifics such as organization names and contact lists. If possible, these highly volatile data items should be kept to a minimum and isolated, to ease the document maintenance process.


Architecture Review and Approval Process
This process defines a structured approach to reviewing and approving decisions, which should be made in accordance with the architecture. Formal design and product evaluation reviews are the key control points for this process.


Critical Success Factors for a successful enterprise-wide Architecture implementation.
While the AMG has overall responsibility for the successful implementation of the Enterprise Architecture, the BSC and architecture stakeholders must work with them to achieve the following critical success factors:

  • Clear ownership of the Enterprise Architecture by the Architecture Management Group (AMG)
  • Active sponsorship and championship of the Architecture Management Group (AMG) by the Business steering Committee (BSC)
  • Proactive stakeholder and business involvement throughout the process
  • Clear definition of conformance / compliance standards
  • Clear decision making authority / boundaries for the Architecture Management Group (AMG) and the Business Steering Committee (BSC)
  • Ongoing education and communication of the Enterprise Architecture (EA) throughout the organization
