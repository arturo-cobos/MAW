Executive Summary

In support of client business vision to provide uniform security architecture across the enterprise, client has identified a requirement to implement a centralized user account management framework. This project specifically focuses on centralized user identity management and user account provisioning.

An analysis of the client business needs uncovered several important initiatives intending to leverage the power of the Internet, and make government more responsive, reliable and accessible anytime/anywhere. These initiatives include risk management, quality of service, IT best practices, and strategic advantage.

Addressing the identified client Strategic Business Initiatives and clientâ€™s complex person-user-technology environment require adding an Identity Management Framework (IMF) to the existing client security administration infrastructure.

The project of Centralized User Account Management implementation includes phases for planning, requirements analysis, design, implementation, pilot, and deployment.

Recommendation

As part of an overall security infrastructure program, this report recommends initiating development of a limited scope identity management framework using IBM Tivoli Identity Manager (ITIM). The recommendation limits the initial implementation by both departmental scope (ITD and e-Government) and resource administration scope (NT4 and Exchange 5.5). This limited scope development pilot will give client significant exposure to identity management functions and capabilities while providing value in the form of demonstrating the provisioning of baseline resources. The pilot will similarly allow maximum knowledge transfer to client staff with respect to ITIM functions, capabilities and design requirements.

The follow up implementation of client IMF includes adding remaining client departments and targets (Secure Gateway, UNIX, RACF and FYI).

Current XXX identity management environment

Client has approximately 30,000 employees distributed over 40 departments. Client has approximately 197 applications. Most of them are managed by Information Technology and e-Government departments. These applications are used by either client employees, or external users such as federal government, state government, vendors, contractors, business owners, and other.

The client Information Security Policies and Procedures Manual contain information critical for identity management implementation. The full report contains a detailed analysis of identity management related security policies.

The information on current client identity management environment has been collected from interviews with client IT and management personnel; interviews with IBM architects implementing county security infrastructure applications; reviews of client documents and technology recommendations, the workshop and meetings discussions.

GATHERED DATA Analysis

For this project, the analysis approach, the identification of requirements and the definition of a design plan all follow a structure determined by the predominant ITIM features and components. Namely, the analysis, requirements and design plan are broken down into the areas of authoritative identity source, organizational units, people, organizational roles, services, provisioning policies, workflow, identity policies, password policies, and audit and reporting, and administrative groups.

A gap analysis identified several functional gaps that will require adjustments to the ITIM implementation at client. The list of functional gaps includes the lack of a single authoritative identity source, no ITIM agent for IDMS, creation of missing attributes for compliance with client security policies, the need to revise procedures surrounding new hires and employee terminations, and no out of the box reports that comply with security policies. Additionally, there are a few operational gaps that need to be addressed for the project to proceed. These gaps include procurement of product licenses, set up of hardware and network resources, and the designation of client identity management staff.

Centralized user account management plan

The centralized user account management plan section provides critical management information needed to make decisions for going forward. The identified management issues include a discussion on staffing models, a high-level project plan outline, and several potential implementation roadmaps. These sections provide management with some of the information necessary to allow them to assemble and discuss a variety of implementation and management scenarios.
