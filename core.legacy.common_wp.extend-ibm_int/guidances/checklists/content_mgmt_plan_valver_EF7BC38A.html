Checklist: Content Management Plan Validation and Verification

Check Items
The scope of work includes clear completion and success criteria
Where possible, establish/gain concurrence on prioritization criteria as well, and verify these criteria with all participating content owners/units in advance.
Content selection validated with a user study/survey/focus group

Work with the team/content owner who defined the content requirements to develop a user study/survey/focus group, or some other mechanism to use for validattion.  This includes not only the content itself, but it’s usability and effectiveness for its stated purpose.

Findings reviewed with those in key roles within each process before final delivery
Findings include;results against completion, performance criteria as established ‘up front’, and key roles are: creators for major areas of content, web manager, site manager, etc.
“Field tests” conducted of the revised/transformed processes
Obtain participate evaluations and update the processes as appropriate.
On-going reviews conducted of the content and process requirements

 Identify new processes needed, breakage points within the processes, and changing process needs.


Specific measurements reviewed, and performance evaluated against specific targets
See Content Management Process Guide for examples of measurements, specific targets may include publication cycle time and the like.
Benchmark testing conducted for defined performance targets
Use this to establish baseline values, average values, peakload and/or stress values and, if possible, load failure points. Establish change triggers to monitor and initiate change (upgrade, etc) of infrastructure as needed to meet performance objectives and avoid failure thresholds.
