Capabilities are described at the proper level of abstraction

A high-level capability should be decomposed to two or more lower-level/specific capabilities if:

  • A capability provides different types of support to other capabilities/segments, e.g., segment one receives one type of support and segment two receives a different type of support
  • The output of the capability is needed by another capability, but the overall capability itself does not completely support the other capability.
  • The capability requires input from another function within the company, e.g., a sales capability should not include the capability to produce products, this should be split out and identified as a manufacturing capability
  • The engagement is at the capability costing or design stage where the detailed “how-to” of the capability must be pinned down

Two or more capabilities should be combined when they support all of the same value propositions and capabilities and have no major differences in delivery, e.g., same business function, etc.
