Main Description

From experience, the Engagement Plan is best developed during one or more planning workshops, after initial discussions with the client. The project team should refer to the appropriate technique papers for more detailed guidance on defining this work product. These give ample guidance and samples on what should be accomplished during these workshops.
The main steps to producing an Engagement Plan are as follows:

  • Agree planning approach with client: for example, the use of planning workshops - proper preparation for these workshops is key to their success:
    • Finalize workshop agendas and attendees - The list of attendees will likely vary depending on the business and technical scope and level of detail of the study. A maximum of eight attendees is the most productive number.
    • Customize client briefing materials and distribute to attendees, for pre-workshop reading
    • Prepare presentation and workshop material. Customize any method / approach presentation materials, to meet specific client needs.
    • Confirm facilities and arrangements
  • Conduct workshops - The workshop(s) are crucial to setting the stage for a successful project. They define the project scope and set expectations of results. It is important that at least two IBM team members run these meetings so that one can focus on facilitating while the other takes notes and thinks ahead to the next agenda item:
    • Run planning workshops
  • Review existing client documentation: the objective here is to understand the usefulness of the documentation available for the proposed engagement, its completeness and acceptance within the client organization. Based on this assessment, the engagement manager will be able to more effectively plan and allow time for interviews, document summaries and information analysis.
  • Run IBM consultant team planning workshop. The objective here is to:
    • Agree the scope and level of detail of the work products that will be produced.
    • Draft an information-gathering plan (based on current understanding of existing client documentation and work products to be produced).
    • Perform an internal QA / Peer review.
  • Prepare Planning Workshop deliverables
    • Prepare Draft Engagement Plan and review with the Sponsor and Architecture ‘Owner’.
    • Prepare a draft Engagement Plan that reflects the discussions, agreements and results of the workshop (s). Make the plan as clear and concise as you can - you will continually need to reset expectations about the scope and level of detail, as the project progresses and this report will be your base point.
    • Finalize Engagement Plan and attach to the SOW and Contract.
  • Distribute Engagement Plan to core project team and setup project.
