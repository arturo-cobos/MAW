Main Description

Developing the Industry Environment Analysis is an iterative approach around the environmental themes, using a combination of interviews, workshops, secondary sources, and individual analysis.

The following analysis steps may be performed (for details, refer to the technique paper):

Scope & Participation:

  • Confirm and/or document ‘as-is’ and future scope and participation.

Value analysis:

  • Map the client’s business environment or value system (e.g. value system).
  • Analyze the creation and capture of value (e.g. value nets, value chain).
  • Generate ideas for improving the value system (by using outputs of previous analyses). 

Industry forces analysis:

  • Analyze industry forces (e.g. ‘5-forces’) and external influences and trends (e.g. ‘S.T.E.E.P.’) around the area of focus.

Attractiveness assessment:

  • Choose the industry or market/segment/arena of focus (e.g. scenarios).
  • Assess attractiveness of market/segment/arena (by using outputs of all analysis).
  • Decide client’s role & competitive response - whether to ‘play the game’ or ‘change the rules’ (change the industry structure).

Although the numbering scheme suggests a certain order, it is important to understand that the Industry Environment analysis is an iterative process and depending on the issues to be addressed and information available the starting point may differ.
