Main Description

The future tasks and worksteps of the process and characteristics of the package are the bases for the analysis.  They can be developed either before or at the same time as a detailed gap analysis. 

If the future tasks and workstep designs are created before the package gap/fit analysis is performed, a review session is held.  The process owners explain the process designs, and the application experts ask clarifying questions and point out potential gaps.  Both process owners and application experts develop strategies for resolving the gaps.  This is best done in small groups, with a dedicated scribe to produce the work products.

If the future process designs are created concurrently with this work product, the package may be used for inspiration.  The current business process is reviewed and desired improvements are noted.  The equivalent package processes are reviewed, and the future process design is negotiated by taking the best of the current process, the best of the package, and the most important of the desired improvements.

Short (1 - 2 day) workshops are an effective way of producing this work product.  Review and commentary by participants are required to ensure completeness.

Experts in the package and process should estimate the implementation effort (time and resources) for each option in their respective areas of expertise.

Document the analysis including the security implications.
