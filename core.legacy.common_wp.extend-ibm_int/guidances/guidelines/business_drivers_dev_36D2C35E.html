Guideline: Developing Business Drivers

Main Description

Prior to a workshop, the team will:

  • "Draft" a set of drivers using external and internal research, previously summarized documents (e.g. existing business strategy, existing driver statements, initiative descriptions, project lists etc.) and interview notes

Working in a workshop mode, the core team will:

  • Test the "draft" drivers during various executive workshops ("Killer-e", "Environmental Analysis", Strategic Scenario" and "Competitive Positioning" etc.). As opportunities and threats are identified, get consensus as to the degree of opportunity and threat. (Value exchange analysis is a good technique to understand the magnitude or strength of a driver).
  • Produce a matrix linking each Key Driver to one or more business strategic goals, business CSFs or distinctive business capabilities (if these exist).
  • Produce a matrix showing the Impact of the Key Drivers on both the current IT environment and the business.
  • Prioritize Drivers
  • Document assumptions
  • Review the documented Drivers and Assumptions and obtain agreement in discussions with senior client management.

After External drivers are complete, focus should move to Internal drivers during capability modeling workshops. Again, consensus, prioritization and reporting processes should be performed.
