Main Description

The following development approach is proven:

1. Select and validate with user management the asset categories present at the organization.

2. Work with the assets defined in a Business Impact Analysis model as a starting point (if available).

3. If not, survey key user management to identify assets within the selected categories to:

-identify critical assets.

-understand what the impact on the business would be if these critical assets are compromised.

-understand current risks.

-obtain relevant costs (e.g. replacement cost, cost per hour of loss, etc.).

4. Produce a comprehensive review of the organizationâ€™s assets, their relevance to their key business issues, security threats and requirements, ownership and use. Where asset lost can not be expressed in tangible terms, estimate their intangible loss (e.g. loss of customer confidence, stockholder confidence, etc.) over time on a subjective scale.

5. Document an inventory of assets including their estimated loss.
