Guideline: Developing Hypothesis and Data Framework
Migrated development approach from existing IBM Global Services Method work product.

  • Hypothesis and Data Framework
Main Description
  1. Use a starter set of hypotheses appropriate for the engagement.  For example, if the objective of the engagement is to determine the client’s readiness for Customer Loyalty, the hypotheses should reflect those processes, data accesses, data availability, and organizational maturity required to enable the identification, selection and specialized treatment of client customers.  If the objective of the engagement is to assess a client’s vendor relationships and procurement processes such as commodity, supplier and purchase management, the hypotheses are statements which reflect an ideal ability to measure supplier performance, identify alternative sources of commodities, and determine when, by how much, where, from whom to replenish inventory.
  2. As needed, tailor the starter set of hypotheses for the particular client’s situation.  Some additional hypotheses may need to be developed; some existing hypothesis may be eliminated.  In the example for a set of Customer Loyalty hypotheses, the starter set of hypotheses are that the client already has the right things regarding knowledge of customers and business capabilities and infrastructure to be ideal to their customers.
  3. Establish, with the client engagement manager, a list of the appropriate client individuals who can participate in a facilitated session to establish the hypotheses and determine the source of data to validate or refute the hypotheses.  Ideally, these individuals should also be able to identify the sources of data, which could quantitatively support or refute each hypothesis.
  4. Conduct a standard facilitated session or sessions to obtain the information.  Resolve any conflicting positions and document the results.
