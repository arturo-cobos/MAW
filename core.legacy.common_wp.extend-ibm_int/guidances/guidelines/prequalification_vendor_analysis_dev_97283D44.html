Main Description

The pre-qualification analysis portion of the work product is developed by the IBM Selection Team members upon receipt of the vendor responses to the Request for Information.

Prepare the pre-qualification analysis according to the following steps:

  1. Collect the vendor responses to the RFI.
  2. Clarify responses, if needed, and validate information from the vendor where appropriate.
  3. Research any missing information from the vendor.
  4. Populate the template and adjust to meet the specific needs of the client.
  5. Evaluate information with the work product to determine elimination of vendors based on client criteria, document reasons for elimination.
  6. Generate the “interim list” of vendors for review with the client.

The development of the presentation portion of the work product to the client is primarily the responsibility of the IBM consultant.  The client should be used as input into what vendors they have considered, what vendors they have already seen, and what vendors their competitors use.  It is important that any vendor that the client is still considering be included in the long list of vendors.

The main points to include are:

  • Overview of the Process and Sources

This section should step the client through the selection process and the sources that are used to perform the justification.  Examples could be:

  • Third-Party Research
  • IBM Industry Research and Subject Matter Experts (SME)
  • Vendor Research
  • IBM Intellectual Capital Repository – Best Practices
  • Vendor Screening Criteria

This section outlines criteria that are being used to perform a high-level screening of the possible vendors.  The following are only examples and will vary depending on the client environment:

  • Major Contender in Enterprise Applications
  • Stable vendor in business for at least 5 years
  • Solution that utilizes sound technologies to support client “self-service” (e.g. Internet, Voice Response, Dial-up)
  • Solution that incorporates electronic commerce for operations (e.g. Internet, EDI, EFT, etc.) 
  • Solution that promotes integrated workflow between functionality
  • Flexible, re-configurable system that is easily changed as business requirements change
  • Integrated solution for Functional Areas
  • Integration into security controls and support desired risk posture
  • Open systems architecture, object oriented technology if possible
  • Vendor has history of stable software (vulnerabilities and patches)
  • Client / Server technology that runs on specified platform
