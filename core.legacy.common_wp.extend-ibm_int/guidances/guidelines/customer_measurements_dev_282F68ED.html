Main Description

This work product is initially created from information provided to the project team. 

In the case of organizations that have a measurement program already in place, this may be obtained by interviewing the appropriate personnel at the account and obtaining any supporting documentation from them. 

In the case of prospective clients, this may be obtained through the request for proposal (RFP) and from other documents obtained during the proposal phase.  It is updated with information obtained in the due diligence phase.

One approach is by developing a measurements survey questionnaire (or a measurement maturity questionnaire) and using it as an assessment vehicle to establish the state of measurement collection, analysis, and use.

Such a measurements survey questionnaire could be tailored from a standard questionnaire (based on tools such as the Global AMS Delivery measurements catalog), depending on the goals of the organization.  The consultant (or other appropriate role) on the team would initially fill out the questionnaire using whatever information is gathered through the proposal phase.  The information would be updated during the due diligence phase prior to conducting the measurement gap analysis.  The questionnaire itself or subsets of it could be sent out to the appropriate groups within the organization to collect the needed information.

Other means of collecting the information may also be used as appropriate.
