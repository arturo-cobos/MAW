Guideline for GTS/IGA Conditions of Satisfaction (COS) Management

The Conditions of Satisfaction (COS) work product is developed through a series of simple, enhanced, structured discussions with the client to set and manage their expectations. The output from these discussions are recorded in the standalone COSCard and stored with the project/engagement documentation.

Scope of COS

Conditions of Satisfaction (COS) help define and manage client expectations by keeping us aligned with their priorities and expectations throughout the delivery of an individual engagement. The client’s conditions of satisfaction are captured and recorded to ensure that both the client and the IBMer understand what needs to be done to achieve the highest level of satisfaction.

COS statements frequently address subjective matters that reflect those things the client views as critical to the success of the project. However, COS statements should neither repeat what is already in the contract or Statement of Work, nor should they change the scope of the contract or Statement of Work. Help the client understand this distinction if they initially propose new or different contractual commitments.

Subject areas to consider when helping your client define their expectations include such things as create value, communications, timeliness, skills transfer, leadership, commitment, flexibility, and responsiveness. Here are some examples of COS statements:

  • Focus on solving the business problem rather than on implementing cutting edge technology for its own sake.
  • Deliver a financial system per the project schedule that meets all the defined and agreed upon requirements.
  • Respond to all inquiries/issues within agreed upon timeframes.
  • Clearly communicate via e-mail any delays or priority changes that will impact the timely completion of project.
  • Demonstrate the ability to adapt to changes and the flexibility to meet the client’s organizational needs.
  • Keep IBM project team members aware of project goals, processes, and targets.

Value of Using COS

Why should I develop and use the Conditions of Satisfaction?

  • Enables your client to state and solidify their priorities and make their satisfaction more objective
  • Enables you to understand what actions you can take to achieve a satisfied client at the end of the engagement
  • Identifies when client requirements and expectations have changed during an engagement
  • Helps you negotiate changes required to meet expectations
  • Ensures that the client’s vision is realized
  • Strengthens your relationship with the client

Why should I complete the COSCard?

  • Maintains a record, for both you and the client, of what the client said was important at the time of the last COS meeting
  • Maintains a record that shows we are aware of their expectations and priorities
  • Maintains a record of what actions we committed to take
  • Standard format that the client can recognize
  • Standard recognizable format to communicate COS to team members

What COS is NOT?

  • COS is not a survey, not a replacement for the Client Value Analysis-Transaction (CVA-T) survey done at the end of the project by a third party survey firm
  • COS is not a form to be filled in by the client, but is to be completed by the Project Manager after discussion with the client
  • COS is not a contract or an amendment to the contract/SOW
  • COS is not a vehicle to allow the client to demand and/or document changes or additions to contractual requirements or performance
  • COS is not something to be kept a secret from your project team

Instead, share the client's expectations and COS review results with the project team

Be selective about which Conditions of Satisfaction and COS results you share with subcontractors

Basic COS Steps

Timing

Step Description

Prior to Start-up

The Project Manager and/or the delivery lead should discuss with the Opportunity Owner the expectations the client expressed during the solution design phase.

Establish a Conditions of Satisfaction Plan during Project Launch for the solution delivery phase of the project/engagement with dates for conducting the Start-up, Interim and Closeout Reviews with the client. Establish:

A. Who will meet with the client to setup the initial COS?

B. If there will be Interim Reviews, who will do them?

10- 30 Days into Start-up

SET CLIENT EXPECTATIONS (COS)

Meet with the client and ask them what is important to them, relative to their overall satisfaction with the solution delivered. Review with them any delivery expectations that were identified by the Opportunity Owner. Discuss with the client, their priorities and measures of success. Help the client articulate how they will assess IBM on meeting, exceeding, or missing their expectations. Discuss and set Interim Review dates, if appropriate, based on the project size.

Use the COSCard to record the client’s conditions of satisfaction. Planned Interim Review and Closeout Review dates should be entered into the project plan by the PM.

Review the COS with the IBM Team, develop and assign action plans to address them. Review the plan with the client to ensure that these plans will satisfy their requirements.

Execute Project Phase

INTERIM REVIEW

Interim Reviews should be conducted quarterly with the client by the PM. These reviews are informal meetings with the client to review/monitor IBM’s performance in meeting the conditions set (or later revised) and to identify gaps between the client’s expectations and what you are delivering. It is not unusual that client priorities may change, resulting in new, reprioritized conditions of satisfaction. Discuss them and adjust the COS list accordingly.

If your client’s expectations are not being met, develop an action plan that will address the necessary improvements. Communicate these action plans to the IBM Team. This gives the IBM Team the opportunity to resolve or close them before they become serious client sat issues.

Close Project Phase

CLOSEOUT REVIEW

At the end of the project, meet with the client to determine if we have met or exceeded their expectations, also covering performance, outstanding issues. Use this opportunity to discuss with the client future business for IBM.

Remind the client that IBM surveys their clients at the end of a project because we value their feedback. Inform them they may be contacted to participate in a survey.
