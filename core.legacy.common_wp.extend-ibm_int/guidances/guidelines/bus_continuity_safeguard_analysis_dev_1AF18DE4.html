Main Description

After an in depth review of the client’s assets, vulnerabilities, and the propensity of a threat occurrence a series of safeguards is identified to address each threat with a high percentage of occurrences. In each case, recommended additional safeguards must be cost-effective, unless law, regulation, or contractual agreement specifically required them.  The cost of implementing and operating the safeguard should be less than the reduction in the Annual Loss Expectancy (ALE) associated with the threats against which a safeguard was effective unless specifically required by law. 

Costs and savings are amortized over the lesser of the estimated safeguard, system, or facility life cycles. Money to be spent or saved in future years is discounted to reflect its value at the present time by using discount factors based on the inflation adjusted, cost-of-capital rate of 10%. Multiple effects -- that is, the reduction of more than one ALE, from more than one threat, by a single additional safeguard – is evaluated by analyzing the difference in ALE of all affected threats.

After applying these analytical techniques to the costs and savings associated with each proposed additional safeguard and the ALE's which it affects, a savings figure, normalized to the present time, is obtained, to assist management in deciding whether or not to implement the recommended additional safeguard.
