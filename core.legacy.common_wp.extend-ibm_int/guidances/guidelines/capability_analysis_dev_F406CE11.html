Main Description

The development approach for this work product varies by the instrument(s) used to obtain the data. However, in general, if the format of the information collected is primarily numeric, graphs, tables and charts provide the clearest form of presentation. 

If the information is primarily textual, the following steps may be taken:

  • Develop a set of logical categorizations of information based on the data collection objectives.
  • Consider such questions as the following:
    • What are the areas that the client has consistently identified as weaknesses?
    • What are the areas that the client has consistently identified as strengths?
    • What are the areas where there are significant variances among client respondents?
    • Are there significant variances between executive management responses and line management responses?  If so, what are they?
    • What is the desired future state of the client?  (e.g., How good do they want to be?)
    • Is the clientâ€™s vision of the desired future state good enough to meet their Vision, Mission, Objectives and Strategy (VMOS) and remain competitive?  Is the client under-achieving or over-achieving?

  • Categorize textual information and document how information supporting more than one category is treated.
  • Develop the presentation appropriate to the audience to whom it is directed.
  • Another representation of the information collected could also reflect a distribution of data about the respondents (department, number of years in the company, number of years in the industry, whether or not they have managerial responsibilities, etc.).
