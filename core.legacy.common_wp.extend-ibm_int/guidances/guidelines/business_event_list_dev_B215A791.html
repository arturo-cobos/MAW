Main Description

The starting point for the Business Event List is the set of business context models or process maps.   Identifying all the external involved parties is very important.  The interactions shown on these diagrams will provide a basic set of channels where business events may occur.  It is worth examining the classified business terms for any terms that the business actually uses to describe specific events.  It is rare that a business has an exhaustive list of such terms, however, business thinking is usually focused on what happens inside the business.  Process-oriented terms from the classified business terms give some indication of events that occur in the business.  Based on the context interactions and process terms from the classified business terms, a good list of events can be generated and can then be extended by interviewing or observing users and process experts.  To generate the list, ask users and experts "what kinds of things happen?" in this area of the business.  This list can also be captured in a "brainstorming" session.  This would be done in a workshop setting.

Another means of generating this is to observe workers in action and generate the list through observation.
