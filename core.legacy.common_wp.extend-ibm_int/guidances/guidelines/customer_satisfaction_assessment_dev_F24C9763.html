Guideline: Developing Customer Satisfaction Assessment

Main Description

To successfully develop a Customer Satisfaction Assessment, it is important to follow the following generic steps:

  • Understand the purpose for collecting customer feedback
  • Determine the customer segment to be surveyed or interviewed
  • Cultivate relationships with the customers
  • Listen to the customer's requirements
  • Focus on the customer's needs
  • Understand the customer's perceptions of that which you seek to measure their satisfaction with
  • Determine the approach for gathering feedback
  • Develop survey(s) / interview plan(s) etc.
  • Administer survey(s) / interview(s) etc.
  • Analyze all (quantitative and comment type) feedback and summarize the results.  If the client sponsor has given permission, send thank you and results to those who participated.

Typically, a customer satisfaction survey serves as the only feedback vehicle, but other types of feedback are essential as well.  Formal surveys are a valuable source of feedback when carefully designed and conducted.  Poorly conceived surveys, however, can themselves be the cause of customer dissatisfaction when not tailored to a particular situation or when conducted too often. 

A well-constructed survey collects quality information, in the right format, at the right time, from the right people, and for the right reasons.  The following considerations should guide survey design:

  • Define the objective of the survey and the specific topics for the survey. What information is required? How will the results be used?  What actions will be taken?
  • Keep the survey as short as possible.  Ask just enough questions to get the information needed to make decisions and take actions.
  • Select the survey style and form based on the type of information desired.  Closed-ended questions, such as, 'Overall, how satisfied are you with the information technology services you receive?' restrict responses to specific categories.  Open-ended questions allow respondents to speak more freely and may provide information that is not anticipated.
  • Determine the flow of the questions.  Responses to one question may influence the responses to questions that follow questions.  Questions should be clear, understandable, and unbiased.
  • Select the method of survey / interview phone, mail, on-line.  The choice will depend on the audience, the subject, the use of the results, the cost and  the time frame.  

A Customer Satisfaction Assessment should utilize feedback that comes from any contact with the customer.  Meetings, forums, and conference calls produce concerns which drive issues and  "To Do's."  All complaints and many help desk calls are the result of a concern or problem and require immediate action. All of these sources of customer feedback need to be aggregated and analyzed for customer satisfaction implications.  The data should be categorized and combined with survey data into a logical framework, thus providing a powerful source of information that can guide the executive management decisions. 

Tapping into all sources will provide a range of information, from subjective to objective, providing both measurement and diagnostic capability.
