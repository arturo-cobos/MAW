Main Description

The Harmonization Model is a vision of how corporate management wants the organization to operate.  It includes four main areas, processes, systems, structure, and job roles.

Development of the future business and IT architecture  (Harmonization Model).

  • The “harmonized” business process model (the Harmonization Model) becomes the future business architecture.
  • The high-level business process model developed as part of the business scope is matched with the harmonization principles.  This matching will define the structural set up of the business architecture.
  • The logic of the future business architecture is validated in team based work sessions with the client team.  The business scope is tested and verified in terms of all dimensions (customers/market segments, product & services, geographies, processes, capabilities and core competencies) and supporting dimensions such as management support, financial services, human resource management and information technology management.
  • The IT architecture designed when applying the harmonization principles and after defining the IT infrastructure becomes the system. It will be a description of the final system, i.e., the main system, any additional legacy systems, the interfaces between them, the database structure.
  • The definition of the business goals and the supporting IT architecture should as a consequence determine the structure. It is however still a decision that must be taken by the client company.
  • The system will more than likely change the structure and processes of the organization. Consequently the structure, roles and responsibilities of the jobs will change.  The final job roles should be checked against the processes for compatibility.  A training need analysis will then need to be completed for input into the training plan.
    • The changes generate will not be uniform across all sites
    • From the Model will flow the system installed at each site
    • These four areas must support each other
