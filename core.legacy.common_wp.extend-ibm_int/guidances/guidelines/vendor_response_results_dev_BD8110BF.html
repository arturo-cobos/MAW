Main Description

The Vendor Response Results is developed by the IBM Selection Team members upon receipt of the vendor responses to the Requirements Matrix.

Prepare the Requirements Matrix according to the following steps:

  1. Collect the vendor responses to the Requirements Matrix.
  2. Clarify responses, if needed, and validate all requirements received a score from the vendor.
  3. Obtain any missing scores from the vendor.
  4. Determine the decision model from the priority or directives determined during the business process definition task.
  5. Create the Excel spreadsheet to calculate the scoring method determined in the decision model.
  6. Populate the decision model.
  7. Create the summary Excel spreadsheet to reflect the scoring results for all vendors and convert the results into a percentage-based format.

The development of the presentation portion of this work product is primarily the responsibility of the IBM consultant.  The IBM team brings the research and experience to analyze the vendor’s responses and develop the justification behind vendor elimination. 

The main points to include are:

  • List of Interim Vendors
  • Overview of the RFI/RFP Results

This section should step the client through the RFP results. The client should understand the relative strengths of each vendor in the different application areas. The scores should be weighted according to the client’s environment.

  • Preliminary Cost Models

This section provides a high level preliminary cost model for each vendor in the following areas:

  • Software Costs and Licenses
  • Hardware Costs
  • Training and Education Costs
  • Implementation Costs
  • Vendor Analysis

Highlight the Pros and Cons of the different vendors.  Items may include:

  • Functionality
  • Market Strength
  • Resource Availability
  • Implementation Cost and Time
  • Flexibility
  • Customer Service
  • Tools
  • Vendor Disqualification
  • Shortlist of Vendors
