Main Description

This technique paper focuses on how to develop a Network Conceptual Design work product instance. A Network Conceptual Design is the first step in crafting a network solution to support business requirements. It establishes a common understanding between the stakeholders and the people architecting and designing the network by describing the network in a way that indicates its support of client business requirements. It is the first design presented to a client and is intended to provide an executive level understanding of how the network design addresses stated business goals and objectives.

The Network Conceptual Design describes the relationship between the building blocks or functions (i.e., connections for point of sale terminals) that comprise the network and services required to meet business needs. At this level, technology is not specified but support of the business purpose is shown. Business requirements are mapped to the network through guiding principles, conceptual network design diagrams and profiles. The Network Conceptual Design uses material from the two activities in the Client Environment phase, Understanding the Business Environment and Objectives, and Understanding the Current IT Environment and Plans. Its development is the foundation for starting the Network Specified Design.

There are two rules of thumb to address to determine if the Network Conceptual Design is at the appropriate level:

  • If a non-network professional or an executive cannot understand the presentation materials the diagrams are probably too detailed.
  • If the typical network specialist finds little value in the diagrams they are probably about right. If the specialist finds a lot of value they are probably too detailed.
