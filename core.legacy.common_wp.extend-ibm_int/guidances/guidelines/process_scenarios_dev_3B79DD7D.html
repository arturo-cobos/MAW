Main Description

The design team should have a clear understanding of the scope of the engagement, the business strategy and desired capabilities.

Review the Future Process Flow Definition

The starting point for developing Process Scenarios is the definition of the future processes, which documents the activities/actions to be performed.  The team should also review the design objectives (desired capabilities) and attributes for the future processes.  The project team should also have a common high-level understanding of the major process changes to be implemented.

Prepare for the Scenario Workshop

  • Review the key findings from the customers strategy development, when available, otherwise refer to the project goals and objectives
  • Conduct client education about strategy, scenarios and the fit between the two
  • Investigate stakeholder objectives by reviewing Stakeholder Analysis (refer to WPD, if there is one) or conducting interviews with key stakeholders. Observation of potential users is invaluable.  Focus groups and interviews provide excellent input into the scenarios, as can online surveys.
  • Gather additional data and review Process Analysis results
  • Identify and define the focal issue for scenario development based on Process Analysis and the initial Process Definition
  • Develop the scenario workshop briefing package to prepare customer
  • Schedule workshop participants, facilities and other logistics

Define Key Process Scenarios

Each business process team must define the main business situations that the future business processes should address.  If necessary, additional information may need to be sought from members of the reference group, key business users, line managers, or operatives, depending on the level of detail required.  Innovative thinking and brainstorming techniques can also be used.  This should be done in advance of the workshop to provide a “straw model” for discussion and refinement.

The Future Information should highlight the differences from the Current state.  This may include:

  • redundant processes,
  • additional processes,
  • revised processes, and/or
  • potential organizational changes
  • change in roles
  • major variations or exceptions to the customer scenario should be identified
    Eliminated actions should be noted as "deleted".

Conduct workshop(s) to:

  • Develop scenarios framework (outlines, story ideas, potential impact areas, trigger events)
  • Develop scenario stories (future state, logical sequence of events, etc.)
  • Analyze impacts to the business based upon engagement issues
  • Assess how processes enable desired capabilities and existing gaps
  • Identify key issues or decision points based upon scenarios
  • Discuss application of Business Process Enablers within the scenario framework
  • Identify gaps in chosen package or technology
  • Prioritize scenarios and identify main exceptions to the scenarios for consideration
  • Define the expected outcome

Validate Documented Customer Scenarios

  • Test the scenario based on the expected outcome

Customer Scenarios are used to validate the process design and provide a basis for building User Scenarios to map against a package or technology as well as for 'procedures guides' or test scripts, which can be published or automated as on-line performance support systems.
