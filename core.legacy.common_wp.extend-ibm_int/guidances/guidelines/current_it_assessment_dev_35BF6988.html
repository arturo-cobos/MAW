Main Description

The Current IT Environment and Current IT Assessment work products, will normally be conducted with some degree of parallelism and you need to make sure these efforts and the collection of input data is co-ordinated effectively.

A suggested approach would adopt the following outline.

Work in workshop mode using a core team of business users, IT management (including application development project managers) and senior IT professionals - people who understand the business requirements and priorities, the current IS/IT environment and the existing project portfolio.

Organize and brief the team of assessors - these will be people who have familiarity with the current business and IT environments and a sound background in the issues that are likely to be raised during the assessments. They will also have credibility within the organization to perform this work.

Agree which elements of the current environment are to be assessed - Using the outputs from previous engagement activities/work products, plus any other relevant information, agree the areas and systems to be assessed.

Define the assessment criteria and process - agree assessment criteria, scoring system and documentation standards. In situations where criteria are established via regulatory requirements or industry standards, agreement of interpretation of these external standards and criteria may be necessary.

Conduct the assessments - each chosen area will be assessed and, where appropriate, an assessment matrix completed. Any issues will be documented within the matrix, with comments to clarify.

Moderate the results - when all assessments are complete, the results are compared, and optionally plotted on one or more comparative graphs. The findings should be summarized in a short paper for senior management.

Document gaps and additional support requirements - highlight any major observations, areas of concern, issues and recommendations

Communicate the results and conclusions from the assessment - all assessment findings will be documented in a form suitable for presentation to senior management and other interested parties
