Main Description

The development of any market research plan can be completed once the consultant fully understands the clientâ€™s objectives and constraints. The research objectives largely will drive the appropriate approach, methodology, timing and selection of respondents.

The general approach for any primary market research will follow the steps outlined below:

1. Define the Research Objectives

The issue-based hypotheses developed early in an engagement will help determine the need for market research and also be key inputs to defining the research objectives. In planning and conducting the research, the consultant should continually revisit to the research objectives to ensure that the project remains focused. The practitioner may wish to draft the preliminary research objectives and then consult a market research SME to help finalize the specific objectives.

2. Develop the Analytic Plan

Once research objectives have been articulated and agreed to, the analytic plan describes the methodology i.e., how the objectives will be met and what research techniques will be utilized. Given the inherent benefits and drawbacks of various research techniques, the SME can provide insight to finalize the analytic plan.

3. Collecting the Data

3-1. Create the Participant Screener

This short questionnaire is designed to determine if a respondent meets the target criteria.

3-2. Recruit Participants

When a large study or focus groups are used for data collection, recruiting will be conducted by the research vendor. However, when a smaller number of interviews are being done, a practitioner may be responsible for recruiting and scheduling participants.

3-3. Create the Research Instrument

For a qualitative research study, the instrument is a discussion guide. For a quantitative research study, the instrument is a questionnaire.

3-4. Administer the Instrument

For a qualitative research study, conduct the interviews or focus groups. Most focus groups facilities can provide an audio and videotape of the discussion. It may be helpful to record one-on-one interviews. For a quantitative research study, the instrument is a questionnaire. Normal output is a  data file.

4. Analyze the Data

Research data normally need to be processed prior to creating data outputs to ensure they are consistent, usable and logical. Prior to analyzing the data set, it should be cleansed. Cleansing involves identifying inconsistent or incomplete responses.

In analyzing the data, practitioners will order and reorder the data to look for meaning or the key points. As the key points and meanings are gathered, the data can be interpreted. Interpreting is drawing conclusions from the analysis and findings. Ensure these address any hypotheses set forth at the beginning of the study.

5. Prepare Findings, Conclusions and Implications

Once the data have been analyzed, the practitioner will synthesize the findings to determine conclusions. From the conclusions, it is often beneficial to articulate any implications.
