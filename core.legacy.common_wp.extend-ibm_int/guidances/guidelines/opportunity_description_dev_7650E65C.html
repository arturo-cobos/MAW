Main Description

Opportunity Descriptions are normally developed during an Opportunity Identification Workshop.

For each opportunity, complete each field of the template.  The prompting questions for each field help ensure a thorough answer.

Opportunity Description:

  • If implemented, how would this work?  What would it look like?  What would the user be able to do?
  • Are there links to other sites?
  • What does this opportunity allow to happen?
  • What does this opportunity enable? 
  • Does this opportunity enable a chain of events?
  • What are the underlying capabilities that enable this opportunity?

Assumptions:

  • For this opportunity to be implemented, are there any technologies that must be available?  Any partnerships/alliances formed or broken?  Any changes in customer or market trends, customer wants/needs?  Other?

Which value net players receive value and what is the value received:

  • Who are the users?  A specific customer segment or player?  An internal group? 
  • Why will the users find the opportunity valuable or useful?

Which value net players contribute to value creation and what do they contribute:

  • Does the opportunity require the involvement of other value net players?
  • What is the player’s contribution to value creation?
  • What does the player receive in return for their contribution?

NOTE: For internal opportunities, the term “value net player” may be replaced by internal departments or other organizations as appropriate.

What is the value captured by the company:

  • Why should the company provide this opportunity?
  • How will it benefit the company?
  • Consider qualitative benefits as well as quantitative

Three additional fields – Opportunity Name, Processes Affected, and Implications / Issues / Dependencies / Other – are self-explanatory.

Additional fields may also be useful, depending upon the engagement. 

  • Opportunity owner
  • Identification code
  • Capabilities (a section separate from the description, including high level, obvious capabilities)
  • Scenario Name (for engagements including Scenario Planning or other methods producing multiple future environments)

After the workshop, it will be necessary for the core team to refine the opportunity descriptions.  This should be done as quickly as possible after the workshop and may include the combination of like opportunities into a single opportunity.  Make sure that each description can stand on it’s own and is easily understandable by someone who has not participated in the engagement.
