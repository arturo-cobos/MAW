Guideline: Developing Content Management Plan
Migrated development approach from existing IBM Global Services Method work product.
Relationships
Related Elements
  • Content Management Plan
Main Description

1. For the necessary content, define the audience content requirements, and the content gaps: where must content be created or added; where must existing content be migrated; and where must content be externally acquired.

2. Determine the current processes and staffing levels that are being used to

- Identify and select
- Assign a Content Owner to create and/or acquire
- Publish
- Measure, and
- Maintain content for a site.

Ensure that all current manual and automated processes are identified for each element of content and content sources being used. Identify the control point (or measurement ‘clickpoints’ or ‘clickstreams.’) Identify the input, outputs, dependencies and performers.

3. Review and/or document the current, “as-is” manual and automated processes looking for duplications of effort and/or unnecessary steps; ways to consolidate; mismatches of inputs, outputs and dependencies; etc. Develop and document the recommended new “to-be” processes to support the current requirements. Note: Automation of a current process is not process re-engineering or transformation. Deep process changes may be required to enable transformation from a static web publishing environment to a dynamic, transaction enabled one.

4. Identify everyone who is associated with all content within the identified processes. In other words, identify all the content creators, reviewers, approvers, operational personnel, etc. Document the roles and the responsibilities of each.

5. Conduct a consistency review of roles & responsibilities as currently in use, and develop a common set of roles and responsibilities that is acceptable to all groups. Review the roles and responsibilities to ensure that all groups needing to select, create, acquire, review and approve, publish and maintain the content have been included. Look for ways to simplify, consolidate roles, etc. Document the updated roles and responsibilities.

6. Identify technology and tool requirements to automate the content management processes. As with roles and responsibilities, first the tools that are in use should be identified. Next, the tools should be evaluated, and a comparison and/or recommendations developed.

7. Identify the technology and infrastructure necessary to store and support the delivery of the content including the requirements to define and create the input and publishing styles, information architecture and templates. Document the requirements, including response time, page load time, download time, etc.

8. The technology requirements should be reviewed for appropriateness to implementing the process, current legacy system integrating, new industry offerings, etc.

9. Identify and document any requirements for change management and/or a common requirements process to support content management. As appropriate, identify common tools to support these. This should include growth projections in both content and access levels (hits), and content vitality (percent of change over a defined time period, e.g. 20% change over 6 months, vs. 80% change.
