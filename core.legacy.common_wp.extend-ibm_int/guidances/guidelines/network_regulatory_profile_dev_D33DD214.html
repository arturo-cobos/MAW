Main Description
  1. During engagement marketing, determine the countries where the client currently uses telecommunications to support business objectives, client experiences to date and where additional geographic coverage is planned.  This provides the list of countries whose telecommunications environment must be investigated.  It also assists you in evaluating the clientâ€™s experience and in-house expertise in providing networking services in multiple countries.
  2. During engagement planning, decide what attributes to include in the Network Regulatory Profile.  You need to select the services to be researched and the characteristics of these services that are important as input to your recommendations.  You also need to determine the depth of information that will be provided to the client.  For instance, a network strategy engagement may provide an overview of available services in each country with an indication of the ubiquitous and emerging services.  An engagement with the objective of producing a detailed wide area network design or a service provider recommendation would need to consider cost tradeoffs, lead times and quality or service guarantees. 
  3. Examples of services include private circuits, frame relay services, ATM services, virtual private network services, Internet access, analog dial, ISDN and xDSL.  Examples of characteristics include availability of the service, service providers, cost, lead times and the availability of service level agreements.
  4. Gather data for the Network Regulatory Profile from client personnel and research.  Cross check critical information from client personnel with your research results.  For critical information, you may want to ask a networking trained individual from IBM in the target country to review and comment on your findings.
