Guideline: Developing Request for Information

Main Description

The IBM selection team early in the project should develop the Request for Information work product. Team members use information gathered on the client and complete the RFI for delivery to the vendors.

Prepare the Request for Information according to the following steps:

  1. Utilize the Statement of Work and any pertinent current models and work product components available to identify the business areas to be evaluated in the RFI.  Gather known metrics, volume information and detail known constraints.
  2. Develop the purpose description for the RFI, which will establish to the vendors the desired result of the software selection.  Emphasize the priority and focus of the software selection in the purpose to ensure a clear understanding of the client’s goals.
  3. Determine the schedule for the selection process to highlight key dates to the vendor.
  4. Establish the response content and format desired from the vendors.
  5. Outline the RFI response content and specify the number of copies desired. Content may include product literature, white papers, technical descriptions, demonstration diskettes or CDs, references or case studies, standard pricing and contract terms.
  6. Identify the key points of contact for the vendor with regards to the RFI.
  7. Include a statement of confidentiality for the vendor to sign and return with the RFI response.
  8. Develop a summary of the project scope and the desired business processes to be facilitated by the software.
  9. Provide a summary to accompany any models or work product components to be provided to the vendors.  It should describe what the models or work product components represent and how they may be used.  Include any metrics that may be useful to the vendor in determining the client’s software solution needs.
  10. Describe any known constraints affecting the software selection.  These are often technical or regulatory in nature. They may include standards or business and security policies.
  11. After receipt of the responses, work with the client team toreach consensus on the vendor’s proposed solutions by focusing on content, reliability, serviceability, and performance issues.
  12. Carry out a quality check on the responses to ensure adherence to the intent of the RFI.  If lacking, work with the client to develop a method to gather the correct response.
