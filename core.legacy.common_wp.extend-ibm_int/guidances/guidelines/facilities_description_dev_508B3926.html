Guideline: Developing Facilities Description
Migrated development approach from existing IBM Global Services Method work product.
Main Description

The main activities and tasks involved are:

  • Brief client team on activity objectives and approach
  • Agree data collection approach
  • Gather information required:
    • Gather data from documents and other client materials
    • Review blueprints/floor plans of the facilities and business area layout
    • Review the client’s utilities bills and/or maintenance records

    • Review the client’s facilities budget

    • Review the client’s lease/rent agreements and certificate of occupancy

    • Review business area Policies & Procedures Manuals

    • Review the client’s Workman’s Compensation Report (usually available from HR) to identify trends in facilities-related illnesses or injuries.

Review any news articles, publications, and surveys pertaining to the current and/or potential locations

    • Conduct supporting interviews and/or workshops
    • Executive management, facilities management, and business area management interviews

    • Talk with local Realtors, Chamber of Commerce representatives, etc.

    • Talk with other locally established business area management

    • Conduct a walk through of the current and/or potential facilities. Record each of the components in the facility and their exact location. The location of each component is very important to understand when doing an assessment of the facility.

  • Document the information gathered, and store in a central repository of information that is accessible to the whole team

  • Document constraints and user satisfaction

  • Summarize the current status and information gathered for presentation to client management
    The suggested approach implies:

    • Each interview be documented in a call report, using a standard template

    • The key points of each reviewed document summarized using a standard template

    • Any workshops be minuted from flipcharts and other material

    • A common issues/assumptions log be maintained
                       

All should be held in a document repository (e.g., LOTUS Notes, LAN directory) accessible to all team members.

If required, synthesize this data into presentation materials that can then be reviewed by a larger group of stakeholders for accuracy.
