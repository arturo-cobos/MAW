Main Description
The Knowledge Model is developed as the summary documentation of the organization’s overall knowledge and knowledge management environment. Many tools, techniques and surveys may be used to determine the environment. Refer to the Knowledge Management AssetWeb under the category of Method/Method Tools for the latest instances. Tailor the summary expression to conform to the specific objectives of the engagement. Key points to address include:
  • The organization’s overall readiness for the knowledge management initiatives should be summarized.
  • The current strategic objectives of the organization need to be understood and reflected in the model.
  • The knowledge required to support the processes that are critical to the key strategies must be documented.
  • The organization’s unique approach to the generic knowledge management processes (collect, identify, organize, etc.) needs to be understood.
