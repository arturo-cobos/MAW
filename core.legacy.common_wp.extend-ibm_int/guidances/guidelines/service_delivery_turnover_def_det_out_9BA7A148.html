Guideline: Service Delivery Turnover Definition - Detailed Outline
Relationships
Related Elements
  • Service Delivery Turnover Definition
Main Description

A typical instance of the Service Delivery Turnover documentation should contain the general topics and types of information.


Content
Comment

Turnover objectives
The SD team will describe the set of objectives the SD turnover must achieve based on the understanding of the Project Workbook Outline or a similar document (if the Development/maintenance team is from a third-party organization).

Application development/Maintenance project background

Business needs
Description of the business needs for the project.

Solution background
Description of the technical environment, solution and constraints faced by the team.

Key requirements
List the high-level requirements that the project met.

Turnover scope

Scope
Identify the boundaries of the SD turnover.

Key points and turnover plan
Identification of specific SD turnover needs (if any) to be addressed and provide key dates and states in the turnover plan.

Major deliverables by the Development/maintenance team to the SD team
Outline the major deliverables that will be delivered by the Development/maintenance team to the SD team. Sample deliverables are shown below:
· Business process documentation.
· Organization charts and missions.
· High-level technology architecture diagrams.
· System context - all the documentation which describe the system with respect the to client requirements.
· Logical design components - all the design documentation which generically describe the system.
· Physical design components - all the design documentation which describe the actual representation of the system with respect to its generic description.
· Hardware and software inventory – a listing of all the technologies in use and any known information regarding its selection, long-term viability, maintenance, and impact on the project objectives.
· IT standards - all the support documentation which direct the development of the system.
· Operating processes and procedures - all the documentation which assists users in utilizing the system and operators to keep the system available.
· Operating environment - all the assets which support in running the service.
· Training and support materials - all the documentation and structures that are used in the provision of the required readiness needed by both operators and users to function properly.

Customer satisfaction criteria
Document any informal understanding of customer satisfaction issues that should be transferred, if needed, from the development/maintenance team to the SD team, especially the Service Level Agreements (SLA).

Planning framework

Brief description of each work unit
Break the turnover down into its major work units. (High-level WBS).

High-level turnover schedule
Provide a high-level schedule for SD turnover.

Key assumptions and dependencies
List the key assumptions, which, if invalid, would make it necessary to revise the turnover plan. List any key dependencies.

High-level estimates
Provide a high-level estimate of the amount of effort involved in knowledge transfer, assessing the application assets, creating checklists and devising acceptance criteria. The estimate is required to plan for turnover duration and resources before committing to start maintenance activities.

Overall risks and risk mitigation plans
List any risk factors associated with the turnover. Identify risk mitigation measures.

High-level training and HR plan
Document any necessary skills and resources that will be needed by the SD team.

Team Structure

Customer organization
Provide information about the customer organization in the form of an organization chart. This may be necessary for issue and/or risk escalation to the customer and for continuation of seamless interactions with customer after maintenance activities are initiated.

SD team organization
Provide information about the SD team structure in the form of an organization chart. This is necessary for issue and/or risk escalation and communication between development/maintenance team and the SD team during the turnover process and warranty period.

Turnover Asset Identification, Acceptance Approach and Turnover Assessment
Depending on the scope of turnover and engagement context, the following is to be determined:
· Type of asset and asset status
· Acceptance criteria
· Handover and takeover methodologyHanHan
· Configuration control procedures
· Packaging considerations
The above serve as inputs for the creation of:
· Turnover specific asset checklist.
· Turnover specific assessment checklist.

Usage Context

  • Service - Specific IT function [or output], performed on behalf of or in support of, a customer [consumer], that provides measurable value. Services are a basis for doing business with the customer [consumer of IT capabilities], require an understanding of their needs, and are delivered through a series of implemented activities. 
  • Process - A collection of related activities that take inputs, transforms them, and produces outputs that support an enterprise goal. Implemented processes are enabled through people, tools and information. 
  • For the application, data and infrastructure components, it is likely some structured form of data presentation will be helpful, including:
    Site The physical location of the component.
    Component The class of component being listed.
    Product If "bought": the name of the manufacturer and model of the component, or if "in house": some form of appropriate identification.
    Version The identification, as appropriate, of specific versions of release level data on the component.
    Owner The person or organization responsible for managing the component.
    Notes High level data providing context about the component.
  • For the IT Management components, the following should be present:
    - A model or list of the client's IT recognized management processes.
    - High-level process diagrams (where appropriate).
    - An IT organization chart.
    - A matrix of IT management processes and owners/participants.
