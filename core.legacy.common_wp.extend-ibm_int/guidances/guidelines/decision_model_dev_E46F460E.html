Main Description

The development of the Decision Model is the responsibility of the client core selection team with guidance from the IBM consultant.  It is very important that the final set of decision criteria represents a consensus among the core selection team members.

The development of this work product is usually performed in a “workshop” setting.  All members of the core selection team should be present or at least represented to achieve consensus of the final decision model. The IBM consultant should facilitate this working session and perform the following steps:

  • Define the major categories for solution evaluation.  These will typically represent the different areas of concern (i.e., business area, functional areas). 
  • Define the minor categories for each major category as appropriate.  This may include key process areas, key functional components, or other areas that the client feels is an essential differentiator in evaluating the alternatives.  Define sub-categories as appropriate depending on the level of complexity of the client process requirements (but only if it aids in the analysis of the results from the solution evaluation process).
  • Assign a weight value to each major category relative to the other major categories.  One example would be to start with 100 total points, and allocate them to each category until the "correct" balance is achieved.  The "correct" balance attempts to represent the importance of having, and the risk of not having, a strong fit to the client preferences and requirements in a category.
  • Allocate a weight value down through the hierarchy to the lowest level of detail.

In doing this, attempt to match business benefits to the detail levels to guide the allocations.

It may be helpful to produce a scoring guideline to improve the objectivity of the weighting of each category.  Use the guides to provide a "minimum/maximum" advice to the decision team.
