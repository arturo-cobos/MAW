Main Description

Plan Evaluation

Identify stakeholders and potential interviewees/workshop participants from business, IT, project and test teams and quality and test organizations. Customize the Interview Guide/Workshop Agenda and Presentation Template to explore indications of problem areas in testing for this project. Look for strengths as well as weaknesses.

Prepare for Evaluation

Interview sponsor. Interview stakeholders or hold workshop and capture data. Interviews are generally conducted individually to allow for free speech and candid discussions. In contentious or touchy situations, it is often useful to give (and keep) assurance of not attributing comments and interview content to individuals. Workshops are conducted for small groups, six to eight people and consensus sought on major issues.

Perform Evaluation

Collect, confirm, organize and analyze the data. Look for gaps in:

  • Quality management, policies and procedures
  • Test plans and processes
  • Project structure and plans
  • Skills
  • Measurements
  • Tools and technology
  • Methods
  • Organization, roles and responsibilities, organization structures
  • Cultural barriers to testing effectiveness
    Identify associated risks, perform risk assessment and identify risk mitigation strategies. Recognize strengths and build on them. Identify recommendations and do trial runs with key stakeholders to validate findings and conclusions.

Report on Evaluation

Prepare and present the final recommendations report to sponsor and stakeholders.
