Main Description

The following are the guidelines for developing a Program Management framework;

  1. Identify and understand current client approach to program management and performance measurement
  2. Agree on scorecard and success criteria for transformation
  3. Match appropriate goals and success criteria to current performance measurement systems
  4. Design and enhance program management framework
  5. Implement program management and measurement approaches
  6. Monitor and revise
Identify and Understand Current Client Approach to Program Management and Performance Measurement

Before you develop a Program Framework, you must first know what exists. The organization may already have a method or strategy for achieving its desired transformation. It is important to complete this task with the client sponsor(s) in order to understand how they envision success and the transformation methods they have used in the past.
You must also understand the current performance management system in place for the client. You will be linking to this system, so it is important to know how people and systems are measured. Keep in mind that some organizations or departments may have very little in the way of formal performance measurement. Others may have complicated and poorly understood or implemented systems.

Agree on Scorecard and Success Criteria for Transformation

The success criteria or goals are the outcomes or results that the organization wants to achieve. They must be agreed upon or developed with your executive sponsor(s). They should be measurable and actionable and should cascade throughout the organization. IBM uses the Balanced Scorecard approach to help define these goals. The Balanced Scorecard defines goals and measurement from four perspectives:

  1. Financial Perspective: How do we look to shareholders? (ROI, cash flow)
  2. Customer/Client Perspective: How do customers/clients see us? (quality, services)
  3. Internal Business Perspective: What must we excel at? (cycle time, productivity)
  4. Innovation and Learning Perspective: Can we continue to improve and create value? (new products, improved efficiency)

Make sure when you are developing the goals for the transformation that these goals can be cascaded, for example, from organization to department to team to individual.

Match Appropriate Goals and Success Criteria to Current Performance Measurement Systems

Based on your understanding of the existing performance management system from Step 1 and the goals and success criteria developed in Step 2, you must now link those goals into the performance management system in order to encourage individuals, teams, and departments to achieve their success measures and to reward them for doing so. See tools above.

Design and Enhance Performance Management Framework

Based on your understanding of the existing program management framework from Step 1 and output from the integrated transition plan, you must now design or enhance the existing framework for managing the overall program. When there are multiple projects happening concurrently, it is extremely important to have one coordinated and organized framework in place to make sure there is consistency and efficiency across the program. IBM uses the Program Office tool (insert link here) to produce one document that includes:

  • Transformation vision and mission
  • Program scope, objectives, and responsibilities
  • Program organization and structure
  • Program management approach
  • Consistent procedures and policies, i.e., status reporting, conflict resolution
  • Goals and success criteria for the transformation
  • Intellectual capital capture and transfer
  • Resource management
  • Individual project scope, metrics, and deliverables happening under the program

This document should be developed with the help of the entire project team and validated with the executive sponsor(s).

Implement Program Management and Measurement Approaches

The new framework and measurement criteria need to be implemented according to the Integrated Transition Plan. Make sure you consider who you need to complete the program and what you need in terms of space and supplies.
In communicating the changes to the program management goals and success criteria, keep in mind that people can help the transformation succeed only if they understand what the objectives are. Work closely with the communications team for these announcements.
Consider the role of incentives or other motivational programs to promote awareness and interest in change activities.

Monitor and Revise

This work product should be continually monitored and changed as appropriate, but keep in mind that the framework should not be changed repeatedly as the goal is to create consistency for the transition program.
