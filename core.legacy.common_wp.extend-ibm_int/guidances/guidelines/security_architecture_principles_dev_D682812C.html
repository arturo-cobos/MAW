Main Description

The Security Architecture Principles work product development may consider two different levels of Principle.

Information Security Guiding Principles are umbrella guidelines that form the terms of reference in determining what to do in relation to the security of corporate information. Generic Principles can address

- 'Trust Levels of Users and Business Partners'
- 'Who will own the management and governance of security'
- 'Will security be service driven'
- 'Investment vs Risk'
- 'Who owns the assets'

Information Security Design Principles are specific Technical guidelines that form terms of reference for the Architecture. They must comply with the Guiding Principles. A spectrum of security design principles can be defined:

- enterprise wide.
- part of a specific architecture component.
- part of a specific system.
- related to a specific security issue.
Specific Principles can address.
- important decisions later in the architecture solution design.
- address typical risks/vulnerabilities in the environment.
- help select designs that are easily supported by commercial products.

The development approach for the work product would include the following steps:

  • A review of the organization’s business strategy and related security requirements.
  • A review of the organization’s IT strategy, current security concerns, and future security requirements.
  • A review and analysis of the organization’s current security policy, standards and management processes against the needs of the organization’s business and IT strategies.
  • Draft principles, based on the reviews can be augmented at a stakeholder’s workshop. The audience for guiding principles would consist of business and IT management, while specific principles would consist of domain specialists. The specific principles need to comply with the generic principles.
  • Publishing of the draft Principles for comments and agreement prior to final agreement.
