Main Description
The Knowledge Gap is developed as the documentation of the organization’s overall knowledge management capabilities, actual and potential. The gap analysis is performed only after the assessment of the knowledge management environment has been made and after the business’ strategic opportunities have been identified. The summary expression of this gap analysis should conform to the specific objectives of the engagement. The development steps include:
  • Review outputs from the knowledge management assessment activities or from individually selected tools/techniques (e.g. Social Capital Analysis, Process Knowledge Mapping). This review should consider the following findings of the assessment:
    • The content sources and locations of the selected knowledge areas
    • The high-level social capital issues associated with the selected knowledge areas
    • The existing knowledge management processes for the selected knowledge areas
    • The existing knowledge management roles and responsibilities
    • The existing technical infrastructure capable of enabling the knowledge management function
  • Compare the assessment findings against leading knowledge management best practices and capabilities that are relevant to the organization (relevant to its needs and requirements, relevant to its business strategies, processes, organizational readiness, IT capabilities, etc.).
  • Determine the gaps between the existing and relevant knowledge management practices.
  • Analyze the gaps, defining the importance of the knowledge management capabilities to the organization and the current capabilities of the organization.
  • Propose and document potential solutions or initiatives that could close the gaps.
  • Summarize and document the results of the gap analysis.
