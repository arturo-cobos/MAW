Guideline: Developing Industry Networking Profile
Migrated development approach from existing IBM Global Services Method work product.
Relationships
Related Elements
  • Industry Networking Profile
Main Description
  1. During engagement planning, use the statement of work to determine if you will be collecting data on the client’s industry or competitors or both.
  2. If you are researching the client’s industry, decide what industry segments are represented by your client’s business and the aspects of those industry segments are important as input to your recommendations.  You also need to determine the level of depth of information that will be provided to the client.  For instance, the client may need detailed information on common industry networks if a part of your recommendations involve buying vs. building networking services to support electronic commerce between your client and their business partners in related industries.  If it is a high level overview of trends and directions in the industry, much less detail may be required. 
  3. If you are researching the client’s competition, decide what companies are your client’s key competitors and which aspects of the competitors’ telecommunications operations are important as input to your recommendations.  Typical areas of interest are service levels, telecommunications spending, headcount and any articulated business value of the network to the overall business. 
  4. As you develop your data matrix cross check it against the Industry Networking Profile.  End users, other personnel from the client’s business units or technical personnel who are close to the client’s business are in-house resources for information about networking in a client’s industry.  Sometimes they may reference associations or trade publications that are leads to additional information.
