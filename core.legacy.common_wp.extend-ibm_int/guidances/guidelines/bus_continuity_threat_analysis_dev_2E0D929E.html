Main Description

1. Identify threats.

Once the assets are identified in the Business Continuity Asset Analysis, the next stage is determining what to protect the assets from. Using the three threat categories as a basis for investigation, the practitioner examines threats present at or near the client site based on his/her research of the geography, area, and social climate through client and emergency management personnel interviews or surveys.

2. Determine the loss categories from which the client is most susceptible. Loss categories are:
  • Disruption
  • Direct Loss
  • Compromise
  • Intangibles
  • Personal Injury/Loss of Life
  • Indirect Loss
  • Theft

3. Determine threat probability, or the number of times a threat may occur that will affect the asset. This is shown in the loss event probability or threat frequencies. These typically will depend on factors surrounding the three threat categories and are evaluated through site walkthroughs, accessing government statistical databanks, companyâ€™s Cap Index™, governmental and political issues, and historical data (internal or external) which can affect the probability of threats.
