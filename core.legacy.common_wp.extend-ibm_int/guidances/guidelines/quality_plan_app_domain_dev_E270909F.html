Main Description

The business unit or competency of the delivery organization will typically provide a standard quality plan that satisfies the quality requirements for its organization.  The quality criteria for each work product/deliverable and quality contributors are defined using deliverable acceptance criteria, as specified in the Agreement.

Define Quality Criteria

Quality criteria represent the internal or build view of acceptance criteria.  Quality criteria are the characteristics that must be considered as the deliverables and work products are being built.  Quality criteria are used to further clarify emergent properties associated with acceptance criteria. 

Using a car as an example may help clarify the relationship between acceptance criteria and quality criteria.  If the deliverable is a car and an acceptance criterion is that the car must be comfortable, quality criteria must be assigned to the parts of the car that are built to contribute to comfort (such as the seats and the tires).

  • Example of quality criteria for efficiency: Path length and access time will be no greater than xyz.
  • Examples of quality criteria for maintainability:
    • A software program will never be more than 500 lines of code. 
    • Each software program will be documented as described in standard ABC.

  • Example of quality criteria for performance:
    • Response times less than 1 second > 90% of time.

Define Quality Contributors

Quality contributors are project factors that, when applied, will assist in achieving the quality objectives of the project.  Quality contributors include skills, standards, tools, methods, and procedures.

  • Examples of quality contributors for maintainability:
    • Program structure will be designed using a CASE tool.
    • Code reviews will be held on all programs.

The quality criteria and contributors are then used to determine what level and type of static and dynamic testing are required.  Entry/exit criteria and roles and responsibilities for quality tracking and reporting are also defined for each level of testing.

Produce the Quality Plan for Application Domain
 Using the information outlined above:

  1. An initial version of the Quality Plan for Application Domain is produced during the preparation phase.
  2. It is then expanded/refined at the start of the execution phase and maintained throughout the life of the project.

Typically, the top-level project organizational unit creates one project quality plan to address the entire project:

  1. The top-level project organizational unit identifies quality criteria associated with all deliverables. 
  2. Quality contributors and assurance checkpoints are identified. 
  3. This information cascades down to project organizational units within the organizational breakdown structure. 
  4. The application development and testing project organizational units may add to or expand on the quality criteria, and also assign additional quality contributors to cover the work at its level.
