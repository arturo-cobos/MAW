Main Description
Transition Initiatives are derived using the following inputs:
  • Client's existing inventory of strategic initiatives, programs and projects
  • Newly identified and defined strategic direction and/or strategic opportunities (including capabilities and enablers)
  • Prioritization criteria (identified early in the engagement and validated, and/or revised based upon strategic decision criteria as identified above, or other criteria as identified below)
  • Critical issues, transition issues gathered during the course of the engagement
  • Business and IT Principles - with associated implication statements
  • Analysis of gaps between the existing business environment and the proposed business process, organization, and information models
  • Analysis of gaps between existing and future project portfolios and application initiatives
  • Recommendations for legacy systems enhancement and replacement, plus the evaluation, procurement and implementation of third party software packages
  • Analysis of gaps between the existing technical infrastructure and the proposed enterprise architecture
  • Assessment of the organization’s process maturity
  • Analysis of gaps between the present and proposed management system, in terms of organization, skills and management controls
  • Recommendations on capability sourcing in each of the above areas
  • Analysis of the organization’s ability to accept, incorporate and manage change

The general approach to identifying the Transition Initiatives is to:

  1. Identify core and extended team members who are knowledgeable about the focal areas being investigated within the context of the engagement scope and objectives.
  2. Reach agreement on and finalize Initiative documentation format, level of detail and prioritization criteria
  3. Convene workshops to:
  • review the results from previous engagement activities (see Description for list of inputs)
  • combine content into logical groupings where appropriate to establish preliminary list of Transition Initiatives
  • gather additional data and document transition initiatives using agreed to format and level of detail
  • prioritize initiatives based upon agreed to prioritization criteria
  • select initiatives to recommend and carry forward
  • gain client commitment to proceed
