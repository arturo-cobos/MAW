Main Description
Financial Benchmarks effort varies greatly depending on the issues the client wishes to address and the amount of information publicly available about relevant client businesses. Pre-proposal discussions with the client should address the specific concerns /issues the client wishes the consultant to resolve and the scope of the engagement. Based on the issues and scope, an experienced consultant can quickly determine which financial metrics needs to be benchmarked in order to formulate hypotheses and to subsequently test those hypotheses.
 
Most information should be obtainable through one or two interviews with key financial executives querying financial reporting and trade association databases. Often non-public data on "best of breed" companies outside the clientâ€™s market is available through data sharing agreements that are already in place. Team members should ask about this information in preliminary client interviews. Early in the proposal process access to specific information should be authorized by the client. Appropriate IBM and client personnel should sign confidentiality agreements, where appropriate.
