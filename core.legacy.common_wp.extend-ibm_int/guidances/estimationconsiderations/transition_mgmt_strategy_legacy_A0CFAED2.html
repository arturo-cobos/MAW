Main Description

Defining a Transition Management Strategy can take 2-4 weeks, if it is run in parallel with a larger engagement, and it can be resourced by consultants and appropriate client staff. This depends on the size of the organization involved, the scale of the transition and the duration of the implementation, and the number of scenarios or iterations that are required to adequately consider the tradeoffs and ensure that the leadership is adequately informed before making decisions.   

The appropriateness and success of the transition management strategy will be reviewed throughout the life cycle of the implementation.  In particular, have a major review after each phase of the project is completed. Make revisions if the strategy is not working.
