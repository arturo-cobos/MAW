Main Description

Because of the many data collection and analysis techniques that can be used in primary research, specific estimating considerations are included in the relevant technique papers. Factors, which will influence the timing and cost, include the availability of a research vendor, the ease with which potential respondents can be located and recruited, the amount of information sought from respondents. When planning research with business respondents, more time is usually required for the engagement because of the increased difficulty in scheduling respondents, particularly when the research occurs at quarter- or year-end.

Timing considerations are:

  • planning time,
  • time required to identify potential respondents,
  • time required to schedule potential respondents to participate, 
  • number of responses desired, 
  • cooperation rate of potential respondents, 
  • survey programming (only for web survey), 
  • time required to assimilate and compile data, 
  • time to analyze data.

Budgetary considerations are:

  • purchase a list of potential respondents (Internet, phone or mailing lists),
  • cost of interview facility,
  • vendor cost for each completed interview or questionnaire,
  • cost to recruit participants,
  • cost for a vendor to host a web survey,
  • incentives for all participants, and
  • refreshments for focus group participants.
