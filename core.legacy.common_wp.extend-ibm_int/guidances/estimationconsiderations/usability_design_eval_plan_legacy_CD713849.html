Main Description
A Usability Design and Evaluation Plan for a small-scale project can be developed in 3-5 days. A Usability Design and Evaluation Plan for a large-scale project may take 1 to 4 weeks or longer to develop. Multiple iterations will increase the overall time.

A usability design and evaluation plan is written and refined iteratively over the course of a project. For large (50K hour) projects the total effort can be expected to take on average 230 hours with:

  • An average of 100 hours being expended during outlining of the solution
  • An average of 50 hours being expended during high level design
  • An average of 40 hours being expended during detailed design
  • An average of 40 hours being expended during deployment (provided a second is planned).
For small (5K hours or less) projects, the total effort for a usability design and evaluation plan can be expected to take on average 40 hours with:
  • An average of 12 hours being expended during outlining of the solution
  • An average of 18 hours being expended during high level design
  • An average of 6 hours being expended during detailed design
  • An average of 4 hours being expended during deployment (provided a second release is planned)
