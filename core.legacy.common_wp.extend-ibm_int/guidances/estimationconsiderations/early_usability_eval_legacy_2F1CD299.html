Main Description

To obtain a rough sizing of the project, first perform a top-down estimate, and then use that result as described here to size the effort for this work product.

Early Usability Evaluation occurs during the (high level and detailed) design phases of a project. For large (50K hour) projects the total effort can be expected to take on average 200 hours with:

  • An average of 120 hours being expended during high level design
  • An average of 80 hours being expended during detailed design

For small (5K hours or less) projects, the total effort for early usability evaluation can be expected to take on average 58 hours with:

  • An average of 40 hours being expended during high level design
  • An average of 18 hours being expended during detailed design

There is a minimum amount of time that must be allocated for setting up the evaluation environment, regardless of the size of the project, thus explaining the disproportionately high estimated effort for the small project version of this work product.
