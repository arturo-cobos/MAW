Main Description
Depending on the amount of available customer data and/or the number of targeted customer segments, the time could range from 2 to 6 weeks.
 
Most of the timing considerations will depend upon the depth of analysis built into the engagement. If the engagement is wholly qualitative, the number of focus groups will drive the duration of the engagement. At least two focus groups should be held for each needs-based market segment (e.g., young professionals vs. retired people would represent two different needs-based segments). Each focus group should include people who have disabilities in order to obtain their point of view.
 
Preparation for each focus group requires time to perform a number of tasks:
  • Identify customer names and telephone numbers, if client customers are to be invited, or to identify non-customers from the marketplace;
  • Select a professional focus group facility, if used, requiring approximately one week to locate and negotiate that firmâ€™s contract; and
  • Recruit attendees (usually done by the focus group facility firm), requiring time to locate 8-10 qualified persons who will agree to attend. Invited parties need lead-time of at least one week to be able to commit to the focus group on their personal calendars.
 
When quantitative analysis is included, the time frame for the engagement must be extended to include data collection and the required analyses. The time required for data collection will depend most upon how the survey is administered (Internet, phone, mail) and how easy it is to find qualified respondents. Budgets for each of these data collection methodologies are another important consideration.
