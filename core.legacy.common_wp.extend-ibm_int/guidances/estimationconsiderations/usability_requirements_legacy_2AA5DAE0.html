Main Description
Usability Requirements are iteratively refined over the course of the early phases of a project. For large (50K hour) projects the total effort can be expected to take on average 265 hours with an average of:
  • 160 hours being expended during outline of the solution.
  • 70 hours being expended during high level design.
  • 35 hours being expended during detailed design.
For small (5K hours or less) projects, the total effort for defining usability requirements can be expected to take on average 53 hours with an average of:
  • 24 hours being expended during outline of the solution.
  • 15 hours being expended during high level design.
  • 14 hours being expended during detailed design.
The time required for thorough requirements gathering is highly dependent on the quantity of existing information, resources available to gather requirements, and recruiting and availability of representative users, among other factors. A CRTS requirements gathering session can be performed in 1 or 2 days and can provide most of the requirements for a small project, although planning for it and the analysis and report can take an additional 2 weeks. Larger projects with multiple target audience segments may require additional days for gathering of requirements (up to 1 or 2 weeks). Site visits, interviews, and surveys are time intensive and can extend this activity by several weeks.
