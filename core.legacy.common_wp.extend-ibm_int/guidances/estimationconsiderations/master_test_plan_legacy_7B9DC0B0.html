Estimation Considerations: Master Test Plan (Legacy)
Estimate considerations for preparing master test plan for large and small projects.

Main Description
The effort for putting together a master test plan work product should not be underestimated. For projects of size greater than 50,000 hours and where the business and technical risks are high, it could take as much as 200 hours for an experienced test-planning specialist to organize the test-planning workshop, to run it, and to document the outcome and a master test plan.
 
For a project of about 10,000 hours, it could take anywhere from 100 to 160 hours, especially if it involves many different applications and stakeholder groups.
