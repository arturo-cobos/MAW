Main Description
There are two major factors in estimating the effort to develop the Solution Areas work product. The first is based on an assessment of the level of detail developed in the capability definition. If the capability definition is simply a vision statement such as ‘By 2001, ABC Corporation will become a customer-centric retailer of automobiles and automotive parts’, considerably more effort will be required to develop this capability definition into a set of development projects.
The second major estimating factor is determining the method to be used in soliciting the breadth of capability requirements and confirming the results. The major methods of information gathering used are:
  • Interviews (individual or group)
  • Facilitated group sessions
  • A combination of interviews and facilitated sessions
The selection of which method to use is dependent on factors such as the number of individuals to be interviewed, the culture of information sharing in the organization, the time limitations of the project, and the relative difficulty of coordinating calendars for group sessions.
Once the method of information gathering has been determined, the important estimating factor is the number and length of information gathering and verification sessions to conduct. In addition, adequate time to prepare and summarize each session should also be set aside.
In general, the larger the number of stakeholders to interview and the greater the outside demands on their time, the greater the impact this entire work effort will have on project duration.
