Main Description

The Industry Networking Profile is developed in conjunction with the Network Status Profile and other profiles used to organize data collected preparatory to analyzing the data and developing network recommendations.

Completing the profiles is part of the data-gathering phase of an engagement. The initial data gathering phase typically can take from one to three weeks depending on how many interviews are done, how many locations are visited, how much revalidation of current data is required, etc. Specifically, the industry segment portion of the Network Industry Profile usually is research based. Completion time is very dependent on how much detail is required. For a high level look at an industry, finding and absorbing the material might typically take one to two days for someone already familiar with the technologies. In depth research can easily extend to four or more days if using an industry network is one of the vendor options under serious consideration. This is because the Industry Networking Profile would need to be compared in detail against client requirements. Also, besides research time, a segment of the time earmarked for client interviews would be used for asking interview questions about the industry.   

For the key competitor segment of the Industry Networking Profile, the engagement team and client should set a finite time period for research. The desired data may or may not be available to the engagement team or the client no matter how long the research period is. Typically, you may want to start with eight or sixteen hours of research time on competitors. If you and the client agree, you can PCR the statement of work to devote additional hours to this research.
