Main Description

Just as unpredictable as the number of defects that will be discovered, the time to capture and complete the defect analysis detail varies by the complexity of the test environment and the skill and familiarity of the defect analyst with the business and system components.

Defects found in unit test or earlier may be relatively straightforward and require 1 â€“ 4 hours.

Defects require more time in later test levels and production defects are the most time consuming.

An average of 1- 2 days can grow to as much as a week or more spread over a much greater elapsed time period.

Once an organization has a Defect Management Process established, the heuristics captured from several testing projects on the time and resources involved in creating and using this Work Product, will provide more accurate guidelines for future testing projects in that environment.
