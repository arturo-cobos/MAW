Estimation Considerations: Application Management Plan (Legacy)
The Application Management Plan must be kept simple and not overworked. A high ambition easily results in overspending of the resources in creating and maintaining the Application Management Plan.

Main Description

It is more difficult to comply with a detailed and comprehensive Application Management Plan. If your estimates are far from the given estimates, you should consider adjusting the ambition level.

Consider these factors when estimating the effort for creating an Application Management Plan:

  • Size and complexity of the applications.
  • Size of the team.
  • Scope of the contract and included work-types.
  • Process maturity.
  • Availability of the information in the contract.
  • Previous experiences with the same account.
  • Means of documenting: On-line or hard-copy.

Four to six person-weeks based on the above factors plus the effort to elicit the information if not available from the contract plus the implementation effort if an on-line database is to be created. This estimate assumes use of established procedures and methods. For instance, if this is the first application management contract for an IBM organization, it will take time to put the necessary organization in place and deploy available procedures and the IBM Global Services Method.
