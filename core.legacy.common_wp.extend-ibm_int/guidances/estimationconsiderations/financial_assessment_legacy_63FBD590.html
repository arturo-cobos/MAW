Main Description
Financial assessment effort varies greatly depending on the issues the client wishes to address and the amount of information the client is willing to divulge. Pre-proposal discussions with the client should address the specific concerns /issues the client wishes the consultant to resolve and the scope of the engagement. Based on the issues and scope, an experienced consultant can quickly determine which financial information needs to be reviewed to formulate hypotheses and to subsequently test those hypotheses.
 
All key information should be obtainable through one or two interviews with key financial executives; e. g., controller. Early in the proposal process, the client should authorize access to specific information. Confidentiality agreements should signed by appropriate IBM and client personnel. Corporate P&L, Balance Sheet, and Statement of Sources & Uses of Funds should be available form public sources. Comparable statements each business unit should be available from corporate or divisional financial personnel. Access to price lists, product cost detail and other proprietary information should be discussed during initial discussions as the lack of such information can seriously limit the teamâ€™s ability to formulate a solution to the problem.
 
Assuming "full cooperation" from client financial staff, allow three-person days per business unit to collect and generate a Financial Assessment work product for each business. Allow more time if client does not have requested information readily available.
