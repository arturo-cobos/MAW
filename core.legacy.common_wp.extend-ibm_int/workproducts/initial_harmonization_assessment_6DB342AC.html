Artifact: Initial Harmonization Assessment (ART 0683)
This outcome describes the Business and IT structures that have been identified to bring the required degree of commonality to the business in support of the Clients' business strategy and goals.
Domains: Business Strategy
Work Product Kinds: Assessment
Description
Main DescriptionThese structures are defined through the harmonization principles (i.e. the process that will be computerized, the degree of harmonization in the process across the entities and, the sites to be implemented and the roll out strategy).

To develop this assessment, the following factors need to be considered:

 
The Business Case
  • This is defined by the current situation and the business issues: These items being a description of the current situation and its associated problems (e.g. the current systems being unable to support the emerging market, inconsistent data across sites, information only available after manual intervention).
  • The ERP & Technology: This describes what package has been selected, why and what it can do for the Client.
 
The High Level Scope
  • The Goals and Strategies: What the company is hoping to achieve and how it proposes to achieve it. For example, the goal may be to be the lowest cost producer of product X; the strategy may be to be supplied by one supplier on a global basis.
  • The High Level Drivers: The Processes that will enable the goals to be achieved e.g. economies of scale through single site production or the production of components at single sites.
  • Definition of the scope of the project (i.e. what processes in what countries, or what processes in the various corporate entities within a geography).
 
The Harmonization Model
 
The Harmonization Model is the vision of what the company will look like in the future. This will define the structure, described in terms of the package (i.e. shared services center, the systems, the interfaces and the databases).
