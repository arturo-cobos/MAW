Artifact: Hypothesis and Data Framework (BUS 340)
The Hypothesis and Data Framework work product is a collection of statements of fact, which, if true, support a client’s ability to enable a desired business or IT capability.
Domains: Business Strategy
Work Product Kinds: Model
Purpose

The purpose of the Hypothesis and Data Framework is to:

  • Provide a structured approach to establish a baseline or definition of an ideal end-state for a client capability or set of capabilities
  • Use the baseline to probe the client for an assessment of their current abilities in reference to their desired capability
  • Determine the facts that need to be collected to prove or disprove the hypotheses
  • Identify relevant sources of information needed to evaluate the client’s position relative to their desired state
  • Use the results of the framework to estimate resources and time required to complete the hypothesis analysis
  • Use the results of completing the collection of facts and proving/disproving the hypotheses to develop recommendations to close the gaps for hypotheses that were disproved

This work product is useful for bringing structure to an unstructured business problem. It assists the engagement team in leading the client through an understanding of a desired capability state and the high level requirements needed to support the ideal state.

Description
Main Description

This work product is used to establish a baseline for an ideal (e.g. end-state) situation. Using issue-based consulting techniques and this work product, a gap between a desired state and an actual state is developed. The following are examples of client situations which may be addressed:

  • Determine gaps between a client’s business strategy and ideal customer-centered strategy
  • Determine the gaps between a client’s current capabilities and the end-state described in a customer-centered strategy
  • Determine the gaps between a client’s current infrastructure (capability-enablers: process, organization, and/or technology) and those required to support a desired end-state capability
  • Establish the detailed scope of an engagement through the use of hypotheses and the data required to support or refute the hypotheses
  • Determine client’s level of understanding of a desired capability. Is there a clear understanding of terminology and concepts related to the capability? Do all client divisions affected by a capability agree to its purpose, scope and influence?
  • Determine client’s readiness to undertake a desired capability. What gaps exist in required processes, organization, technology, or data?
  • Evaluate the level of consensus and support for any recommended next steps or actions. Are client groups who must work together to successfully implement a capability ready to do so? Do they have a common understanding of the overall approach to be used?
  • Determine the sources (e.g. personnel to be interviewed, etc) within the client to validate the current level of a capability or a capability enabler in the infrastructure (i.e.. process, organization or technology described in the end-state hypotheses)

The hypotheses are typically tested by asking the client a series of supporting questions to challenge the original statements of fact. Under the presumption that most clients are not able to affirm all the hypotheses, the use of the Hypothesis and Data Framework work product is generally extended to determine the facts, organizational data sources, system data sources and methods of capture required to confirm or refute the hypotheses. The results of the Hypothesis and Data Framework are then used to estimate staffing, level of effort and duration requirements to close the gaps between the reality of the statements of fact and the actual situation.

Brief Outline

The notation is largely tabular. 

The hypotheses and test questions are complete sentences.  The hypotheses are stated as if the desired baseline exists in its fullest form already; the test questions should thoroughly probe each hypothesis to determine the validity of the hypothesis.

The Data Sources are filled out with an “x” denoting where the data can be obtained.

Key Considerations
  1. The objective of this work product is to determine the client’s view of the hypotheses and identify the data needed to validate the hypotheses.   This work product establishes a starting point for a typical consulting engagement.
  2. A second objective of the work product is to obtain consensus within the client organization surrounding the hypotheses and data sources.  Significantly varying conclusions or results around any one hypothesis should suggest to the engagement team that further investigative work is required to determine the true state of affairs.   
  3. This work product can be very effectively used to determine the scope of downstream engagements. For example, if a key hypothesis cannot be validated or refuted but is determined to be critical to the nature of the investigation (e.g. determining a client’s readiness for establishing customer loyalty processes), a related but separate engagement could be scoped to focus on obtaining additional information regarding just the hypothesis in question. This work product provides a structure under which the client can be led to draw their own conclusions and action items.
  4. This work product can also be used to determine gaps in the client’s processes, organization, technology or data required to enable the capability under investigation.  Based on the size of the gap, this work product can be used to establish a detailed, methodical work plan to obtain the information to close the gap.
Tailoring
Impact of not having

If a Hypothesis and Data Framework is not produced, the following difficulties may occur:

  • The ideal end-state of a capability may not be sufficiently defined to bound an engagement or an assessment. As a result, the engagement scope can expand
  • Without the framework structure, critical information required to support or refute a hypothesis can be overlooked
  • Without the framework, it may not be apparent that the same client contact person is the "source" of information regarding multiple hypotheses or fact-finding questions, resulting in client dissatisfaction when repeatedly approached, piece-meal, by several engagement team members pursuing different hypotheses
Reasons for not needingThis work product is not required if the client’s business problem is well understood, well structured and well documented. If the scope of the hypotheses is limited, supporting information requirements are not considered to be challenging, or the supporting information is readily available, this work product not required.
