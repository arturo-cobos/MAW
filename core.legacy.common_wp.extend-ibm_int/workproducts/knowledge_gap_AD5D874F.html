Artifact: Knowledge Gap (ORG 413)
The Knowledge Gap work product documents the variance (gaps) between an organization’s existing knowledge management environment and its desired knowledge practices, between what it does know and what it must know.
Domains: Knowledge Management
Work Product Kinds: Assessment
Purpose

The development of the Knowledge Gap serves to document and highlight the differences between the current and the desired future state of knowledge management at an organization. The main reasons for developing the Knowledge Gap are:

  • To identify the gap between what an organization must know to execute its strategy, and what it does know
  • To provide a summary overview and understanding of the difference between the current and future knowledge management practices and capabilities of an organization
  • To provide the potential list of knowledge management opportunities for subsequent assessment and prioritization
  • To provide the planning constructs necessary to develop and define the future knowledge management initiatives
  • To enable and support a proper alignment between the potential knowledge management initiatives and the goals and objectives of the enterprise. To provide a useful vehicle for communication and ownership of the follow-on knowledge management initiatives
Description
Main DescriptionThe identified gaps become the input to the determination of potential knowledge management initiatives for the organization. The Knowledge Gap includes the conclusions derived from an assessment of the current and future state of knowledge and knowledge management in the organization. The Knowledge Gap documents desired capabilities, the current capabilities, what was found during data gathering (high level requirements and key assumptions), the difference between the "Current" and "Future" Knowledge Models and the next steps needed to deal with the gaps.
Brief Outline

The notation of the Knowledge Gap may take either a text or graphics format approach appropriate to the engagement and should include the following components:

  • Description of the potential capability of the knowledge management processes (identify, collect, adapt, organize, apply, share, create) or enablers (the leadership, culture, technology, measurement).
  • The importance of the capability (in terms of strategic support for the business).
  • Summary of current capability of the knowledge management processes or enablers.
  • Description of the difference between the potential and current knowledge management capabilities.
  • Proposed initiatives or solutions to resolve the differences.

Key Considerations
Ensure that the gap analysis is done with a thorough understanding of the current Knowledge Model. This model should have been verified before the gap analysis begins. Ideally, the knowledge analysts developing the gap analysis will have been involved throughout the knowledge assessment activities so that they possess a full understanding of the issues relevant to this client.
Tailoring
Impact of not having

Without this work product:

  • Planning for specific knowledge management initiatives would not occur in context of the comprehensive view of all the potential opportunities for improvement documented by this work product
  • An organization’s knowledge management endeavors might not be aligned with the current business processes and strategies of that organization
Reasons for not needingA Knowledge Gap is not required if the difference between current and future knowledge and knowledge management states has been developed by other means.
