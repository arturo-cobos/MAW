Artifact: Process Capability Definition (BUS 402)
The Process Capability Definition work product defines the process design points and the capabilities of each of the processes within scope of the engagement and their related requirements that are necessary to support the Strategic Capabilities.
Domains: Business Process
Work Product Kinds: Model
Purpose

The reasons why this work product is produced are to:

  • Provide overall context and understanding of critical business process requirements for a given transformation effort
  • Assist Client’s in recognizing trade-offs of different process configuration choices. Helps "think through" the required Strategic Capabilities and how they impact requirements to process capabilities
  • Drive the development of business process capabilities
  • Identify and document the specific business process capabilities that will be included in the scope of a transformation initiative and enable a plan to be created
  • Provide the basis for sizing the effort required to perform the (re)-design of business processes
  • Assist in gaining executive agreement to the scope and boundaries of the process design effort
  • Provide a record of the scope and boundaries and assist in managing expectations regarding the scope
  • Provide a foundation for defining capability enablers (Organization, Knowledge/Information and Application(s) and determining ownership responsibilities
  • Assist in communicating the impact of the redesign across the business
  • To identify what skills, knowledge, and background are required from the project team
Description
Main Description

The Process Capabilities may be identified at the process group, process, sub-process or activity level and address process design points (major process elements) and process capabilities.

The Process Capabilities Definition Work Product:

  • Identifies and describes, with text and graphics, the design points of the process and its sub-processes
  • Identifies and describes, with text and graphics, the required capabilities of the process and its -sub-processes
  • Identifies and describes the linkage to the strategic capabilities

The Process Capabilities represents those critical things that a business process must be able to do to ensure that the supported Strategic Capabilities can be enabled. Process Capabilities provides different strength of impact, i.e. High, Medium or Low, to the supported Strategic Capabilities.

Brief Outline
A Process Capability Definition template and a Process Design point diagram contains the following major elements:
  • Process Name (Process and/or Sub-Processes)
  • Process Level – identify whether at Process Group, Process, Sub-process or Activity level
  • Process Description - short description of the process and its purpose
  • Supported Strategic Capabilities – lists the strategic capabilities that this process supports
  • Parent Process name
  • Process Capability Detail
    • Capability Id
    • Capability Name
    • Capability State: Realized (currently available), Specified (need to be developed) or Obsolete (need to be removed)
    • Capability Description – brief description of the process capability (text)
    • Capability Priority: (High, Medium or Low)
    • Metrics – brief description of metrics to be used to measure process performance
    • Target – quantitative measure of target performance
    • Enablers Required
    • Benefits – brief description of expected benefits derived from this process capability

Process capability requirements may be realized or specified. Realized means that the capabilities are currently available specified means that the capabilities are not currently available and must be provided. Both of these taken together will be defined as those capabilities that are required.

Key Considerations
  • The process capabilities are best defined and documented in workshop environments where the project team and the client subject matter experts can develop the documentation together.
  • In a large project where multiple teams are working on different processes it is important to verify the detail and level of the documentation for consistency.  Since multiple people will be creating and writing the files, there is always a very high potential for the information to be documented at various levels of detail.  An example of a completed template should be provided, as well as, a quality check performed after each team member has completed one or two.
  • The process capability requirements should be held and maintained centrally.  Individual project team members will be responsible for updating the work products allocated to themselves or to their team.  All team members should have display access to the full set.  This will help everyone to be informed about proposals made by other teams, especially regarding integration issues.
  • Implement change and version control of Process Capability Definition documents.
Tailoring
Impact of not having
  • Client may haphazardly identify and select a set of process capabilities related to required strategic capabilities that may in fact diminish process support due to capability incompatibility
  • It would be difficult to effectively identify, develop and implement the "right" business process capabilities
  • Process capability requirements that support multiple strategic capabilities would not be identified
  • Capability requirements that are in conflict of one another would not be understood and client expectations to recognize these facts would not be effected
Reasons for not needing
  • There is suitable information available from other sources - either previous consulting efforts or client efforts
  • The Client has other means of getting process capabilities established
