Artifact: Customer Measurements (BUS 357)
The Customer Measurements work product describes the current state of the measurements program of a client.
Domains: Business Value & Financials
Work Product Kinds: Measurement
Purpose
The main purpose of Customer Measurements is to capture the base level measurements currently being collected, analyzed, and used by a client.
Description
Main Description

The Customer Measurements work product describes: [see (1)]

  • The measurements currently being collected, analyzed, and used (what).
  • The reasons for collecting, analyzing, and using these measurements, including any metrics derived from them (why).
  • The mechanisms for collecting measurements, analyzing and using them (how and where).
  • The frequency of measurement collection, analysis, and use (when).
  • The persons/organizations responsible for collecting and analyzing them and feeding back the results to the users of the information (whom).


(1)The term “measurement” as used in this document reflects the sorts of entities that must be elicited when defining and developing the measurement program and system for an organization. Generally, the term “measurement” can refer to either a computed metric, which involves a calculation among primitive metrics, or a primitive metric that reflects a directly observable or countable event or data point. The terms are defined as follows:
  • Measurement: A logical group of metrics whose collected data has at least one dimension of observation that can be normalized and presented to provide a cross-organization view of performance. A measurement is also one of a logical collection called a measurement category and can have several computed metrics associated with it.
  • Computed Metric: An entity, generally of a calculable nature, reportable to a client, to the target organization’s management, or to both. Computed metrics are, by and large, derived from two or more primitive metrics by a mathematical calculation or algorithm.
  • Primitive Metric: Countable entities or distinct observable events that are manifest in the processes and/or behaviors occurring in the measuring organization. In any implementation, the sampling for primitive metric data should be from the closest available point of service for the process or behavior being measured.
Brief Outline

The Customer Measurements work product would be represented as text, a simple table, or a series of tables that can be produced by typical word processors such as Microsoft Word, depending on format preference.   The format is not as important as the content, which should include the following:

  • For each computed metric:
    • Computed metric name
    • Computed metric definition
    • Primitive metrics
    • Calculation
    • Frequency of data capture
    • Reporting frequency
    • Reporting process
    • Organizational owner
  • For each primitive metric associated with a computed metric or directly reported:
  • Frequency of data capture
  • Source of data
  • Mode of data capture (manual, automated)
  • Organizational level of data capture (e.g., project level, application level, business unit)

Any other useful information related to the metrics should also be captured.

Key Considerations

Use all information material provided by the client in the Request for Proposal (RFP) and other sources to glean the measurements that their organization currently collects, analyzes, and uses.

Where practical, create a measurements survey questionnaire based on the selected measurements from the measurement model and use it as a tool for capturing measurement information.

Try to determine why the client measures what they measure.  Which of their business goals, if any, do the measures support?   Why are those measurements important to them?  Answers to these questions will be particularly important during gap analysis.
Tailoring
Impact of not havingDeveloping the Customer Measurements work product is important to understanding the current state of the organization’s measurement program (if it exists). This is critical input for the measurement gap analysis between the organization’s measurement goals as embodied in the measurement model and the status quo. Without this work product, it would be difficult to establish a measurements program that would utilize as much of the existing measurement infrastructure as possible in order to minimize change and maximize acceptance.
Reasons for not needingThis work product is critical input for the measurement gap analysis. There is no reason for exclusion of this work product. If there is no measurement program or measurement capability in place, this work product simply degenerates to a formal statement indicating the absence of such a measurement program or capability.
