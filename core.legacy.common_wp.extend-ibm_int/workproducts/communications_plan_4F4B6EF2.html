Artifact: Communications Plan (ORG 110)
The Communications Plan details the actions to be taken to fulfill the communication role identified in the Transition Strategy.
Domains: Organization Change Management
Work Product Kinds: Plan
Purpose

Communications are a key success factor for any project: they get the organization connected and updated before, during, and after the change initiative. The information becomes a foundation for training and other roll-out activities, helping to ensure that these activities take place given a solid understanding of the project and its implications.   

It is important for people to receive timely, meaningful information to feel part of the change initiative. The more that people know about and understand how and why changes are happening, the more likely they are to feel committed to the change efforts. Communications are strongly linked to a stakeholder/participation management plan and are supported by change leadership activities, because executives and other members of management are often the most credible message carriers.

Description
Main Description

The Communications Plan work product is based on a communications strategy and a campaign structure. It identifies the messages needed to accompany and support the project and the media to use to bring specific information to people in the client organization as well as other stakeholders who need information (e.g., customers, suppliers). In addition to detailing the individual communication mechanisms, the Communications Plan work product has a work plan that details timing and responsibilities for preparation (including approval) and delivery.

Key Considerations

Choose the communications process owner carefully.  This person should be able to focus on execution and know about and be experienced with existing channels of communications within the organization.

Communications effectiveness can be partially determined by the level of organizational resistance to and acceptance of the project.  Appoint an individual or process team (who could be an extension of the core project team) to review the communications plan and to monitor its effectiveness.   A formal review cycle at key milestones of the project will help to ensure the execution of the communications plan, as well as maintain its harmony with project objectives, which may change.

The following Commitment Model is used extensively in this artifact.

Tailoring
Impact of not havingThe rumor mill is often one of the strongest and potentially most destructive forces in an organization planning a change. Lack of correct understanding about change initiatives can be one of the greatest producers of resistance to any change effort. If the resistance is strong enough within an organization, the change initiatives risk failure. The Communications Plan is designed to provide the right information, at the right time, to the right audience.
Reasons for not needingA detailed or extensive communications plan may not be necessary when the transformations are very minor in scope or they affect a contained area of the client organization. A contained area means relatively few people, usually fewer than 10, are involved or the area is tightly controlled and managed.
Representation OptionsThe Communications Plan is usually laid out in tables with accompanying notes. It is a matter of taste whether they are in a spreadsheet, a presentation, or a word processing document. The materials selected to convey the messages vary widely by project.
