Artifact: IT Services Assessment (OPS 306)
An IT Services Assessment defines the gaps between a client’s current IT services and a future set required for the support of business capabilities.
Domains: IT Management & Monitoring
Work Product Kinds: Assessment
Purpose

The purpose of this work product is to identify any deficiencies in the current IT service portfolio and individual services so that they may be addressed by subsequent design and transformation initiatives.

This work product provides an understanding of how well current IT services supports business capabilities by identifying IT service quality issues and their causes, and by identifying gaps that may exist in the current IT services portfolio.

The focus for this assessment technique is the services provided by IT groups, that is, the results produced for customers by the many and varied activities executed by those who work in IT departments.  This assessment looks at several attributes for each service, and will assist in uncovering gaps in the customer - provider relationship.

There many different aspects of an IT environment that might be assessed in order to gain insight into current strengths and weaknesses.  The unique value of the IT Services Assessment is that it explicitly considers the perspective of the customer, their expectations and delivery of IT services against those expectations.

A service assessment will provide an understanding that will guide other types of assessments. For example, typically one could assess a service to discover "What is good or bad" and then the underlying processes required to deliver the service to discover "Why" the services are being delivered as they are.  Such a process assessment would determine the weak links that have to be improved to fix the service or the customer perception of the service. Thus the IT Services Assessment is not intended to replace other IT process-oriented assessments, but rather to complement them.  This assessment should assist in determining the quality of IT deliverables themselves, and an IT process assessment would determine the health of the delivery vehicle.
Description
Main Description

The definition of customer/provider relationship gaps in this work product provides an understanding of how well IT services supports those business capabilities.

The focus of the assessment is on the "services" provided by IT groups. These "services" can be viewed as the customer’s perception of the many and varied activities executed by those who work in IT departments in support of the customer. The assessment looks at several attributes for each service.
Brief OutlineThe IT Services Assessment work product is collection of textual documents, charts, tables and graphics.  The specific format will vary according to the service assessment technique being used and the level of level of detail being presented.

Key Considerations

Further information on this work product and the associated techniques is available in a paper titled IT Services Assessment Techniques.

This work product should be validated against the other assessment work products specified in the engagement model being used.  In particular, it should be validated against any customer satisfaction assessment or IT process assessment work products.

Tailoring
Impact of not havingWithout this work product, it may not be possible to address issues with existing IT services or potential problems with IT Support requirements for the future environment. There is also an increased risk that the future design may not adequately build on or integrate with the current information technology support services or that there may be unnecessary duplication of function.
Reasons for not needingThis work product may not be necessary in situations where no new information technology is introduced in support of the functions within scope. In addition, this work product may not be necessary in narrowly focused engagements where the assessment of the current IT environment showed no serious issues involving IT Services.
