Artifact: Application Management Plan (ENG 408)
The Application Management Plan is the main reference for the team delivering the application management services.
Domains: Project Management
Work Product Kinds: Plan
Purpose
The primary objective of the Application Management Plan is to establish a common understanding of expected activities and thus ensure that client expectations are managed and satisfied while meeting the cost and schedule targets.
 
Maintaining a current and quality Application Management Plan is indispensable for managing application management services, particularly with cross-organizational delivery teams. It is also of great value when introducing new members of the delivery team, when analyzing client complaints, or when winding up and terminating the contracts. The Application Management Plan can also be the basis for Quality Assurance reviews as well as ISO9001 audits or CMM assessments.
Description
Main DescriptionOne of the most important documents produced during the Transition Phase of a contract for Application Management Services is an Application Management Plan that is based on the contract and IBM procedures and describes how maintenance and enhancement activities are to be performed and managed.
 
The Application Management Plan contains concrete descriptions of the activities required by all involved organizations for delivery of the contracted services and for maintaining the promised service levels. It further includes a description of roles and responsibilities, infrastructure, and the necessary work-instructions, methods, and tools (preferably by referring to generic procedures and methods) for performing the required activities.
Brief Outline

The Application Management Plan must cover the following:

  • Application Management Plan review and approval information
  • Deviations from or additions to the IBM procedures
  • Scope of maintenance (copied from the contract)
  • Clear definitions of what applications and areas are within the scope of the contract and what is outside the scope of the contract
  • Maintenance work-types that are included (e.g., corrective maintenance, perfective maintenance, enhancement, new development, and application helpdesk)
  • Master Plan comprising Milestones including a release schedule, resources and skills required for maintenance activities, assumptions and client responsibilities
  • Initial status of the applications (e.g., reliability and documentation status)
  • Identification of client’s responsibilities with clear interfaces to what IBM is responsible for
  • Reviews and approvals (copied from the contract) identifying what needs review and approval of IBM and the client:
    • Review and approval requirements for Application Management Plan and maintenance activities
    • Pre-authorization” or generic approvals, if any, for maintenance activities
  • Organization, responsibilities, and authorities identifying who has what responsibility and authority:
    • Steering committees or similar management organization (copied from the contract)
    • Roles and responsibilities of involved IBM teams including any delegations from Project Executive or Delivery Project Executive/ Project Manager for reviews, approvals, or assignment of activities
    • Client responsibilities (copied from the contract) identifying client representatives who are authorized for review and approvals (e.g., review and approval of plans, Service Requests, estimates, and acceptance criteria)
  • Meetings
    • Formal meetings with the client (copied from the contract) identifying the frequency, participants, and minutes distribution
    • Formal internal meetings identifying the frequency, participants, and minutes distribution
    • Informal internal and client meeting
  • Service Level Agreements (copied from the contract and/or from other agreements with the client).
  • Reports
    • Maintenance Status Reports to the client (copied from the contract), identifying contents, reviews before distribution, timing, and distribution
    • Internal reports defining what measurements are made and reported at what frequencies
  • Problems and Service Requests
    • Problem Incident Report and Service Request forms with status and severity definitions
    • Requirements on monitoring and acting on problem log and Service Request log
    • Requirements on how often Service Requests must be prioritized and by whom
    • Planning activities including description of the Master Plan, which is a work-product of its own.
  • Infrastructure and Configuration Management describing development, test, and production environments and the library structure, tools, and routines used for controlling configuration items in those environments. These are usually documented in a Configuration Management Plan.
  • Use of subcontractors (copied from the contract)
  • Security regulations and guidelines to be followed (copied from the contract)
  • Risks with impacts and mitigation actions
  • Other relevant instructions (e.g., time reporting)

Key Considerations
  • Keep the Application Management Plan simple. It costs to create and maintain it and it costs even more to comply with it!
  • Be realistic! Describe the activities and work-products such that the delivery team can live up to the expectations.
  • Include only services that are contracted and paid for.
  • Do not rewrite the contract! Copy from it.
  • Do not rewrite the procedures and methods! Refer to them.
  • The Application Management Plan is revised, reviewed, and re-approved at least once a year to ensure that it remains current. Particularly, those parts that include interfaces to or describe services that must be provided by other organizations must be formally verified by the related organization before submission to the client.
Tailoring
Impact of not havingWithout an Application Management Plan the scope of the services, responsibilities, and expected activities will not be clearly specified - neither for the client, nor for the delivery team. Consequently, a legal contract, normally with ambiguous and high-level descriptions, will be the main reference for delivery of the services. This situation can result in ad hoc, ineffective, and uncoordinated activities with different interpretations of the committed services where risk for customer dissatisfaction and cost overrun would be very high.
Reasons for not needingIf the engagement is not an ongoing maintenance contract then this work product may not be necessary.
Representation OptionsThe Application Management Plan may either be a hard-copy document or be implemented on-line. A Lotus Notes Team Room implementation is recommended for a much higher usability, better accessibility, and simpler data control. Other relevant work-products and data (e.g., Master Plan, reports, and minutes of meetings) may also be gathered in the same Teamroom.
