Artifact: Financial Assessment (BUS 302)
The Financial Assessment work product identifies, documents and quantifies the metrics, trends and relationships that characterize the financial condition of the client’s business at the time the Transformation project is commenced.
Domains: Business Value & Financials
Work Product Kinds: Assessment
Purpose

The purpose of the Financial Assessment is to:

  • Demonstrate and validate the consultants’ understanding of the client’s business
  • Establish a common base of concerns and priorities between the consultants and clients
  • Identify high-level business metrics that will be used to assess the success or failure of business transformation projects
  • Identify hypothetical problem areas and opportunities for improvement

The Financial Assessment also identifies "as is" assumptions used in the Business Case to calculate net costs and benefits

Description
Main Description

The scope of the Financial Assessment addresses:

  • Calculating generic, as well as industry-specific business metrics (e. g., ratios) for each relevant line of business;
  • Assessing historical trends and projections for key business metrics,
  • Identifying areas of concern indicated by current or projected metrics,
  • Formulating preliminary hypotheses concerning problems and solutions, and
  • Documenting data sources and assumptions used in calculating the key business metrics, trends.

The Financial Assessment process is undertaken during the early stages of the Transformation project (ideally, during the proposal phase.) The projected impact of proposed transformation initiatives on business metrics identified at this stage should be explicitly reflected in the Business Case work product.

Key Considerations

The Financial Assessment work product should be produced in the proposal phase to the extent possible.  Doing so will enable you to use it in working with the client to establish base-line assumptions.  It will also provide valuable input to creating the Financial Benchmarks and Financial Work Plan & Model work products.

Tailoring
Impact of not having

Without the Financial Assessment work product there is:

  • No quantitative mechanism to validate the consultants’ understanding of the client’s business
  • No quantitative mechanism to establish a common base of concerns and priorities between the consultants and clients
  • No quantitative mechanism to objectively identify high-level business metrics that will be used to assess the success or failure of business transformation projects
  • No "objective" quantitative mechanism to identify hypothetical problem areas and opportunities for improvement
Reasons for not needingIf the client has hired IBM to "execute" a business transformation effort with predefined tactical priorities and metrics, it is not necessary to produce the Financial Assessment work product. This work product is only necessary where the client needs to generate a quantitative Business Case to justify a proposed effort or to quantify the benefits that an effort actually achieves.
