Artifact: Preliminary Cost-Benefit Estimation (BUS 017)
The Preliminary Cost Benefit Estimation work product is a document that describes the financial justification for considering the implementation of the project.
Domains: Business Value & Financials
Work Product Kinds: Assessment
Purpose
This work product provides information on the economic improvements that might be realized should the project be implemented.
Relationships
Parent Deliverables
  • Case for Change Deliverable
  • DesignFlow Phase Report
Description
Main DescriptionThe "preliminary" nature of the Preliminary Cost Benefit Estimation document is an indication of the level of detail and perhaps the method used to compute the justification. The document has enough detail to provide information to project sponsors, but does not have enough detail to determine specific benefits (personnel reductions of a certain type, inventory reductions in a certain product line, etc.).
Brief Outline

The artifact should contain summary and detailed sections.

The summary section attempts to put on one page (or at least very few pages) the essence of the economics, and is supported by the detailed section.

The detailed section provides the lowest level of information that the project team has gathered. Each major point below might be a spreadsheet of its own.


Benefits

Cross references to the sources of information

Costs

Annual, or incremental, costs; calculations supporting the same

Profit/Cash Impact

Key Considerations

If there is the need for a new software package, then this work product can be produced either before or after the package has been selected.  Obviously, if the package has been selected, the cost side of the equation may be better understood.  However, even if it has not, the rough cost can be obtained from expert resources or market level data.

The benefit side of the equation is primarily driven by the opportunities that exist in the company.  The package cannot typically create the benefit opportunity.  The package can, however, influence the people who will need to sponsor the benefits by illustrating the potential benefits in a very concrete form.

Inclusion of client financial staff can help to ensure that the analysis is reasonable, and prevent challenges to its accuracy.  Inclusions of the vendors in the process (by soliciting quotations) can help to ensure that final costs for those elements will not be in conflict with this work product.
Tailoring
Impact of not havingWithout this work product the economic rationale for investing in the project will not be understood by management.
Reasons for not needing

This work product is not needed if:

  • The project has already been funded
  • The project is being driven by technical considerations
  • The project is being driven by strategic considerations, such as to obtain a business capability fundamental to compete
