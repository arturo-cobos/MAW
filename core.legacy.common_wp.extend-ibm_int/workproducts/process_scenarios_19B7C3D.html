Artifact: Process Scenarios (BUS 405)
The Process Scenarios work product defines how business operations should occur for a client needs to satisfy with its future business processes.
Domains: Business Process
Work Product Kinds: Specification
Purpose

The Process Scenarios work product is produced to:

  • Describe the business operations desired to achieve desired capabilities and value capture outlined by the client
  • Provide a narrative description of how the business operations should occur using future business processes
  • Illustrate desired interaction and outcomes when performing future business processes
  • Document the prioritized business requirements for the future organization (organization structure and job roles/skills) to applied desired Business Process Enablers
  • Link roles to the business situations for understanding and acceptance of future business processes and business process enablers
  • Provide a framework for Business Process Education and User Scenarios which link the future business processes to the package or technology selected for the business solution
  • Define what is needed in terms of organization and technology in order to enable the future process definition and future process/application design
  • Agree on the priorities of the business processes and requirements
  • Facilitate evaluation of these elements during the selection or evaluation of a solution
  • Enable issues to be resolved early and risks to be mitigated
  • Provide awareness of assumptions that have been made

Customer Scenarios are used to help explore opportunities associated to the future business processes, validate their use and assess impacts to the business upon adoption of those new processes.

Customer Scenarios help the design team understand the needs of their client and the functions that will meet those needs.

Description
Main Description

The Process Scenarios describes a sequence of events, triggers, actors, inputs/outputs, and business rules that govern segments or paths of a process associated to each area of business operations.

The work product applies to business processes within an enterprise and outside an enterprise, such as processes performed with business partners. It also applies to IT processes as well as IT support processes related to things like systems management and help desk.

Process Scenarios enhance client understanding of the future business solution by relating processes to daily business activities. They act as a means of positively communicating business process enablers as well as set expectations as to the limitations of the package or technology to be used.

The design team should have a clear understanding of the scope of the engagement, the business strategy and desired capabilities. The project team should also have a common high-level understanding of the major process changes to be implemented.

Process Scenarios help the design team understand the needs of their client and the functions that will meet those needs.

Brief Outline

Process Scenarios are generally depicted both graphically and textually.  The textual aspects include outlines, story ideas, potential impact areas and trigger events.  Tables or statements are usually used to depict future state, logical sequences of events, impacts to the business based on issues identified by the engagement), as well as known gaps in the business solution that will need to be addressed in order to satisfy each key scenario.  The graphical representations may be conceptual, showing pictures of the business process and roles as well as utilizing the process definition.

Process Scenarios typically have the following sections:

  1. Business Profile: A description of the business situation and what value it provides.  A description of the customer, location, preferences, and any other details that are appropriate to the scenario.  It should represent a unique path of business operations.
  2. Background: the business situation driving the scenario.  This section contains information about how the customer will utilize the business process and the interactions required.
  3. Narrative (in the picture mentioned ‘Scenario Description’): what happens.  A story is told about what the customer experiences and does.  Included are decisions that the user makes and the consequences.  Each scenario describes the interaction at the action level and contains, per action, the Current description, the Future descriptions, the gap in capabilities and the process enabler that may be employed to fill that gap.  The Process Scenario identifies the frequency of the work step/task, changes or improvements and geographic location for the action.
  4. In the picture: Expected Outcome
  5. Illustrations: enriching the communication.  Sketches of the customer’s experience are often used to supplement the text. 
  6. Risks & Assumptions: The risks and assumptions are typically documented in lists or tables, which may include supporting information (e.g., for risks the potential impacts and actions to mitigate). 
  7. Process Enablers:  identification of which enablers can be leveraged to support the customer scenarios.
  8. Rules/Exceptions: describes business rules, conditions and potential variations that may impact the customer scenario.

Key Considerations
  • Process Scenarios provide the bridge from the process to technology, as they look at the practical application of Business Process Enablers to specific customer situations. 
  • Process Scenarios developed in this work product will be at a high level and for example will not include definition of data elements. 
  • In large projects where multiple teams are working on different processes it is important to verify the detail and level of the documentation for consistency.  Since multiple people will be creating and writing the files, there is always a very high potential for the information to be documented at various levels of detail.  An example of a completed template should be provided, as well as, a quality check performed after each team member has completed one or two.
  • Process Scenarios should be held and maintained centrally.  Individual project team members will be responsible for updating them.  All team members should have display access to the full set.  This will help everyone to be informed about proposals made by other teams, especially regarding process and role changes.

Extensive preparation before the Scenario Development Workshop is necessary to create the necessary tools and environment.  Education at both the executive and working team levels is critical to successful scenario development as it will make them more knowledgeable and productive throughout the process, and will help set realistic expectations.

In instances where new scenario development is not feasible or desired, consideration should be given to using previously developed scenarios (by industry, industry segment, business area, etc.) as available.

Tailoring
Impact of not having

If this work product is not produced, then:

  • The process design may not fully satisfy the client’s needs
  • The design team may not have a sufficient understanding of what business situations must be satisfied by the solution, which could result in rework, project delays and client satisfaction issues
  • Business Process Enablers may not be fully utilized, or expectations of those enablers may be too high
  • Future strategic decisions (positioning and plays) may be flawed, or have a higher degree of risk
  • It may be difficult to associate business value and priority to the process requirements and potential enablers
  • The issues might not be resolved early, and might become risks
Reasons for not needing

This work product will not be required when:

  • The business solution has limited impact to the current business processes and a clear understanding of the package or technology exists
