Work Product Kinds: Measurement
Purpose
The purpose of the Benefits Control Book is to associate benefits detailed in the Business Case with functional processes. This work product:
  • Provides a tracking mechanism that will assist the client analyze benefits achieved
  • Documents justification of the project by identifying short and long term benefits
  • Measures success of the project
Relationships

Description
Main DescriptionThe Benefits Control Book provides a detailed benefit analysis that is summarized in the Business Case. This is produced after the final selection of the software vendor or software product.
Brief Outline

This artifact includes the following information:

  • Header information including the entity, currency type, process, process owner and sub-process.
  • Best practice information including the best practice name, description, enablers, constraints and the benefit owner.  The benefit owner is the person who identified the benefit associated with the process.
  • Metric details including the metric, calculation method and measures.
  • Yearly breakdown of the benefits.
  • Summary section containing detail notes, the process status and a total benefit figure.

Key Considerations
The importance of the information contained in the Benefits Control Book will need to be communicated to the client on an on-going basis.  There may be some hesitation to commit to specific benefit figures due to the accountability associated with the numbers.
Tailoring
Impact of not havingThe benefits associated with implementation of the particular software package or product cannot be accurately documented without the use of the Benefits Control Book. In order to track tangible benefits in the future, current and target measurements must be identified and documented. Without this documentation, the client may not remain focused on achieving goals throughout the life of the project and in future periods.
Reasons for not needingIt is highly recommended that all engagements produce a Benefits Control Book. However, if the client refuses to quantify benefits associated with process improvements, a Benefits Control Book would not be produced.
Representation Options

The Benefits Control Book is a document or spreadsheet that should be created to capture the information.
