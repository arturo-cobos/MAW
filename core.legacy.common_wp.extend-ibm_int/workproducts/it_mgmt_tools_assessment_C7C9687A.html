Artifact: IT Management Tools Assessment (OPS 304)
The IT Management Tools Assessment work product assesses the degree to which the current IT or systems management tools satisfy current and future IT service requirements.
Domains: IT Management & Monitoring
Work Product Kinds: Assessment
Purpose
The purpose of this work product is to identify the strengths and weaknesses of the current IT management tools. It assists the design of the "to be" IT management environment by identifying those areas where deficiencies currently exist, where enhancements could be made, or where there are opportunities to better exploit the current management tools. This work product helps communicate to the client the additional technology that must be acquired in order to provide a complete solution for the desired capability. It also positions the client for the subsequent tool selection activities.
Description
Main Description

The IT Management Tools Assessment work product identifies existing gaps in tools being used in the delivery of services by comparing the generic tool profiles (or tool requirements) with the current tools being used by the client. This work product includes:

  • A mapping of current tools to required tools.
  • Identification of those requirements which are not met by existing tools.
  • Documentation of the generic tools that must be acquired to fill the gaps.

Optionally, this work product could also include some preliminary analysis of potential vendor tools that could fill the gaps.

This assessment is generally focused on two complementary views of functional and technical quality. The first "view" is concerned with the extent to which the tools support the critical IT management and the quality of that support, while the second is concerned with conformance to the future management model and agreed upon guiding principles. This includes assessment of the coverage provided by the current tools and the degree to which their functional capabilities have been implemented and exploited. The technical quality assessment is concerned with the technical efficiency of the current IT management tools, (e.g. their age, stability, reliability, maintainability) and their longer term technical potential.

The specific criteria used to frame the IT management tools assessment will depend on the aspects being assessed. The criteria may include:

  • Functionality
  • Integration
  • Level of automation
  • Usability
  • Reporting
  • Completeness and fit of data
  • Communication
  • Customizability
  • Openness
  • Performance
  • Cost
  • Vendor support

Typically these criteria will be assessed from each of three different perspectives:

  • Capability - How completely the specific tool can meet the requirements of the client for a particular IT management process
  • Implementation - How completely the tool is currently implemented at the client?
  • Requirement - The level of implementation of the tool that the client requires for this IT management process.

Analysis of the IT management tools from each of these perspectives will identify gaps in the functionality of the current tools and in the way they have been implemented.

Brief Outline

This work product will generally have several components. These will usually include: 

  • An inventory of the current IT management tools. This will typically be a simple list of the current management tools. 
  • A mapping of the current tools to the management processes and managed elements they support. This will generally be in the form of a matrix. The matrix will have management processes along one axis and managed elements along the other axis (as does a Systems Management Framework Design matrix). Within each cell will be the name of the tool (or tools) used to perform that management process for that managed element. 
  • An assessment of the current implemented and required level of capability for each tool area. This will usually consist of one or more completed tool assessment score sheets. 
  • An analysis of the gaps, strengths and weaknesses of the current management tools portfolio and recommendations for improvement. These will usually be a text report, sometimes supported by other analytical tools used to assist in prioritizing the recommended actions.

Key Considerations

Creation of this work product will generally require some degree of technical expertise or familiarity with the tool suite currently being used by the client.  It is advantageous if the team member responsible for this activity has this expertise so that they are able to adequately explore and recognize how well the tool has been implemented and the degree to which its true capability is being exploited.

The matrix framework used to analyze the current tool coverage is based on techniques used within the Systems Management Framework Design (SMFD) method.  Therefore it is also advantageous if the engagement team is familiar with this method and the construction of the SMFD matrix.  If structured appropriately, this IT Management Tools Assessment can also provide a strong linkage to the subsequent design activities based on SMFD.
Tailoring
Impact of not having

Management tools are an essential component of an IT management system. They support the automated execution of many management tasks that would be impossible or prohibitively expensive if performed manually. They are one of the key enablers of efficient and consistent IT services delivery.   
Without this work product, it may not be possible to effectively address issues with the current IT management tools or identify enhancement requirements during the design of the "to be" environment.

Without an adequate understanding of the current tool suite and the tool requirements it will be difficult to ensure that the future design will support the clientâ€™s requirements. There is also an increased risk that the "to be" design may not adequately build on or integrate with the current management tools suite or that there may be unnecessary duplication of function.

Reasons for not needing

This work product may not be necessary in situations where there are no tools being used to support the management functions within scope. This may be the case for a new venture or for an existing function that is being performed completely manually. Alternatively, if the client has determined that none of their current management tools will be retained in he future environment then it will not be necessary to assess them.   

Additionally, this work product may not be necessary in more narrowly focused engagements where the analysis revolves primarily around other aspects of the IT environment (such as process or organization) or other assessment work products.
