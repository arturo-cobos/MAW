Artifact: Vendor Visit and Demonstration Schedules (BUS 073)
The Vendor Visit and Demonstration Schedules contains the specified dates, time, and agendas for two very important schedules that should be created.
Domains: Selection
Work Product Kinds: Plan
Purpose

The Vendor Visit and Demonstration Schedules promote an orderly vendor visit with a high-level question and answer session. By setting a schedule, both the vendor and the client can provide the appropriate personnel at the site visit.

This work product description also enables the vendors to demonstrate their product, in an order defined by the client, to ensure that the client’s business requirements are covered. The timed agenda within the Demonstration Schedule is important to pace the vendors during the demonstration to ensure that all areas being scored are viewed.

Description
Main DescriptionThe first, Vendor Visit Schedule details the specified date, time, and agenda for each of the short list vendors to visit the client and/or to conduct a site visit with the client, where applicable. The purpose of the meeting is to allow the vendors an opportunity to discuss the demonstration script requirements in detail with the client. The schedule also identifies the client resources that will be participating.
 
The second schedule within this work product description, the Demonstration Schedule, contains the dates and a timed agenda for each functional area being demonstrated by the vendors. The schedule outlines the script sequence and the client audience for the vendor software demonstrations.
Brief Outline

The Vendor Visit Schedule should be listed in a document containing the following information:

  • Overview Detailing the Visit Agenda
  • Vendor Names
  • Dates
  • Times
  • Attendees

The Demonstration Schedule should be in a document containing the following information:

  • Overview
  • Dates
  • Subject Matter
  • Time Scheduled
  • “Users” (Client team members that will be scoring the individual component of the script)

The subject matter and allotted times should be adjusted to fit the client's business requirements.  Also, additional days may be necessary to cover the demonstration script.

Key Considerations

Each vendor should have an equal amount of time during the site visit.  Site visits should be scheduled at least one week after distributing the RFI/RFS and demonstration script to the vendor.

The Demonstration Schedule for each vendor should be exactly the same.

The Vendor Visit and Demonstration Schedules should be verified with the client management prior to release to the vendor.
Tailoring
Impact of not havingThe appropriate people may not be present if a Vendor Visit Schedule is not set. Also, unwanted vendor visits could occur as a result of not formally planning vendor visits. In addition, without a Demonstration Schedule, all of the client’s business requirements and/or all of the application’s functions may not be covered.
Reasons for not needingThe Vendor Visit and Demonstration Schedules are not necessary when there will be neither site visits or vendor demonstrations.
