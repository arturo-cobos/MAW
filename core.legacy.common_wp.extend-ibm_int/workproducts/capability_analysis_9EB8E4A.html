Artifact: Capability Analysis (BUS 345)
The Capability Analysis work product documents the results of data collection related to understanding a client’s current capabilities, its identified current capability enablers and, where applicable, its desired future state capabilities and enablers.
Domains: Business Strategy
Work Product Kinds: Assessment
Purpose

This work product is produced for one or more of the following reasons:

  • Identify baseline performance metrics for future measurement and assessment of capability performance improvement
  • Provide the base data to start to qualify / quantify the potential benefits of capability improvement
  • Understand the costs of the current capability enablers supporting a given capability
  • Identify the root causes of the current capability problem areas that need to be resolved by the new capability design
  • Provide the base data to enable the generation of improvement ideas
  • Identify "quick wins" and prioritize the other improvement ideas
  • Examine potential opportunity areas in more detail to confirm whether this is really an opportunity that should be pursued
  • Do a preliminary qualification of benefits / costs (high, medium, low) as an indicator for prioritization
Description
Main Description

Capabilities represent those critical things that a business or organization must be capable of doing well to insure the delivery of the ideal customer-defined high value outcomes called "value propositions".

To avoid wasting time this work product should only be produced for those areas within the engagement scope that have been identified as problem / opportunity areas requiring further investigation based on one or more of the following reasons:

  • there is a problem with a capability or it’s enablers’ performance as identified by the assessment
  • there is a lack of effective management of a capability or it’s enablers as identified by the assessment
  • there is a need to invest in capability improvement (critical to business growth and competitive advantage) which may include application / package adaptation to support the critical processes
  • there is a potential opportunity to harmonize capabilities across multiple divisions, geographic locations, etc.
  • there is a potential opportunity to move work supporting capabilities to / from customers, suppliers, business partners, etc.
  • there is a potential opportunity to automate the capabilities with enabling technologies

This work product is a collection point for any data that supports the current state of a client’s capabilities. The data may be documented in a number of ways: as a set of survey results, interview results, as a result of application or technical monitoring, or a log of maintenance requests.

Brief Outline

This work product should produce a presentation and/or report, comprised of both text and graphics, which could contain any or all of the following contents:

In the case of survey results:

  • A compilation of each respondent’s answer for each survey question
  • An average of the respondent’s scores for each question reflecting their measurement of current state capabilities
  • An average of the respondent’s scores for each question reflecting their measurement of desired future state capabilities

In the case of interview results:

  • A logical categorization of statements which clearly demonstrate the major issues requiring attention

In the case of application or technical environment monitoring data (for example):

  • A categorization of maintenance and enhancement requests by sub-application, or a graph of transaction durations by transaction type

Key Considerations

This Work Product can produce high value to the client and the engagement team.  The keys to success are:

  1. Conduct the survey early in the engagement (as part of the kick-off or immediately following the kick-off).  Use the information discovered to focus the assessment.
  2. If key individuals were unavailable or could not participate, it may be wise to schedule a meeting with the individual(s) to complete the survey
  3. Ensure that there is sufficient representation across the client organization.
  4. Determine in advance to whom the information will be distributed, under whose sponsorship, and include the relevant disclaimers.
  5. Review the proposed structure of the work product in advance with the project sponsor.

It is also very important to share the results of the data collection with the IBM project team so everyone understands what has been obtained.
Tailoring
Impact of not having

If this work product is not produced, then:

  • Individual capabilities will not have a known baseline performance for future measurement of performance improvement therefore it will be virtually impossible to directly quantify any cost reduction, cycle time improvement, or defect reduction achieved by the redesigned future capability
  • The capability redesign will address problem symptoms rather than the problem causes (e.g. fix billing whereas the root cause is poor data in order entry)
  • It will not be possible to estimate the degree to which the package has to be adapted to support the critical processes
  • It will not be possible to separate "potential" opportunities from "real" opportunities
  • It will not be possible to identify "quick" wins and prioritize other improvement ideas
Reasons for not needingThere are very few instances when this work product is not required, except perhaps when a decision has been taken to install new technology, packages, or applications without needing to measure the resulting improvement in performance or to understand the causes of problems experienced with the current capability.
