Artifact: Capability Enablers (BUS 317)
The Capability Enablers work product documents the physical things that must be present in the future design to enable the capabilities identified to deliver on the value proposition or customer value.
Domains: Business Strategy
Work Product Kinds: Specification
Purpose

This work product is produced in order to:

  • Identify and prioritize the enablers based on the critical capabilities necessary to deliver the targeted customers’ needs and wants.
  • Evaluate the various future design options at a high level, i.e. linking to the business strategy and customer wants and needs before getting into the details of design.
  • Agree on and obtain buy-in to the business results that are required before getting into the details of design.
  • Provide a checklist to test whether the design meets the required enablers and targets.
Description
Main Description

The capability enablers supporting one or more capabilities are typically comprised of process, organization/people, skills/resources, policies/practices, information and information technology, management/measurement systems, etc.

This work product documents the impact each enabler has on the capabilities. It also links the enablers to the processses / sub-processes and defines the design points in terms of future targets e.g. time, cost, quality, number of service encounters, etc.

This work product applies within an enterprise and across supply chains. It also applies to the business and to IT (in this context IT refers to the business area).

Key Considerations

It is advisable to use a trained Customer Value Management (CVM) practitioner to do or mentor the development of this work product. 

The approach typically generates more data than can be managed manually.  CVM trained consultants have an automated tool designed for this function.

It is imperative that the key managers and executives from all impacted divisions agree on the enablers and their relative priorities. 

  • If key individuals are not able to participate, it may be wise to schedule either one-on-one meetings or a group status update meeting where all of the decisions and results are reviewed by the key individuals.
  • Make sure that the Capability Enablers have been understood and agreed with the senior managers and executives before proceeding to the design sessions. 

Selecting an appropriate style of notation is very important in communicating at senior levels.
Tailoring
Impact of not having

If this work product is not produced, then:

  • The client may know which customer needs / wants should be focused upon, but will lack identification of specific actions to take.
  • Important business decisions may not be addressed by executive management.
  • Functional managers may not buy-in to important decisions regarding the business.
  • Staff may be less willing to accept the new processes, organization and technology.
  • Transition/change management will be harder.
  • The staff who do the detailed work on processes will make most of the significant decisions regarding process, organization and technology. They may not consider all the options. These may not be the right decisions regarding the business strategy and customer wants/needs.
  • The design may not meet the required business targets of time, cost, quality, etc.
Reasons for not needing

This work product will not be required when:

  • The current enablers/targets continue to apply to the future and have been documented. For example, this may be when:
    • The project is small
    • The client does a "one-to-one/replace" of existing current processes
    • The client wants to implement the process design that is dictated by the application / solution
Representation Options

This work product must be documented in a format that will ‘bring to life’ the vision for the future state.  A combination of graphics and text, that fit the style of the client executives, should be selected (using any tool that the client is comfortable with).  The documentation must provide enough information to act as the "design points" for the future design.  The linkage of enablers to processes / sub-processes as well as the targets e.g. time, cost, quality etc. should be documented in tables.
