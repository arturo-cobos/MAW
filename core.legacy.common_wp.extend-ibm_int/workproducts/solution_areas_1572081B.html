Artifact: Solution Areas (BUS 330)
The Solution Areas work product documents tangible project initiatives from a set of desired and documented capabilities.
Domains: Business Strategy
Work Product Kinds: Model
Purpose

The main purpose of a Solution Areas Work Product is to:

  • Document a recommended and prioritized set of project initiatives based on a customer’s declared capability model.
  • Verify and confirm how each initiative supports the set of capabilities.
  • Verify and confirm that each initiative as scoped represents a well-defined project initiative with clear performance indicators.
  • Document the major objective of each initiative and its implied subject area data dependency as well as organizational alignment.
  • Communicate to the customer the industry-specific as well as customer-specific benefits and risks associated with each project initiative.
  • Develop a document around a set of development initiatives that represents the consensus built by customer stakeholders.
  • Provide input into a detailed release plan and project estimates for the highest priority and recommended project initiative.

This work product also represents a hand-off point between various IBM competency groups. For example, it is hoped and anticipated that an Industry Solution Unit (ISU) or Customer Relationship Management (CRM) practice might work with a customer to develop their core value propositions, capability models, and capability enablers. At some point, however, in the identification of capability enablers, there may be a realization that one or more of the enablers suggests a Business Intelligence (BI) (or Electronic Document Management (EDM) or E-business) solution. If the Solution Areas work product is within the scope of the consulting engagement, it is appropriate to engage a qualified competency SME (i.e. BI practitioner, or EDM or E-business SME) to join the team. If not, the Solution Areas work product represents the starting point for a consulting engagement in which a BI (or EDM or E-business) competent practitioner would document the project focus areas specific to enabling the BI (or EDM or E-business) value proposition.

Description
Main Description

The techniques used to gather the necessary information to prioritize solution areas are dependent on a high degree of interaction, participation and involvement by the business stakeholders of the capability. A critical by-product of this work product is the client consensus built around the final, prioritized list of solution areas.

The Solution Areas work product develops the customer’s capability model and capability enablers into a list of possible development projects. Each of the potential solution areas will, by definition, support the customer’s value propositions developed in the capability model. For each proposed development project, the Solution Areas work product documents the affected organizational units in the customer’s current organizational structure, the key subject area data within scope, and the customer’s primary performance indicators used to measure success. In addition, industry-specific, tangible and intangible business benefits and risks as well as customer-specific benefits and risks are used to prioritize the solution areas. A high-level development approach is outlined for the highest priority solution area.

This work product is generally applicable in response to a customer’s query, "How do I get started enabling this capability (i.e. Customer Relationship Management, Business Intelligence, e-business)?" It is critical to those consulting engagements in which the outcome is a list of possible projects. This work product is not required when decisions and budgetary commitments have already been made to undertake a particular project such as the replacement of a payroll system.

Key Considerations

The development of the Solution Areas work product depends greatly on two key skills, 1) Industry/functional subject matter expertise and 2) facilitation experience. For example, a customer whose primary product is women’s apparel, whose main distribution method is through catalog sales, and whose business intelligence interest is centered around controlling costs and increasing the average purchase quantity and total dollar amount per order would need an industry subject matter expert on the project team who is familiar with the main drivers of cost control and customer buying habits in this environment. Facilitation skills are important because it is a primary technique used to engage the customer in articulating their information needs and the decisions the desired information needs to support.

A significant and essential by-product of the development of this work product is the consensus built around the results.By inviting input from all stakeholders with an interest in the enablement of a defined capability, and by leading them all through a structured and impartial process for the rational prioritization of identified solution areas, the consultant achieves the ‘buy-in’ based on a business case analysis that justifies the subsequent expenditure of funds, resources and time that each solution area represents.

Depending on the level of detail available as input into the activities to develop this work product, the project team may have to iterate several times through steps 3 and 4 of the development approach outlined above.  

Because the results of this work product are a set of possible development areas, no one solution area will have been developed to sufficiently detail that a subsequent analysis and design effort is not needed.

  • Verify that the client understands the purpose of the Solution Areas work product – what it represents, how it was developed and its importance in guiding the subsequent development work.
  • Validate, as early in the development cycle of the work product as possible, the scope of this work product, especially as it relates to the identified stakeholder groups whose input will form the basis of the results.
  • Validate that clent stakeholder groups and client executive management agree to the results documented in the work product and can articulate the value of the process used to develop the results.
  • Verify that the Solution Areas work product is perceived as a 'living' document to be referred to at major review points or after major organizational changes for the continued validity of its results.
  • Verify that the client understands what a Solution Areas work product is and the risk inherent in not developing this work product.
  • Verify that the client understands the key skills required to develop this work product in the event that they wish to assume ownership of its maintenance in the future
  • Verify the client understand the implications of each Solution Area outlined in the work product and that client is in fact committed to proceeding with the various steps and activities to attain the strategic objective.
Tailoring
Impact of not having

This work product is at the heart of projects in which a customer wants to turn their declared set of capabilities into actionable project initiatives. It is critical to those consulting engagements in which the outcome is a list of possible projects. This work product is not required when decisions and budgetary commitments have already been made to undertake a particular project. Should this work product not be prepared, then any combination of the following may occur:

  • The customer will not understand or remember how current project initiatives are linked to their stated value propositions or capability models. Project initiatives, which are developed, may ‘solve the wrong problem’
  • The customer embarks on project initiatives without benefit of a rigorous business case analysis
  • Members of the customer community whose capabilities are addressed in lower priority initiatives may not understand and support the priority ranking and choose to initiate actions which ultimately jeopardize the subsequent completion of any of the projects
  • The project team begins an Analysis and Design effort in which the scope and vision of the project have not been established. This situation jeopardizes both requirements definition as well as project estimation activities
  • There is no communication document to refer to in the event of turnover in customer management or the project team
Reasons for not needing

Under certain project circumstances, this work product may not be needed, or it may need to be prepared in an abbreviated form. These circumstances are presented below.

A Solution Areas work product may not be needed in circumstances where:

  • The client organization or another vendor or IBM consulting organization has formulated this information and can provide it in documented form
  • Client management has mandated that a particular solution area is addressed, the scope of this solution area is documented and is sufficiently bounded that the project team agrees that release plans and project estimates can be developed

This work product should not be optional when documenting and developing a candidate list of project initiatives.

Representation Options

This work product is typically developed as a text-based document. It is sometimes also accompanied by a presentation-style summary document.

The Solution Areas work product is generally packaged into a major consulting engagement deliverable by combining its content with the results of other work products, which are input to this work product.
