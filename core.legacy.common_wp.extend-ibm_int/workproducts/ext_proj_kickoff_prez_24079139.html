Artifact: External Project Kickoff Presentation (ENG 001)
The External Project Kickoff Presentation is a series of slides giving an overview of the project background, scope, goals and objectives.
Domains: Project Management
Work Product Kinds: Record
Purpose
The External Project Kickoff Presentation is used to educate the project team on the engagement, its strategy and goals for the project. It provides a project overview to the client describing the engagement approach, high-level timeline, deliverables, roles and responsibilities, and other activities to set the proper direction for all team members.
Description
Main Description

It describes the project approach, roles, responsibilities and timeline and is the vehicle used to present the engagement to the Client’s core team. The Client’s project manager is generally encouraged to give the presentation from the Client’s point of view, in order to start the buy-in process for the core team. This WPD is prepared jointly between the IBM and Client project managers.

The objectives of the External Project Kickoff Presentation are to:

  • Educate the client members about the project methodology and the project approach
  • Overview the client’s environment, problems and goals
  • Achieve consensus on the project’s vision, goals and objectives
  • Begin project "team building"
  • Achieve consensus on the project scope, roles and responsibilities
  • Identify individuals who fulfill those roles and responsibilities
  • Review the high-level project timeline
Brief Outline

The External Project Kickoff Presentation is a presentation document consisting of slides that overview the project background, scope, goals and objectives.  It describes the project approach, roles, responsibilities and timeline.

A presentation agenda appears at the beginning of the presentation.

The External Project Kickoff Presentation may include the following:

  • Agenda
  • Introduce the Project
  • Review and Validate the Business Strategy
  • Confirm the Scope and Objectives of the Project
  • Identify Key Issues and Risks
  • Review Project Approach
  • Review Roles and Responsibilities
  • Review Project Timeline
  • Discuss Team Expectations and Obtain Commitment

Key Considerations

Because of the information required to conduct the presentation, this activity is to be performed after the Method Adoption Workshop is performed.  This will allow the management team to customize the presentation based on the work products that been decides as valid for the give engagement.

You may want to consider the following when preparing the External Project Kickoff Presentation:

1. Identify the Kickoff Meeting Audience
Identify the project team members as well as their roles on the project. The audience should consist of client team members that are able to set directions and make commitments for their business areas.

2. Notify Audience and Confirm their Attendance
Once the Kickoff Meeting time and location is determined, the participants should be notified and their availability confirmed. This is typically a client project management responsibility.

3. Manage Logistics for Kickoff Meeting
Facilities, equipment and any refreshments needed for the Kickoff Meeting should be arranged with the client project management well in advance of the meeting.

4. Learn About Client Audience
Obtain organization charts with participant’s names and titles. Talk with the client project management to understand each audience member’s responsibilities and interests in the project, as well as any issues or concerns they may have.

5. Create Presentation
Obtain prior method work products, or other relevant materials that may already have been developed for background information.

  • Incorporate this material into your presentation.
  • Follow the example given to prepare the appropriate content for the project initiation.

The External Project Kickoff Presentation should be validated against the project statement of work (SOW) prior to validating it with the client management. A final review of the presentation contents and structure should be performed with the client management. Agreement on the engagement approach and scope of the project must be achieved with the client management prior to the kickoff presentation with the project team.

Tailoring
Impact of not having

The External Project Kickoff Presentation facilitates the early client involvement, commitment, and sponsorship of the project. It aligns the project team’s understanding of the scope and objectives for the engagement.

The impact of not having this work product may include:

  • A misunderstanding of project scope
  • Lack of ownership or involvement of client team members
  • Misdirected effort by team members due to lack of understanding of the software selection approach
Reasons for not needing

This work product would not be required when the following conditions exist:

  • There are very few client team members involved in the particular engagement
  • Commitment is not secure from the client executive management and client attendees cannot be secured to hold a kickoff meeting
  • A clear written outline has been delivered to the project team in lieu of this work product
