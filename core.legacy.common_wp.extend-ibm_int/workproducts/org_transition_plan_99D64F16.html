Artifact: Organization Transition Plan (ORG 314)
Organization Transition Plan details how to implement the structural, behavioral, and enabling characteristics of the required organization changes.
Domains: Organization Change Management
Work Product Kinds: Plan
Purpose
The purpose of this work product is to assist in the implementation by planning the people and organization changes. These changes are required to organize, train, and orient the staff of an organization to the new ways of working. The organization needs to retain and build those capabilities that are deemed critical to the success of the change.
Relationships
Parent Deliverables

  • Transition Management Strategy Deliverable
Description
Main Description

The plan is completed and agreed to initially at a high level, before more detailed planning is completed in conjunction with plans for other areas of implementation. For large-scale change, the plan is likely to extend a significant time after the rest of the project has been completed, and may involve severance, recruitment, or relocation of staff.   

The plan includes a reassessment of the likely benefits of the future organization.

Key Considerations
Communication of the transition is critical, as this is a confusing and disruptive time.
Tailoring
Impact of not havingIf this work product is not used, the changes to the organization and people will not be coordinated, and can disrupt much of the project. Important steps may be missed or occur out of sequence.
Reasons for not needingThis work product is essential for all implementations that entail changes to organization structure (including jobs), behaviors, and enablers. Only very small-scale projects are unlikely to require such changes.
Representation Options

Notation for this work product consists of Microsoft project plan or Gantt charts, with supporting detail in text or presentation format.

A version of the plan that is easily understood and usable for communication events (not likely to be a Gantt chart or a Microsoft project plan) should also be created.
