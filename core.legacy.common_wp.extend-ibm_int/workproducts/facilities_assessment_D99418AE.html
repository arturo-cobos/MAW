Artifact: Facilities Assessment (OPS 320)
The Facilities Assessment work product is a report card on how the client's current or planned facility is or will perform in relation to the client's needs.
Domains: Facilities
Work Product Kinds: Assessment
Purpose

The purpose of the Facilities Assessment is to objectively evaluate the facility's performance. It tells what components meet the client's requirements, where improvement is required and whether the facility has the potential to meet the long term needs of the client. It determines the integrity of the existing facility and its efficiency and effectiveness. Provides the basic data and analysis used to:

  • Assess the client’s physical facilities and environments in order to assist the client in determining risks associated with the physical and/or geographic locations.
  • Identify gaps between the current facilities capabilities and those required for planned capabilities 
  • Ensure working conditions are conducive to quality performance by evaluating the internal and external environmental factors of the facility. 
  • Maximize the usage of the capital investment in facilities, by reviewing consolidation, relocation or outsourcing opportunities. 
  • Provide insights into subsequent transition planning activities
Description
Main Description

This work product’s scope can be adjusted so as to be applicable for multiple engagement types and facilities areas. (i.e., data centers, call centers..... development environments, research labs, etc..)

The work product describes the facility and the supporting facility infrastructure and performs an evaluation against industry best practices in relation to the client’s facility requirements to support facility selection, maintenance, and relocation decisions. It consists of drawings and text that describe where problem and opportunity areas exist (for example, servers scattered in multiple locations in the facility that should be located together). As well as a report explaining how the various components are performing, highlighting the positive aspects of the facility and pointing out the areas that are deficient. The following items are included in the assessment:

  • Real Estate Statistics (i.e., square footage, cost per square foot, etc.)
  • Lease vs. Own Conditions
  • Physical Plant and Layouts
  • Climate control/HVAC Conditions
  • Equipment Conditions
  • Fire Protection, Safety and Security Conditions
  • Power, Telecommunication and System Infrastructure and Connectivity
  • Telecommunication Costs
  • Geographic Locations
  • Labor Pool Availability (i.e., labor costs, unemployment rates)
  • Cost of Living
  • Transportation
  • Weather
  • Political and Social Potential Hazardous Conditions
  • Government Support (i.e., tax relief, incentives, etc.)
  • Business Recovery Conditions

Key Considerations

Use an existing assessment as a starting point. The latest examples of the Facility Assessments (Evaluation) can be found in the Intellectual Capital Product Platform Knowledge Network Category of Site Services.

Be sure that a good thorough review has been done and that all the aspects of the facility infrastructure have been covered to the needs and satisfaction of the customer (both IT and facilities).

A person with a strong facilities background, should write the major portions of the assessment and recommendations. The assessment should be validated against all of the facility's current components to make sure no items were missed.

Tailoring
Impact of not havingWithout having an initial assessment of the facility performed, the IBM personnel who are trying to understand the integrity of the facility would not be able to do so. There would be no information on the facility's ability to meet client requirements. Any problem areas would not be addressed in a new facility selection and design or modifications to the existing facility and the new design/modifications may therefore not meet all of the client's needs. Without a comprehensive assessment of the client’s current or planned facilities, selection, design, transition planning, and investment decisions activities will not be soundly based.
Reasons for not needing

Although this work product can deliver significant value, it may not be necessary in the following situations:

  • The client does not have any existing facilities or has stated that the current facilities can be completely disregarded.
  • The information has already been gathered. In this instance, time should still be allowed for reviewing the completeness of this material.
