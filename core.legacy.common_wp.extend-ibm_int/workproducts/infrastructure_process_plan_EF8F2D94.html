Artifact: Infrastructure Process Plan (ARC 011)
The Infrastructure Process Plan is a plan for the development and implementation of the operational processes needed to support and maintain the hardware and software of the new package or system.
Domains: Infrastructure
Work Product Kinds: Plan
Purpose
The plan is used to define the tasks, deliverables, skills and resources required to develop and implement the IT operational processes and procedures. It also defines the IT operational process milestones as they relate to the overall project timeline and milestones.
Description
Main Description

The following planning templates are involved:

  • Asset Management 
  • Backup/Recovery 
  • Batch Scheduling 
  • Change Management 
  • Disaster Recovery
  • File Transfer 
  • Help Desk (Problem Management) 
  • Network Management 
  • Output Management
  • Security 
  • Service Level Agreements 
  • Storage Management 
  • System Management 
  • Workstation Management

Key Considerations
  • Get client team consensus at every step.
  • Do not assign position titles to tasks; rather use skill categories that provide clearer definitions of required resources.
  • Use a process strategy matrix to validate initial tasks to be completed by the milestone dates. 
  • Validate overall project dates and milestones with project management.
  • Attempt to document a complete list of tasks for each operational process (such as, output management, change management) to simplify the implementation phase of process development.  If the list of tasks is incomplete, there may be some critical items left out of the plan that may have adverse affects on the implementation of processes and procedures.
  • Conduct Planning Template review meetings regularly for work in process to let the entire process planning ream review content and progress.  Conduct a task level review to ensure understanding by the entire team.
  • Discuss the final Planning Template review process with the client.  Options may include: 
    • Each team is responsible for final review of the Planning Template(s) they are assigned. 
    • A designated group is responsible for the final review of all Planning Templates. 
    • An individual is responsible for the final review of all Planning Templates. 
  • If time permits, review each Planning Template developed with a technical SME (subject matter expert) to validate that the task list and level of automation are within the timeframe and scope of the implementation project.
  • If the gap analysis has determined that the client will require very robust IT enterprise processes and procedures, and a major gap exists at the enterprise level, an enterprise system management consulting “framework” engagement may be required. 
Tailoring
Impact of not having
  • A poorly planned project. The overall project plan may not include the IT infrastructure activities required to manage and support the installation.
  • The person(s) responsible for the IT processes and procedures may be required to react rather than plan ahead to meet installation management and support requirements.  
  • Resources may be limited or non-existent when they are required for certain tasks and may not possess the correct skill sets or skill level resulting in missed deadlines.
Reasons for not needing
  • Professional IT planners, skilled and experienced in infrastructure implementations, are available to create detailed development and implementation plans without first documenting the high level plan.
  • The client believes adequate processes are in place today to meet the needs of the implementation.
