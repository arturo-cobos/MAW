Artifact: Human Performance Improvement Strategy (ORG 407)
The Human Performance Improvement (HPI) Strategy work product documents the human performance that is necessary to achieve business goals, by examining current performance and causes for performance gaps, and defining solutions to close the performance gaps.
Domains: Human Capital Strategy & Management
Work Product Kinds: Strategy
Purpose

The HPI Strategy is conducted to document an organization’s human performance and determine what performance is necessary to achieve the organization’s business goals.   

A Human Performance Improvement Strategy enables organizations to maximize the productivity of their workforce thereby enhancing business results. The purpose of this work product is to ensure that human performance improvement interventions are aligned with business goals of the organization. It supports the efficient and effective application of improving human performance.

Description
Main Description

The focus of the HPI strategy is to identify appropriate solutions (training and non-training) that will improve the performance of a company’s human resources and thereby help the organization reach their business goals. The HPI Strategy identifies:

  • The business drivers that should guide the solutions implemented,
  • How to improve the productivity of people,
  • People’s needs in terms of the gap between ideal and current performance,
  • Measurable results and ways of making continuous improvement,
  • How to integrate human resource management with workplace learning systems, and
  • How to motivate the workforce to higher levels of quality.

The HPI Strategy also contains recommendations for client management that [if/when implemented] can improve workforce and business results. Often a productivity improvement prediction is included within the final report, which illustrates how these recommended solutions can have a positive impact on workforce productivity and business results.

Key Considerations

To develop a Human Performance Improvement Strategy, aligned with the business strategy, it is critical to be able to assess the value/state of the organization’s human capital.  The gap analysis distinguishes all of the differences between desired performance and actual current performance.  Determining the gap may require different formats for data collection.  The most common are focus groups, interviews, and surveys.

Because of the types of information required it is essential to work collaboratively with the client whenever possible, setting up a knowledge sharing teamroom can be an excellent resource for this.  It is also critical to validate any assumptions made before presenting the final report.

Tailoring
Impact of not having

If this product is not produced then:

  • There may be a tendency to address symptoms rather than genuine root causes.
  • It will be difficult for a company to accurately determine the proper interventions needed to improve individual performance.

There will be lack of agreement; a high level strategy is a major early step in building consensus around the improvement effort. Without consensus at a high level, it is nearly impossible to build a plan that the client can be expected to take ownership of and fully support.

Reasons for not needingIf assessing human performance issues is the client goal, then this work product is essential and should be mandatory. Parts of the work product may be used because a client may determine that they only want the assessment, analysis, and recommendations.
