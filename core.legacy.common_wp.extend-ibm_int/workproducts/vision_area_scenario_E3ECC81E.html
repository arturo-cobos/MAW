Artifact: Vision Area Scenario (BUS 000)
A scenario is a fictional narrative account illustrating the significant observable characteristics of a vision area. There are three levels of scenario: scenario précis, scenario kernels, and full scenarios.
Domains: Business Strategy
Work Product Kinds: Concept
Purpose
  • To provide scenario exploration for a vision area.
  • To communicate the essence of a vision area to the client.
  • To stimulate further analysis of possible futures.
Description
Brief Outline

Scenario objects include scenario préces, scenario kernels, and full scenarios. 

Scenario précis

A scenario précis is a one to three sentence description of a scenario that serves to identify the scenario and the vision area for which it would be typical. The length should be between 2 and 50 words.

Scenario kernel

A scenario kernel is a one to two paragraph summary of the plot and major elements of a scenario. The length should be between 10 and 500 words.

Scenario

A scenario (aka, full scenario) is a narrative account that illustrates most significant observable characteristics of a vision area. Scenarios may be any length, but are usually between 200 and 2000 words.

Theme

A theme is a phrase that serves as a title or name for a vision area.

Vision Area

A vision area is a collection of ideas about a future state of significance to the client. The ideas are often the result of brainstorming exercises. The collection into a vision area is based on shared characteristics of the ideas. Typical shared characteristics include one application area or one technology area. They also include a shared estimate of feasibility in the sense that the ideas are deemed to belong to the same part of the future (e.g. deep future, intermediate future, near future)

Key Considerations
Scenario work is one of the main ways futurists analyze vision areas. A scenario communicates the essence of a vision area through fictional narrative. The scenario specialist should be a good story teller. The more gripping and evocative the story, the more likely the story will stimulate further valuable analysis. A good story will also help in the discovery of precursor vision areas and in the elaboration of alternative technological disciplines that may contribute to the realization of a vision area.
