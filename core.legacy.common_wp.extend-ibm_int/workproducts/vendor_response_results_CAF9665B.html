Artifact: Vendor Response Results and Shortlist (BUS 066)
The Vendor Response Results and Shortlist work product summarizes the analysis of the vendor's responses to the Request for Information (RFI), findings of the Pre-Qualification Analysis as well as responses to the Request for Proposal (RFP) or Request for Solution (RFS).
Domains: Selection
Work Product Kinds: Assessment
Purpose
The Vendor Response Results and Shortlist work product is the summarization of the completed Requirements Matrix using the Decision Model designed with the client. The results are used to assess the initial functional "fit" of a vendor’s software solution to the business needs of the client.
 
This work product also summarizes the justification of moving from the list of vendors on the interim list to deciding which vendors are to be brought in for vendor demonstrations. The presentation document’s purpose is to walk the client through the following processes that were used to reach the shortlist of vendors.
  • Details the summary results of the RFI/RFP results
  • Shows the analysis performed on the interim vendors
  • Justifies the elimination of vendors that should not be pursued
  • Defines the shortlist of vendors that should be scheduled for demonstrations
In addition, the work product is the document where the preliminary cost models for the vendors that will be demonstrating are presented. The document will also serve as a reference guide for the client to justify, to their organization, which vendors will be part of the detailed demonstration selection process.
Description
Main DescriptionThis work product details the scored results of the vendor responses to the Requirements Matrix using the Decision Model. In addition, it outlines the initial "pros and cons" of each vendor solution, initial cost information, elimination criteria of the shortlist evaluation, and concludes with the vendor shortlist identified for the software demonstrations.
Brief Outline

The Vendor Response Results and Shortlist provides a summary view of each vendor’s ability to meet the functional, general, and technical requirements desired by the client in their software solution.   It begins with the completed Requirements Matrix with additional spreadsheets. The additional spreadsheets detail the decision model and summary results of the vendor responses.

Vendors respond by populating the Requirements Matrix using a scoring model to indicate their ability to satisfy the functional, technical, and general requirements with the current release of their software.  A comments section is included to describe anticipated enhancements (& release dates), customizations, etc.

Vendors score their software using the following model:

  • 2 - Now Standard: Supported in Current Version
  • 1 - Planned as Standard: Definitely Supported in Next Release
  • 0 - Not Supported or Planned: Requires Custom Modifications

Vendors are able to provide additional detail to each requirement to clarify their response using the "Comments" column within each worksheet.

The Vendor Response Summary should consist of the following:

  • Title Page
  • Completed Requirements Matrix
    • The Requirements Matrix Excel Workbook should be completed in its entirety.
  • Decision Model Worksheet
    • An Excel spreadsheet should be created to reflect the scoring decision model created with the client.
  • Summary Results Worksheet
    • An Excel spreadsheet should be created to reflect the vendor scores by each component of the Requirements.  It should follow the scoring method determined in the decision model.  As part of the results summary, it is suggested that a percentage performance is reflected by each business area or evaluation component.

The information from the vendor response results analysis can then be displayed in a text document, via spreadsheet tables, or as a presentation.  However, the main advantage of the work product at this point is to take the client through the process of the shortlist selection; therefore, the presentation format seems to be the preferred approach to creating this interactive discussion session. 

The main sections of the work product for the presentation would include:

  • Cover Sheet
  • Table of Contents
  • List of Interim Vendors
  • Vendor Elimination (Who and Why)
  • RFI/RFP Results
  • Preliminary Cost Models
  • Vendor Analysis (Pros and Cons of Shortlist Vendors)
  • Calendar of Demonstration Weeks and Vendors
  • Next Steps

Again, since the format can vary according to ones preference, a standard template is not used.

Key Considerations

The decision or scoring model determined to assess the vendor scores should emphasize the key business areas and process existing in or desired for the client environment.  It should match the level of depth determined in the business process definition task and subsequent meetings with client management. 

Again, the format of this work product can vary tremendously.  The content emphasized for a client presentation is recommended as a subset of the analysis material to present.  The consultant should add any research information that is unique to the client, industry, or solution that would differentiate one solution vendor from another.  The important outcome, however reached, is to gain the client’s agreement that the vendors on the shortlist are the correct vendors to invite in for demonstrations.

The Vendor Response Results portion of this work product should be validated by checking the vendor responses to the Requirements Matrix to the scores listed in the decision model spreadsheet.  The summary worksheet should be validated with the client team lead to ensure agreement on the decision model weights.

In addition, this work product should provide the necessary detail to lead the client executive team through the process and the justification of selecting which vendors should be considered in detail for the client solution.  The document recommendations should be validated by the client for completeness to avoid the client coming back later and questioning why certain vendors were eliminated.

In most cases, the Vendor Response Results and Shortlist will be presented to the client team.  It is necessary that the client agrees with the shortlist findings and that they consider the list complete.  This verification at this point will avoid having them second-guess the process and vendors consideration in the latter stages of the engagement.
Tailoring
Impact of not having

Without the advantage of the Vendor Response Results and Shortlist work product, the client may always be questioning why other vendors were not selected to conduct demonstrations. Since the work product provides a vehicle for defining the best vendors for their environment, the lack of this work product may leave the client open to still be considering or wondering about other solutions or vendors.

One of the main advantages of the work product is to lead the client through the thought process behind the shortlist selection. Without the work product, the client may not understand why certain vendors were chosen or eliminated.

Reasons for not needingPerhaps the only reason to not include this work product would be if the client has already done a shortlist selection and a RFI/RFP was not issued. In some cases, the client has already had other consulting groups perform this work, or the organization has outlined standards that need to be followed. In either case, if the client has a shortlist of vendors that he will only consider for demonstrations, then the process can start with the scheduling of those vendors and this work product will not be used.
