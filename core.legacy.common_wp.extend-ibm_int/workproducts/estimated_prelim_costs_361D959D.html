Artifact: Estimated Preliminary Costs (BUS 074)
The Estimated Preliminary Costs documents and quantifies the configuration, costs and assumptions required to acquire and to implement the proposed system.
Domains: Business Value & Financials
Work Product Kinds: Measurement
Purpose
The purpose of the Estimated Preliminary Costs is to provide an estimate on which to approximate system acquisition and implementation costs and assist in the planning and decision making process. The preliminary costs are based on the best available information at that phase of the project and are not recommended for final budgeting, actual quotes from vendors or actual completion cost guarantees. If due to the project timing, the Estimated Preliminary Costs may be used in the Business Case to calculate costs and savings, in lieu of any better numbers, however this is not the preferred method.
Description
Main Description

The costs or estimated costs are the best available information given the level of knowledge of the installation, the estimated number of users, the number of interfaces/integration points with other systems, and other facts which will come to light during detailed study and planing tasks. The scope of the Estimated Preliminary Costs addresses:

  • The estimated software costs to the client
  • The estimated hardware costs to the client
  • The estimated implementation and integration costs to the client
  • The timing and order of installation based on a high level implementation plan
  • The estimated total costs of the system for software, hardware and services
  • All assumptions used in calculating the costs and schedule
The Preliminary costing process is iterative throughout the Selection phase and will be finalized prior to the Integration phase.
Brief Outline

The Estimated Preliminary Costs is a spreadsheet or presentation document with inserts of tables, charts and graphs for data presentation. 

The Estimated Preliminary Costs may have the following Table of Contents:

  • Process Summary
    • Describes business and functional components of the project and how they interrelate.
    • Defines how costs were gathered and the timing and requirements of the project.
    • Describes recommendation with summary of other solutions considered.
    • May define a preferred configuration or vendor.

  • Vendor Details
    • Breakdown by vendor of each vendor's or vendor team offerings.
    • Describes summary of vendor's recommendation of software, hardware and services.
    • Details of vendors offering (pricing algorithm, required and optional modules, including database and hardware requirements and if appropriate strategic partnerships.
    • Details of implementation costs.

Documents vendors recommendation for training, hardware maintenance, software licensing, software maintenance, and other post implementation components.

Key Considerations

The Estimated Preliminary Costs is usually the initial cost document for the discussion, planning and acquisition of the new system.  As such, it is expected that the original numbers will be approximates that are refined as the actual requirements become known.  Early in the Estimated Preliminary Costs task, the Project Manager should meet with the Client Team Manager and appropriate client financial personnel to identify and address the basis and purpose of the numbers.  Typically these numbers are “list” prices from the vendors and illustrate typical hardware, implementation and support costs.  During the final definition phase and negotiations these numbers may change significantly.

Other points of consideration:

  • Identify the project recipient of the estimated vendor costs and understand their confidentiality requirements.  Some clients do not want software costs to bias the functional scoring of the vendor packages while others used this information freely within the selection team to determine the vendor finalist.
  • Be explicit in all documents that use the vendor cost information from this Work Product and clearly state that these costs are estimates only and are not representative of the vendors actual software cost or cost to implement their solution.
  • Review the estimated preliminary costs with the IBM project manager before delivering this information to the client.  Some IBM managed projects may want to control the clients expectation for implementation and require the estimate include costs for “risk” or other “up-lifts” associated to the project as defined within the customer agreement.
  • Since the cost numbers are estimates only, round all final values to the nearest thousand.  Although clearly stated throughout this work product and highlighted in all the reports within the template, some clients may misinterpret the precision in the numbers and concluded that the costs are actuals.  The template for this Work Product already performs this level of rounding.

The Estimated Preliminary Costs may be validated by the functional teams, the project team or by client management and financial personnel.  The values collected and presented should be checked for approximate scale, knowing that final values will be collected and negotiated later in the process.

Tailoring
Impact of not havingThe Estimated Preliminary Costs are usually required, depending on the needs and desires of the client, and therefore will usually be developed to assist with the planning and decision making process.
Reasons for not needingThe Estimated Preliminary Costs are usually required, and can be omitted with the written concurrence of the client.
