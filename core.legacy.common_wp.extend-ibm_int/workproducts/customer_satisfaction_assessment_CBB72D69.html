Artifact: Customer Satisfaction Assessment (ART 0659)
Customer satisfaction is an expression of perceived versus expected service or value. The Customer Satisfaction Assessment work product describes the degree with which the customer is satisfied with the level of service received, and/or the ability of a given tool or product to meet stated and unstated business requirements.
Domains: Relationship Management
Work Product Kinds: Assessment
Purpose
Customer Satisfaction Assessments are conducted to gain an understanding of the degree of a customer's satisfaction with the specific subject area of the engagement. In general the goal of knowing a customer's degree of satisfaction at the beginning of an engagement provides a baseline from which to improve. While assessing satisfaction at the end of an engagement provides a validation of the perceived effectiveness and value received for the engagement. In addition, quantitative customer satisfaction figures are useful tools for attracting and retaining customers.
 
Customer Satisfaction Assessments provide the information required to support the management of customer satisfaction. This is the process of identifying the gaps and the necessary actions to improve satisfaction.
Description
Main Description

This assessment does not presume who the provider is. The provider may be internal to the customer's organization, or an external vendor, or IBM.

Generally for the delivery of satisfaction to the customer, the provider must:

  • Understand the customer's requirements for business performance, value and effectiveness
  • Seek varied input by many encounter opportunities (survey, workshop, hotline, planning session) and understand the feedback they receive
  • Understand the customer's perceptions and expectations, satisfiers and dissatisfiers, and
  • Continuously improve performance profitably and correctly deliver on user needs

Key Considerations

Various approaches are available for collecting customer feedback.  Experts in designing instruments or in statistical analysis may need to be included on the engagement team.  Consideration must be given to:

  • The customer's satisfaction with a giving aspect of service, or component of a product versus the relative importance of that aspect or component
  • The tool(s) used for the administration of surveys and/or questionnaires
  • The accommodation and inclusion of all forms of feedback including unsolicited
  • Any customer pre-dispositions, biases or priorities
  • Required customer investments in terms of time and availability of key personnel
  • The possible use of incentives
  • End user and executive expectations

Impact of not havingWhere the gathering of other quantifiable results is not feasible, or may potentially require measurements over a longer period of time than can be accommodated in the engagement, the use of Customer Satisfaction Assessments may be the only way to gauge the success of the engagement.
