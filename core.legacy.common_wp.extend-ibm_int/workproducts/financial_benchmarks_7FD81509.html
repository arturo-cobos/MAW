Artifact: Financial Benchmarks (BUS 303)
The Financial Benchmarks work product compares and contrasts the metrics, trends and relationships that characterize the financial condition of the client’s business at the time the engagement is commenced with the same metrics for other relevant firms and groups of firms [e. g., industry averages].
Domains: Business Value & Financials
Work Product Kinds: Measurement
Purpose

Determine how much change is really necessary or possible in any given financial metric. It enables the client to determine what the world class standard for each financial metric is and how the client compares to that standard.

Additionally, the Financial Benchmarks work product is intended to:

  • Demonstrate and validate the consultants’ understanding of the client’s business
  • Establish a common base of concerns and priorities between the consultants and clients
  • Identify high-level business metrics that will be used to assess the success or failure of business transformation projects
  • Identify hypothetical problem areas and opportunities for improvement
Description
Main Description

The scope of the Financial Benchmarks work product addresses:

  • Calculating generic, as well as industry-specific business metrics (e. g., ratios) for each relevant line of business;
  • Assessing historical trends and projections for key business metrics;
  • Identifying areas of concern indicated by current or projected metrics;
  • Formulation of preliminary hypotheses concerning problems and solutions; and
  • Documenting data sources and assumptions used in calculating the key business metrics and trends needed for competitor/industry comparisons.

The Financial Benchmarks process is undertaken during the early stages of the project (ideally, during the proposal phase.) The projected impact of proposed initiatives on business metrics and trends identified at this stage will typically be reflected in the Business Case work product.

Key Considerations
The Financial Benchmarks work product should be produced in the proposal phase to the extent possible.  Doing so will enable you to use it in working with the client to establish base-line assumptions.  It will also provide valuable input to creating the Financial Assessment and Financial Work Plan & Model work products.
Tailoring
Impact of not having

Without the Financial Benchmarks work product there is a:

  • Limited quantitative mechanism to validate the consultants’ understanding of the client’s industry and competitive situation
  • Limited quantitative basis to establish a common base of concerns and priorities concerning the industry and competitive situation between the consultants and clients
  • Limited quantitative mechanism to objectively identify high-level business metrics that will be used to assess the success or failure of business transformation projects
  • Limited "objective" quantitative basis to identify hypothetical problem areas and opportunities for improvement
Reasons for not needingIf the client has hired IBM to "execute" a business transformation effort with predefined tactical priorities and metrics, it is not necessary to produce the Financial Benchmarks work product. This work product is only necessary where the client needs to generate a quantitative Business Case to justify a proposed effort or to quantify the benefits that an effort actually achieves.
