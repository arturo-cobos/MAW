Artifact: Community Design Blueprint (ORG 402)
The Community Design Blueprint provides a unified view of how a community of practice will engage its members and provide a vehicle for knowledge creation, sharing and use.
Domains: Knowledge Management
Work Product Kinds: Specification
Purpose

The Community Design Blueprint is needed to provide clients with a clear understanding of:

  • The initial members of the community
  • The enablers (i.e., roles, tools, events, and content strategies) that will enable the community to create, share, and apply knowledge more effectively. 
  • The arrangements regarding community membership, authority, support, measurement, and accountability that will comprise the community’s relationship to the formal organization. 
  • The basis in evidence for these design recommendations and the important dependencies among design elements
Description
Main Description

The Community Design Blueprint work product details the new structure, performance enablers, and behaviors required to achieve the business vision.   

The Community Design Blueprint addresses 1) the initial community membership, 2) the mechanisms or "enablers" by means of which community interactions will be facilitated, and 3) the nature of the community’s relationship to the formal organization. The Community Design Blueprint also describes the rationale for design recommendations and the key dependencies among the various design elements.

Brief Outline

Both text and graphics may be used to illustrate the community blueprint. This should include descriptions of:

  • The identification and formal location of community members
  • Output from the Social Capital analysis that describes the current and future communication patterns of community members,
  • The types of activities and roles that community members will undertake,
  • The relationships between the community and the formal organization,
  • The types of technological interventions that will be necessary to enable the community members
  • The processes and standards that the community will incorporate
  • Use word processing software to describe a narrative of the community characteristics. Graphics/presentation software should be used to summarize the key characteristics of the Community for executive briefings and community sign-off and acceptance.

Key Considerations
It is very important to have members of the client working group involved in the Community Design blueprint.  They have the greatest knowledge of the potential needs and benefits of a community approach and can provide insight into the most beneficial design elements.  Further, than can refine the design to incorporate the cultural subtlies that often define the success or failure of such efforts.
Tailoring
Impact of not having
  • The client may fail to understand the elements of the recommended design and so may experience difficulty in planning for successful implementation.
  • The client may fail to understand the community concept that unifies the elements of the recommended design and so may fail to accurately communicate the nature and objectives of the recommended design.
  • The client may fail to appreciate the basis in evidence for the recommended design and so may lack confidence in the design.
  • The client may fail to appreciate the dependencies among elements of the design and so may be unable to accurately project the consequences of changing one or more design elements.
Reasons for not needingThis work product is not needed if, during the assessment phase, it is decided that there is no need to provide support for the specified community.
