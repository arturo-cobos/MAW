Artifact: Internal Project Kick-off (ENG 006)
The Internal Project Kickoff Presentation is a vehicle to discuss the project scope and objectives and develop team "buy-in" for the project.
Domains: Project Management
Work Product Kinds: Record
Purpose

The Internal Project Kickoff Presentation is used to educate the IBM project team on the client, its strategy and goals for the project. It transfers information on how the project will be conducted from a process and procedure standpoint. Team members learn the tool infrastructure and how they will work with it.

Through the Internal Project Kickoff Presentation, the team learns how project communications will take place. In addition, any issues that might be sensitive with the client are presented and suggestions on how to deal with them are proposed.

Description
Main Description

This presentation is given prior to the External Kick-off Presentation in order to ensure that the IBM team (including subcontractors if needed) is fully briefed on the engagement before meeting with the Client’s core team for the first time.

The Internal Project Kickoff Presentation is a series of slides giving an overview of the project background, scope, goals and objectives. It describes the project approach, roles, responsibilities and timeline and is the vehicle used to present the engagement to the IBM project team.

The objectives of the Project Kickoff Presentation are to:

  • Overview the client’s environment, problems and goals
  • Begin the IBM internal project "team building"
  • Achieve consensus on the project scope
  • Describe the roles and responsibilities of team members
  • Review the high-level project timeline
  • Review project deliverables
  • Review key work products for the Phase
  • Review tracking process and deadlines
Brief Outline

The Internal Project Kickoff Presentation is a presentation document consisting of slides that overview the project background, scope, goals and objectives.  It describes the project approach, roles, responsibilities and timeline as well as technical environment, project processes and procedures.

Attendees: Should be all IBM employees as well as other individuals from affiliates and contracting companies that will be working under our guidance.

A meeting agenda appears at the beginning of the presentation.

The Project Kickoff Presentation may include the following:

  • Agenda
  • Introduce the Project
  • Review the Business Strategy
  • Confirm the Scope and Objectives of the Project
  • Identify Key Issues and Risks
  • Review Project Approach
  • Review Roles and Responsibilities
  • Review Project Timeline
    1. Review of Project Processes and Procedures
    2. Change Control
    3. Communications
    4. Issue Management
    5. Escalation Procedures
    6. etc.
  • Review of Technical Environment
  • Review of Tools
  • Review of work products to be produced in first phase
  • Review of Contracted Deliverables
  • Review of Project Plan
  • Review of Tracking Procedures
  • Review of Tasks not to be performed such as:
    1. Client responsibilities
    2. Tools creation/modification
    3. etc.

Key Considerations

The concepts are that the project team learns how to conduct themselves on the project and how to communicate within the project team but especially with the customer.  You should find a great deal of the process and procedure information that you need in the WWPMM templates.  It is imperative that this information is gathered, understood and communicated to the team.  Also, you may want to assign certain pieces of the internal kick-off to your team leaders.  This will get them in the mode of directing parts of the project.

The Internal Project Kickoff Presentation should be conducted prior to the Client (External) Project Kick-Off Session.  Any internal issues that are identified by the IBM team need to be addressed prior to creating the External Kick-off Presentation.

Tailoring
Impact of not having

The impact of not having this work product may include:

  • A misunderstanding of the project processes and procedures
  • Confusion about project standards
  • Poor planning by team members
  • Ineffective tracking against the project plan
  • Poor communications
  • Duplication of Work Effort
Reasons for not needingThis work product would not be required if IBM is not responsible for Project Management of the project.
