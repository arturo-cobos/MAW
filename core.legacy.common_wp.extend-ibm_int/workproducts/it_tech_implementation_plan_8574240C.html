Key Considerations

The following guidance is recommended:

  • Get client team consensus at every step.
  • Do not assign position titles to tasks; rather use skill categories that provide clearer definitions of required resources.
  • Validate overall project dates and milestones with project management.
  • Validate configurations with vendors or subject matter experts.
  • Some or all of the above data (points 3,4) may need to be gathered to complete the infrastructure design. Use common sense in gathering information needed to complete the infrastructure design.
  • Ensure that any gaps identified during gap analysis are addressed in the project plan or are documented as addenda, issues, or action items in the overall project plan.
  • If the gap analysis has determined that the project will require a very robust IT enterprise network, and major gaps exist at the enterprise level, a separate enterprise network design and implementation initiative may be required.
Tailoring
Impact of not having

The impact of not having this work product may result in:

  • A poorly planned project. The overall project plan may not include the IT infrastructure activities required to support the project.
  • The person(s) responsible for the IT infrastructure may be required to "react" rather than plan ahead to meet project requirements.
  • Resources may be limited or non-existent when they are required for certain tasks and may not possess the correct skill sets or skill level resulting in missed deadlines.
Reasons for not needing

Reasons for not needing this work product are:

  • Professional IT planners, skilled and experienced in infrastructure implementations, are available to create detailed implementation and support plans without first documenting the high level plan.
  • The client believes there will be no changes required to the current servers, WAN, LAN and workstations
