Artifact: Interview or Survey Specifications and Materials (BUS 343)
This artifacts provides a description of a interview or survey instrument and its components, as well as the associated delivery and acceptance conditions.
Domains: Business Strategy
Work Product Kinds: Specification
Purpose

The purpose of this artifact is to formalize what the releasing and accepting parties have agreed about:

  • What the interview or survey study needs and objectives are
  • What data gathering method is most appropriate to the study needs
  • What the data gathering instrument is and what it is composed of
  • When the survey instrument will be released or when the interviews will be scheduled
  • What data collection and analysis methods are most appropriate to the study needs
  • Which acceptance criteria have to be met by the study, and how those criteria will be checked
Description
Main Description

The interview or survey instrument can be the initial repository of responses depending upon the media selected or as in the case of internet surveys the media through which that repository is populated with the results.

In the case of a typical consulting project, this work product would capture the targeted area of study, respondents, timeframes, hypothesis and questions to be tested, delivery media, analysis approach, and resource requirements.

Brief Outline

This work product consists of the following information:

  • Hypothesis and questions to be tested for the targeted area of study
  • Scope and source for respondents to be surveyed
  • Agreed upon timeframes for design, construction, analysis, and review
  • Either a schedule for conducting the required interviews or a timeframe for disseminating and collection of the survey materials
  • Documentation of the agreed upon delivery media, analysis approach, and resource requirements and responsibilities

These materials are typically represented as a set of planning documents containing the specifications, and the designed materials themselves in the agreed upon delivery format.

Key Considerations

Check what the client's previous experience has been in interview or survey situations.  Learn what you can about the organization's culture and previous reactions and what works and what does not work in their environment.

If survey is to be delivered in several languages, consider how translation is to be handled and what is the best way of organizing the material.  Be realistic about the time needed for the translation of the material (it takes longer than you think!).

The study will have been successful if an adequate number of responses are obtained, representing complete coverage of the targeted respondent groups to ensure no skewed results in order to be able to provide insights into the organizations performance relative to the areas of study. Therefore it is important to keep in mind the business reasons for investigating the area of study at all times when developing the specifications and the actual material.

Ensure the specifications and materials:

  • Meet business, performance, and documentation requirements
  • Address the objectives of the study and the constraints of the engagement
Tailoring
Impact of not having

Consequences for not using the artifact include:

  • What is released may not be what is expected, unclear definition of the scope of study could result in poor survey construction or dissemination
  • What is released is not what is needed, unclear definition of the scope of study could result in poor survey construction or dissemination
  • The results may be incomplete according to the plans of the accepting party
  • The quality of the interviews or survey may be insufficient, creating a significant risk of the project team making uninformed decisions regarding the proposed solution thereby leading to poor quality or inappropriate solutions and ultimately poor customer satisfaction
  • The delivery dates may be inadequate
  • Rework, delay and budget overhead occur for survey administration for all parties
Reasons for not needing

Although this artifact can deliver significant value, it may not be necessary in the following situations:

  • The information has already been gathered. In this instance, time should still be allowed for reviewing the completeness of this material
  • The audience is relatively small
  • The number of questions is very limited (i.e. 2 - 3 questions)
