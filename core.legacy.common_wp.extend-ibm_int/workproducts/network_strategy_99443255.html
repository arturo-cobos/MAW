Artifact: Network Strategy (ARC 325)
The Network Strategy work product describes a desired future set of services that must be provided by the network in order to satisfy the client’s business requirements.
Domains: Networking
Work Product Kinds: Strategy
Purpose

The purpose of the network strategy is to:

  • serve as a communication vehicle between the network organization and the rest of the business
  • provide guidance to architects and network designers in making choices and decisions  
  • provide linkage to the business to ensure that the network supports strategic initiatives
  • provide policies to enable the network and the networking organization to provide the required support
  • provide decision criteria or thresholds to determine future changes in networking services.
Description
Main DescriptionThe Network Strategy work product describes a desired future set of services that must be provided by the network in order to satisfy the client’s business requirements. These individual services (or commonly called network requirements) are described in a number of different contexts. Examples would include network performance and capacity levels delivered to certain user classes, availability of the network at various client locations, network services provided such as fax, voice, or video.

Key Considerations

This is a key step to any network consulting engagement so it is critical that experienced network consultants and architects be an integral part of the engagement team.  Strengths of team members would include:

  • Interviewing skills
  • Executive/business/management background and presence
  • Client industry and competitor knowledge
  • Networking industry knowledge
  • Ability to think at the ’50,000 foot’ level

It is also key at this point to have excellent communication with the client sponsor.  This is not a point where the team should be straying too far from where the sponsor is expecting it to go (unless there are clear and specific reasons for doing so that can be communicated to the sponsor, as well).

A network strategy work product should:

  • clearly relate to key business initiatives and client requirements
  • conform to the mission, culture and direction of the enterprise
  • consider external components, such as industry, competition, customer/supplier relationships, etc.
  • consider internal components, such as infrastructure, processes, resources, assets, etc.
Tailoring
Impact of not having

Without an agreed upon network strategy, there are no clear network goals nor is there a level of consensus or a firm basis for moving forward. The absence of a clearly defined network vision indicates a lack of management support for the network direction - making conscious, informed decision-making difficult. The organization will:

  • not know if they are getting value for the dollars spent on the network environment.
  • not be able to plan for enterprise networking.
  • not have a clear understanding of the importance of the network.
  • not be able to take true advantage of new network technology.
  • not be able to differentiate between various vendor hardware/software/services claims.
Reasons for not needing

The client may only be requesting an assessment of the current network with a corresponding gap analysis - this effort may not require doing a network strategy.

The client may already have a ‘strategy’ group that is responsible for setting client strategic direction across many or all of the IT departments. 

The engagement may be defined at a ‘lower’ level than strategy - it could be purely a design engagement that uses a network strategy/architecture defined by other sources or previously by the client.

Representation OptionsThe notation used to describe a client network strategy is usually a combination of text statements reflecting the needs of the client network, coupled with ‘high-level’ diagrams showing things like industry trends, competitive uses of networking, etc.  All of these methods work together to give the client an appreciation for what sorts of network changes the client may expect in the future.
