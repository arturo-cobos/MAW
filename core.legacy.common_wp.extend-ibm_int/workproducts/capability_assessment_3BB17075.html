Artifact: Capability Assessment (BUS 316)
The Capability Assessment work product comprises the details of the assessment of a capability.
Domains: Business Strategy
Work Product Kinds: Assessment
Purpose

The purpose of the Capability Assessment work product is to establish the relative strengths and weaknesses of business or IT capabilities against a desired future state or value proposition. This assessment is the foundation for selecting and assessing processes, organization and technology that enable each capability.

The reasons why this work product is produced are to:

  • Provide overall context and understanding of the current state of business or IT capabilities.
  • Determine the current status and improvement opportunities within each of the selected capabilities.
  • Provide an understanding of the impact on current processes, organization, and enabling technologies.
  • Identify and prioritize the specific client business and IT capabilities and enablers that will be included in the scope of this transformation initiative and enable a plan to be created.
  • Assist the client in thinking through their business and IT strategy.
  • Assist in gaining executive agreement to the scope and boundaries.
  • Provide the basis for sizing the effort required to understand and assess the current environment.
  • Provide a record of the scope and boundaries and assist in managing expectations regarding the scope.
  • Assist in communicating the impact of the redesign across the business.
  • Identify what skills, knowledge, and background are required from the project team.
Description
Main Description

The Capability Assessment work product is a:

  • Comparison of the current state of a capability compared to a future requirement of that capability
  • Determination of the strength or weakness of a current capability to support a value proposition or outcome
  • Determination of selected enablers' (Organization, Process and Technology ) strengths and weaknesses that comprise the capability
  • Prioritization of the capabilities based on an importance ranking and performance rating of each capability to the business, IT, or project goals or value propositions
Brief Outline

Depending on the number of processes or capabilities selected, the output for this work product can be of considerable volume.  The minimum output is:

  • One page of tabular data showing the assessment ratings for each process or capability and their overall priority rank.  
  • One page of graphics that positions each selected process within the dimensions of performance versus leverage importance
  • One page for each selected process that contains a formatted definition and description summary
  • One page for each selected process that lists its assessment ratings along with supporting rationale
  • One page for each selected process that lists identified opportunities for improvement (i.e. capability gaps)
  • A list of all sub-processes (within the project scope) that have been classified as "Critical", "Industry Standard" or "Basic" for the purpose of further definition and package function fitting.

Additional supporting documentation:

  • Flow diagrams of each selected process, showing the key functions/ activities, their relationships and identifying their current performers, is very desirable. 
  • The additional outputs of a full process definition and assessment workshop will also include formatted documentation on process inputs, outputs, sub processes and measurements.

Key Considerations

When facilitating the process or capability assessment exercise, allow client team members to use a more granular rating scale (i.e. L+, H-, etc.).  This will also help achieve a consensus opinion on the rating of each assessment category and goal linkage.  In some e-strategy engagements the assessment may be as simple as identifying it as a strength or weakness and then providing some descriptions as to the weakness and potential corrective actions rather than even doing high level process flows.

Tailoring
Impact of not having

If the capabilities that will be transformed to support required value propositions are not defined and assessed, then:

  • It will not be possible to scope and size transforming initiatives.
  • There will be no basis for prioritizing the focus on various enablers (people, process, technology including package functionality) for fit and closing capability gaps
  • Trade-offs in terms of misalignment of packaged enabling functions to current process capabilities or capability gaps will be difficult to make
  • It will be nearly impossible to estimate the degree of impact to the organization of implementing packaged enablers or leading practices functions
  • Individual processes or capabilities will not have a known baseline performance for future measurement of performance improvement due to packaged or leading practice implementations
Reasons for not needingThere are very few instances when this work product will not be absolutely required, possibly when:
  1. In the instances where process or capability selection activities have been performed in prior engagements and when a proposal includes the information found in a process or capability selection document, this additional effort may not be required.
  2. The package or capability selected of the business problem to be addressed is very limited/focused in scope and the future state will be totally defined by the functional and operational requirements of the package.
