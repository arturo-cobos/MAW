Artifact: Content Gaps (BUS 410)
The Content Gaps work product outlines the source content needed to develop a solution.
Domains: Knowledge Management
Work Product Kinds: Assessment
Purpose

The Content Gaps work product serves three main purposes:

  • It outlines in detail the content needed to develop a solution
  • It proposes a solution for acquiring the needed content
  • It establishes agreement between the development team and the customer regarding the importance of the needed content
Description
Main Description

This work product is based on the evaluation of existing source content, as compared to the content needed to complete the solution as outlined in design and strategy documents.

The Content Gaps work product should outline not only the information that is needed, but should recommend means for obtaining the needed content, including the identification of appropriate Subject Matter Experts (SMEs). The work product should also list the implications for failing to obtain the needed content, and the dates by which the content must be secured.

Key Considerations

When identifying Content Gaps, it is helpful to imagine yourself walking through the motions of developing the learning materials. Ask yourself the following questions:

  • “If I were charged with creating this learning solution today, do I have enough information to do so?”
  • “What are the questions I need answered by a SME before I could develop the learning solution?”
  • “If content gaps exist, am I able to fill them (even partially) myself by researching the subject matter?”

In instances where enough content gaps exist that the development schedule will be compromised, it is helpful to present the Content Gaps document to the client, along with a plan outlining what content is needed and what may happen if it is not received by a particular date. Doing so will help secure buy-in from your client, and will help the client understand the importance of filling in content gaps before development begins.

Always keep the Project Manager informed of any content-related issues that may impact the development schedule, especially in those cases where a Project Change Request (PCR) may be needed.

When working with a SME to identify and address content gaps, be wary of “scope creep” – the addition of “nice to have” content that is not essential to the learning solution, but may be requested by the client or SME for inclusion in the learning solution. Work with your client and SME to keep them within scope of the project. The goal is to fill gaps, not to create more complicated or in-depth learning materials.

Content gaps will often be filled by interviewing SMEs and capturing the results. In these instances, you will find it helpful to use a template to capture the needed information and distribute it to members of the development team.

The Instructional Designer and a lead Information Developer should evaluate the Content Gaps work product to ensure that it is complete; failure to do so may result in overlooked content needs, which may cause slips in the production schedule.

Tailoring
Impact of not havingOmitting the Content Gaps work product could result in a situation in which appropriate and needed content is not available to the development team, which may bring development to a halt. Alternately, it may result in a situation in which it is unclear how needed content will be obtained, or whose responsibility it is to gather this content.
Reasons for not needingThe Content Gaps work product is not needed in situations where content is complete, well organized, and up to date. It is not needed in situations in which learning solutions are being translated or otherwise repurposed, and therefore no new content is needed.
Representation OptionsIt is helpful to present Content Gaps in the form of a table, where needed content is identified in one column and provided (or referenced) in another by a SME.  It is also helpful to organize the needed content as it will be presented within the learning solution itself.  In this way, for larger projects, content needed early in the development process may be made a priority.
