Artifact: Program Management Framework (ORG 303)
The Program Management Framework work product, in conjunction with an integrated transition plan, produces a single integrated framework for successfully executing multiple or complex projects with a single program focus.
Domains: Organization Change Management
Work Product Kinds: Model
Purpose
The Program Management Framework work product is used to ensure ongoing focus on and commitment to the successful achievement of both the program deliverables and the desired benefits by establishing one, consistent framework.
Description
Main DescriptionOrganizations often have multiple change projects within one change program, which can potentially cause great confusion and overlap. The Program Management Framework work product uses IBM’s program office tool to identify the requirements to monitor, track, and report on the progress of a complex project or multiple concurrent projects and creates a framework for managing the entire program. Additionally, the Program Management Framework work product links existing management reporting and performance management systems to program milestones and measurements to ensure that individuals and teams are motivated, managed, and rewarded in resolving issues and delivering program benefits successfully.
Key Considerations

The development of a program management framework for the client can be a long and difficult process if the prerequisite client understanding and commitment is not in place.

  • Ensure that there is strong leadership for an integrated program management approach since key stakeholder groups often resist the perceived loss of power and control when projects are integrated and managed centrally.
  • Identify the client’s current or previous approaches to program management and use these as a basis for the work product. Often the Change Readiness assessment will identify how these systems have failed or succeeded in the past.
  • Ensure that the framework balances the needs for leadership, monitoring and control of both central and business unit/local management.
  • Ensure that the framework links to the clients financial control systems – annual and quarterly business planning, business case processes and capital approval, authority limits – since these are often the closest the client has to a project control system.
  • Tailor the system to take account of the client’s culture and political situation.

This work product involves altering the client’s performance measurement systems to reflect the project outputs and goals.  As such it can only be done with the full involvement of the clients staff.  Validate the specific measures and targets with sponsors, department heads, and other users.

Tailoring
Impact of not havingWithout a common framework, there will be confusion and overlap within one transformation program. Additionally, the work of the people responsible for executing the transformation actions needs to be measured and rewarded in a manner that supports the transition goals. Without consistent methodologies and frameworks and attention to performance, the transformation projects or program might fail.
Reasons for not needingThere is no reason not to have this work product, as it produces consistency and alignment across change projects and rewards performance from the key performers in the engagement.
