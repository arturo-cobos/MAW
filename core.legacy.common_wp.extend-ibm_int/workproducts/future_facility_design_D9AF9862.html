Artifact: Future Facility Design (OPS 319)
The Future Facility Design work product is a detailed design of a new or revised facility that will house the IT solution for a client.
Domains: Facilities
Work Product Kinds: Specification
Purpose
The purpose of this document is to develop the specifications and detailed design drawings for building a new facility and the related facility infrastructure. The design must be at a sufficiently detailed level so that construction activities can be accomplished from the design.
Description
Main DescriptionThe Future Facility Design work product is a detailed design of a new or revised facility that will house the IT solution for a client. The work product consists of detailed CAD drawings that depict all of the components within the facility and the exact location of each component as well as text which describes the design and identifies specifications and additional information that cannot be depicted in a drawing. The design must include the physical space, equipment, hardware, software, cabling and wiring, capacity, security, fire protection, etc.
Brief Outline

The drawing is typically done using AutoCad dwg and dxf files with the supporting text written in a word processing tool.  The supporting text might include the following categories:

  • Space planning
  • Architectural systems
  • Fire protection systems
  • Mechanical systems
  • Electrical systems
  • Monitoring systems
  • Physical security
NotationThe drawings should always be done softcopy.  The drawings should include all of the components in the facility including any equipment, hardware, software, cabling, fire protection systems, mechanical systems, security systems, etc. 

CAD drawings would have a numbering scheme as follows:

  • S – Site Drawings
  • A – Architectural Drawings
  • M – Mechanical Drawings
  • E – Electrical Drawings
  • FP – Fire Protection Drawings

Key Considerations

An architectural and engineering firm that has designed facilities for IBM and through IBM for IBM clients should be used for the work if no IBM professional with the appropriate skills is available.

If an architectural and engineering firm is brought in, you must be careful to establish the value of including IBM in this work.  The client may ask why IBM is needed if the architectural and engineering firm is doing all of the design work.

A person with an engineering background or a very strong facilities background must be the project manager to work with the architectural and engineering firm during the design phase.  In some instances, IBM engineers would want to perform design review of the work that is being done by the architectural and engineering firms to assure to the client that the specifications and drawings adhere to the client requirements and meet the IBM recommendations.  These reviews are typically done at 50%, 75%, and 100% of the creation of the drawings. The client may also require design reviews with their staff.  IBM engineers and the architectural and engineering firm must be present during these reviews.

There are no references, however, be sure that all local, state, and national codes and standards (NEC, ADA, building codes, ASHRAE) have been followed.

Tailoring
Impact of not havingWithout having any formal specifications and design drawings, IBM cannot propose to a client to build a new facility or make modifications to an existing facility. There would be no information from which the facility could be constructed.
Reasons for not needingIf a new facility were not required, IBM would not need to have specifications and design drawings prepared. However, even if significant renovations to an existing facility were required, detailed specifications and design drawings would still have to be prepared.
