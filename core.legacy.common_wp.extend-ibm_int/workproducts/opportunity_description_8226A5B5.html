Artifact: Opportunity Description (BUS 400)
The Opportunity Description work product provides instructions on how to document opportunities identified during strategy engagements.
Domains: Business Strategy
Work Product Kinds: Inventory
Purpose
Opportunity Descriptions are used to thoroughly document all internal (e.g., around operational efficiency or employee communications) and external opportunities (e.g., market offerings) that a company wishes to consider for inclusion in their strategic portfolio. These descriptions communicate the essence of the opportunity, the reasons for or value of the opportunity, and the implications or impacts of the opportunity. The descriptions are a key element of a smooth transition to Transition Initiatives and ultimately, to business design.
Description
Main DescriptionOpportunities are defined as stand alone (independent) ideas or concepts that satisfy the client’s business objectives or issues. Opportunities can be developed in one of three ways.
  1. Leading Practices - This approach leverages pre-existing intellectual capital with little to no modification. For example, an obvious opportunity for Electric Utility XYZ may be Electronic Bill Payment and Presentment (EBPP). This opportunity would be taken directly from pre-existing intellectual capital, e.g., the ICM database.
  2. Variation - This approach starts with Leading Practices and adds the customization and enhancements needed to satisfy the client’s unique business objectives, issues and unique environment. For example, a client’s general needs pointed the consulting team to a Leading Practice Opportunity around mobile messaging, but the Opportunity needed to be significantly modified to meet the client’s specific needs.
  3. Creation - This approach involves "creating" entirely new or significantly modified opportunities that are extended beyond the original intent, e.g., e-bay - new concept or online garage sale. For example, an Opportunity at a manufacturing company to deliver unique, first-of-a-kind services to end customers.

The approach(s) used to develop opportunities is based upon the specific client situation.  In cases where clients are focused on operational efficiency, the primary approach will most likely be Leading Practices and some Variation.  In cases where a company is trying to “change the industry,” the recommended approach is Variation and Creation.  It is not uncommon in engagements for all three approaches to be utilized.

Brief Outline

Opportunity Descriptions are presented using the “Opportunity Description Template.”   This template walks users through Value Exchange analysis and ensures thorough description of potential offerings.  The fields of the Opportunity Description Template include: opportunity name, opportunity description, players receiving value, value gained by the offering company, implications, assumptions, and processes involved or affected.  These fields should always be considered, although other fields may be added as appropriate for the engagement.

Key Considerations

The success of an engagement, and even the strategy as a whole, rests largely on accurate, rich, and complete opportunity descriptions.  Strong descriptions assist in capabilities analysis, business cases, initiative development, all the way through to design and implementation work.

An effective method for ensuring completeness, full understanding and buy-in is to assess temporary ownership of each Opportunity to a client team member (or extended team member).  This also helps to maintain interest and participation.
