Artifact: Process Capability Enabler Definition (BUS 404)
This Process Capability Enabler Definition work product is used to define and document the required capabilities of the process enablers.
Domains: Business Process
Work Product Kinds: Model
Purpose

This work product is produced to:

  • Define and prioritize what is required in terms of organization job roles & skills, knowledge/information and application capabilities in order to enable each of the required process capabilities
  • Agree on the priorities of the required capabilities of the process enablers
  • Drive the development of application requirement specifications for application architecture, selection and or for application development
  • Allow a cost/benefit assessment to be made
  • Enable issues to be resolved early and risks to be mitigated
  • Provide awareness of assumptions that have been made
Description
Main Description

The Process Capability Enabler Definition workproduct documents capabilities of processes related to :

  • Organization job roles/skills necessary to realize required process capabilities
  • Knowledge and/or information necessary to realize required process capabilities
  • Application functions necessary to realize required process capabilities

And, in addition will:

  • Document the risks (note: a risk is an unresolved issue)
  • Document the assumptions that have been made

This work product is prepared when the understanding of the business environment and required process capabilities has been documented. It will be prepared for each process capability identified as necessary to support the strategic capabilities.

Key Considerations
  • In a large project where multiple teams are working on different processes it is important to verify the detail and level of the documentation for consistency.  Since multiple people will be creating and writing the files, there is always a very high potential for the information to be documented at various levels of detail.  An example of a completed template should be provided, as well as, a quality check performed after each team member has completed one or two.
  • The process enabling requirements should be held and maintained centrally.  Individual project team members will be responsible for updating the work products allocated to themselves or to their team.  All team members should have display access to the full set.  This will help everyone to be informed about proposals made by other teams, especially regarding integration issues.
Tailoring
Impact of not having

If this work product is not produced, then:

  • The capability definitions for the process enablers may be defined at too high a level, and may not be measurable for the purpose of ensuring that the required strategic capabilities can be realized by the process design
  • The capability definitions are defined at too low a level, and become a "laundry list", difficult to place a business value on. The raw number of requirements becomes difficult to manage
  • The capability definitions are not placed in a business context, i.e., as part of a process; it becomes difficult for the process designer to remember "why" the requirement is important
  • The capability definitions cannot be easily "configured" into an efficient real-life scenario, the basis of a script for the demonstration or assessment of alternative solutions
  • The capability definitions are not understood at a level, which is useful once an application has been selected, i.e., for release planning and configuration
  • The issues cannot be resolved early, and become risks
  • The assumptions that have been made cannot be verified as true or false
Reasons for not needing

This work product will not be required when:

  • The capability definitions have been previously defined at a granular enough level and the issues and assumptions have also been documented
  • In small and simple process re-design engagements where the capabilities of the process enablers are clearly understood
