Domains: Business Process
Work Product Kinds: Measurement
Purpose

This work product is produced to:

  • Assess the client’s current process performance compared to other organizations or best practices, thus ensuring that the client will be able to identify improvements to existing processes and not maintain current performance levels
  • Help to define metrics to measure the performance of specific aspects of the business process (The scores on these measurements could be used in the future to perform a regular evaluation on the actual performance improvements against the plan or other organizations)
  • Identify the "word-class-standard" or "best-in-class" performance on specific aspects of the business process
  • Quantify and understand the positive or negative gap between the client’s and others’ performance on specific aspects of the business process
  • Provide information on business process changes that are possible or necessary in order to stay or become competitive
  • Support in establishing realistic and defendable targets for performance levels of specific aspects of the business process
Description
Main Description

The Process Benchmarks work product provides the results of benchmarks on the performance of specific business processes in an organization against the performance of comparable processes at the same organization, at competitors or at other organizations. The competitors and other organizations concerned most preferably include organizations that are perceived to be "best-in-class".

Benchmarking selects or defines metrics to quantify the performance of a business process in terms of efficiency and effectiveness. The measured results are compared with those of the selected other organizations. Based upon this comparison potential business process improvements are identified and related to the target values that are defined for the new or revised process and against which the success of the improvements can be measured.

Process Benchmarks usually contain spreadsheets and graphics showing the comparison of the clients’ selected measurements and those of the other selected organizations. Furthermore they contain narrative text and figures that explain differences between the client’s and the best performing organization’s process. The analysis if the comparison will suggest potential improvements in the process(es).

Brief Outline

Process benchmarking is typically captured in a table or matrix that contains the following types of information:


Name
Purpose/Description

Metric
The name of a measurement (return on sales, gross profit, sales/salesperson, etc.).

Calculation
The method used to determine a value for the metric.

Current Performance
The level, which the company is currently attaining.

Best In Class Performance
The best performance on this metric the team was able to find.

Improvement Target
The net change in the metric versus the current performance.

Key Considerations

A key question to answer at the beginning of the benchmarking effort is to determine when to use existing data and when to survey other companies.

This leads to an additional question as to how to approach companies to assure their participation?

Are there any guidelines on confidentiality, etc?

Validate critical information with the business area managers periodically.

When conducting any form of benchmarking with an external partner, ensure that all parties agree to abide by the "Benchmarking Code of Conduct", including restrictions on dispersion of information. Do we have the code of conduct somewhere available?

Ensure that there is a valid basis of comparison (comparing apples with apples) in terms of process scope, definition, activities, before performing any metric analysis of your process's performance with that contained in any database.

Refer to Advisory Tool what is advisory tool for information on metrics and benchmarks.

Use industry sources to obtain relevant metrics and benchmark information using tools that IBM has available.

The relevant business area managers need to validate the target companies, the current state measurements and the future targets.
Tailoring
Impact of not having

If this work product is not produced, then:

  • It would be necessary to judge the performance of the business process by using already available information, for this purpose the team could involve Subject Matter Experts and Consultants who have gained knowledge and experience elsewhere
  • The team could see publications describing success stories and best-in-class processes
  • The team could heavily involve management and end users responsible for the business process concerned and for the processes related to it to learn about possible improvements
  • If there would be no benchmark result or alternatives for that available
    • Improvement targets might be set too low (because the team missed significant developments) or too high (because competitors derive their competitive advantage from different capabilities than the team expected)
    • It would be difficult to determine priorities
  • It would be difficult to convince stakeholders that the improvement targets are realistic
Reasons for not needing

This work product is not required when:

  • The client has selected a business process for which the team cannot find processes in the client’s or other organizations that are sufficiently comparable to perform a benchmark
  • There is a general consensus that a business case to implement specific process improvements exists
