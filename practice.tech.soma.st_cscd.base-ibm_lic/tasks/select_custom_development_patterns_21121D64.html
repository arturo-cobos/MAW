Task: Select Custom Development Patterns
During this task, we will select the appropriate R4SC patterns based on the scope for the implementation based on R4SC.
Purpose
The purpose of this task is to select the appropriate custom development patterns and document the realization decisions made.

      Main Description
      During specification, for Custom development, we begin selecting the design patterns that are appropriate to support our specified services, components, and flows. There are variety of pattern selections offered depending on type of custom  development to support a J2EE, or .NET type of implementation.  Since SOMA is platform-agnostic, it will select the most suitable patterns based on types of custom development approach.  In 3.1, we will only focus on R4SC custom development pattern and other patterns that may be available, such as Enterprise Component Pattern.  We will further extend the capability to support other patterns in the following release.
      Steps
      Review Solution Specification

      The output of the various specification activities are examined here: Service Specification, Information Specification, and Sub-System Analysis. The specification provides the requirement for determining which R4SC patterns can be applied for the realization of the service components.

      This activity will augment the Component Specification Capability Pattern

      Determine Applicability of R4SC Service Component Pattern

      During this activity, based on the specifications, the applicability of the R4SC Service Component Pattern is determined. This is performed for all the components that are potential candidates for using the R4SC patterns.

      The Recipe for Service Component (R4SC) development pattern focuses on the components that implement the SOA Service on through to the integration with the operational systems, data sources, and other SOA services, themselves.  In cases where you are integrating SOA services to make a larger grained SOA service, the components of the R4SC will also be acting as the Consumer.  There are two patterns identified to support R4SC development, namely the OOAD type of implementation.  They are the Service Component Pattern and the Service Integration Pattern.  These two patterns may be used combinely to meet the needs of the development.


      Determine Applicability of R4SC Service Integration Pattern

      During this activity, based on the specifications, the applicability of the R4SC Service Integration Pattern is determined. This is performed for all the components that are potential candidates for using the R4SC patterns. It should be noted that the realization of a Service Component may potentially involve the application of R4SC Service Component Pattern as well the application of the R4SC Service Integration Pattern.

      The Recipe for Service Component (R4SC) development pattern focuses on the components that implement the SOA Service on through to the integration with the operational systems, data sources, and other SOA services, themselves.  In cases where you are integrating SOA services to make a larger grained SOA service, the components of the R4SC will also be acting as the Consumer.  There are two patterns identified to support R4SC development, namely the OOAD type of implementation.  They are the Service Component Pattern and the Service Integration Pattern.  These two patterns may be used combinely to meet the needs of the development.
