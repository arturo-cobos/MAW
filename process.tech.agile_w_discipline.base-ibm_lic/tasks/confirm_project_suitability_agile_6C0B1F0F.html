Task: Confirm Project Suitability for Agile
This task describes how to determine if or to what extent a project is suited for an agile approach.
Purpose

Evaluate the project to determine if it a suitable fit to the Agile development approach.

Relationships
RolesPrimary Performer:
  • Project Manager
Additional Performers:
Outputs
  • Project Suitability Assessment
    Main Description

    There are often barriers to agility within and across the traditional business and application development organizations. It is recommended that a formal assessment or barriers to agility workshop be conducted prior to, or in parallel with, identifying one or more pilot projects using the steps in the steps section. This assessment can help to establish the sponsorship required for successful agile adoption.

    AWD Project Teams have developed perspective and assets around decelerators, and how to identify and plan for decelerator avoidance. For example, while experience shows that the daily stand-up meeting is a very effective means of identifying current or very imminent blockers so they can be addressed, deceleration avoidance techniques promote earlier identification, planning and action before issues materialize. Decelerator avoidance and other related consulting assets can be obtained from the AIS Accelerated Solution Delivery Practice.

    Steps
    Review guideline
    Review the "Project Suitability Assessment" guideline.
    Hold meetings with customers
    Hold meetings with customers to understand high-level business needs and requirements, along with tactical and strategic priorities, and to make an evaluation.
    Gather project information
    Gather high-level information on each project, product, value proposition, infrastructure, technology, team and accelerator usage to perform this evaluation.
    Document the decision as to whether to precede using AWD
    Document the final reasoning on whether or not the project was suitable based on the detailed suitability assessment for that project.
