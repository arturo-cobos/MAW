Task: Develop Statement of Work
For engagements between IBM and a client, or to add formality to a client approval process, a statement of work is developed to formally define the in-scope iterations.
Purpose
To formally document the responsibilities of the various parties which will be involved in the project delivery.

        Main Description

        The Statement of Work (SOW) is the agreement between IBM and a client, or a more formal agreement between the client project delivery organization and the client business unit.

        The SOW is drafted and refined throughout the open phase to document the scope and assumptions of the first Definition Iteration, and perhaps multiple Development Iterations, if they can be defined to some level of agreement, with supporting assumptions.

        The SOW may be modified throughout the Definition phase, along with the Agile Project Definition and Charter, to adjust the agreement based on the elaboration of the solution.

        Key Considerations
        If the stakeholder will not accept a fixed priced, variable scope approach defined in the statement of work, then a Time and Materials approach can be used to mitigate risk to the delivery team (vs. commit to fixed price, fixed time, fixed scope).
