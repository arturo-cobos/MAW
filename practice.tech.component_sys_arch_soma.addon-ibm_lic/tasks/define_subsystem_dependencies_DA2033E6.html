Task: Define Subsystem Dependencies
A Sub system consists of: 1) Functional Components 2) Technical Components 3) Service Components and their interactions.
Purpose
Decide how to build a system by understanding the dependencies between its subsystems. A subsystem depends on other subsystems and relies on them to provide functionality that it relies on in order to fulfill its own processing.

      Main Description

      A subsystem dependency diagram is created to show the subsystems that a given subsystem relies on. This diagram shows the mutual interrelationships of a number of subsystems. This information is necessary when constructing a system out of parts were subsystems because the dependencies can show the order of construction: the subsystems that you rely on have to be built first before you can utilize them in the construction of your own subsystem. If the construction of those subsystems are beyond your reach or influence with in an organization or relies on external organizations over which you exercise no control over then considerations have to be made such as applying the virtual provider pattern.

      This activity of Subsystem Analysis is to formulate the dependencies and associations between subsystems. A subsystem that relies on a service from another subsystem is said to be dependent on that subsystem. This includes dependencies on data that may be accessed via services that provide the required data. 

      There are three types of dependency relationship:

      Post-condition dependency: another service requires this service to execute successfully.
      Processing dependency: another service invocation is required to complete the successful execution of the current service.

      Pre-condition dependency: another service invocation must have executed successfully before the current invocation can begin execution.

      Key Considerations
      The availability of subsystems you depend on are essential in order to construct your own subsystem.  Therefore, technical and business considerations off availability of those subsystems become significant in estimating when your component can be completed. You may consider different alternatives for constructing the subsystems that you rely on out of existing legacy systems rather than building new subsystems from scratch, leveraging legacy applications or purchasing ready-made applications, etc.
