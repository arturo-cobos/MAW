Task: Assign Services to Service Components
Allocate/assign services to suitable service components that will realize the services. A service is realized by a service component. A service component may realize more than one service.
Purpose
To assign services to service components so that they can be realized.

    Main Description

    The organization of services and their assignments to their component containers for realization of functionality is called 'service allocation'. Allocating services to service components drives the organization of services, based on the service component that will ultimately be responsible for implementing, managing and securing the services.

    Service components realize services in a SOA; otherwise service descriptions are merely externalized interfaces with no implementation. Within the service component, functional components provide application specific logic such as Reserve Vehicle, Check-out Vehicle, etc. Technical components provide non application specific (i.e. generic functionality) such as Authentication, Authorization, Audit, Logging, etc.

    A service implementation by an service component often needs both functional and technical components to provide business functionality. For example, a user may need to be authenticated (technical component) before a reservation can be made (functional component).

    ISV Considerations: The catalog of ISV (and legacy) services may be cross referenced at this time to perform a preliminary alignment of exposed services with services that are planned to be realized within the package. Services should be cross-referenced with the services that an ISV exposes to leverage the opportunity to reuse existing functionality.
