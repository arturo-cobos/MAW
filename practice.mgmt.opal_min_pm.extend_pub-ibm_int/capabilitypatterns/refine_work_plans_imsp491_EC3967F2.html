Main Description

Document the preliminary milestone list and the known dependencies, and also develop the staffing plan.  Analyze assigned duties to verify and documents the existence of an appropriate separation of duties, ensures that actions are taken, appropriate documentation exists incase of separation of duties conflict and obtains approval on the separation of duties matrix and related artifacts. Ensure that the risks are up-to-date and that a high-level schedule exists, so the project can be planned and started with a clear understanding of these items.

This task includes the following steps:

  • Document the preliminary milestone & develop staffing plan
  • Analyze assigned duties and obtain approval
  • Review the risks and build project management schedule
Steps
Document the preliminary milestone & develop staffing plan
  • Document the preliminary milestone list, including known dependencies
  • Develop the staffing plan
    • Documenting the high-level staffing plan which shows the resources required by time period, for example week, month or quarter, by role through the life of the project
    • Defining technical skill requirements by role, where there are unique requirements
    • Identifying skill gaps and the plan to address them
    • Recording the organization and people information in the human resource plan

The key outputs of this task are the Project Management Schedule and Milestone List.  Additional output may include the Human Resource Plan, which may be part of the Project Management System Summary.

Analyze assigned duties and obtain approval
  • Use the separation of duties matrix to analyze the assigned duties and verify that appropriate Separation of Duties (SOD) exists. 
    • The IBM Framework of Internal Controls (FICs) Key Controls over Operations (KCOs) for Application Services mandates a clear Separation of Duties (SOD) is documented by each Project or Application, or each group of small support and maintenance Projects or Applications.
    • Refer to the 'Required Separation of Duties Table' below for information about the duties that should be separated (that is to be assigned to different staff members.)
  • If a separation of duties is not possible and a SOD conflict does exist, the following must be in place:
Secondary control
Note: A secondary control may be that another Subject Matter Expert (SME) reviews the artifact for where the SOD conflict exists.
  • Artifacts to support the secondary control
    Note: In the example above the supporting artifacts will be the completed project quality plan to verify that the peer review has been planned and the work product inspection form to verify that the reviews have been completed.
  • A risk raised and managed as required
  • If an SOD conflict exists and there is no secondary control in place, any actions that are required to implement secondary controls must be transferred to the project status report.
Note: The completed separation of duties matrix is required to be reviewed, and revised if required, at least annually or whenever.

The key output of this task is the Separation of Duties Matrix.  Additional output and input may include the Human Resource Plan and the Project Quality Plan, which both may be part of the Project Management System Summary.

Review the risks and build project management schedule
  • Review the risks, creating or updating the Risk Management Plan (or PMSS) and Risk Definition (or Event Log)
  • Build the high-level Project Management Schedule, which will be expanded when starting the project

The key outputs of this task are the Project Management Schedule; the Risk Management Plan which may be part of the Project Management System Summary (PMSS); and the Risk Definition which may be part of the Event Log (risk tab).
