Main Description

The Supplier Selection Report documents how a particular supplier was chosen to provide products or services to the project. It includes both a description of the selection process and the results of executing that process.

Sometimes it is decided to use a single supplier. In this case, the Supplier Selection Report documents the justification for this decision.

Key Considerations

Usually, the project management team and the purchasing department have a joint responsibility for supplier selection and the supplier selection procedure is often dictated by purchasing department policies.

Generally, supplier selection takes place in two stages:

  • A list of candidate suppliers is drawn up. A formal request for information (RFI) may be issued to help identify suitable candidates.
  • The candidates submit proposals, perhaps in response to a formal request for proposal (RFP), which (possibly, with further information) are used by to choose one supplier.

The Candidate selection component of the Supplier Selection Report is written to document stage 1 and the final selection component is completed during stage 2.

Validation and verification requirements are defined by the policies and procedures of the delivery organization's purchasing unit.

The Supplier Selection Report would typically be reviewed by the top-level project manager and the delivery organization's purchasing unit.

Supplier Selection Reports may be subject to formal audit.
