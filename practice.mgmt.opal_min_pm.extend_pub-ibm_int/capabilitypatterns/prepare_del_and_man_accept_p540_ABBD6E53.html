Task Descriptor: Prepare delivery and manage acceptance of deliverable (IMSP540)
This task verifies that deliverables are ready and actually delivered to a sponsor and the sponsor is ready to receive and acknowledge them. This task also monitors the sponsor's acceptance of deliverables, analyzes and responds to any findings by the sponsor, such as controversial defects.

Main Description

This task is based on the WWPMM work pattern HD3: Prepare delivery and HD4: Manage acceptance.
Steps
Prepare delivery

This step is launched when a delivery date is close (for example, a few weeks away). These delivery dates are extracted from the milestone schedule in the sponsor Agreement. They are also part of the Deliverable Definition(s) related to this delivery.

To make sure the delivery will occur on time and with the expected content, the progress of deliverable completion must be monitored closely. Work may be reassigned, if necessary. Sponsor readiness to receive the deliverable must also be assessed to ensure it will not negatively impact the project plans.

Formal deliverable reviews are usually performed before delivery to ensure that no open items remain and that it is ready to be delivered. This is normally defined in the business process of the delivery organization and is required by the international standards for quality assurance. If necessary, assure that any rework needed is compliant with the time constraints. Synchronize the work throughout the project structure.

Formally document and acknowledge release of deliverables to the sponsor. Formal release occurs when there is an acknowledgment of the delivery, as specified in the sponsor Agreement. This task is performed during both the final stages of the project team's testing/verification activities as well as the final internal validation of the deliverables that are part of the technical work.

The key outputs of this task are the Delivery Control Documentation (to the sponsor), and Quality Review Documentation.  Small or less complex project may choose to use the Event Log to record Findings and associated Issues.

The task includes the following:

  • Assess progress of deliverable completion
  • Assess readiness of sponsor to receive deliverable
  • Assess conformance to sponsor acceptance criteria
  • Formally notify sponsor of delivery
Manage Acceptance

This step is launched each time a deliverable has been released to the sponsor and the sponsor's acceptance must be obtained. It is important to monitor the sponsor's (or his representative's) validation of the deliverable to be sure that progress is being made and that any rework of deliverables can be done promptly. This will facilitate obtaining the sponsor's acceptance as soon as possible.

Ensure sponsor acceptance activities begin as scheduled and committed in the Agreement. Ensure that all sponsor findings are being researched and resolved. Determine if corrective actions or issues should be raised.

The key outputs of this task are the Delivery Control Documentation, the Findings Log (replies and decisions), and the Operational Schedule. Small or less complex project may choose to use the Event Log to record findings and associated issues or actions.

The task includes the following:

  • Monitor sponsor's validation process to ensure acceptance on time
  • Analyze and respond to sponsor findings
  • Re-release and obtain formal sponsor acceptance
  • Archive deliverable and return sponsor components
  • Initiate invoicing for delivery acceptance (if delivery is a payment milestone)
