Activity: Build agreement with supplier (IMSP491 and IMSP711)
This activity will negotiate the commitments of a supplier for a project and formalize them in a supplier Agreement.
Extends: Build agreement with supplier (IMSP491 and IMSP711)
DescriptionWork Breakdown StructureTeam AllocationWork Product Usage
Purpose

To select the appropriate supplier and negotiate their commitments, with a plan to deliver products, services, or resources to the project on-time and on-budget while minimizing related risks and issues.

Relationships
Parent Activities
  • Planning
Description

This activity is based on the WWPMM work pattern P2: Build agreement with supplier.

This activity is launched when the definition of the solution to be produced, or the project organization to deliver the solution, are designed to make use of products, services, or resources that will be procured from external suppliers.

It encompasses selecting suppliers, and obtaining from them commitments on a price, a delivery time frame, and acceptance criteria, contingent upon the project being confirmed by the sponsor.

Each project organizational unit (POU) may need to launch this activity for one or more potential supplier or subcontractor.

Note that having dependencies on external suppliers is an important risk factor for the project. On the other hand, it may be a good equation for the project economy. Thus, this activity must typically be executed in compliance with the policies and procedures for procurement in the related performing organizations and should be customized accordingly.

During a selling process, as described in the Client Value Method (CVM), this activity interacts with the phase "Develop and agree to client solution" within the bid phase.

This activity includes the following:

  • Select supplier, working with the purchasing department to improve the match between the project and the supplier
    • Identify possible suppliers and define the criteria to be used in selecting one or a short list
    • Work with the purchasing department to proceed to the appropriate process for receiving supplier offers
    • Analyze and rate their proposals from the project point of view
    • Come to an agreement with the purchasing department on the offer to be selected and document in the Supplier Selection Report
  • Build Agreement with supplier
    • Determine how to manage the supplier's performance and the key checkpoints to control progress and quality
    • Identify the internal (project) and external dependencies
    • Identify the inspection and acceptance procedures
    • Review the statement of work, planned milestones, and obligations that constitute the agreement; identify and resolve conflicts
    • Obtain commitments from supplier
