Activity: Close the Project (IMSP240 and IMSP711)
This activity will review and assess the completion of all commitments between stakeholders (sponsors or suppliers), including obtaining sponsor feedback, and perform the administrative closure of an agreement (or contract) with a sponsor or supplier, at the end of a project.
Extends: Close the Project (IMSP240 and IMSP711)
DescriptionWork Breakdown StructureTeam AllocationWork Product Usage
Purpose

This activity is performed to assess the completion of mutual obligations by all stakeholders, followed by administrative closure of the agreement or contract.

In a multi-GDC engagement, the PM in the Primary GDC is accountable to ensure Project Close activities for the entire GDC (Primary & Supporting) are executed.

KCO-809:

Completion of Effort Migration to Production - Client and IBM management approval is obtained prior to promoting code and applications into production and confirm the client approval for completion of IBM effort.  A data migration plan is used to document the process and activities for migration to production.

 1. Final acceptance from sponsor - Properly documented approval for completion of effort by IBM and Client management.

KCO 811, KCO 812, KCO 813, KCO-814, KCO 815, KCO 816, KCO 818:



DRA and Security Planning - Data Security & Privacy risk assessment provides a means to identify the baseline foundational  DS&P risks and issues that may be encountered during the project, and through systematic planning address and manage the risks and issues inherent in the project.



Staff Management On-Boarding and Off-Boarding - Ensure that there is a defined set of activities that are executed or completed as workforce members join and leave the project to assure they understand the project requirements.



Management Review/ Activities -  Ensure that management has reviewed and evaluated evidence of compliance with Data Security & Privacy controls required per the DS&P Security Plan, and taken corrective action to mitigate issues identified



Quarterly/ Annual revalidation  compliance for Access, Passwords - Ensure controls exist for routine access revalidation of continued business need and employment verification for access to production or non production environments on a quarterly or annual basis as outlined in the User ID Administration process.



Monitoring Activities for Production, privileged and Shared or emergency IDs - Ensure that actions performed by IBM workforce members  with privileged production access rights  (including greater than read access to production,, or when shared or emergency Ids are used)  are monitored in accordance with the DS&P Plan or User ID administration plan



Data protection management in Application environments and during testing - Data protection techniques are employed when IBM has access to or controls the environment or applications where PI/SPI/ BSI exists



User ID Access Administration - Ensure controls exist for granting and maintaining access to client systems, applications, tools, databases, and environments (for example: develop, test, quality, etc.). Ensure the appropriate controls exist for removal of the access to systems, applications, tools, databases, and environments containing PI/ SPI/ BSI

 1. Evidence of timely removal of access (or notification) for terminated workforce members (within 24 hours), or reassigned employees (within 72 hours)


2. Compliance with offboarding requirements
