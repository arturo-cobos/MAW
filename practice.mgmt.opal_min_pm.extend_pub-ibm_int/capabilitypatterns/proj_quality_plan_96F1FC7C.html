Work Product Descriptor (Artifact): Project Quality Plan
The project quality plan describes all the activities and other arrangements intended to achieve the planned level of quality for technical work products and project management work products.

Main Description
The Project Quality Plan describes the organization, activities, and quality contributors that have been put in place to achieve the planned level of quality for technical, project management, and services work products.

Key Considerations

The business unit or competency of the delivery organization will typically provide a standard quality plan that satisfies the quality requirements for its organization.

Typically, one Project Quality Plan is created by the top-level project organizational unit to address the entire project. The top-level project organizational unit identifies quality criteria associated with all deliverables. Quality contributors and assurance checkpoints are also identified by the top-level unit. This cascades down to project organizational units within the organizational breakdown structure. Each project organizational unit may add to or expand the quality criteria, and also assign additional quality contributors and quality assurance steps to cover the work at its level.

A health review will typically be performed on the Project Quality Plan to ensure it is complete, unambiguous, reflective of the work planned in the Agreement and consistent with business unit and competency quality procedures. Quality assurance compliance and health reviews conducted during project execution should ensure that the Project Quality Plan is maintained throughout the life of the project.
