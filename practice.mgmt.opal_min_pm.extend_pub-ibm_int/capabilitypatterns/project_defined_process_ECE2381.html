Work Product Descriptor (Artifact): Project Defined Process
This work product describes the process that a project is to follow in order to produce the project's desired results.

Main Description

A project process typically describes or references the following items:

  • what organizational processes and policies must be adhered to
  • what standard process, if any, is being adopted by the project
  • any tailoring of the standard process, or deviations from policy mandates
  • rationale for tailoring and deviations (and approvals if required)
  • which work products are reviewed at which milestones, and their level of completion
  • guidelines and information that the project wants to use in addition to the information contained in the main process
  • what reviews will be performed, and their level of formality
  • what approvals are required, by whom, and when

This artifact lists a project's adopted process assets and documents the results of the project tailoring activities, which includes details about the adoption, un-adoption or tailoring of the organizational process assets and the associated decisions, explanations and rationale, it provides:

  • guidance on the potentially applicable assets, based on work classification
  • a record of the adopted, un-adopted and tailored process assets
  • identification of common, organization-specific and/or client-specific required process assets
  • a record of the process-related tailoring decisions, cross-referenced where applicable to authorized policy or process exceptions (for non-standard tailoring)
  • a record of the review by, and authorization from, the organization's authorized approvers
