Main Description
The purpose of domain modeling and decomposition, is to partition and decompose the scope of the project from the perspective of functional areas, processes, information, rules and policies as well as the variations and commonality encountered in difference in our new types within the domain and to identify candidate services from complimentary sources.

Domain Modeling and Decomposition (DMD) identifies five fundamental constructs, namely, service, flows/processes, information, components, rules and policies, for building solution based on service oriented principles. Domain modeling and decomposition partitions and decomposes domains into functional areas and to identify processes, information, rules and policies as well as the variations and commonality encountered in difference in our new types within the domain and identifies services required for the scoped based on the process, information, rules and variations.
