Concept: Service Orientation
Describes the concepts in service orientation.

Main Description

Service Orientation (SO) is a paradigm and has set of values and principles. Core values are business value over technical strategy, strategic goals over project-specific benefits intrinsic interoperability over custom integration, shared services over specific-purpose implementations, flexibility over optimization, and evolutionary refinement over pursuit of initial perfection. See detail at SOA Manifesto.

Where as, Service Oriented Architecture (SOA) is an architectural style that enables the service orientation paradigm and associated principles. It is a way of building software; a way of constructing the foundations of how you construct software within your organization, or how your clients construct software within their organization. An SOA is an enterprise-scale IT architecture, meaning that it provides a set of artifacts that should be maintained and governed and considered to be enterprise-level assets. Now, whether they are used at a simple project level or a line-of-business level or used in the interactions between business partners—whatever scale and scope they are used in—still they need to be funded, maintained, and governed, and they should be considered as enterprise assets. That is why we talk of SOA as an enterprise-scale IT architecture. Now, it is an enterprise-scale IT architecture, but it is a special one in which we are able to invoke and link resources on demand. When we need a resource, when we need some functionality, wherever that functionality is, if it is on the same machine, if it is on another machine that a business partner has across the world over the Internet, you can access that information using a service call; one implementation of an SOA is Web Services; Web Services is the most prevalent way to implement services at this point of time. Remember that SOA is the general case, and Web services are a special implementation of SOA. So you could implement SOA in multiple ways and shapes and forms. These resources that can be invoked and linked on demand are represented typically as business-aligned capabilities or services which can participate and be composed into applications. So these services or business capabilities can be choreographed in a workflow, and that is essentially what your application would constitute. These applications can participate and engage at various levels of scope. It can be an application at the value-net, at the value chain level, at the enterprise level, a line of business, or indeed a small project. They are all geared towards fulfilling business needs. And the primary structuring element of SOA-based applications is the service, not the object, not the component, not the subsystem or system, but the service.

In brief, a Service-Oriented Architecture (SOA) is an enterprise-scale IT architecture to invoke and link resources as needed (on demand):

  • These resources are represented as business aligned services, which can participate and be composed into applications participating in many levels of scope: a value-net, enterprise, or line of business to fulfill business needs.
  • The primary structuring element of SOA-based applications is the service, which is a business capability accessible through a specific architectural style, as opposed to subsystems, systems, or components.

And where a Service is defined as:

  • From business perspective: A service is a well defined, encapsulated, and business-aligned capability.
  • From IT perspective: A service is a discoverable, invokable software resource having a service description / interface, and configurable using policies. The service description is available for searching, binding, and invocation by a service consumer. The service description implementation is realized through a service provider who delivers quality of service requirements for the service consumer.
