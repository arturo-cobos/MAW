Task: Discover and Incorporate Assets
Identify and discover existing assets that have the potential of being leveraged to realize one or more elements of a service analysis, design or implementation. Rather than building the asset from scratch, consider searching for, discovering and incorporating the asset as is or with modifications.
Purpose
To facilitate and expedite the process of building a service-oriented architecture by leveraging existing assets rather than creating them from scratch.  This particular step involves the attempt to discover and find a relevant asset that can be a candidate for fulfilling a requirement for a given asset type.  For example, services for the banking industry may be searched for in a business service repository for the banking industry.  If such a service meets the qualifications required by the project, consider incorporating the asset rather than building it from scratch.

      Roles: Business Analyst,  Architect

      Customize business process (Activities within a Business Process) to meet organizations' need

      As part of customization, each activity within the scoped process should be analyzed for its applicability and validity to the project under analysis. This will result in the addition of new activities, and the removal of unnecessary existing activities from the business process. Note: An activity should always produce consistent output triggers in any process context. If conflicting outputs are required, then a new activity should be specified. Each activity should be checked to determine whether it is a manual activity, a front end application task, or a back-end system task Next key aspect of customization of business processes is to identify control and data flows. Control flows bring together the Initiating (Input) and Completing (Output) Trigger Logic analyzed earlier. Data Flows can be deduced from the detailed description of activities.
