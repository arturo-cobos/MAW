Guideline: Rollout Checklist
This paper helps practitioners discover customer needs that can further define the rollout scope as well as identify additional IBM Services opportunity.
Main Description

1 Context

The paper provides examples of the probing questions to ask customers to define their current organizations capabilities and requirements. The answers can then be provided to an IRM specialist who can help the team to determine which solution areas are of the next highest priority.

2 Steps

Prior to completing the Rollout Checklist, a practitioner should become familiar with the information in this paper, perform some initial probing or observing of the customer environment, and engage an IRM specialist.  The following activities should occur:

  • The practitioner should observe and explore the customer environment to determine the scope and if there are additional opportunities.  Depending upon the relationship the practitioner has with the customer, this could include visiting customer sites and operations and interviewing staff, or it could simply be a discussion with the Account Representative. 
  • The practitioner should schedule interview(s) with the customer. The interviews can include the CEO, CIO, CFO, Marketing management or other staff as appropriate to determine business direction and initiatives.
  • The practitioner should ask probing questions and maintain a written record of the answers provided by the customer(s).  Sometimes, it will be more appropriate to provide the customer with a questionnaire for completion prior to the interviews. 
  • The sample questions under “Examples” can be used as a starting point. The practitioner can add more questions as required to determine customer requirements, the scope, and additional service opportunities.
  • The practitioner can involve the regional Infrastructure Resource Management specialist during the discussion with the customer, or can request assistance from the IRM specialist to assess the results obtained from the customer.
  • The information obtained from the customer should be assessed and categorized into services requested by the customer, services needed to complete the solution, and future services required by the customer.
  • Review the results with the Account Rep, Principal or Consultant assigned to the customer, make revisions, if necessary.
  • Validate the findings with the customer.
  • Use the information to refine the scope, prioritize the services identified, and identify the next areas of services that will be needed by the customer.

4 Examples

The following questions were developed to aid in determining rollout project scope as well as additional follow-on and/or starburst opportunities associated with Infrastructure Resources Management.  They follow the ITS Blueprint and are organized according to IRM offering elements, ITM offering capabilities, and additional ITS offerings that are typically integrated with IRM.

4.1 Background

The ITS Blueprint is the basis for the service offering discussion.  It uses the assumption that IRM provides the umbrella or holistic approach to the following offering elements and capabilities:

IRM offering elements

  • Procurement
  • Configuration (PDP)
  • Rollout / deployment
  • Move, add, change
  • Tracking and reporting
  • Wall-to-wall inventory
  • Disposition
  • Remote infrastructure support

IRM key offering capabilities:

  • Asset Management
  • Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)
  • Help desk
  • IT Architecture

Other ITS key offerings and services that are closely linked to IRM include the following:

  • Technology Support Services category
  • Maintenance for IBM and OEM hardware and software
  • Hardware services
  • Software Services

Infrastructure and Systems Management category

  • Systems management solution services, i.e. out-tasked support services (e.g. help desk, remote system monitoring)
  • Systems management consulting and design services (specifically change, problem, and configuration management)

4.2 Checklist Questions

The following questions were developed to aid in determining current project scope as well as additional follow-on and / or starburst opportunities associated with IRM.  They are organized according to IRM offering elements, IRM offering capabilities, and additional ITS offerings that are typically integrated with IRM.

NOTE:  There are a number of duplicate questions due to integration and interrelationships between offerings and capabilities

IRM offering elements

Procurement

  • How effectively are your assets supporting your business goals?
  • Are your vendors leveraged and meeting contractual obligations?
  • How easily can you rapidly purchase new assets?
  • How rapidly can you deploy new assets and dispose of old assets?
  • Do you need to simplify, standardize, support and control the processes and technology to procure assets?
  • What financial elements would need to be updated?

Configuration (PDP)

  • How is new software distributed?
  • How many configurations do you maintain and support?
  • What testing procedures do you employ?
  • Have you identified any platform / software changes required to support current or future business initiatives?

Rollout / deployment

  • How rapidly can you deploy new assets and dispose of old assets?
  • Do you need assistance with deployment of technology on time within budget?  Within the US?  Globally?
  • Are you seeking assistance with adhoc IMACs?
  • Where are the assets located?  What is the replacement criteria?
  • What are the linkages between your customer processes, internal processes, and any out tasking / vendor services that you employ?
  • Have you defined the schedule and associated roles and responsibilities? 
  • What success criteria has been identified?
  • Are a static number of sites going to be installed and how many? (IE 1203 locations)
  • Do you have a schedule in mind for the deployment? (IE 3 months)
  • Do you know approximately how much equipment will go into those sites. ( 5 pc 1 printer per loc)

Move, add, and change

  • How accurate is your inventory?  What methods do you use to update the inventory?
  • Does your problem management technology integrate with asset management life cycle technology?
  • Does your change management technology integrate with asset management life cycle technology?

Tracking and reporting

  • Are you effectively sharing business data across the enterprise (financial, configuration, demographics?)
  • What trending analysis is performed to determine technology refresh requirements?

Wall-to-wall inventory

  • What is your method for baselining and updating your inventory?
  • What technology do you use to aid in this process?

Disposition

  • How effectively are your assets supporting your business goals?
  • Are your vendors leveraged and meeting contractual obligations?
  • How easily can you rapidly purchase new assets?
  • How rapidly can you dispose of old assets?
  • Do you need to simplify, standardize, support and control the processes and technology to dispose of  assets?
  • What financial elements would need to be updated?

Remote infrastructure support

• What infrastructure support is out-tasked?
• What improvements are required to your current support?
• How do you distribute software upgrades and new software?

IRM offering capabilities:

Total Cost of Ownership:

  • Do you have accurate records that let you know where all of your assets are and what they are costing you?  If not, how much do you think that it is costing you?
  • Do you have underutilized technology cost?
  • Do you need to reduce total IT spending in support of business initiatives?

IT Architecture:

  • Does your IT architecture support rapid change and growth?
  • Do you know if your assets can support new strategic business applications?
  • Do you need to evaluate technology to consolidate and streamline the infrastructure resource management architecture?
  • Do you need to re-examine and rationalize application platforms?

Asset Management:

  • How effectively are your assets supporting your business goals?
  • Are your vendors leveraged and meeting contractual obligations?
  • Are you effectively sharing business data across the enterprise (financial, configuration, demographics?)
  • How easily can you rapidly purchase new assets?
  • How rapidly can you deploy new assets and dispose of old assets?
  • Do you need to simplify, standardize, support and control the processes and technology to manage assets?
  • What financial elements would need to be updated?  For example:
  • Assets not completely capitalized (active and inactive)
  • Assets not returned at end of lease
  • Maintenance contracts make a payment

Help Desk:

  • Does your help desk provide a SPOC for asset management and maintenance requests?
  • Does your problem management technology integrate with asset management life cycle technology?
  • Does your change management technology integrate with asset management life cycle technology?

Key ITS Offerings and Services

Technical Support Services:

Maintenance

  • Do you need to better manage service level agreements?
  • Do you need to better track required maintenance to make informed decisions regarding technology refreshes?
  • Do you need to centralize management of multiple vendor hardware/software and associated upgrades/ fixes?

Hardware

  • What hardware upgrades do you anticipate?
  • What platforms are in place?
  • Who maintains these systems?

Software

  • What software is in place?
  • What upgrades do you anticipate?
  • Who maintains these systems?

Systems management solution services

  • Are you interested in outtasking any of your support services?
  • If you outtask now, what improvements in service have you identified?
  • What technology is in place to help automate systems management tasks?
  • Is technology fully integrated among operational and support processes?

Systems management consulting and design services

Change management

  • Is your asset inventory accurate?
  • Do you effectively and efficiently manage, track, & control installs, moves, adds, changes?
  • How rigorous is your change management processes & procedures?
  • What is the long-term impact of your capabilities in this area?

Problem management

  • Are problems reported accurately with sufficient performance of first-level problem determination to avoid unnecessary repair calls by field technicians?
  • What is the procedure required to determine that on-site assistance is required?
  • Who maintains your equipment?  Are there any improvements you’re seeking in this area?

Configuration management

  • How many configurations do you maintain and support?
  • What testing procedures do you employ?
  • Have you identified any platform / software changes required to support current or future business initiatives?
  • Have you evaluated impacts regarding your rollout plans?
