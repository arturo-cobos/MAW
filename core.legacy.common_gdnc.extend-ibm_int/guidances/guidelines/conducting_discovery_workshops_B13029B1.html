Guideline: Conducting Discovery Workshops
Main Description

Description

The KM Discovery Workshop (DW) is a tool-driven session focused on identifying a clear direction for application of Knowledge Management (KM) in the client's organization. The Discovery Workshop is designed to simplify the gathering and collection of information, through a transition from theoretical enthusiasm to concrete ideas. The session will enable participants to understand the major issues associated with Knowledge Management, discover what the value of KM is to their organization and agree how their company needs to proceed in undertaking a Knowledge Management (KM) initiative.

The workshop consists of a series of presentations, team-building, experiential and collaborative sessions presented through a set of steps and exercises. This workshop enables participants to carry out simultaneous discussions, vote, etc. in a fast moving, anonymous way, thus facilitating the achievement of true-commitment and buy-in. Tailoring the workshop to meet client needs and requirements is encouraged.

Note: It may be useful to review the e-business Focal Area ("Killer-e") Workshop technique paper. It documents alternative and helpful techniques for determining how e-business (or KM) can be used to change the nature of business relationships.

  1. Perform Consultant Team debrief
  2. Generate a summary document for participants

    Conduct a Research Visit to gather data for proposal and work plan development

Benefits

  • Minimal investment to identify a pilot project opportunity
  • Rapid process and decision
  • Know whether your organization should do anything and what you should do
  • Agreed way ahead - plan

Target Audience

The target audience are Senior Business Executives, KM Team or the key stakeholders.  The emphasis here is on business, since it’s the business that needs to go under the magnifying glass in order to identify any obvious KM opportunities in the company.

Target Buyer

  • Business sponsors of a potential KM initiative
  • Manager who wishes to get moving with KM, but doesn’t know how to start
  • Individual in an organization with a budget to pay for a DW, is “devoted” to KM, and is seeking senior-level commitment and buy-in

Pre-Workshop Steps

Step 1 – Identify Participants

In order for a KM project to succeed, it is important to have the right people involved.  The following list highlights what should be regarded as "required" criteria for participation in a KM project. 

Engaging those who understand the impact of such issues like collaboration and innovation, and who represent lead cost and revenue drivers for the business will lead to the success of a KM project.

Make your selections wisely!

  1. Identify groups within the client’s organization to participate in the KM initiative based on the following criteria:
    •  High-value at the core of the group
    •  Strong, passionate sponsorship within it
    • Globally dispersed (unless that poses an unrealistic challenge
    • Workable size
    • Represents or controls a major cost driver for the business
    • Represents or controls a major revenue driver for the business
    • Strategically significant to the business
    • Willing and able to undertake a KM project
    • Agreement from executive sponsor
  2. Identify individuals within the client’s organization to participate in the KM initiative based on the following criteria:
    • Subject matter expertise (of the work done by their group)
    • Respected by their peers
    • Proactive participation
    • Authority to undertake a pilot KM project
    • Interested in KM

Step 2 – Perform a Background Interview And Refine Candidate List (collect information on participant’s issues)

There should be an unbroken chain of sponsorship in place with the client.  Individuals should be briefed by their management prior to conducting interviews.

Avoid using an interview guide for this step.  This should be more of a discussion to obtain the insights of the participant.

Note:  There may be a need to iterate between Steps 1 & 2 as information and insights gathered in the interviews may warrant revisiting group and individual selection.

Schedule conference calls with candidates

  • Quick brief on what is KM and their business
  • What comes to mind with KM
  • How do you work?  (a day in the life of a …)

Review shelf documentation (org charts, etc.)

Update client sponsor

Produce a purpose statement in conjunction with IBM rep and client interface

Step 3 – Schedule Workshop

Like all workshops we conduct, consider which times of the day and particular days of the week will be most effective in a given client environment (e.g., do not schedule a workshop for a Friday afternoon prior to a holiday weekend).

Be sure you clearly communicate the meeting room needs to your client including supplies, audio-visual equipment, refreshments, etc.  (Materials required to conduct each exercise are included in each of the exercise tools presentations which can be found on the KM ICM database).  Where possible, have the client make the necessary room arrangements.

Also, it is good practice to keep the number of participants for each workshop to 4 – 8.

  • Premised on IBM consultant coming out for one trip to do all the workshops (assume 4 hours per exercise)
  • Identify key people within groups and check their availability
  • Other invitees at request of client interface
  • Client to handle meeting room arrangements and logistics
  • Letter to sponsor with three selected groups and their available dates
  • Develop simple kickoff packet including letter to participants from client sponsor

Step 4 – Invite Attendees

This step is most effectively executed by the client sponsor and/or the individual's manager.  Be prepared to help your client craft an appropriate invitation for distribution to the desired participants.

Sample Workshop…

  • Discovery Workshops
  • Staffed by lead facilitator (Rich) and a co-facilitator (Nancy).  
  • "Burning Building" exercise to identify key pieces of knowledge.  Output is a prioritized list

Post-Workshop Steps

Step 10  – Perform Consultant Team Debrief

Arrange to conduct this debrief as soon after the completion of the Discovery Workshop as possible, while thoughts and perspectives are still fresh.

Verbally debrief with the delivery team - - discuss what we each saw.

Step 11 – Generate a Summary Document

Put together a brief summary document of the workshop.  For an example see the Sample DW 4.2A found on the KM AssetWeb database. 

The document should include:

  • Introductory material
  • List of observations...problems or challenges (those reflecting major themes from the workshop) are individually linked to KM business issues
  • Recommendations...best opportunities to pursue (with argument for the business value of each)
  • Appendix is actual workshop output

Also, consider that the summary document be discussed on conference call with workshop participants to identify:

  • How would they go forward?
  • What problems do they foresee?
  • Consider the proper scope of the pilot work or research visit (s) - where do we start?

Step 12 – Get Feedback on Summary Document

After preparing summary document, either meet with participants or conduct a conference call to discuss.  This both validates findings and perspectives and continues to foster participant involvement and buy-in.

Step 13 – Develop Report or Discussion Document

In most cases, this document is a validated, finalized version of the Summary Document.  It now takes the participant feedback into account. 

There may be cases where the client will not want to proceed beyond this step (e.g., due to budgetary constraints).  It is therefore important to be sure that the document and the discussion around it delivers value in and of itself.

Some key issues include:

  • Report is generated and sponsor is engaged in a discussion on next steps.  See the Sample DW 4.4A located on the KM AssetWeb database.
  • Input from participant discussion is used to validate hypotheses and draft report to sponsor
  • Report in and of itself should deliver value, whether or not client is in a position to go forward with projects
    • Each opportunity is discussed separately
    • Includes background, possible projects, business value, potential problems
  • Obtain go-ahead to pursue a project or projects
  • Determine whom to speak to to gather data for the development of a proposal and workplan
  • Target and plan the research visit (s)

Step 14 – Conduct a Research Visit with Client

The purpose of the Research Visit is to "dig deeper" to identify the specific area for a pilot project and learn enough about it to develop a proposal for our assistance.  Depending upon the circumstances, the Research Visit should take 1-2 days to conduct.  Generally, the Research Visit includes interviews, observation and discussion.  Reach consensus on how to proceed with the client. 
This is to conduct further research within the targeted areas:

  • Develop project outlines for each area (See Sample DW 4.5A in the KM AssetWeb)
  • Identify business issue
  • Assess business value
  • Rate projects on multiple dimensions (pragmatic)
  • Generate rankings
  • Develop a proposal letter to the executive whose purview the project falls under...cc.  To whomever it makes sense to copy  (See Sample 4.5 B)
  • Follow-up calls
