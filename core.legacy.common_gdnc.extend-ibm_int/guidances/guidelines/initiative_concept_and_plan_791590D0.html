Guideline: Initiative Concept and Plan
Main Description

1 Description

The Initiative Concept and Plan technique provides a number of templates for the detailed specification of Initiative requirements. The templates enable the description of the Initiatives at a sufficient enough level of detail to size the magnitude of change (including updating the business case), and to begin outlining supporting processes, organizations and IT for assessment and design/change.

This technique covers parts of the Refine e-business Initiative Requirements activity in the Plan Portfolio and Initiatives phase of the e-Business Assessment & Strategy engagement model. The development of the Initiative requirements uses the templates described in this technique.
 
Primary sources of information are:
  • Documented e-business Strategy and initiatives
  • Key findings and outputs (templates) from previous work efforts.
  • Executives and other key stakeholders


2 Purpose

The purpose of the Initiative Concept and Plan technique is to provide an adequate level of detail to implement and launch a Transition.

2.1  Contexts of Use

This technique is used when e-business initiatives which have been identified and prioritized, need to be further specified for design and implementation scopes of work.

3 Steps

The Initiative Concept and Plan technique is completed by the Project Team by using templates that have been provided to capture key information.  The key information includes business/capability requirements, content requirements, functional requirements, preliminary architecture changes needed, and key activities needed for design and deployment (along with their duration and other important factors).

Each Initiative should be documented according to the following headings.

3.1  Requirements

For each initiative a table in the following form is created:

Initiative ID:________________   Initiative Name:_____________



Audience  Wants & Needs/ Value Propositions  Business Capability/ Requirement  Process  Content Requirement  Functional Requirement


3.2  Preliminary Business Architecture Capability Description

For each initiative a table in the following form is created:

Initiative ID:________________   Initiative Name:_____________



Requirement Process Capability   Content Capability  Organization Capability  IT Capability
    

3.3  Business Architecture Change Requirements

For each initiative a table in the following form is created:

Initiative ID:________________   Initiative Name:_____________



Capability Process Content Organization IT


3.4  Key Activities and Implications

For each initiative a table in the following form is created:

Initiative ID:________________   Initiative Name:_____________



Architecture  Change Activities Estimated Duration Dependencies Interdependencies 


3.5  Work Plan

For each initiative a work plan in the following form is created:

Initiative ID:________________   Initiative Name:_____________

Using the key activities and implications identified previously, a work plan is developed depicting the individual activities and timing (based upon the estimated duration, interdependencies, etc.), as well as the overall duration for the initiative.

Note: Multiple projects may be suitable based upon the size and complexity of the initiative, and the areas affected.  The initial high level roadmap for the strategy should be adjusted based upon initiative plans and schedules.

3.6  Business Case

For each initiative a business case in the following form is created:

Initiative ID:________________   Initiative Name:_____________

Using the preliminary architecture changes, and key activities and implications identified, update the high level business case previously developed for the initiative to reflect changes in cost/investment needed as well as benefits.

Note: The initial business case for the overall strategy should be updated based upon changes to initiative level business cases.

4 Estimating Considerations

Specification of the requirements for Initiatives will vary from project to project, depending on the detail required by the client. In general a useful estimating technique is to work out how much effort will be required to document the requirements for simple, normal and complex Initiatives, then to categorize each of the Initiatives and multiply the totals by the estimates for each category.
An initial range to assist with estimating the time and effort required for completing the Technique follows:

Simple Initiative - 2-3 days

  • Single Business Unit
  • Minimal change to process and organization
  • Some application development, customization, and integration with legacy systems

Complex Initiative - 5-7 days

  • Single or multiple Business Units
  • Moderate to major change to process and organization
  • Moderate to major application development, customization, and integration with legacy systems
