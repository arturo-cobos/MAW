Guideline: Enterprise Architecture Gap Analysis
Main Description
The Enterprise Architecture (EA) Gap Analysis technique is used in conjunction with the ARC 004 Infrastructure Gap Analysis work product. The technique expands the scope of the work product and is used to understand the gaps between the current business and IT environment and the future desired environments as defined within an Enterprise Architecture and its application, data and infrastructure component parts. When a gap analysis of the business components of an Enterprise Architecture are required, the BUS 311 Process Gap Assessment and BUS 339 Knowledge Gap work products should be used.

Note that enterprise-level architectures have a much wider scope and content than solution architectures, which are normally focused on a single business area/solution. The Enterprise Architecture underpins the business strategy and the key capabilities required to implement the desired business and IT environment. It takes an enterprise-wide perspective of what is required, where the main integration points are and, identifies opportunities for the sharing of application function, data and infrastructure, etc. The objective of these activities (using this technique) is to understand the gaps and use this understanding to identify the initiatives needed to make the transition from the current environment, to the future desired environment. This is a key step in the translation of the theory of an enterprise-level architecture into solutions, which can be effectively implemented, in support of business objectives.
