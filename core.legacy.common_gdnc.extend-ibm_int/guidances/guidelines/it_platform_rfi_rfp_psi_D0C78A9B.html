Guideline: IT Platform RFI/RFP (PSI)
Main Description

Introduction

The Platform RFI/RFP is a set of documents requesting specific information from prospective vendors about their products in order to help the client perform vendor selection. The requested information may include hardware, software, or services. The WorkProduct also includes the evaluation and documentation of the vendor responses and comparative analysis.
The purpose of the RFI/RFP is to solicit qualitative and quantitative information from vendors about the use of their products as components in the infrastructure to support the selected package. It also serves to record the responses and the client’s subsequent evaluations. The data should be used in the tool selection process to ensure the technical architecture and constraints of the package are considered. The result helps estimate the costs of some of the IT components in Infrastructure Cost Plan.

Steps

1. RFI/RFP General Data

Purpose

Customize the RFI/RFP document with client general information

Description


Customize the RFI/RFP template with client data

  • Review the RFI/RFP document with the client to determine which of the general data sections (1-9) will require customization 
  • Revise sections 1-4 and 6-9 as appropriate 
  • Determine the review schedule based on the dates hardware and software will be needed by the Functional Team project plan, the availability of client personnel to review vendor responses and the date the RFI/RFP will be available to vendors 
  • Complete the Proposal Preparation and Review Schedule, section 5 
  • Review Appendix F with the client for applicability to client needs, revise as appropriate 
  • Review the completed general data section with client project management for revisions and approval to proceed 
  • Result: Completed RFI/RPF, sections 1-9 and Appendix F – Example 1

Product: Client general information RFI/RFP

2. RFI/RFP – Infrastructure Data

Purpose

Customize the RFI/RFP document with infrastructure-specific data

Description


Customize the RFI/RFP template with infrastructure data

  • Review the Service Level Requirements, Appendix A, section with the client to determine specific information requirements, revise as appropriate. This information should be available from the results of Current IT Architecture, or from the Functional Team 
  •  Revise sections 1-4 and 6-9 as appropriate 
  • Complete the tables in Appendix B based on information documented in IT Infrastructure Gap Analysis, Model of Server Technical Architecture, and IT Model of Network/Workstation Technical Architecture – Example 2 
  • With client management and specialists complete the Notes portion such that vendor responses will meet client requirements – Example 2 
  • Insert the landscape diagrams completed during Model of Server Technical Architecture into Appendix C 
  • Insert the completed sizing questionnaires from Model of Server Technical Architecture into Appendix D and Appendix E 
  • Review Appendix G with the client for applicability to client needs, revise as appropriate, most probably in reference to an early development system 
  • Review the completed Infrastructure-specific data sections with client project management for revisions and approval to proceed 
  • Result: Completed RFI/RPF, Appendices A-E and G – Example 2

Product: Client infrastructure-specific information RFI/RFP

3. RFI/RFP Distribution

Purpose

Enable vendors to respond to the RFI/RFP

Description


Submit RFI/RFP to vendors

  • With the client, determine the appropriate vendors for distribution of the RFI/RFP. The IT Architect should use related experience, and independent research on vendors to help the client complete the vendor selections 
  • Distribute the RFI/RFP to the selected vendors. This may be done via mail or by requesting vendors to schedule a time to meet with the client to receive/review the RFI/RFP. In the latter case an overview of the detailed RFI/RFP should be prepared to stimulate vendor questions 
  • Take vendor questions during the period allowed for preparation of responses 
  • Result: Support for vendor response to the RFI/RFP

Product: Enhanced quality of vendor responses

4. RFI/RFP Vendor Evaluation

Purpose

Project vendor selection

Description


Evaluate vendor responses

  • Receive the RFI/RFP responses from the selected vendors. This may be done via mail or by requesting vendors to schedule a time to meet with the client to present their responses (normal case). In the latter case any initial client questions can be handled quickly by the vendors 
  • With the Functional Team and IT Infrastructure decision makers, evaluate the individual responses based on the results of IT Infrastructure Gap Analysis, Model of Server Technical Architecture 
  • Depending on the number of vendors involved, reduce the vendor list to 2 to 3, review this list and reasons for selection with IT executive management for concurrence 
  • Select the hardware/software vendor, review with IT executive management for concurrence 
  • Place orders for initial equipment and software 
  • Result: Vendor selection and initial equipment order placement

Product: Primary equipment vendor selected, initial orders placed
