Main Description
This paper describes the process and techniques for managing defects. The purpose of defect management is to:
  • Effectively manage the tracking and resolution of defects found during the project lifecycle; and to
  • Capture the information needed in order to perform root cause analysis, or for ODC (see Defect Categorization under Advice and Guidance Section) and analysis.

The successful identification and resolution of defects found during testing to agreed predetermined targets is a key enabler to efficient progress through each phase of the project. A well-managed defect management process which is an integral part of the project activities is essential to accomplish this. This process involves compiling information on defects found throughout the project, including static testing, test management, dynamic testing, and infrastructure or system operation, and analyzing that information so that trends and significant events or problems with the overall systems development process are recognized and resolved timeously and cost-effectively.
