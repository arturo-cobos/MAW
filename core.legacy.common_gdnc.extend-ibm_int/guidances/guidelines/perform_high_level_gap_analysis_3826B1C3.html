Guideline: Performing a High Level Gap Analysis
This technique paper details the various steps that need to be performed in order to create the BUS 080 High Level Gap Analysis work product. This includes a brief definition of the steps, participation necessary, and key additional work products required in the gap resolution process.
Main Description

Steps

Step 1 – Create Gap Analysis Report 

During to-be process design, the teams will identify areas of desired functionality that are suspected to be outside the limits of the standard IBM asset.  These areas need to be documented as gaps in order to document their existence.  At minimum, the gap's name, brief description of the desired business functionality and the asset's functionality should be included in the documentation.

Who: Process teams

Time: 1 day

Work product: Gap analysis report

Step 2 – Perform Gap Functional Detail Analysis

In order to properly determine the impact and risks associated with the suspected gap, the functional needs of the gap must be documented.  These needs must also have four other pieces of information tied to them: business benefit and value of the function, risk associated with not delivering the function, project risk associated with delivering the function, and impact of deferring the functionality past implementation.  The gap analysis team is notified of the gap.

Who: Process teams

Time: 1-2 days

Work products: Gap functional detail document(s)

Step 3 – Evaluate the IBM asset Coverage

The functional analysis matrix will be used by the IBM asset experts to confirm that the desired functionality is indeed outside the limits of standard the IBM asset.  These conclusions will be made based on discussions with the proper experts, experiences of the IBM asset resources, and the IBM asset help files.

Who: IBM asset consultants and IBM asset skilled resources, gap resolution team

Time: 1 day

Work products: Updated Gap functional detail document(s)

Step 4 – Refine Business Process Design

Using the benefit / risk / defer matrix, process teams critically evaluate the business drivers behind the suspected gap. The business process is then redesigned to encompass only the functionality that is deemed to be essential, for which there is a strong business driver. These alternatives have the smallest impact on project timelines and the IBM asset software.  At this point, the gap can remain at the sub-project level. If the over-all business process can be executed in the standard IBM asset with minimal impact or an acceptable work-around can be developed, this option should be selected as the solution for the gap.  If no functional extension outside standard IBM asset functionality is needed, the process skips to Step 19: Close Gap.

Who: Process teams, the IBM asset experts, bolt-on functional experts, gap resolution team

Time: 1-2 days

Work products: Updated gap analysis report, and revised process flows.

Step 5 – Identify and Rank Functional Requirements for Gap Resolution

The functional analysis matrix is updated to include all needed functionality for the gap resolution as per the redesigned process.  These items are prioritized in order to define an objective list of standards for the intended solution.  Additionally, the business case for the functions is refined with more rigor than above.  A log is created by the gap analysis team in order to consolidate the individual functional requirements, rate them, and summarize their costs or benefits.

Who: Key process team members from all affected processes and gap resolution team

Time: 2 days

Work products: Updated Gap functional detail document(s), gap functionality log

Step 6 – Estimate Coverage and Propose Gap Resolutions

Estimate the costs and the level of coverage that the available solutions (e.g. bolt on, extension, etc.) could provide.  The process team will match the functional needs identified above with the features typical of the intended solutions.  The analysis includes both functional requirement coverage and feature/functional extensions that have a high degree of compatibility with the client goals. The solution will need to be evaluated not only by a coverage rating, but a rating that will give quality points for probability of success of the enhancement and the project. A preliminary decision is made as to the type of the gap resolution.  This investigation needs to take into account the risks and costs associated with the various options available and the business needs of the solution. Section II - Gap Analysis of the gap resolution document is updated with these conclusions.

A key part of this investigation is future IBM asset software release functionality.  This alternative is investigated with IBM asset experts.  By analyzing the details of future functionality and the timing of its availability, the solution to the gap functionality may be determined to be met by future IBM asset functionality.  If this is determined to be the case, an explanation of the future functionality is drafted, and key users sign-off on the solution.  The gap is considered on-hold pending the availability of the future IBM asset release functionality and upgrade plans.

Who: Process teams & gap resolution team, with the IBM asset and bolt-on functional experts as appropriate

Time: 1 day

Work products: Updated gap analysis report

Step 7 – Steering Committee Approval of Gap Resolution

The gap functionality log, gap functional detail reports, and gap analysis report will be used in order to support the determination the type of the gap resolution.  The type of the gap resolution is confirmed and signed off on by the steering committee, and a subset of the steering committee is identified for final approval of the gap (if necessary).

Who: Steering committee, gap resolution team, subject matter and process experts

Time: 2 days

Work products: Updated gap analysis report

Step 8 – Investigate Process for Improvement Opportunities

The functional limitations of the design teams are expanded to include only those functions that were agreed above to be critical needs within the proposed gap resolution. At this time, the process should be investigated to insure the optimal process within these new constraints is being designed.  Parallel or redundant processes and functions that it makes sound business sense to do outside the IBM asset are potentially moved into the gap resolution object.

Who: Process teams and gap resolution team, with the IBM asset and bolt-on functional experts as appropriate

Time: 1 day

Work products: Updated gap analysis report, process flows, and gap functionality log

Step 9 – Apprise IT Infrastructure Personnel of Gap Resolution

At this point, IT infrastructure personnel should be advised of the nature of the gap resolution so they can start to incorporate its additional needs into their workplans, and so they can determine additional infrastructure requirements.

Who: Gap resolution team and IT infrastructure team

Time: 1 day

Step 10 – Generate Solution Analysis Scenarios

The Gap functional detail document(s) serves as a driver for the generation of test scenarios to insure that the eventual solution meets the needs as defined by the process teams.  Unless the desired solution is a bolt on, the next step will be Step 15: Recommend Solution Strategy & Tactics.

Who: Gap resolution team and process team members

Time: 2 days per function (est. 1 week overall per gap)

Work products: solution analysis scenarios for gap

Step 11 – Develop Long Vendor List and Educate Selection Team

A long list of capable vendors is generated from IBM and team member experiences and research.  Only vendors with capabilities in the functional area of investigation who are known by team members will be considered.  As a part of the generation of this list, all team members are educated about the capabilities, histories, and products of each of the vendors.  Vendor market literature and delivered functionality is captured throughout this process.

Who: Gap resolution team

Time: 1-2 days (3 weeks overall)

Work products: Market intelligence for long list vendors within the gap resolution team.

Step 12–  Prepare Evaluation Matrix

The analysis of the vendors’ material will generate a list of functions that each vendor’s product has.  The next step is the development of a matrix, rating the vendors’ ability to meet the functional needs of the client, along with other factors such as technology, distribution, IBM asset integration capabilities, access to capital, installation base, management, vision, and intangibles.  A ranking of importance of each feature will be documented.  IBM knowledge capital will be leveraged as a part of the matrix generation process.  The primary input to this is the functional analysis matrix generated above.

Who: Gap resolution team

Time: 2 days.

Work products: Ranked analysis matrix

Step 13 – Develop Short Vendor List

Select the “best fit” vendors, preferably two and usually no more than four, based on the “must have” criteria on the vendor evaluation matrix.  It may be a necessity for these vendors to be IBM asset partners, depending on the client.

Who: Gap resolution team

Time: 1-2 days of team negotiations

Work products: Short List

Step 14 – Solution Selection

  • Conduct Vendor Presentations

This is a full disclosure of the requirements to the vendors.  Depending on the subject, and the competitiveness of the vendors, this may be a single kickoff or individualized meetings.  It is important for the external consultant to be interfacing with the vendors to facilitate a level of continuity, information flow and adherence to the requirements.  IT infrastructure experts should be present in order to clearly communicate their concerns.

Who: Gap resolution team, key users, IT infrastructure personnel

Time: 1-2 days/vendor

Work products: None

  • Conduct Vendor Demonstrations

The vendors will present their solutions to the decision team and the executives, based on the solution analysis scenarios developed above.  The scope of this section can vary greatly from a simple overhead presentation, to a user data demo, or a full conference room pilot.  Depending on the scope of the demonstration the vendor may or may not require funding.

Who: Gap resolution team, users

Time: 3-4 weeks

Work products: Vendor supplied solution document

  • Evaluate References

The team and key users should communicate with all vendor supplied references as a part of the evaluation process.

Who: Gap resolution team, key users

Time: 1-2 days/reference

Work product: Assurance of a workable solution

  • Select Vendor

A vendor is selected and the gap analysis report document is updated with the pertinent information.

Who: Gap resolution team

Time: 1 day, average elapsed time 1 week.

Work product: Selected vendor, updated gap analysis report document

Step 15 - Recommend Solution Strategy & Tactics

In this process, the work effort for the implementation team must be established.  Major building blocks for the solution are established. Document the recommended project plan with time frames and resources estimated.  Additionally, update the projected return on investment in the functional analysis matrix, and document the metrics suggested to be able to measure the progress. At this point, all project elements associated with the gap resolution need to be clearly documented and scoped.  For example, if a gap requires a bolt-on with interface and configuration needs, these efforts all must be clearly documented and workplans need to be generated / updated to include these items.

Who: Gap resolution team, implementation team

Time: 1 week

Work product: Project plan and Benefits statement

Step 16: Deliver Final Gap Resolution to Infrastructure team

The IT infrastructure team will be informed as to the exact gap resolution in order that they may finalize their workplans and develop the infrastructure requirements for the gap resolution object.  If the solution is not a bolt on, skip to Step 19: Close Gap.

Who: Gap resolution team, implementation team

Time: 1 day

Work product: Project plan and Benefits statement

Step 17 – Present to Steering Committee Subset for Approval

All of the above work products are formatted to create a presentation for the steering committee justifying the selection of the vendor and the implementation plan.  The steering committee then signs off on the solution.

Who: Steering committee, gap resolution team, subject matter and process experts

Time: 1 day

Work products: Updated gap analysis report document

Step 18 – Negotiate / Contract with Vendor

Finalize the documentation with a full list of Work products, Terms and Conditions, Staffing, and the most important Completion Criteria.  The inclusion of the vendor specifications in the functional Matrix, should have many of the T&C’s already known disclosed and resolved.  This will optimize the final paper work.

Who: The client’s purchasing with the support of the gap resolution team

Time: 1 week

Work products: Contracts approved and signed

Step 19 – Close Gap

Sign off that the gap has been resolved to the satisfaction of the gap owner, and update the status to “Closed” in the gap analysis report document.

Who: Gap owner

Time: 1 day.

Work products: Updated gap analysis report document

Step 20 – Formal Hand-off to Implementation Team

The gap analysis team and the implementation team meet in order to transfer the knowledge of the gap resolution to the implementation team. The project is officially transferred to the implementation team.

Who: Gap analysis team, implementation team

Time: 2 days

Work products: Knowledge transfer

Composition, roles, and responsibilities of gap analysis team

The client Functional Expert:  Work with evaluation team to identify functional requirements needed from the bolt-on software.  Help the evaluation team to determine both the cost of implementation and the savings coming from enhanced functionality. 

The client Application Consultant: Ensure the process remains on schedule.  Arrange vendor contacts and meetings.  Publish meeting notes.  Develop business case for functionality based on input from other team members.  Ensure the scope of the implementation remains within the necessary functionality to allow project to work effectively.  Opportunities for future broadening of scope in the packages will still be there after the initial crunch of implementation has passed.

The client Infrastructure and the IBM asset Coordination: Representative from the systems area to ensure that the work in the bolt-on areas is well coordinated with activities within the project team, including infrastructure and application configuration and interface issues.

The client Development and Legacy Coordination: Provide cost estimates for configuration and implementation of the bolt-ons.  Provide for a smooth transition to configuration and implementation of selected packages.  Resolve legacy interface issues.

IBM Asset Consultant: Provide in depth product knowledge of the software offerings in the areas under consideration.  Help to arrive at a “short list” quickly after functional needs are known. Assist in working through configuration and implementation issues to ensure important considerations are covered in the analysis phase.

IBM Business Transformation Consultant: Work with the to-be teams to keep the scope within bounds, both in the high level to-be sessions, and after the guardrails are expanded by bolt-on software.  Help to identify and decompose functional requirements to assist in package selection. 

IBM Infrastructure Representative: Help the team to understand the implications and costs of various alternatives from a hardware and interface perspective.  Ensure coordination with the project Infrastructure team.
