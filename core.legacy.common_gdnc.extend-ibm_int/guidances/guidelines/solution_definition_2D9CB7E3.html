Guideline: Solution Definition
The objective of this technique paper is to define an approach that is designed to quickly gain a shared understanding of business issues, requirements and appropriate technical approach.
Main Description

 This technique is powerful because it:

  • Forces a solid understanding of business issues as the basis for requirements.
  • Gets users and management excited while keeping them focused on the most important benefits and functions.
  • Facilitates the client making their own tradeoffs between functionality and cost/schedule constraints.
  • Puts everyone in the same room (users, managers, IT people) to gain a shared understanding of the issues, tradeoffs and agreed-upon approaches.
  • Provides an excellent reference point for weeding out critical requirements from nice-to-have requirements, and for continually keeping the right focus during design and implementation.
  • Contains all the high points of the SIMethod Solution Outline phase, and thus provides a good context for finishing off requirements / solution definition in an optimum manner.
  • Takes almost no time, and is therefore very attractive to clients.
  • Very quickly yields a sense of progress and comfort level with IBM.
  • This approach is recommended as a way:
  • To understand the client's requirements well enough to provide a business justification and initial approach, without requiring the client to immediately commit to a full phase 0 effort (note that a full phase 0 should follow, however, if the business case is made)
  • To kick off a full phase 0 effort, establishing a shared context for further interviews and planning sessions.

Examples of filled-in workshop reports for these projects (following the prescribed agenda) are also on the eCMS ROCK Documentum Docbase.
