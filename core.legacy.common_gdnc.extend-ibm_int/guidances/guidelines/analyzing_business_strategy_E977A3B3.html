Guideline: Analyzing Business Strategy
Main Description

1 Description

The objective of this technique is to assist in discovering the corporate, business, and functional strategy, mission, vision, goals, and objectives of a business. The purpose of this analysis presumes a belief that any and all initiatives to manage knowledge should focus on either the knowledge needed to fulfill the business strategy as well as any excess knowledge within an organization that has the greatest chance of enhancing organizational value. More specifically, only through analyzing gaps between and among existing, required, competitor, partner, and customer knowledge can a knowledge management program be recommended that will in fact prioritize and then manage the most strategic knowledge to the organization. In order to successfully recommend a knowledge management program, the business strategy of the organization must be thoroughly understood. This technique paper provides an outline that covers the core areas of a business strategy. Adapt this outline to suit your client's and they engagement needs. It is rare that this outline will be used in its entirety.

Note: Portions of this outline have been borrowed from-

Thomas L. Wheelen & J. David Hunger. (1998). Strategic Management and Business Policy: Entering 21st Century Global Society. Addison-Wesley, Reading MA.

2 Steps

Current Situation

Step 1A - Current Financial Performance

Chose relevant financial ratios to compute and analyze.  Always benchmark ratios over time (adjusting for inflation) and against industry averages.  The purpose for conducting this analysis is to see where the company is excelling as well as suffering so as to help build your business case for implementing knowledge management and learning programs.  Examples of ratios to be used includeâ€¦

  • Liquidity Ratios: Current, Acid Test, Cash, etc.
  • Profitability Ratios: Net Profit Margin, Gross Profit Margin, ROI, ROE, EPS, etc.
  • Activity Ratios: Inventory Turnover, Asset Turnover, Average Collection Period, etc.
  • Leverage Ratios: Debt to Asset, Debt to Equity, Times Interest Earned, etc.
  • Other Ratios: PE, Dividend Payout, etc.

Step 1B - Strategic Stance

Research the corporation's current mission, objectives, strategies, and policies.  Decide and document whether they are clearly stated or merely implied from performance. 

Mission:  What business is the corporation in and why?  What is the reason for the firm's existence?

Objectives:  What are the corporate, business, and functional objectives?  What does the firm want to accomplish and by when do they want to accomplish it?  Are they consistent with each other, the firm's capabilities, and the internal and external environment?

Goals:  Note that goals are similar to objectives however goals are more open ended and are not quantifiable nor are they given time constraints.
Strategies:  What mix of strategies (comprehensive master plan of how the corporation will achieve its mission and objectives) is the corporation following?  Are they consistent with each other, the firm's capabilities, and the internal and external environment?

Corporate Strategy: Overall direction in terms of general attitude towards growth.

Business Strategy:  Occurs at the business unit level

Functional Strategy: Approach taken by functional unit to achieve corporate and business objectives.

  • Policies:  What are they?  Are they consistent with each other, the firm's capabilities, and the internal and external environment?  These are the broad guidelines for decision making that links the formulation of strategy with each implementation.

Step 2 - Corporate Governance

Step 2A - Board of Directors

Consider who the board of directors is. Are they internal or external and/or do they own significant shares of stock?  Is the stock privately held or publicly traded?  What do they contribute to the corporation in terms of knowledge, skills, background, etc.?  What is their level of involvement in strategic management?

Step 2B - Top Management

Determine who represents top management formally and informally.  This will require an analysis of not only the formal organization chart but informal ones as well.  The informal information is more difficult to find but can usually be secured from a trusted inside source.  One armed with the organization information determine top management's chief characteristics in terms of knowledge, skills, style, background, etc.  How long have these people been with the company?  Were they hired internally or externally?  Length of time with company can hint at how change is viewed by the company.  What is their approach and level of involvement to strategic management?  Is top management sufficiently skilled to cope with future challenges?  How well does top management interact with the board as well as lower managers? 

Step 3 - External Environment: Opportunities and Threats (SWOT)

Step 3A - Secondary External Issues

Which of these forces and factors are the most important to the corporation and to the industries in which it competes at the present time?  Which will be important in the future?

  • Economic
  • GDP Trends
  • Interest Rates
  • Money Supply
  • Energy Availability
  • Inflation
  • Technological
  • Total Govt. & Industry Spending on R&D
  • New Products & Productivity Improvements
  • Political/Legal
  • Stability of Government
  • Attitudes Toward Foreign Companies
  • Antitrust Regulations
  • Special Incentives
  • Sociocultural
  • Lifestyle Changes
  • Growth Rates
  • Life Expectancies

Step 3B - Primary External Issues

This can be done primarily using a "Porter's Five Forces Model".  Here is an outline of a similar model with additional forces to consider as well. Consider which of these forces and factors are the most important to the corporation and to the industries in which it competes at the present time?  Which will be important in the future?

New Entrants: New entrants to an industry.  Consider entry barriers such as knowledge, economies of scale, switching costs, etc.

Competitive Rivalry: The degree of the effect of a competitive move.  Some of these are number of competitors, rate of industry growth, height of exit barriers, etc.

Substitutes:  Those products that appear to be different but can satisfy the same needs as another product. 

Buyer Bargaining Power: Can they force down prices, increase quality requirements, readily find alternatives, etc.

Supplier Bargaining Power: Can they raise prices or reduce quality.  Do they sell a unique product or one with high switching costs?

Specific Industry Dynamics: For example, most high tech firms receive most of their profits from products and services developed within the last 6 months.

Competitor Knowledge/Learning: Does the firm's competitors have substantially advanced learning and knowledge capabilities?

Other Forces: Power of unions, governments, special interest groups, etc.

Step 4 - Internal Environment: Strengths and Weaknesses (SWOT)

Step 4A - Internal Issues

Which of these forces and factors are the most important to the corporation and to the industries in which it competes at the present time? 

Which will be important in the future?

Culture: The collection of beliefs, expectations, and values learned and shared by your client's employees.

Marketing: The market position and marketing mix of the firm.

Finance: The source, use, and control of funds.

Research and Development: The mix of basic, product, and process R&D.

Operations & Supply Chain Management: The system that provides the desired number of products/services at the best mix of quality, time, and cost.

Human Resources: The system that decides the match between individuals and job requirements.

Information Systems: The systematic flow of information that improves communication, productivity, decision making, innovation, etc. within your client's organization.

Knowledge Management & Learning: How your client is efficiently and effectively managing and obtaining tacit & explicit knowledge.

Core Business: The products/services that constitute your client's main productivity.

Competitive Advantage: The products/services and/or processes that your client's company does better than their competition.
