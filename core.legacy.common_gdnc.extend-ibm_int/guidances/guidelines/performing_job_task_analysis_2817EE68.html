What is a JTA?
 
Provides a detailed description or depiction of the hierarchical breakdown of work performed by the target audience of a particular solution, providing the foundation for targeting content.
 
Description:
 
The JTA breaks down job accomplishments into their component sub-accomplishments (tasks or sub-tasks), and takes the form of a detailed description or depiction of the breakdown of work performed by the target audience. Note that the JTA is an analysis of accomplishments, not subject-matter content. The JTA not only identifies the job accomplishments that students will learn to produce, it identifies the specific behaviors (actions, activities, or tasks) that must be performed on the job to achieve those accomplishments.
 
A job accomplishment is a valued job outcome or output. Job accomplishments are the products of one or more job tasks (activities). At the top of the hierarchy, a JTA displays the overall relevant job title and job accomplishment. The next level down in the hierarchy displays the major accomplishments that together make up the overall job accomplishment. The third, fourth, and lower levels break these major accomplishments into their component parts (sub-accomplishments, tasks, subtasks, and steps) until the desired level of detail is achieved.

A JTA can be represented graphically (similar in appearance but not substance to an organization chart) or textually (using an indented or numbered outline to show the hierarchy).
