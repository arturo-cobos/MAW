Guideline: DesignFlow Analysis Diagram Guide - Using Visio or WBI Modeler
This paper describes the technique for creating DesignFlow analysis diagrams. These diagrams provide a graphical depiction of complex processes.
Main Description
DesignFlow analysis diagrams are at the heart of process definition, redesign, and implementation engagements. These diagrams are time, role and process synchronization-based schematics of the entire process. They are generally, drawn using the VISIO or the Websphere Business Integration (WBI) Workbench modeling application called WBI Modeler.
 
The purpose of the diagram is to represent a business process with its activities organized within the roles that perform them and assembled in time sequence showing synchronization and process decisions.
 
The DesignFlow Analysis Diagram, is easily understood, and becomes a key communication device that focuses subsequent design discussions among process experts, end users and implementers. This capability is especially important for discussions that must focus on one activity at a time without losing information about their position within the process and their relationships to other activities.
