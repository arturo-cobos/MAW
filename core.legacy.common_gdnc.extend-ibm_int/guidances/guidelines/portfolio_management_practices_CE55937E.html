Guideline: Portfolio Management Practices
Main Description

Preface

Portfolio Management is a key process in the Business Technology Management (BTM) Framework. The BTM Framework outlines a holistic approach for managing IT investments that provides for the continuous identification, selection, control, lifecycle management, and evaluation of IT investments. The framework describes how the portfolio of IT investments is planned, developed, and evolved in a continuous end-to-end closed loop. Central to this framework is the notion that IT is not viewed as something separate or special, but rather as a part of a fully integrated picture of the "what" and the "how" of business strategy.

Key Elements of the BTM Framework

The BTM Framework is made operational through four key components:

  1. The Processes component - three integrated processes that help ensure consistent, high quality results
  2. The Management Structure component - the organizational structure for making decisions and getting work done
  3. Tools/Enablers component - that enable or automate process execution, decision making and reporting of results
  4. Measurements component - assessments of the effectiveness of BTM in supporting business goals
