Tool Mentor: e-business Business Case Tool
This tool mentor describes the use of the new e-business - Business Case Tool that will allow the business consultant to apply a number of financial analysis techniques to any e-business project.
Main Description

Introduction

The tool contains all the necessary forms to collect, analyze and present the results of the Business Case (e.g. Business Case Work Product) in financial and non-financial terms. From a financial perspective, the tool calculates ROWI (return on Web-Investment), states the project/initiative financial benefit, reports intangible benefit impact, states the estimated e-business investment and operating costs, calculates the number of months for "double" the payback and presents the relative e-business initiative risks.

The intent of the tool is to provide a repository for gathering financial and non-financial information throughout the life cycle of all projects from small to large. The tool is designed to facilitate the gathering of data by providing worksheets that can be distributed to client team members for completion or used as an interview guides for the IBM Consultants. The tool also provides a "point and click" navigation aid to access the data sheets from the WBS guide. This technique paper will provide the roadmap for using the tool and guidance on the data content, analysis and reporting. The key sections of the tool are:

  • Executive Summary - a summary for senior management review of the Valuation assessment by initiative
  • Financial Impact Schedule - a summary of the financial costs & benefits over time typically viewed by a controller
  • Financial Assumptions Worksheet - an outline of significant assumptions backing the detail data
    • ROWI & Payback - a summary showing the IRR details, NPV and payback
    • Investment & Operating Cost by Initiative - a detail list of all investment and operating costs for the initiative
    • Financial Benefits Schedule - a summary page showing benefit to sales, cost of sales, operating costs, working capital and capital expenditures
    • e-business Initiative Risk - a risk assessment page showing financial, operational, organizational, market, technology and other types of risks
    • Significance of Intangible Benefits - a qualitative assessment of benefits from customer, internal business and innovation & learning perspectives
    • Assumptions - these worksheets (5each) support the detail financial data for the five line items on the "Financial Benefits Schedule"
    • e-business Investment & Operating Cost Data Collection - individual data sheets to capture cost data
    • Financial Benefits Data Collection -- individual data sheets to capture financial benefit data
    • Initiative Priority Sheet - a qualitative evaluation section to assist the client with setting priorities for alternative initiatives using a qualitative evaluation technique. This section would be used when the client has "NOT" been able to establish the initiative priorities.
  • Source Description of Intellectual Capital (IC) - a summary of the ICM and other sources used as reference materials. The user locates the key and IC reference on the summary sheet and the tool hyper links to the detail reference section.

Steps

The purpose of this technique paper is to guide the user through the use of an e-business – Business Case Tool to be used in the financial and business case analysis of e-business initiatives.  Its intended use is with the e-business Strategy & Design Offering and the “Fast Path” version that is designed to deliver an e-business pilot within 12 to 16 weeks. The tool can be used to track and evaluate multiple e-business initiatives and pilots.  The tool is also intended to be used End-to-End within an engagement to allow the project management team to gather initial data and refine the data with concrete results as the project matures. 


EM = engagement model; WPD = Work Product; Tool: =  tool used to gather and analyze valuation data

Contexts of Use

The e-business – Business Case Tool can be used in context with and as output from several Business/Financial Work Products including:

  • Estimated Preliminary Cost -- this work product is used to estimate preliminary costs to acquire and implement a proposed solution.  The cost data sheets of the e-business – Business Case Tool can be used to collect this information.
  • Preliminary Cost-Benefit Estimation -- this work product is used to estimate preliminary costs, benefits, cash flows and profits of an initiative.  The work is typically done as the initial justification of the initiative.  The e-business – Business Case Tool can be used as the initial repository and analysis for this work product.
  • Benefit Impact Analysis -- this work product is used to define strategic success indicators of the solution and to track targeted versus actual quantifiable performance.  The e-business – Business Case Tool can be used to capture the initial estimated costs & benefits and the repository of actual results. 
  • Business Case -- this work product is used to justify initiatives based on estimate preliminary costs, benefits, cash flows and profits.  The e-business – Business Case Tool can be used to calculate financial and non-financial benefits supporting the business initiative. 

Phases

The following diagram shows the basic phases that define the process model for completing the e-business – business case tool.  Note that the organization hierarchy of Phase, Activity and Task is used to comply with the nomenclature used by the authoring tool used for creating Work Products (WPD’s), Engagement Models (Em’s), Technique Papers and Templates.

Process model


 


This document identifies the Phases and Activities for this process model only.  The detail tasks are not included.

Phase 1 – Establish Business Case Team

Purpose of this phase is to identify team members and assign project tasks and deliverables. 

Activities:

  1. Assemble Business Case Team
  2. Charter Business Case Team

Assumptions:

  • Client has prioritized its e-business initiatives
  • Client team members have been assigned
  • Stakeholders and sources have been identified for gathering costs, benefits, intangible benefits and risks

Phase 2 – Establish Baseline

Baseline metrics are the financial and operating data available from the client including

  • Revenue
  • Department and operating costs
  • Department and operating FTE
  • Units of measure for the operations (e.g. unit volumes, etc.)
  • Cycle times
  • Processing times
  • Other baseline metrics

Activities:

  1. Determine baseline information available
  2. Enter baseline data into template

Assumptions:

  • Client has baseline information
  • Client has or uses performance measures 
  • Client wants to use performance measures as success indicators

Phase 3 – Estimate Costs

Costs include all direct, indirect and overhead types of costs for such categories as: 

  • Hardware and infrastructure
  • Software
  • Personnel (FTE)
  • Outsource vendors / Consultants / Contractors
  • Data Communications, and
  • Other

Activities:

  1. Determine projected cost ranges
  2. Establish cost range confidence

Assumptions:

  • Client will provide the cost data or subject matter experts to extract the costs from source applications
  • Client has financial systems for tracking costs

Phase 4 – Estimated Tangible Benefits

Tangible benefits are positive financial impacts to existing or anticipated line items including the following:

  • Sales
  • Cost of  Sales
  • Operating expenses
  • Working Capital
  • Capital Expenditures

Working capital benefits include positive, sustainable impact to balance sheet accounts (e.g. reduction in inventory, reduction in A/R balance and days outstanding, etc.)  Capital expenditure benefits are reductions or elimination of existing or planned capital projects and budgets.

Activities:

  1. Determine Tangible Benefits
  2. Identify Revenue Streams
  3. Determine Cost Savings Benefits Ranges
  4. Determine Future Revenue Based on “as-is” business & IT system
  5. Determine Revenue increase benefit ranges
  6. Establish Tangible Benefit Confidence Values
  7. Calculate Confidence-Adjusted Tangible Benefit Ranges

Assumptions:

  • Client will provide the benefit data or key stakeholders who can identify tangible benefits
  • Client has financial systems for tracking benefits

Phase 5 – Estimate Intangible Benefits

Intangible benefits include positive impact to the business in view of its customers, its internal business processes, its innovation and learning (e.g. of its employees).  Some intangible benefits may be translated into financial measures given sufficient time to establish linkages to measurable financial levers.

Activities:

  1. Determine Projected Intangible Benefits
  2. Determine Cascading Benefit Value Proposition
  3. Reassess Intangible Benefits
  4. Assign Intangible Benefit Weights
  5. Estimate Quantitative Value of Intangible Benefits
  6. Establish Intangible Benefit Confidence Values
  7. Calculate Weighted Confidence-Adjusted Intangible Benefit Ranges

Assumptions:

  • Client will provide the key stakeholders who can identify intangible benefits

Phase 6 – Assess Risks

Risks are the possible events or factors that could have a negative impact on the overall success of the project.  Risk factors are assessed for the following categories:

  • Financial impact
  • Operational impact
  • Cultural and Organizational impact
  • Marketing impact
  • Technology impact, and
  • Other potential areas of concern to the client

Each risk is assessed in terms of severity of impact and the potential occurrence.  Using these two measures, adjusted risk is calculated.

Activities:

  1. Identify Potential Risks
  2. Assess How each Risk Affects Tangible & Intangible Benefit Potential
  3. Assess Potential for Occurrence
  4. Develop Risk Mitigation / Contingency Plan
  5. Assign Score Values to Each Risk

Assumptions:

  • Client will provide the key stakeholders who will work with the team to identify and evaluate risk factors

Phase 7 – Calculate Metrics & Update Assumptions

During this phase, the financial and non-financial valuation measures are calculated and analyzed.  The assumptions are also reviewed and updated to ensure that all factors and measures are properly aligned with the initiative objectives and that all stakeholders and management teams are apprised of the results.

Activities:

  1. Update Assumptions
  2. Calculate Confidence-Adjusted ROWI Ranges and Other Metrics

Assumptions:

  • Client project management and senior management team will review the e-business – Business Case results.

Tools

The e-business – Business Case Tool is designed with an internal roadmap to guide the user to the appropriate data sheet to be completed for each Phase defined above.  The tool is written in Excel and provides hot links to help the consultant navigate to the appropriate worksheet from the following “Spreadsheet Organization”. 


Hotlinks are also provided to quickly help the consultant find reference materials that could be helpful for a specific client need.  Simply scroll down to the IC source column heading (e.g. 1. ROWI) and click.  The user is transferred to the specific reference material worksheet.

Estimating Considerations

The estimated time frame for completing the business model using the e-business – Business Case Tool is about 6-8 week elapsed time or a baseline estimate of 40 person-days.  This is a preliminary estimate at this time and assumes that the client meets the “medium” expectations for the five-(5) key levers that affect consultant hours.  These levers are:  Available Baseline Cost Data; Status of a Strategic Business Plan; Client team participation; Client’s communication plans; and, Executive Champion for the initiative. 

Assumptions for the following baseline estimated hours are:

  1. This is a “fast path” e-business strategy & design engagement with a total elapsed time of 12 to 16 weeks.
  2. This business case effort is for a “single” e-business initiative and pilot of typical size and complexity from a technology perspective.
  3. That the client team will perform most of the data gathering using the data gathering templates provided in the e-business – Business Case Tool.

The steps for using this guideline is includes the following:

  • Evaluate the five key drivers of consultant time giving a H=High, M=Medium, or L=Low capability.
  • Apply the value for each driver to the baseline hours where they apply (indicated by X’s).
  • Summarize the revised hours for each phase.

Estimating Guideline Template:


Here is an example of an evaluation of a client’s readiness to support the business case:  

Availability of Baseline Cost Data   = L

Strategic Plan    = H

Client Team Participation   = M

Communication Plan   = L

Executive Champion   = H


This example resulted in a 58.5 increase in total hours over the base, which is about a 15% increase.  Upon further analysis, the reason for this increase is primarily due to the fact that the client does not have baseline cost data to support this initiative.  While the “Communications Plan” rating is low resulting in a 33% increase for several phases, this increase is partially offset by a strong strategic plan and executive champion.
