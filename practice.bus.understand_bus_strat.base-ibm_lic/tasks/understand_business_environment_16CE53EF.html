Task: Understand the Business Environment
Examine the client's position within their industry.
Disciplines: Business Requirements
Purpose
The reasons for performing this task include:
  • To understand the domain of interest by looking at its interactions with other human systems that exist in its environment
  • To articulate roles of entities within the enterprise and roles of external entities that relate to the enterprise
  • To clarify the customers and other key constituents of the business, as a preparation for determining use case actors
  • To understand the sources for information flows within the business
  • To understand issues in the client's industry and how they may be addressed by the client's strategy

      Steps
      Define the boundary of the enterprise
      Define the boundary of the enterprise or business area under consideration within their industry
      Obtain Business Drivers

      Understand the principles the client uses to guide the direction of their business.

      Key items to look for are goals, motivational, or directional items that will impact the business operations and security of the organization. This would include any or all of the following:

      • Applicable Industry Legislation
      • Consortiums, Interest Groups, Consumer Groups
      • Regulatory Bodies
      • Board of Directors or CEO Directives
      • Mission Statements
      • Business Conduct Guidelines
      • Statements of Direction in Financial Reports, etc
      An example of a Business Driver would be ‘Grow the business in new markets’ or ‘Protect the company image’.

      From a security perspective, it is important to understand the goals of the company in order to determine what challenges security may face. If the business is planning on expanding into a new country, assessments and evaluations need to be made to ensure that the business is protected from any potential threats of that expansion. Security can also prioritize its focus on drivers of the organization.
      Obtain IT Drivers

      Understand how the IT environment is currently directed and the principles used to guide IT is the goal of this task. A series of interviews and some document review is usually needed to identify IT Drivers.

      Key items to look for are motivational or directional items that will impact the IT operations and the security of the organization. This would include any or all of the following:

      • Applicable Industry Legislation
      • Mission Statements
      • Business Conduct Guidelines
      • Director/CIO memos, directives, etc
      • IT Decision Documents or directives
      • Security Organization documents and directives
      An example of an IT Driver would be ‘Reduce security incidents by 30%’ or ‘Comply with ISO-17799 before 4/05’. Some items that are Business Drivers may also be IT Drivers. Again, knowing the Business and IT Drivers helps security know the goals and challenges of the organization and helps to prioritize efforts.
