Task: Understand Business Drivers
Understand the principles the client uses to guide the direction of their business.
Disciplines: Business Requirements
Purpose
To understand the business drivers that are motivating the need for change.
Relationships

      Main Description

      Identify the top business issues, challenges or priorities for the enterprise. They can be thought of as high-level immutable requirements, which the strategy and its infrastructure (business and IT) must support.  These priorities, plus the overall business direction, are used to define a set of business drivers, which will underpin all subsequent architecture activities.

      Obtain for answers to the following questions:

      • What major changes are occurring in the industry?
      • What are the major environmental and legislative considerations?
      • What is the major basis of competition in the industry?
      • Has this changed recently or is their an expectation that this will change?
      • Is there major merger and demerger activity occurring?
      • What changes have taken place in the industry in the last 5 years?
      • What is anticipated in the next 5 years
      • What partnerships have been made in the industry recently?
      • What is expected in the future?
      • What partnerships have you formed recently?
      Key Considerations

      When documenting the business drivers, keep the documentation brief and to the point. Some pointers for documenting business drivers include:

      • Identify the business drivers that will affect the technology strategy during the next two years.
      • Prioritize the changes needed in the short term (six months to one year out).
      • Document any assumptions.
      • Include industry trends as appropriate.
      • List the drivers without identifying the technology solution.
      • Restrict the business drivers document to only the business problems; general technology problems, such as "transaction rates are increasing" are appropriate. However, keep specific technology issues, such as "network capacity needs to be increased", reserved for the architecture documents.
