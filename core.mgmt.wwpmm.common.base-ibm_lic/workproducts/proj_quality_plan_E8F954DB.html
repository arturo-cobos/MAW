Artifact: Project Quality Plan (ENG 347)
The project quality plan describes all the activities and other arrangements intended to achieve the planned level of quality for technical work products and project management work products.
Domains: Quality Management
Work Product Kinds: Plan
Purpose

The prime purpose of the Project Quality Plan is to provide a plan for ensuring that project deliverables will meet the acceptance criteria specified in the Agreement (or established as internal acceptance criteria), at a cost consistent with that included in the project Financial Plan. In addition to the formal acceptance criteria, criteria to enhance customer satisfaction should also be considered.

A secondary purpose of the Project Quality Plan is to ensure compliance with quality standards of external certification organizations such as ISO. It also increases sponsor confidence in the teamâ€™s ability to achieve quality objectives.

Key Considerations

The business unit or competency of the delivery organization will typically provide a standard quality plan that satisfies the quality requirements for its organization.

Typically, one Project Quality Plan is created by the top-level project organizational unit to address the entire project. The top-level project organizational unit identifies quality criteria associated with all deliverables. Quality contributors and assurance checkpoints are also identified by the top-level unit. This cascades down to project organizational units within the organizational breakdown structure. Each project organizational unit may add to or expand the quality criteria, and also assign additional quality contributors and quality assurance steps to cover the work at its level.

A health review will typically be performed on the Project Quality Plan to ensure it is complete, unambiguous, reflective of the work planned in the Agreement and consistent with business unit and competency quality procedures. Quality assurance compliance and health reviews conducted during project execution should ensure that the Project Quality Plan is maintained throughout the life of the project.

1. Validation and Verification

A health review will typically be performed on the Project quality plan to ensure it is complete, unambiguous, reflective of the work planned in the Agreement and consistent with business unit and competency quality procedures. Quality assurance compliance and health reviews conducted during project execution should ensure that the Project quality plan is maintained throughout the life of the project.

Tailoring
Impact of not having

Without a Project Quality Plan, neither the sponsor nor the project management team can be confident that project deliverables will meet their agreed acceptance criteria.

All projects performed by ISO certified organizations require a Project Quality Plan.

Reasons for not needing

It is not always necessary to produce a formal Project Quality Plan.

  • In a small project, the manager can often simply provide direction to the team about how the project will complete its quality objectives.
  • In a project with a familiar context (for example in a specific consulting engagement with experienced staff), all the people involved may be well acquainted with how the work will be carried out and the quality requirements satisfied.
