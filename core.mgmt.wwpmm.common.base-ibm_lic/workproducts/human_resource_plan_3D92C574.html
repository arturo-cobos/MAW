Artifact: Human Resource Plan (ENG 329)
A plan showing the number of people required for the project by human resource category , with required experience and skill levels, timeframe, office and materiel requirements, and an education plan for each category.
Domains: Project Management
Work Product Kinds: Plan
Purpose

The purpose of the Human Resource Plan is to provide the project manager a tool to help in staff planning, estimating staff costs, coordinating staff acquisition and controlling staff levels, staff related needs, and team-building activities over the life of the project.

The project manager uses the Human Resource Plan when building up or restructuring the resource pool for the project, especially during the startup period of a phase.

The Human Resource Plan is also used to provide the project manager with a high level view of the progress in obtaining and starting staff and, consequently, where are the bottlenecks and the areas where management effort should be focused. Additionally, each time the staffing situation is reviewed, the associated team-building activities are reassessed and augmented accordingly.

Description
Main DescriptionThe Human Resource Plan shows the number of people required to staff the project, analyzed by human resource category and time. It also specifies the facilities and training required for each human resource category and the planned team-building activities.
Brief Outline

The Human Resource Plan is structured as three components.

  • Human resource requirements
  • Facilities and training requirements
  • Team-building plan

The Human resource requirements component is structured at the human resource category level. Note that the Staff List and the Staff Schedule contain information about individual project team members. The second component includes the team building plan. A team building plan is prepared at the project level but, each project organizational unit (POU) may also prepare a team building plan.

Key Considerations

The Human Resource Plan is created during Planning and kept up to date as the project proceeds.

Early in the planning phase, this work product is no more than an estimate of the skills required and the number of people needed within each skill category. The numbers are derived from the initial Project Management Schedule and the project manager’s experience. As planning proceeds, the Human resource plan becomes more solid and acts as a constraint on the Project Management Schedule.  During Starting, when the people are joining the project, it is expanded to include summary information about the actual project term.

Throughout the project, any plans for team-building activities can be added to the plan.

The Human Resource Plan may be revised if staffing requirements change because of any Change Orders that arise during the course of the project

On large projects, each project organizational unit may build its own Human Resource Plan. In that case, a summary Human resource plan is created for the entire project.

The project manager verifies that the Human Resource Plan is consistent with all the other project management plans, in particular the Project Management Schedule.

Tailoring
Impact of not having

Lack of a Human Resource Plan could result in the failure to obtain appropriately skilled resources when they are needed on the project.

It could also result in failure to provide new team members with the facilities and training they need, with a consequent impact on productivity and progress.

Reasons for not needingA formal, detailed Human Resource Plan is not required for small projects with simple staffing requirements.
