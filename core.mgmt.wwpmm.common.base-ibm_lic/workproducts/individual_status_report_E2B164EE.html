Artifact: Individual Status Report (ENG 330)
A report prepared by the individual team member which documents the status of work items , effort expended, planned absences and any comments.
Domains: Project Management
Work Product Kinds: Record
Purpose

The purpose of the Individual Status Report is to provide the team leader or project manager of the sub-project or overall project with an accurate account of time spent on work by each individual and an assessment of the effort that remains to complete the assigned work.

It is typically an input to the update of the operational schedule with the effort spent to date. It is also used as an input to update the management schedule when no operational schedule is implemented. It also provides a means of documenting comments regarding topics that may impact an individual’s or team’s ability to complete work or should otherwise be brought to the attention of the project manager.

Description
Main Description

An Individual Status Report is a scheduled report that is created by each project team member on a periodic basis, typically weekly or biweekly, as defined in the Project Procedures Description.

This report captures the effort (time) spent on the work that is in progress, an estimate of the effort required to complete the work in progress, and a list of comments for the project manager.

The level of granularity at which individuals report their effort and status is defined in the Project Procedures Description. The lowest level that could be reported is the work item level. The information provided in the Individual Status Report is aggregated to assess and report on the status of the sub-project or overall project.

Brief Outline

The Individual Status Report contains four components:

  • Team information
  • Status
  • Planned absence
  • Comments

Key Considerations

The Individual Status Report is completed by project team members according to the policies of the business unit and procedures in the Project Procedures Description. Reports by individuals are typically collected and validated by a team leader or by the project manager at the next level of the project hierarchy.

Keep the Individual Status Report format and content simple and easy to collect. Project management software is often used to collect and report the effort information in the Individual Status Report to avoid duplications in the process of updating the Operational Schedule.

The team leader or project manager for the sub-project validates Individual Status Reports. For more information on this activity, refer to process TC.10.10. Collect and validate effort-related data.

Tailoring
Impact of not havingLack of Individual status reports will lead to inaccurate accounting of effort and, hence, an invalid assessment of sub-project or project status.
Reasons for not needing

The policies of the delivery and performing organization’s business unit typically define when Individual status reports are not required.

Projects may not need Individual status reports if they do not track progress at an individual level. Such projects are typically characterized as:

  • Single-level projects with a small number of people who are located in a single location,
  • Projects based on standard offerings with few unique characteristics, or
  • Projects below a cost threshold.
