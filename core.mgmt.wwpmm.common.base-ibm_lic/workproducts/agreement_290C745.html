Purpose

The purpose of the Agreement is to specify the mutual commitments of the parties.

The purpose of any amendments is to keep the Agreement up to date in the light of any agreed changes that occur as the project or sub-project proceeds.

Description
Main Description

An Agreement is the formal definition of the mutual commitments of the delivery organization and the sponsor, of a subcontractor and the delivery organization or of other parties, such as co-contractors. There can be more than two parties to an Agreement.

The structure and content of the Agreement can vary markedly from project to project. In particular, agreements with external sponsors and subcontractors are usually very different from those with internal organizations.

When the parties belong to different enterprises, the Agreement is a legally binding contract. A contract usually has two parts:

  • base terms and conditions
  • a transaction document, often called a statement of work (SOW).

The terms and conditions, which establish the legal framework within which business is carried out but do not describe a particular project, are usually defined in a base agreement. Typically, such a base agreement is already in place when a particular project starts and remains in force after that project finishes. The base agreement is outside the scope of WWPMM.

The transaction document formally defines, for one particular project, what products and services are to be supplied and how they are to be delivered within the framework of the terms and conditions.

When the parties belong to the same enterprise, the Agreement is less formal, for example a document of understanding (DOU) or an offering proposal.

In the remainder of this document, the parties to the Agreement are referred to as the "provider" and the "recipient" of the goods and services with which the project or sub-project is concerned.

Considering that at any point in time an agreement is represented by both an initial agreement and possible amendments, the Agreement work product description reflects this by containing two components: one for the initial Agreement and another for any amendments. Therefore, when reading the term Agreement, one can also take this to mean the Agreement (and any amendments).

This is written to contractually obligate the client and IBM to support an agile approach, and define the light deliverables that the team is empowered to tailor more than on a traditional engagement.
Brief Outline

The format and content of the Agreement are heavily dependent on the business practices of the parties concerned and may be dependent on the local legal conventions or governmental regulations.

The following description of the content should be regarded as indicative rather than definitive. The Agreement is described in two components: an initial or revised agreement and any amendments (zero to many).

Key Considerations

Typically, the provider of the goods and services prepares a draft Agreement by restructuring some of the information contained in the project or sub-project plans into an existing business unit template. The recipient then proposes a set of changes and, after a period of negotiation, a mutually acceptable compromise is agreed.

The validation and verification requirements for amendments are defined by the policies of both parties' business units.

Simple changes are often approved by the parties' project managers. More complex ones or those that exceed a defined cost threshold, may require the approval of an independent quality assurance function.

When nonstandard terms and conditions are changed, they are usually validated by means of reviews with the legal and business practices functions.

The validation and verification requirements for Agreements are defined by the policies of both parties' business units. Typically, this involves approval by an independent quality assurance function.

Tailoring
Impact of not having

Where the parties to the Agreement belong to different enterprises, without a contract, there is no legal obligation for the provider to supply the goods and services or for the recipient to pay for them.

Where the parties belong to the same enterprise, lack of an Agreement can lead to serious misunderstandings and a poor business relationship between the parties, and the unnecessary invocation of the escalation process.

Reasons for not needingA formal Agreement may not be required when the parties belong to the same enterprise and it is decided that the understanding between them will be documented using normal business processes, such as the minutes of meetings.
