Artifact: Risk Log (ENG 436)
The Risk Log is a list of known and open risks to the project. It is sorted in order of importance and associated with specific mitigation or contingency actions.
Domains: Project Management
Work Product Kinds: Record
Purpose
The purpose of the Risk Log is to provide summary information of all the risks identified for effective management of the project or contract. The log is needed to assess the status of each risk.

Description
Main DescriptionRisks that require recording are listed in the Risk Log. The Risk Log is updated each time a new risk is identified. Depending on the amount of detail associated with the risk, a seperate Risk Definition form is created. The Risk Log will be updated when the status of the risk changes.

The Risk Log is reviewed periodically to monitor resolution of pending risks.

Key Considerations

The Risk Log is created at the start of the project during risk identification, and updated throughout as dictated in the project Risk Management Plan.

Use the information in the Risk Definition to keep the Risk Log current.

Tailoring
Impact of not having

Lack of a Risk Log could result in having no visibility to the total number of risks, their mitigation, or closure status. It would be difficult to prioritize the risks and their mitigations, if you do not see them all together and in context one against another.

Reasons for not needing

A Risk Log may not be required if there are no risks or minimal risks in the project.
Examples of projects that may not need a Risk Log include:

  • Projects with no or minimal risks
  • Projects using tools to manage Risk Definition and Risk Occurrence Documents
Representation Options

A representation option for the Risk Log is to capture it as a section in the coarse-grained plan for the project. This means the coarse-grained plan has to be revisited constantly as you update risks. The fine-grained plans will contain only the tasks that you will be doing to mitigate risks in the short term.
