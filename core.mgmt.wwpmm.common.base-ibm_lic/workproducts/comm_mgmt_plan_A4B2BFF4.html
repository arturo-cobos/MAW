Artifact: Communications Management Plan (ENG 320)
Document that describes the plan to meet the information and communications needs of stakeholders : that is, who needs what information, when they will need it, and how they will receive it.
Domains: Project Management
Work Product Kinds: Plan
Purpose

The purpose of the Communications Management Plan is to:

  • Identify the information requirements of the individual project stakeholders and team members
  • Define the sources of the information for collection or production
  • Document the flow of the information, time frames, recipients, and frequencies
  • Selecting the technical means and forms of effective delivery including telecommunication conferencing, instant messaging and other collaboration tools such as wikis or team rooms
  • Forecasting the resources that are necessary to execute the plan
  • Recognize and accommodate cultural, and language and time zones challenges within the project team and among project stakeholders such as:
    1. Traditional feedback loops may not work effectively (e.g., the message may be sent, but not received as intended).
    2. Explore new/various uses of technology to assist with verification that a message was received as intended.
    3. Pay attention to cross-cultural differences regarding conflict management and expressing agreement and/or commitment.
    4. Plan for cultural workshops for interacting teams with a focus on minimizing cultural differences and conflicts.
    5. Plan for time so that team members across different time zones and geographies can interact.

Description
Main Description

The Communications Management Plan defines the regular meetings, reporting, and other communications that take place during the project. It identifies the type of information, the medium for communicating, and the audience to whom it is directed.

Brief Outline

The Communications Management Plan contains the following components:

  • Plan overview
  • Media
  • Scheduled meetings
  • Reporting
  • Other communications
  • Schedule
  • Assumptions, dependencies, risks
  • Costs

Key Considerations

The content of the Communications Management Plan is selected by reviewing the key project objectives with focus on those areas where communication and information sharing will make a positive contribution to the project success.

A preliminary Communication Management Plan is built during project Defining and Planning activities. The plan is then expanded at the start of execution and maintained or adjusted throughout the life of the project.

The Communications Management Plan is developed with the involvement of appropriate stakeholders. It is typically approved by the project manager and validated by the sponsor and/or IBM management.

For large complex projects which have complex communications it may be necessary to create several communications plans – for example, one for external use, one for internal use, one for end users.

Tailoring
Impact of not having

Lack of properly managed communications plan can:

  • seriously impact the decision making process;
  • cause misunderstanding and mistrust;
  • lead to poor cooperation and demoralization; and
  • hurt project image.
Reasons for not needing

The policies of the delivery organization typically define the requirements for a Communications Management Plan.

In general, a formal extensive Communication Management Plan may not be needed:

  • If the project is minor in scope, has a single level of project management, is implemented by a small team, or is below a threshold cost.
  • If the project provides a standard, well-defined offering where all participants are familiar with the communications protocol, such as on a consulting engagement with experienced staff for a long-term customer.
