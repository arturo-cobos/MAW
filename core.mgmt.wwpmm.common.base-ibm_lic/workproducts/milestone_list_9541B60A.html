Artifact: Milestone List (ENG 335)
The list identifying project milestones, due date, and status, including history.
Domains: Project Management
Work Product Kinds: Plan
Purpose

The purpose of the Milestone List is:

  • To provide a high-level summary of the Project Management Schedule that the project manager can use to focus on the important events within their scope of control and as a means of communications with the other stakeholders.
  • To provide a single document that shows all the milestones, their descriptions, and the significant events related to them.

Description
Main DescriptionThe Milestone List is a definition of the key milestones contained in one Project Management Schedule together with a log of all the significant events related to those milestones, that occur during the course of the project.
Brief Outline

The Milestone List contains:

  • An identification section
  • A milestone definition section for each milestone
  • A milestone history section for each milestone

Key Considerations

The Milestone List is extracted from the Agreement and the corresponding Project Management Schedule as part of the Starting activities of project execution. At a minimum, it is updated whenever a milestone is revised or is achieved.

It should contain:

  • All the key commitments made by the project manager to their manager (or, in the case of the top-level project manager, to the sponsor as specified in the sponsor agreement).
  • All the key dependencies on reporting project organizational units, including subcontractors.
  • Any other important events to which the project manager must pay particular attention.

The Milestone List is validated by ensuring that:

  • It is up to date and is consistent with the corresponding Project Management Schedule.
  • All the important commitments and dependencies of the project organizational unit are listed.
  • The history of each milestone is recorded accurately.
Tailoring
Impact of not having

Some consequences of not using a Milestone List are that:

  • The project manager may find it difficult to pick out the key commitments from the detail in the Project Management Schedule.
  • Inconsistencies between project organizational unit plans may be more difficult to detect.
  • Communications with the stakeholders may be poorly focused.
  • Important historical information, which might be needed in case a dispute arises, may be lost.
Reasons for not needingThe Milestone List is recommended for even the simplest of projects, unless a very simple Project Management Schedule alone may suffice.
