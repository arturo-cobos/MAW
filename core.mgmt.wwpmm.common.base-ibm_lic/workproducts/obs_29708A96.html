Purpose

The purpose of the Organizational Breakdown Structure (OBS) is to highlight the shape of the project in terms of human resource organization, and to provide a basis for planning the work of each project organizational unit.

Description
Main Description

Projects are organized into one or more project organizational units. Each project organizational unit is assigned one or both of the responsibilities of:

  • Completing one or more sub-projects.
  • Managing one or more other project organizational units.

The Organizational Breakdown Structure (OBS) defines how the project is organized into project organizational units and describes:

  • The relationships between the sub-projects and the project organizational units.
  • The reporting relationships between the project organizational units.
  • The reporting relationships with the delivery organization and with the other performing organizations.
  • The team structure of the project organizational units.

When the OBS is limited to a depiction of its broad constituent parts, it is called a “high-level OBS”.

Brief Outline

The Organizational Breakdown Structure (OBS), contains the following components:

  • Sponsor organization
  • Project organization
    • Project organization chart
  • Project organizational units
    • Identification
    • Team members
    • Sub-projects
    • Teams
  • Delivery organization
  • Performing organization

Key Considerations

The project organizational units and the project organization chart are developed when shaping the project.

In some contexts, the project organization can be already developed within the Project Charter, and so needs only to be confirmed when shaping the project.

The team structure is developed during Planning activities. It needs to be established to start scheduling the project and building the Human Resource Plan.

The team structure is progressively completed when assigning people to the roles. It needs to be updated when people are reassigned or released. More detailed assignments are managed through the Operational Schedule.

The Organizational Breakdown Structure (OBS), together with the other components of the Project Definition, is used to explain to a newcomer his/her role within the project organization.

The Organizational Breakdown Structure is verified by the project manager. It is approved according to the document control procedure for the delivery organization.

Tailoring
Impact of not having

Without an Organizational Breakdown Structure (OBS), members of the project management team may be unclear about their responsibilities in relation to the project as a whole.

In addition project efficiency may be affected by the following lack of clarity:

  • Sub-project responsibility may be unclear.
  • Roles within a team may be unclear.
Reasons for not needing

For some small projects having only one project organizational unit, the Organizational Breakdown Structure (OBS) may be simplified to the team structure.
