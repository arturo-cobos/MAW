Artifact: Findings Log (ENG 404)
A record of the identification and resolution of findings including the description, severity, category, date, proposed resolution, and status.
Domains: Quality Management
Work Product Kinds: Record
Purpose

The Findings Log is needed to facilitate the management of the deliverables during the validation process until they are accepted by the receiving organization. The management of the deliverables is performed by controlling the resolution of any findings that are identified.

Description
Main Description

The Findings Log is a record of the identification and resolution of findings. As validation takes place, the Findings Log is created and updated at three distinct stages of the project:

  • deliverables received from a subcontractor are validated by the delivery organization
  • deliverables that are to be released to the sponsor are verified and validated internally by the delivery organization, often by a testing function that is independent of the development function
  • deliverables released by the delivery organization are validated by the sponsor or a delegated representative.

Key information to be recorded includes the summary of the finding, the date discovered, and agreed resolution.

Key Considerations

The Findings Log is updated on a periodic basis. The period for updating the log may change as the project advances. While weekly may be sufficient in early stages, daily or multiple times a day may be required close to a delivery acceptance.

The Findings Log is reviewed regularly to monitor resolution of findings, and also to analyze trends in findings occurrence and resolution.

The Findings Log must be up-to-date and consistent with the findings reported by or to another party under the Agreement for the project.
Tailoring
Impact of not having
  • Both the sender and the receiver of the deliverable (delivery organization -> sponsor or subcontractor -> delivery organization) need to ensure that an agreed resolution has been approved for each finding. Without maintaining a Findings Log, the project is exposed to high contractual risks when the delivery is between different companies.
  • The resolution of more than a very small number of findings will become difficult to control, resulting in prolonging the validation process.
Reasons for not needingThe Findings Log is not needed on those projects, for example some consultancy projects, that do not have a formal validation stage or when only a very small number of findings is anticipated.
