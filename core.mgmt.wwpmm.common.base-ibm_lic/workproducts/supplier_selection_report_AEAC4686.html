Artifact: Supplier Selection Report (ENG 358)
Documentation resulting from choosing a supplier based on a completed supplier evaluation.
Domains: Project Management
Work Product Kinds: Record
Purpose

The purpose of the Supplier Selection Report is:

  • To provide documentary evidence that the process of supplier selection was thorough and equitable.
  • To capture the experience gained for use in future supplier selection activity.

Description
Main Description

The Supplier Selection Report documents how a particular supplier was chosen to provide products or services to the project. It includes both a description of the selection process and the results of executing that process.

Sometimes it is decided to use a single supplier. In this case, the Supplier Selection Report documents the justification for this decision.

Brief Outline

The Supplier Selection Report contains the following components:

  • Candidate selection
  • Final selection
Notation

The content and format of the report may be specified by the purchasing department concerned but will usually include the items listed in the following table.

Project / Sub-project manager

Selection process A description of the process, for example the Request for Information (RFI) process, used to arrive at the list of candidate suppliers.

Final selection  
Selection process A description of the process, for example the Request for Proposal (RFP) process, used to arrive at the list of candidate suppliers.
Supplier evaluation criteria The criteria used to judge the ability of each supplier to deliver proposed product and/or service.
Product/service evaluation criteria The criteria used to judge the products and/or services proposed by the supplier.
Evaluation results The results of the analysis of each candidate and their proposed products and/or services against the defined evaluation criteria. Includes a summary of strengths and weakness against the evaluation criteria and the results of any quantitative scoring process that was applied.
Recommendation The name of the selected supplier and details of the products and/or services to be delivered, together with the rationale behind the decision.

Key Considerations

Usually, the project management team and the purchasing department have a joint responsibility for supplier selection and the supplier selection procedure is often dictated by purchasing department policies.

Generally, supplier selection takes place in two stages:

  • A list of candidate suppliers is drawn up. A formal request for information (RFI) may be issued to help identify suitable candidates.
  • The candidates submit proposals, perhaps in response to a formal request for proposal (RFP), which (possibly, with further information) are used by to choose one supplier.

The Candidate selection component of the Supplier Selection Report is written to document stage 1 and the final selection component is completed during stage 2.

Validation and verification requirements are defined by the policies and procedures of the delivery organization's purchasing unit.

The Supplier Selection Report would typically be reviewed by the top-level project manager and the delivery organization's purchasing unit.

Supplier Selection Reports may be subject to formal audit.

Tailoring
Impact of not having

Lack of a Supplier Selection Report could mean that the project manager cannot demonstrate the selection process was fair or that the project is non-compliant with the policies of the delivery organization's purchasing department.

Reasons for not needing

Most purchasing departments define a threshold, below which Supplier Selection Reports are not required.
