Guideline: Performance Engineering CDR Criteria Checklist
The Critical Design Review (CDR) ensures that the collection of component designs appropriately implement the overall system design and supporting requirements, are well understood and are agreed to by all parties, after which a baseline is established for both future reference and change control.
Relationships
Related Elements
  • Perform the Critical Design Review
Main Description

1 Description

In an Enterprise SE&A project, the Design Baseline contains the component design for the system described in the Component Baseline.

This technique paper contains the checklists which the PE team uses during the CDR to ensure that the PE goals and objectives have been adhered to during the Design Baseline Phase, It is important that the performance and capacity aspects of the component design are well-conceived and capable of meeting requirements. This proves helpful to the later SE&A baselines as the components are implemented, tested and deployed, which in turn enhances the likelihood that the overall project will satisfy the customerâ€™s requirements. Deliverables which are deemed deficient from a performance and capacity standpoint may lead to the entry of a defect, which is then taken into account during the CDR Scorecard process.

2 Usage Context

This technique paper should be used as guidance while conducting a Critical Design Review in an enterprise SE&A project.

3 Content

The following PE specific items should be verified during CDR:

CDR Preparation

  • PE work products / deliverables from PDR phase are revisited based on any new information 
    • Performance estimates 
    • Performance models 
    • Resulting performance reports (SLCA, Operational Model updates, sizings) 
  • Key performance risks areas in detail design were evaluated using selected estimation / modeling techniques 
  • Results of estimation forwarded to appropriate areas (e.g. design, development, etc.) 
  • Design baseline materials reviewed by PE team, especially 
    • Test plans 
    • RTVM

CDR Performance Questions

  • Are both system and component technical volumetrics being maintained? 
  • Have business volumetrics changed? Will they change our technical volumetrics, sizing, ability to meet SLRs or other performance-critical assumptions? 
  • Has the technology research been completed and incorporated into the estimation/modeling activities? 
  • What results are available for the system and component performance estimation and modeling activities done to date? 
  • Has the capacity plan been updated to take into account the system and component estimation/modeling done so far? 
  • Do the component designs reflect adequate attention to performance considerations? 
  • Do the component developers have the tools and techniques they need to address module performance during code and unit test? 
  • How does the development team plan to track progress against their performance goals? 
  • Has a performance test plan been defined? 
  • Will the performance test environment be ready and available by the time performance testing is scheduled to start?
  • Has the performance risk been reassessed since the PDR? 
  • Will the performance-centric items satisfy the applicable CDR scorecard criteria, i.e.
    • Static Component Design and Test
      • Completeness with the test plans and traceability to acceptance criteria and end-to-end functionality agreed to by the testing team
    • Service Delivery Center (SDC) / Managed Operations and Delivery Organization
      • Agreement with regard to production infrastructure, its capacity and projections
      • Agreement with regard to availability and disaster recovery approach and projections
    • System Testing
      • Test plans traceable to system/component requirements and acceptance criteria

CDR Actions

  • CDR issues are raised for concerns which may affect CDR scorecard findings.
  • PE Strategy and Plan documents are updated as needed to reflect any new information coming out of the CDR, with stakeholders being informed.
