Guideline: Performance Engineering Business Requirements Review (BRR) Checklist
The Business Requirements Review (BRR) ensures that these requirements are well understood and agreed to by all parties, after which a baseline is established for both future reference and change control.
Relationships
Related Elements

Main Description

1 Description

In an Enterprise SE&A project, the Customer Baseline contains the customer’s requirements as a basis for the subsequent technical work needed to implement those requirements.

This technique paper contains the checklists, which the PE team uses during the BRR to ensure that the PE goals and objectives have been adhered to during the Customer Baseline Phase. It is important that the performance and capacity aspects of the customer’s requirements are well-defined, and that the business rationales for these requirements are well understood. This will prove helpful to the later SE&A baselines as the requirements are elaborated and measures of success are defined, which will in turn enhance the likelihood that the overall project will satisfy the customer’s requirements. Deliverables which are deemed deficient from a performance and capacity standpoint may lead to the entry of a defect, which is then taken into account during the BRR Scorecard process.

2 Usage Context

This technique paper should be used as guidance while conducting a Business Requirements Review in an enterprise SE&A project.

3 Content

The following PE specific items should be verified during BRR:

BRR Preparation

  • PE Strategy document developed 
  • PE Strategy document reviewed with and agreed to by stakeholders 
  • Customer baseline materials reviewed by PE team, especially 
    • Non-Functional Requirements pertaining to performance 
    • Customer-supplied volumetric assumptions

BRR Performance Questions

  • Do the performance requirements take into account the information embodied in the rest of the customer baseline? 
  • Is the current or previous performance engineering strategy understood? 
  • Is there a reason to believe that the current PE strategy will need to change based on the information available in the performance requirements? 
  • Will the performance requirements satisfy the applicable BRR scorecard criteria for NFRs, i.e. 
       3.2.1 Validated that requirements were defined
       3.2.2 Ensured that agreed to requirements are clear and unambiguous
       3.2.3 Ensured Stakeholders and Customer signed off on requirements and associated priority
       3.2.4 Ensured requirements are traceable to business scope and objectives
       3.2.5 Identified risks, issues and dependencies 
  • Where appropriate, have acceptance criteria been written to define what constitutes “meeting the requirements”?

BRR Actions

  • BRR issues are raised to mention concerns which may affect BRR scorecard findings. 
  • PE Strategy document is updated as needed to reflect any new information coming out of the BRR, with stakeholders being informed.
