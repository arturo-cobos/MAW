Guideline: Performance Engineering PDW Outputs Checklists
This technique paper contains the checklists that the PE team will need during the PDW to develop a suitable performance engineering strategy.
Main Description

1 Description

In an Enterprise SE&A project, many PE activities go beyond the scope of the SE&A team’s direct responsibilities. For these reasons, it is helpful to manage the PE effort as a subproject in its own right, to be initiated by means of a PE project definition workshop (PE PDW). The objective of the PE PDW is the formation of an extended Performance Engineering team with an agreed-to approach for managing performance risk throughout the life of the project.

2 Usage Context

This technique paper should be used as guidance while conducting a Project Definition Workshop in an enterprise SE&A project.

3 Content

The following PE specific items should be verified during PDW:

Areas Represented

  • Project sponsor and/or customer representative for kick-off 
  • Lead performance engineer and/or PE staff 
  • Lead systems engineer and/or SE staff 
  • SME representing business process/requirements area 
  • SME representing architecture/design team 
  • SME representing development team 
  • SME representing test team 
  • SME representing deployment/operations team

PDW Preparation

  • PE PDW orientation materials (e.g. this paper and supporting materials) distributed to invitees 
  • Performance engineering engineer and/or PE staff prepares PE materials for PDW 
  • Lead systems engineering engineer and/or SE staff prepares SEMP summary for PDW 
  • Each SME prepares area-specific performance presentation 
    • SME representing business process/requirements area 
    • SME representing architecture/design team 
    • SME representing development team 
    • SME representing test team 
    • SME representing deployment/operations team 
  • Scribe appointed for meeting 
  • Moderator appointed for PE “themes” portion of the meeting

PDW Outputs

  • PE PDW “themes” discussion with agreed-upon PE goals for the project, contributing areas, facilities/processes/tools are available, application to SE baseline review (may be in the form of updates to Agenda presentation) 
  • SME Area-specific performance presentations 
    • Business process/requirements area 
    • Architecture/design team 
    • Development team 
    • Test team 
    • Deployment/operations team 
  • PE PDW minutes

PRR Actions

  • PRR issues are raised for concerns which may affect PRR scorecard findings.
