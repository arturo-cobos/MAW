Main Description

An approach to developing the PE strategy is to hold a PE project definition workshop, including representatives from the client or sponsor, performance engineering, business, architecture, development, test and operations organizations.  The purpose of such a workshop is to come to an agreement on:

  • The PE goals for each development phase of the project
  • The PE activities and techniques which can be used to attain those goals
  • The appropriate development phase and responsible organization for applying each PE activity and technique.

Once there is general consensus on how PE can be applied to the project, the PE team can use this information to develop a draft version of the PE strategy and distribute it to workshop participants for their review.  If a workshop is not feasible, the PE team can gather much of this information by interviewing representatives of each of the teams separately, although the gaining of consensus may be more difficult this way.

If there are multiple projects involving the same application or the same set of applications, then it is possible that a sample PE can be created, using the steps described in the Development Approach section above, and then applied as a template. 

The engagement may involve a number of projects which are distinct entities but are similar in terms of business functionality or technology implementation. In that case it may be prudent to create one PE strategy, which, after review by the client and refinements, could form a standard template for creating PE strategies for the subsequent projects.
