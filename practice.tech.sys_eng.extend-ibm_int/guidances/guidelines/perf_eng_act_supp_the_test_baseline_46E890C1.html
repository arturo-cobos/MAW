Guideline: Performance Engineering Activities Supporting the Test Baseline
The Test Readiness Review (TRR) ensures that test plans and test cases fully support the requirements for the system being tested, after which a baseline is established for both future reference and change control.

Main Description

In an Enterprise SE&A project, the Test Baseline encompasses the test plans and supporting documentation for the system described in the preceding baselines.

The performance engineering team has a vested interest in ensuring that the performance and capacity aspects of the test plan are well-conceived and focused on addressing the key performance requirements and the areas of greatest performance risk. Deliverables which are deemed deficient from a performance and capacity standpoint may lead to the entry of a defect, which is then taken into account during the TRR Scorecard process.

At this time, the PE team should have access to results from the development/implementation team which should give an idea whether the system is likely to have trouble meeting its performance requirements. Based on this knowledge, the PE team should advise management on whether performance-related test entry criteria (if any) have been met and whether the test effort should proceed. The testing phase also offers a good opportunity to exercise live monitoring and performance management tools and procedures, which the PE team should coordinate with the appropriate test and production support teams.

This technique paper should be used as a guidance on applying PE activities and techniques in support of the TRR.
