Guideline: Performance Engineering System Requirements Review (SRR) Checklist
The System Requirements Review (SRR) ensures that these requirements appropriately address the customer requirements selected for implementation, are well understood and are agreed to by all parties, after which a baseline is established for both future reference and change control.

Main Description

1 Description

In an Enterprise SE&A project, the System Baseline contains the overall requirements for the system to be developed or enhanced to meet the customer requirements articulated in the Customer Baseline.

This technique paper contains the checklists which the PE team uses during the SRR to ensure that the PE goals and objectives have been adhered to during the System Baseline Phase It is important that the performance and capacity aspects of the system requirements are well-defined, measurable, verifiable and achievable. This proves helpful to the later SE&A baselines as the requirements are elaborated and implemented by component, which in turn enhances the likelihood that the overall project will satisfy the customerâ€™s requirements. Deliverables which are deemed deficient from a performance and capacity standpoint may lead to the entry of a defect, which is then taken into account during the SRR Scorecard process.

2 Usage Context

This technique paper should be used as guidance while conducting a System Requirements Review in an enterprise SE&A project.

3 Content

The following PE specific items should be verified during SRR:

SRR Preparation

  • PE Strategy document developed 
  • PE Strategy document reviewed with and agreed to by stakeholders 
  • Initial Sizing developed using appropriate level of performance estimation or modeling 
  • System viability / feasibility / risk assessed from performance perspective 
  • System baseline materials reviewed by PE team, especially 
    • Functional requirements, such as the use case model 
    • Non-Functional Requirements pertaining to performance 
    • Technical volumetric assumptions

SRR Performance Questions

  • Is the existing (or previous) PE strategy understood? 
  • Are the system performance requirements understood? 
  • Are the system acceptance criteria for performance understood? 
  • Has an initial sizing of the system solution been performed? 
  • Has the performance feasibility and risk for the system been accessed? 
  • Are business and technical volumetrics available? 
  • What system components will require technology research with respect to their performance characteristics? 
  • What system areas will require performance estimation and/or modeling to address areas of performance risk? 
  • Is there an initial version of a capacity plan available? 
  • Has work begun on defining the Service Level Requirements (SLRs)? 
  • Does the Requirements Traceability & Verification Matrix (RTVM) reflect wise choices on when to use estimation, when to use modeling and when to use test to validate performance requirement compliance? 
  • Will the performance requirements satisfy the applicable SRR scorecard criteria for NFRs, i.e. 
    • Business Requirements and Process Definition 
      • Business requirements (functional and non-functional) defined and prioritized 
    • Requirements Traceability 
      • Requirements Traceability Matrix maps business requirements to systems requirements. 
      • Customer concurs with the business requirements and their mapping to system requirements. 
    • Acceptance Criteria 
      • Acceptance Criteria, test methods, and test types have been added to Requirements Traceability Matrix. 
      • Customer concurs with the Acceptance Criteria, test methods, and test types.

SRR Actions

  • SRR issues are raised for concerns which may affect SRR scorecard findings.
  • PE Strategy and Plan documents are updated as needed to reflect any new information coming out of the SRR, with stakeholders being informed.
