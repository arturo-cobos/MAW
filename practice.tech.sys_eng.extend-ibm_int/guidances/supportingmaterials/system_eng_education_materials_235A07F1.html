Main Description
The following education materials are available, with the first two courses recommended for Systems Engineers:

GBSEA001: An Introduction to Systems Engineering & Architecture

The SE&A: Introductions course is designed to educate course participants how to incorporate the SE&A approach into quality proposals for complex projects and how to best use SE&A on complex projects.

The relationship of the SE&A approach to the reduction of troubled projects, the value of using the SE&A approach on complex projects and a study of the SE&A methodology are also presented, so that course participants will feel confident to adopt and describe the SE&A concept to their customers.

PMI PDUs: 3.0, offering code: 1107-SEA001 (Provider ID: 1107; Course ID: SEA001)

3.0 hours - eLearning

GBSEA010 (in EMEA: SEA010): Systems Engineering & Architecture: Foundations

The SE&A: Foundations course details the responsibilities of the Systems Engineer and the entire technical team throughout the full life cycle of a project, with a focus on "Systems Thinking" and ownership of the technical solution. This is done via a series of lectures and in-class exercises.

Note: SE&A: Foundations is a refresh of the Systems Engineering Bootcamp course.

PMI PDUs: 16.0, offering code: 1107-SEA010 (Provider ID: 1107; Course ID: SEA010) 

16 hours - Traditional classroom. Distance leaning option available.

GBS2401: Complex Bid Solutioning

The solutioning of complex bids requires bid managers and contributing SMEs to bring many different things together in a short time period. If this effort is not managed in a structured and methodical way, we reduce our win chances and create downstream challenges for implementation teams.

If the Complex Bid Manager understands the interactions of all elements of the bid and how and where to delegate to subject matter experts, IBM can have better bids, leading to increased win rates. Better bids also allow fewer gaps or "bugs" going into project launch - bugs which cost exponentially more to resolve than if found in the sales and solutioning phases.

This 3-part self paced course prepares senior practitioners to work on complex bids effectively. It provides a big picture view of the methods and processes that need to be applied; it introduces simplified solutioning models based on Team Solution Design; and it provides a granular view of the solutioning process itself and how key work products evolve across the bid lifecycle. The course describes how to use "solution thinking" to help partition and specify complex solutions while minimizing gaps and ensuring that the whole IBM solution is viable, not just the technical elements.

PMI PDUs: None

3.0 hours - eLearning

GBSEA200: Leading Large Services Opportunities

Leading Large Services Opportunities (LLSO): Applied Techniques for Partners, Proposal Managers, Program Managers and Solution Leaders - teaches those entrusted with solutioning and delivering our engagements techniques to avoid troubled projects, client dissatisfaction, and poor financial returns. The course provides solutioning and delivery techniques to:

  • Match solutions to client's requirements and expectations,
  • Crisply define scope,
  • Perform detailed technical planning during solution development,
  • Effectively transition the opportunity from sales to delivery, and
  • Plan for technical insight and control during delivery.

PMI PDUs: 24.0, offering code: 1107-SEA200 (Provider ID: 1107; Course ID: SEA200)

24 hours - Traditional classroom. Distance leaning option available.

GBS2228, GBS2428, GBS2429, GBS2443: Stevens SE&A Education Hub

The SE&A Ed Hub is an intensive year long training commitment implemented as a graduate-level certificate education program for IBM employees developed by Stevens Institute of Technology (Stevens) and IBM.

The goal of this initiative is to equip our best technical talent (you!) with the education and skills needed to create solutions for our complex Systems Integration (SI) projects. The SE&A Ed Hub Program accelerates the learning opportunity for the next generation of technical leaders by establishing a sequence of week long courses that combine key courses required for both IT Architect Professional Certification as well as execution of Complex System Integration (CSI) projects into a coordinated intensive training curriculum.

Long term - our goal is to increase the quality and quantity of IT Architects practicing the Systems Engineering and Architecture discipline by providing education and training required for project execution and needed as prerequisites to gaining the experiential skills needed for certification as an IT Architect. By coordinating this key training with the opportunity to receive graduate credit that can lead to an advanced academic degree, the SE&A Hub Program also is a unique opportunity to further the students professional and career development in our technical community.


PMI PDUs: None

Four - 40 hour offerings - Traditional classroom.
