Task: Production Readiness Review Cleanup
This task describes how to deal with the cleanup activity that is performed to attend the issues raised during the production readiness review for a project.
Disciplines: System Engineering
Purpose
Address issues as a result of the PRR.

      Main Description
      The project team should perform the following cleanup activities at the end of the PRR:
      1. Review the PRR scorecard, defects, issues, risks, and action items with the project team and appropriate level of management
      2. Start working off the defects, issues, and actions items from the review. Normally, these defects, issues and action items have been added to the project's database. Thus, they are worked through the normal project management system, so all project stakeholders are provided with periodic status updates.
      3. Add the risks to the project risk database to be assessed as part of the normal project management system.

      Additional Actions for low scoring reviews:

      If the PRR review score is less than 85 (Red or Yellow status as defined in the review scorecard), then the following actions must take place to ensure that the appropriate management teams are aware of all projects with low scoring reviews. This process will provide customer awareness of the project risks as well as ensure that the corrective actions/issues/defects are closed.

      The project team will develop a Corrective Action Plan to address the review's outstanding defects, issues, and actions. As part of the plan, the team will:

      • Based on the defects, issues, and actions, determine whether testing should proceed.
      • Schedule meetings to track the review defect/issue/action resolutions. As defects, issues, and actions get resolved, the review score will improve. The meetings will be conducted as needed until the review score improves to a value greater than 85 (Green status). At that point, the remaining defects/issues/actions can be worked BAU.
      • Review the Corrective Action Plan with the appropriate management teams and the customer(s).
