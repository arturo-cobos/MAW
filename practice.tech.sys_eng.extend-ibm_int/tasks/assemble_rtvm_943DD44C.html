Task: Assemble Requirements Traceability and Verification Matrix
This task defines the process of creating the Requirements Traceability and Verification Matrix for the business requirements at the customer baseline.
Disciplines: System Engineering
Purpose

The Requirements Traceability and Verification Matrix (RTVM) provides requirements traceability between levels or types of requirements. This activity establishes the RTVM at the start of the engagement by tying the business requirements (including processes and use cases) to their origins in the goals and objectives of the enterprise.

      Main Description

      The Requirements Traceability and Verification Matrix (RTVM) has served its traditional purpose of providing traceability and verification between levels of requirements and their verification methods. This activity, now performed as part of the Customer Baseline (at Business Requirements Review) extends the notion of the RTVM in the following important ways:

      • The RTVM connects the initial set of business requirements (comprising business processes and use cases) to the goals, objectives and high level wants and needs of the project. This is of particular importance in SOMA engagements where the Goal Service Model describes the key goals of the enterprise, the key performance indicators (KPI) and the metrics associated with them. A matrix view of the processes connected with the goals and KPI helps identify processes that are aligned with the goals, potential problem areas and areas of improvement.
      • The RTVM connects the processes and use cases downwards to the system functions or applications thereby establishing bounds on the scope of the engagement and the expected behavior of the system.

      The key parts of the RTVM are:

      1. The source and destination items
      2. The traceability links (indicated by X marks)
      3. Scope and coverage views



      Figure 1: Trace Processes to Goals

      Figure 1 describes how the RTVM is used to trace goals to business processes. The source item is the goals, listed across the RTVM in columns. The destination item is the business process, listed on consecutive rows from top to bottom. The traceability links are denoted by ‘X’ marks. The scope and coverage views, shown in green, are used to ascertain completeness in the goals and conformance in the processes.

      In SOMA engagements, the goals are directly referenced in the Goal Service Model. In other cases, the goals may refer to contracted business outcomes or to wants and needs or to specific enterprise performance criteria. In all cases, it is clear that the business RTVM is a key deliverable that links the business requirements to specific enterprise objectives.
