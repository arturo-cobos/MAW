Artifact: Performance Engineering Strategy (ARC 404)
The Performance Engineering Strategy is a high-level description of major system-wide activities that collectively achieve the overall desired result, as expressed by the performance engineering goals.
Domains: Architecture
Work Product Kinds: Strategy
Purpose
The purpose of the Performance Engineering Strategy work product is to:
  • Define how the appropriate PE principles and practices will be applied in order to mitigate the project risks, manage the performance risks, and identify any exposures.
  • Provide a common approach and a common terminology to use for design, development, testing across multiple organizational structures and business units.
  • Give a general direction for where PE is headed.
  • Emphasize the importance of a comprehensive team approach to PE.
  • Describe the complexity of performance engineering across the lifecycle.
As a communication vehicle to identify any performance issue early rather than late.

Description
Main Description

This artifact starts with a high-level description of these goals as they apply to the current project, and then describes how the various organizations (e.g. architects, developers, testers, performance engineers, delivery) will collaborate during the development process to achieve these goals.

Performance engineering (PE) is a technical discipline which aims to ensure that a development project results in the delivery of a system which meets a pre-specified set of performance objectives. All strategy statements are expressed in this document in high-level terms of physical components and activities, resources (people and machines), the recognized development phases and the performance engineering activities which will be utilized during those phases; i.e., the stuff of which action plans are made at lower` levels. In most situations, these are merely definitions of terms and terminology, used to establish a common understanding and lay the foundation to initiate the next step, which is the development of the PE plan.

It is equally important to note that planning details are NOT included here.

Brief Outline

The following is the Table of Contents of a PE Strategy document:

  • Introduction: An introduction to the PE strategy work product.  It describes the document, its purpose and, if appropriate, provides project background.
  • Scope: The scope defines the boundaries of the performance engineering effort.
  • Assumptions: Any underlying assumptions for the PE strategy are clearly stated in this section.
  • PE Goals: Sets the direction for performance engineering efforts.
  • PE Focus Areas: Areas of performance and capacity-related non-functional requirements (NFRs) which are of special concern, such as response time, throughput, volume assumptions, scalability, etc.
  • Development phases: The phases of development like BRS, SRS etc., which will be used to manage the larger development project i.e. system development, whether it is a traditional “waterfall” project or an iterative development project.
  • Risk Assessment
  • PE Themes: Overarching performance engineering areas of concern which may span multiple areas, as documented in the Performance Engineering and Management Method (PEMM), along with the activities encompassed in those themes.
  • Organizational Responsibility: Based on project deliverables and interdependence of teams delivering performance-centric work products, defined so that boundaries for hand-off or hand-over are clear. 
  • PE Methods and Tools: Any performance method or tools strategy, at the client organization or IBM, relevant to the project.
  • PE Decision Checkpoints (DCPs): Defined for different project phases and for their importance to project success, clearly defined for each PE-specific phase of the project.
  • Performance Metrics: Any organization, business unit, competency metrics program or measurements strategy relevant to performance acceptance criteria relevant to the project.
  • PE Data Management Strategy: Approach for PE data gathering, analysis and maintenance.
  • Appendices:
    - Appendix A – Glossary of PE Terms: In order to clarify the PE terms that may be new to the client or terms that may be used differently, it is always a good practice to include the glossary of PE terms.
    - Other Appendices as Required

Tailoring
Impact of not havingThe primary purpose of PE is to identify and manage performance-related risks throughout the development project. This includes:
  • Estimating future performance
  • Ensuring that performance requirements for the solution are developed
  • Testing the implemented solution to ensure that performance requirements can be met
PE has a broad scope and needs the cross-organizational cooperation to implement it. Failing to develop the PE Strategy work product may lead to:
  • Lack of common understanding of each organization’s responsibility with respect to performance
  • Inability to reach the project’s performance goals
Without performance strategy work product in place performance will not be taken seriously enough throughout design and development and therefore may be expensive or impossible to retrofit once problems are discovered.
Reasons for not needingA separate PE strategy work product is not always required in simple projects or engagements. If the project is one of many similar projects or iterations, the PE strategy is created once and re-used in the subsequent projects.

A PE strategy may not be needed in situations where a PE strategy can be created for one project and it can then be used as a template with some variations, for subsequent projects of the same program. It is also possible to publish the PE strategy and the PE plan as a consolidated document.
