Deliverable: DesignFlow Phase Report (DCD 274)
The DesignFlow Phase Report is a deliverable produced in the each of the three phases of a DesignFlow engagement: Process Selection and Definition, Transformation Requirements, and Process Design.
Purpose
The contents of this deliverable provide senior executives with the approach, status, findings and planned next steps of the engagement. In addition it will also provide a clear understanding of the prioritization, resources and funding required for the follow on phase of this engagement
The content of this deliverable is used in several ways:
  • To inform senior executives of the progress and status of the engagement to show that it is meeting the intended objectives
  • To provide senior executives with a set of recommendations and short-term management decisions and actions that will achieve the objectives of the follow on phase. This includes an agreement to proceed and recommendations for follow-on IBM involvement.
Main Description
The deliverable provides an overall executive briefing package and contains a summary of all the outputs produced during the phase. The contents are structured to present the approach, status, findings and planned next steps of the engagement. This will enable senior executives to understand the business benefits of proceeding to the next phase.
This content of this deliverable should include:
  • A summary of the overall scope, objectives and deliverables of the engagement
  • A summary of all the outputs documented in this and any previous phases.
  • A summary of recommendations, benefits and a short-term Management Action Plan for the next phase

Some work products that are inputs to the deliverable, may be extensions and/or revisions to previously generated work products or instances of work products.

Packaging Instructions

Format

Use the following format to build the report.
  • Project/Engagement Overview
  • Approach
  • Findings and Analysis
  • Recommendations
  • Next Steps

Analysis Tasks

  1. Collect and organize all of the materials that were created in this Phase so that they can be distributed and reviewed.
  2. Review these material with anyone who is politically important to the project. Collect input/recommendations.
  3. Consolidate all of the input/recommendations received and adjust the outputs as appropriate.
  4. Create the contract for the next Phase.
  5. Informally preview some of the material with the executive to determine if any areas need more work before the final review.
  6. Organize all of the outputs into the final report for this Phase.
  7. Present the report and contract to the executive who can agree to move on to the next Phase.
  8. Obtain the required signatures on the contracts.

Analysis Tips

  1. Build the report along the way; do not wait until the end of the phase to pull the information together.
  2. You may choose to include or not include technology in your transformation at this point.
  3. Get agreement to proceed to the next phase when presenting the Phase Report.
