Deliverable: Business Case and Business Control Book Deliverable (DCD 002)
The Control Book is produced after the final selection of the software vendor. It provides detail benefit analysis that is summarized in the Business Case. The Control Book documents the client’s expected benefits associated with improved processes by compiling the best practices, metrics and benefits associated to a sub-process.
Purpose
The purpose of the Business Case and Control Book deliverable material is to document and quantify the benefits, costs, schedule and assumptions required to justify the acquisition and implementation of the new packaged system utilizing future cross functional business processes.
Main Description

The scope of the Business Case addresses:

  • Identified "hard" benefits to the client using an accepted, balanced, consistent financial evaluation approach
  • Selected identified "soft" benefits to the client
  • Selected "Quick Hit" benefits to the client that can be identified and claimed based on the project, which may or may not be solely due to the implementation of the new system
  • The timing and order of recognizing benefits based on a high level implementation plan
  • The costs of the system based on best available information for hardware, software and services
  • Assumptions used in calculating the costs, benefits and schedule

Packaging Instructions

The Business Case is a graphical presentation with inserts of spreadsheet templates, charts and graphs for certain components of data presentation in approximately 10-30 pages.

The Business Control Book is a document of approximately 8 – 20 pages.

The Business Case deliverable materials will consist of the following, as appropriate:

  • Executive Overview
  • Vendor Selection
  • Benefits
  • Costs
  • Cash Flow Analysis
  • High Level Implementation Plan
  • Next Steps
  • Appendices

The Business Control Book is a report generated from the AdvisoryWare tool.  If the AdvisoryWare tool is not used during the engagement, a Word or Excel document will be created to capture the following information:

  • Header information including the entity, currency type, process, process owner and sub-process.
  • Best practice information including the best practice name, description, enablers, constraints and the benefit owner.  The benefit owner is the person who identified the benefit associated with the process.
  • Metric details including the metric, calculation method and measures.
  • Yearly breakdown of the benefits.
  • Summary section containing detail notes, the process status and a total benefit figure.
