Deliverable: Future Business Process Design Deliverable (DCD 020)
The Future Business Process Design identifies, depicts, and describes the high-level business and IT processes that are necessary to execute the new electronic business model.
Purpose

This deliverable is produced to:

  • Define the scope of the package implementation initiatives
  • Provide a useful vehicle for communication and discussion
  • Assist in gaining executive agreement to the scope and boundaries
  • Provide the basis to identify detailed functions, business processes, and requirements (use cases)
  • Provide a basis for aligning process and data for automation of package implementation processes
  • Provide the basis to define the future environment for architectural design
  • Represent a detailed view of how users will execute the clientâ€™s business process using the packaged application
  • Assist Development Teams in configuring the system and begin development work once the model is completed
Main Description

The models provide graphical representations that effectively communicate and define the e-business initiatives at a high-level for executive management, line management, business users, and IT personnel. A description of the processes ensures the boundaries are well understood at the time the processes are identified. The process design describe in verb / object format "what" is done, not "how" it is done, and identifies the relationships between the processes.

For IT architecture and application design purposes that support the package implementation, the processes and activities to be automated must be identified so that they can be rationalized. In other words, duplicate activities must be removed and the nouns translated into objects defined in the application model.
