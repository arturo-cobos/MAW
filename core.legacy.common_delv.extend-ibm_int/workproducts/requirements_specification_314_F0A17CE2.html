Deliverable: Requirements Specification (DCD 314)
The Requirement Specifications Deliverable is to refine and further document the requirements that must be met by a new solution.
Purpose
The document will be used to gain consensus between the client and IBM on the requirements and it will provide the foundation for all future project efforts. This document will further refine the requirements defined in the earlier requirements documents.
Main Description
The Requirements Specification Deliverable can contain the following information:
  • The Use Case Model which defines the identification-level use cases for the solution and their high level interactions. A subset of the key use cases will be extended and refined to further define the requirements.
  • A system context diagram that identifies the interfaces, data sources, and interface frequencies.
  • The usability requirements and, where possible, the measurement details for specific user profiles, consisting of preconditions and performance.
  • The non-functional requirements with defined minimum and maximum acceptable criteria for the system.
  • A catalog of the business rules, policies, and governances that must be addressed by the solution.

Packaging InstructionsThe final document will be finished as a deliverable to the client. All necessary work products should be modified and arranged for official delivery. Additional texts, items, appendixes, or references should be included accordingly. A portion of the document may be delivered in multiple times or the whole document may be delivered at once, and it will be delivered in a reproducible format. Be sure that the document is in useful style and easy to maintain.
