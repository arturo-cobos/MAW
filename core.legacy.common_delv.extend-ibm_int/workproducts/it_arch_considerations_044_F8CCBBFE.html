Deliverable: IT Architecture Considerations (DCD 044)
The IT Architecture Transition Considerations deliverable is normally produced as an output from phase 5 of the IT Architecture - Quickstart engagement model.
Purpose

The contents of this deliverable provide a clear foundation for the prioritization and funding of future initiatives and projects, to further develop and implement the IT Architecture which has been defined. The content of this deliverable is used in several ways:

  • To build on the work done so far by initiating projects to further define and implement the newly defined IT Architecture and the associated governance framework
  • The gaps highlighted between the current environment and the newly defined architectures, will enable the business to make conscious decisions about how it wants to move forward
  • The outputs from the gap analysis activities are also used to identify ways in which future and ongoing development projects can be refocused (without necessarily changing development budgets) to conform to the architectures and thus more effectively support the long-term business strategy
  • The rationalized set of transition initiatives is prioritized and incorporated into a transition strategy, based on business need, enabling the business to make prompt decisions, regarding future investment, etc.
  • To make recommendations on short-term management decisions and actions (to be taken in the next 3 months), which will help to ensure that the activities started during the engagement are continued, with appropriate levels of management sponsorship. This includes recommendations for follow-on IBM involvement
Main Description

The content of this deliverable should include:

  • A reminder of the overall scope, objectives and deliverables of the engagement
  • A summary of the key business drivers, issues and transition requirements which have been raised during the engagement
  • An overview of the findings from the gap analysis activities with observations on the impact of these, on the current development portfolio of application and infrastructure projects
  • A prioritized set of transition initiatives, based on transition issues and opportunities identified in previous activities, tasks and the gap analysis activities
  • An overview of how the proposed transition initiatives support the desired business capabilities
  • Recommendations and a short-term Management Action Plan

GAP ANALYSIS ACTIVITIES

The Gap Analysis component of the IT Architecture Transition Considerations deliverable focuses on new projects (either under development, or in plan to be implemented within the next 3 years). It documents the gaps between the components of the newly defined IT Architecture and the ‘default IT Architectures’ used in each development project. In this instance, the analysis looks at criteria like support for future business directions, implementation of desired business capabilities and the functional and technical fit of these new projects. The outputs are used to guide requirements gathering, design and product/package selection decisions (which often happen early on in the development lifecycle). The key here is to ‘catch’ individual projects, before major design and acquisition decisions are made, which may not be in line with the overall business objectives and desired key capabilities.

In these gap analysis activities, the principles, desired business capabilities and future architecture models (application, data and technology/infrastructure) defined in the IT Architecture Context and IT Architecture DCDs are used as key inputs.

TRANSITION ACTIVITIES - TRANSITION INITIATIVES, TRANSITION STRATEGY and TRANSITION PLAN

Note: in this ITA-Q engagement model, only transition initiatives are normally defined. If the scope of a specific engagement needs to include the definition of a transition strategy and a plan to implement this, additional time and resources will be required.

Transition activities, from an enterprise-wide architecture perspective, are focused on identifying the key elements of the business and I/T environment, which must change in order to implement the target architectures. During the previous phases of this engagement model, various transition issues and requirements will be identified. These are combined here, into a prioritized set of transition initiatives, which will deliver and implement the desired business and IT environment. The transition initiatives are subsequently used as a basis for defining transition scenarios that evaluate alternative ways of achieving the desired transformation within acceptable time frames. The chosen transition scenario is encapsulated in a transition strategy and finally in a transition plan with a prioritized, budgeted, sequenced set of projects, which can be implemented.

Some simple definitions may help here:

Transition Initiative activities:

  • Define the various initiatives needed to help the business move towards its desired business and IT environments
  • Group initiatives so they can be tied back to the desired distinctive capabilities

Transition Strategy activities:

  • Define various transition scenarios – alternative ways of combining the transition initiatives and projects
  • Consider timescales, benefits, costing and resource options, risk and availability of skills
  • Match each scenario to the desired distinctive capabilities and business priorities
  • Define a recommended transition approach, normally with a 1 to 3 year timeframe

Transition Planning activities:

  • Take the chosen transition scenario and strategy and consider the costs and sequencing of initiatives in more detail
  • Provide a cost plan/timeline for implementation activities over the next 12 months
  • Provide a resource plan

Initiatives:

  • Describe direction or intent
  • Are typically used to change mindset or viewpoint
  • Will be tied to a change program
  • Define groupings of projects
  • Deliver related or linked benefits
  • Are often coupled to a "future vision" or similar motivator

Projects:

  • Define a unit of work that can be described in an RFP
  • Cover activities which can be managed by one "leader" or "supplier"
  • Have a bounded scope, cost and time that are defined
  • Deliver articulated benefits to specified beneficiaries

Packaging Instructions

The IT Architecture Transition Considerations deliverable assembles the work product outputs into a format, which is suitable for the client. The format typically includes two parts:

  • Specific entries in a database or web application – as defined and agreed as part of the Architecture Delivery Environment – see the Architecture Management DCD for further details
  • Presentation material, which summarizes the findings of the Gap Analysis and Transition definition activities for use with briefing executives and stakeholders alike

The content of the deliverable and its (associated) presentation material is used to educate and inform key client stakeholders of the outcomes from the Transition Considerations phase. This is to ensure that they are sufficiently knowledgeable, motivated, support and see business value in the transition recommendations and any associated business funding required.

Whilst some key client executives and stakeholders may have been involved in developing the content of this deliverable, for most this will be the first time they have seen all the material summarized in a way, which provides them with a clear perspective of the transition options and implications of what they are aiming to achieve.
