Deliverable: End-user Deployment Training Materials (DCD 298)
The End-User Deployment Training Material Deliverable covers the Specification, Design, Development and Physical Production of all materials required to deliver the planned training to impacted staff, management, and other stakeholders as a part of the project implementation.
Purpose
The purpose of these deliverable materials is to provide the client with all training materials developed during the course of this engagement.
Main Description
The End-User Deployment Training Material Deliverable includes materials for all business and project areas, including IT- and process-required staff skills; management development and behavioral training; and train the trainer and evaluation materials.
 
The delivered material to each client will vary by specific engagement requirements, material content and deliverable form.

Packaging InstructionsDetailed Curriculum Development

1. The curriculum is organized by course, lesson, and topic and represents the work-flow of the roles using the new business processes, system tasks and recommended audience. For each of the roles and for each type of user within the role (based on: experience, background knowledge, existing skills, etc.) decide on the best sequence to deliver education and training on the related topics. The topics to be covered in the curriculum should include:



  • Education and training specifically tailored to the user tasks and responsibilities
  • Other training which will help the users understand both the technology and the context within which the changes are being made (e.g., organization changes, PC literacy, business knowledge)


2. Identify names of functional leads and other participants who should be involved in the curriculum related interviews and/or workshops. Develop the workshop/interview schedule.

3. Establish the communication culture and rules of engagement required to complete the workshops/interviews.

4. Do a fit gap of the client business process and job work flow. Review how a role performs their job today and validate the sequence of tasks required to integrate new system processes and tasks into the day-to-day activities of the role (tabletop analysis).

5. The IBM consultant will update the curriculum template built in step 1 above, using the data gathered in the workshops/interviews.

6. The consultant reviews the curriculum results with the client. This can also be done by interview or workshop. The purpose of the review is to gain client consensus for the baseline curriculum for each role.

7. During this round of workshops/interviews, the delivery criteria factors (frequency, criticality, complexity, and number of users) for the roles will be validated. Using the curriculum template, determine and validate the delivery media for each recommended course/role. The following are examples of different delivery methods and performance support tools. Decide which delivery method to use for which topics:



  • Self-paced instruction, such as Web-based Training, Computer Based Training (CBT), or self-study
  • Classroom instructor-led training (ITL)
  • Workshops
  • Lectures
  • Demonstrations
  • On-the-job training
  • Job aids
  • Online Help
  • Distance Learning
  • Study Guides


8. Update the curriculum with descriptions and learning objectives for each course and lesson, any prerequisites, and recommended media.

9. Obtain final sign-off of the curriculum. Determine and document the change control process for any changes that might be necessary to the curriculum from this point forward. Changes should be requested in writing and approved by both the client and IBM.

10. Using the curriculum template from the step above, the IBM consultant will create a developers worksheet. This will include all the information from the curriculum, and will add points to cover, issues related to the lesson/topics, and references to any existing system documentation (scripts, process flows).

11. Several documents are created and/or validated at this point including:



  • A development schedule including negotiated hand-offs of documents / support from the project team members
  • Style guide for writing standards and customer acronyms.
  • Prototype templates for the development and delivery of each media type (ILT, on-line, web, etc).


 
Course Materials
 
During the user documentation specification, the following activities occur:
  • Prototype - discuss potential solutions and create a prototype of the courseware, evaluations, and performance support systems
  • Resource needs - re-evaluate resource needs and resource mix and adjust as needed
  • Document review process- determine how the documents will be edited, naming conventions, time allotted for review, and the sign off policy
  • Evaluation strategy - review the formative evaluation plan and make adjustments as needed
  • Document management and tracking system
  • Document maintenance strategy including post-go-live maintenance
