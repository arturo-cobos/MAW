Artifact: Transition Management Strategy (ORG 306)
The Transition Management Strategy summarizes needed changes and defines the overall approach that an organization will take to implement its new capabilities as well as its required direction.
Domains: Organization Change Management
Work Product Kinds: Strategy
Purpose

The purpose of this work product is to enable successful transformation by using a high-level strategy to:

  • Identify the changes that are needed, and recommend the most effective approach to implementing the desired business solutions, and
  • Reduce the depth and duration of disruption to the business by imposing consistency and direction.

The Transition Management Strategy facilitates acceptance by ensuring that the transition is well-thought-through by both the client team and the consulting team, and that critical success factors are in place.

Key Considerations

This work product links directly in to the Integrated Transition Plan and is preceded by the Transition Initiatives work product. When formulating the strategy, think about how the change will add value to the organization and how it will affect the remainder of the organization.

This work product is a living item and will require monitoring as the implementation progresses. As a strategy it should be a stable document but it may require revision if it is not succeeding

Levels of sponsorship, participation, enthusiasm, and the success of the approach need to be evaluated and discussed at regular steering committee meetings.

The development of this work product should begin with the client during the pre-sale process and should be identified as a key deliverable in partnership with the client. The transformation is theirs and they must own it. If this partnership has not been established, then it must be secured before proceeding.

The terminology can get confusing: use what is comfortable for the client, but begin with a logical hierarchy in project definition and stick to it.

Tailoring
Impact of not having

Without a strategy for managing the transition, the likely results are:

  • Failure to achieve the business objectives on schedule and on budget
  • Failure to attain the projected ROI
  • Confusion about responsibilities, timing, and priorities for the initiatives
  • Competitive disadvantage
