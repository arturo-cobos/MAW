Artifact: Process Gap Assessment (BUS 311)
The Process Gap Assessment work product documents the gaps between the current processes and the future processes and identifies proposed process projects.
Domains: Business Process
Work Product Kinds: Assessment
Purpose

This work product is produced to:

  • Ensure a complete understanding of the required process changes
  • Assess the work required to close the gaps and formulate this into logical groupings (proposed process projects)
  • Assist in communicating the changes to the people who will be impacted
  • Provide input for an overall transition plan
  • Identify potential risks from a process perspective

Key Considerations
  • This work product is input to the integrated transition plan.  Many of the risks will only surface when the changes in the organization and in technology are considered together with the changes in process.
  • The process gaps and proposed projects should be reviewed and verified by representatives from both the business and IT management, for completeness, feasibility and to gain commitment to go-ahead with the recommendations.  Changes in the business processes, particularly involving new technologies, usually mean a change in IT processes as well.
Tailoring
Impact of not having

If this work product is not produced then:

  • Changes needed to move from the current to future processes may not all be identified and the appropriate process projects may not be identified
  • It will be difficult to articulate the changes that have to be made without this work product so communication to stakeholders may be inadequate
  • It will not be possible to identify the dependencies across process, organization and technology projects if process projects are not identified
  • The steps needed to make the changes may not be defined in a logical sequence resulting in a higher level of disruption to both the business and staff
  • It will be difficult to estimate the costs / resources required to migrate from the current environment to the future environment
  • Risks to the implementation from a process perspective may not be identified and managed effectively
Reasons for not needing

This work product will not be required when:

  • There are no significant differences between the current and the future processes.
  • For small projects where the changes are obvious, this work product may be very simple to produce and serve merely as a crosscheck for completeness.
Representation Options

The notation is dependent on the nature of the change:

  • Major changes in processes / sub-processes
  • Changes in the sequence of activities (workflow) to accomplish a sub-process or changes in how these activities are enabled (with organization and technology)

Both require supporting documentation outlining the proposed process project, resources, preliminary qualification of costs and risks.
