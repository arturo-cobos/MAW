Artifact: Problem Ticket Form (OPS 417)
The Problem Ticket Form documents a problem that is reported by a user.
Domains: Maintenance
Work Product Kinds: Record
Purpose
The purpose of this work product is to allow problems encountered by users on any operational system to be documented and assigned to an appropriate group for investigation and resolution. The work product also provides the solution and retrospective actions decided for the problem, and provide status on the progress of the resolution.
Relationships

Description
Main DescriptionThe Problem Ticket Form contains details of the problem, the system on which there is a problem, the name of the person reporting the problem, analysis details, and the solution and retrospective actions.

Key Considerations
  • The completion of this work product should be detailed in a Site Problem Management Procedure that will reflect the nature of the contract with the user, in particular the Service Level agreements that are in place. The procedure should define the severity levels, cause codes and other such information.
  • Remember that the purpose of the Emergency Fix is to quickly resolve a problem and to get the system up and running as soon as possible. It will not be possible to provide a thorough explanation of the problem and consequent design of a permanent fix. The Emergency Fix is temporary and a permanent fix will be implemented afterwards in either the Corrective Maintenance or Enhancement path.
  • The Problem Ticket Form provides a framework for providing the minimum amount of detail necessary to perform the fix. It allows those responsible for implementing the permanent fix to know what the problem was, how it was dealt with, and who should be contacted for further information.
  • Be concise when writing the report so that an adequate amount of information is recorded. Stick to the facts and do not over elaborate, but at the same time make sure that anyone performing subsequent work is able to understand the detail.
Tailoring
Impact of not having

Consequences of not having this work product include:

  • Problems may not be properly documented.
  • Resolution owners may not have the complete information about the fixing.
  • Proposed solution may not be documented properly, which may lead to risk of making uninformed decisions regarding the proposed solution.
  • Progress of the problem resolution may not be known.
Reasons for not needingThis is a mandatory work product that must be used to document any problems reported on operational systems. There is no reason not to have this work product.
