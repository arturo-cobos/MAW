Artifact: Candidate Asset List (ENG 100)
This work product, which is a working document, is the controlling document for (relatively small-grain) reuse work on a project.
Domains: Intellectual Property & Asset Reuse
Work Product Kinds: Inventory
Purpose
The Candidate Asset List documents a brief but organized search for assets. It shows what kinds of assets were searched for, what serious candidates were found, and what turned out to be useful.
 
Some projects are based on large assets whose use is part of the early formulation of the project. On these projects, the Candidate Asset List can still be used in the search for smaller assets.

Description
Main Description

The Candidate Asset List is comprised of the following sections as appropriate for the scope of reuse being done:

Target Requirements

This portion of the Candidate Asset List defines the functional and performance requirements of the target environment in a generalized way to help in your search for potential assets.  The requirements are made very general for retrieval of higher-level assets, such as industry models or architectures, but are more specific for retrieval of lower-level assets, such as frameworks or classes.  This portion of the Candidate Asset List also outlines the reuse objectives of the engagement.  For example, â€œWhat kinds of reuse goals are to be achieved?  What practices improve the reusability of assets developed for the new system?  These objectives are tied to the appropriate asset strategies of industries, cross industries or asset managers. 

Define asset reuse objectives that should be met by this engagement and tie to the asset strategy for the sponsoring organization.

Candidate Assets

This list evolves from very early proposal phases through all phases of your development.  Use the IBM AssetWeb to aid in the search.  Search outside the AssetWeb for potentially applicable product solutions.  The assets on this list are categorized along with products considered for use on an engagement to make them easier to understand.  Each asset is described in terms of the scope of its applicability, the capabilities or functionality it provides, and the context under which it is reusable.  Categorize the assets into problem areas.  Either the system points to this information, or the information is provided by the system within this portion of the plan.

  • List all possible assets and the results of the assessment on whether the assets are applicable to the specific engagement.
  • For each selected asset, identify the development needs (tailoring, enhancement).

Selected Assets

This portion of the Candidate Asset List details the adaptations required for an asset to make it reusable for the engagement.  These adaptations also contain a rough estimate of the cost or effort involved to make this adaptation and who will make it.  Where asset owners are required to adapt or develop assets, the DOUs are listed here as well.  This portion also identifies those assets chosen for use on the engagement.  It is preferable that changes be done by the owning organization so enhancements can be made to the base asset.  Ensure that all legal constraints are followed and legal rights adequately protected.

Brief Outline

This work product is produced as a text document that includes decisions regarding the reuse of assets on engagements, including:

  • A short analysis of the areas where asset reuse is desired and seems possible
  • A list of Candidate Assets to be considered. A principle of the approach is that the decision to use an asset need not be made immediately.
  • Assets that are selected for the engagement, including:
    • Where and how the asset will be used
    • Summary of necessary adaptations, with estimated costs
    • Document of understanding (DOU) with the asset owner
    • Summary of how well the selected assets cover the area of search (probably low coverage) (Optional)
  • Assets that were considered, but not selected and the reason(s) for why they were not selected.

Tailoring
Impact of not havingIf the process of investigating and adapting assets and affected models to produce a Candidate Asset List is not performed, useful reuse opportunities may not be realized. That will have direct effects on the project (time, cost, delivered functionality) as well as on the building of a profitable asset-based business.
Reasons for not needingThe Candidate Asset List is not needed if the team is certain that no useful assets can be found, and there is no need to document the search for assets.
