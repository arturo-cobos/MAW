Artifact: Business Continuity Threat Analysis (OPS 407)
The Business Continuity Threat Analysis work product reviews threats, present in the target environment, to determine the type and frequency within relevance to the type of loss the threat can inflict on the continuity of the business operations and/or financial status.
Domains: Business Continuity
Work Product Kinds: Assessment
Purpose

The purpose of performing a Business Continuity Threat Analysis is to ensure that the business is adequately protecting its assets. Once the assets are determined, then this work product determines what to protect from. 

The primary value is the understanding of the threats present in the environment and their probability for loss events.

Description
Main Description

A threat is a force, either natural or man-made, that is usually unforeseen and cannot be prevented. Threats are grouped into three categories: Natural, Human/Social, or Physical.  Examples of natural threats are hurricanes, tornadoes, or earthquakes. Human/Social threats are those that occur through intentional or unintentional harm such as fire, terrorism, or sabotage. A Physical threat is present in the construction or architecture of the facility, campus, local area, or Data Center.   A risk is the effect on the subject from the threat.  These effects, or risks, can be mitigated or alleviated through the installation of safeguards. 

Brief Outline

The report should identify the organization and locale as well as list the threats researched and any supporting materials to substantiate that data.

Key Considerations

The selection of safeguards is directly related to the substantiation of the type of threats and the risk they present to the organization. Careful attention must be paid to the analysis of threats and their probability of occurrence.  This work product is designed to provide the stakeholders with business justification to provide adequate mitigation against their risk to exposure from threats.

Validation and verification of threats is through local emergency management, risk managers, and FEMA sources to name a few.

Tailoring
Impact of not havingWithout this work product organizations will not be effective in the management and securing of key assets and appropriate budgets to mitigate risk of interruption. There will be no sound basis for deciding which threats and risks from which to protect the business.
Reasons for not needingThe client already has a clear understanding of the threats to the organization, how they affect loss on the organization, and how probable they are.
