Artifact: Security and Privacy Policy (OPS 314)
The Security and Privacy Policy work product contains directives that clearly demonstrate management’s commitment to enterprise security and privacy programs.
Domains: Security & Privacy
Work Product Kinds: Standard
Purpose

The Security and Privacy Policy work product prescribes management’s direction to guide an organization in meeting their corporation’s security and privacy objectives according to its business needs. The policies are expected to be used in the implementation of the organization's business and IT strategies and in any specific solution or application.

The content and implementation of security and privacy policies are part of a typical formal business and financial audit.

Description
Main DescriptionThe Security and Privacy policies provide high level statements of management’s security and privacy goals, objectives and beliefs. The scope is enterprise wide: policies typically identify the enterprise wide roles and responsibilities of the managers and organizations accountable for the implementation of the directives contained in the policies.

Security Policy information contains directives related enterprise wide security concerns, such as:

  • A definition of information security and its importance as an enabling factor for information sharing.
  • A statement of management support.
  • An explanation of specific security requirements including:
    • compliance with legislative and contractual requirements.
    • security education, virus prevention and detection, and business continuity planning.
  • A definition of general and specific roles and responsibilities for the various aspects of the enterprise's information security program.
  • An explanation of the process for reporting suspected security incidents.

Privacy Policy information contains the directives related to handling of personal information, such as:

  • Used only for the stated purpose it was gathered.
  • Kept for the amount of time required by law or regulations or as long as it remains relevant for its primary purpose.
  • Not be disclosed without specific consent.
  • Available for review by the individual.
  • Corrected if errors are known to exist or if the individual identifies errors.
Brief Outline

The Security and Privacy Policy work product has a format that matches an organization’s other official corporate-level documents.

Sections typically found in a Security Policy are:
  • Purpose
  • Issuing Authority
  • Scope
  • Information Definition
  • Compliance
  • Exceptions
  • Enforcement and Violation Handling
  • Individual Use
  • Information Classification and Control
  • Confidentiality/Integrity
  • Availability
  • Access Control
  • Information Ownership
  • Review of User Access Rights
  • Security Awareness
  • Incident Response
  • Virus Prevention
  • Secure Disposal of Equipment
Sections typically found in a Privacy Policy are:
  • Chairman’s Letter
  • Preface
  • Scope
  • Compliance Requirements
  • Privacy Principles
    • Collection Limitation Principle
    • Data Quality Principle
    • Purpose Specification Principle
    • Use Limitation Principle
    • Security Safeguards Principle
    • Individual Participation Principle
    • Accountability Principle
    • Special Personal Data Categories Principle
  • Policy Statements
  • Organization Roles and Responsibilities

Key Considerations

After initial development, the policies will undergo a widening circle of distribution.  While this validation process must be tailored to individual needs, it usually involves getting concurrence from all participants and legal department, presentation to executives, and finally publication to the organization.  A mechanism for updating the newly defined policy directives should be devised as part of the overall management framework.

This work should only be performed by Methodology-trained Security professionals.  Additional resources are identified in the IT Security ICAP Database.
Tailoring
Impact of not having

Absence of Security and Privacy Policies indicate a lack of management direction and support for information security and privacy that can result in significant exposure of corporate assets. These exposures include a lack of individual responsibility, the inability to enforce supporting security policies and procedures in addition to a lack of management support for security/privacy initiatives. Comprehensive policies are necessary to ensure that all areas of security and privacy management are covered, avoiding potential oversights and additional exposures.   

Also, as most security exposures can be traced back to human error, security and privacy policies are the foundations of any security awareness program which help employees, contractors and business partners understand what is expected of them, making unintentional errors far less likely to occur.

Reasons for not needing

Client can produce current, comprehensive security and privacy policy directives.

Client indicates that security and privacy considerations are out of the engagement scope (e.g., when engagement is a proof of concept or rapid development of a prototype). In this case, consider adding the approved Security Disclaimer to the SOW (see References below).

On engagements with a very narrow focus (as opposed to an enterprise level), rather than including this work product, consider proposing a separate engagement to develop security or privacy policies for the client.
