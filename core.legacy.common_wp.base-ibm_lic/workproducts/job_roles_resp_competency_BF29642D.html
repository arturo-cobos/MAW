Artifact: Job Roles - Responsibilities and Competencies (ORG 309)
The work product "Job Roles, Responsibilities, and Competencies" describes all of the elements that constitute an employee's role in the future organization.
Domains: Organization Design
Work Product Kinds: Specification
Purpose
The purpose of this work product is to document the new job roles, responsibilities, and competencies required as a consequence of the initiative. Job descriptions should enable employees to understand their relationships to others in the organization or business unit, and how to perform their jobs successfully. They are a key building block of the future organization and an input to plans to close human capability gaps.

Description
Main Description

Job designs should include:

  • Responsibilities and work tasks
  • Accountabilities
  • Objectives
  • Desired outcomes
  • Competencies required (skills and skill levels, aptitudes)
  • Behaviors that should be demonstrated within the job
  • Measurements by which performance levels will be determined
  • Security authorizations required for access to data
Key Considerations

Keep in mind the client's history and demographic profiles.  Be careful and respectful of the effect that this exercise will have on the affected individuals.  Resistance to new jobs and roles can easily slow down the ongoing implementation of the package and may affect the overall success of the project beyond deployment.

This work should be undertaken only by an experienced Organization Analysis and Design practitioner.

Because job redesign can be a sensitive topic, be prepared for objections and questions.  Involving client team members who helped with the redesign can be very effective in the review, because it demonstrates that buy-in does exist.

Tailoring
Impact of not having

If people’s work changes but their job descriptions do not, confusion about who should do what can ensue: some new tasks may not be assigned, or a task may be performed in multiple jobs that could be done more efficiently in one job. It can also cause problems in the appraisal, promotion, and compensation processes.

A future organization design that includes new or dramatically changed jobs cannot be implemented successfully if the design is not carried through to the individual job level.

Reasons for not needingIn theory, it is possible that no jobs are affected by the initiative, in which case this work product would be unnecessary. In practice, however, jobs are always affected by major initiatives, although the extent of redesign needed can vary widely.

If the client’s human resources department has the capability in-house to redesign jobs and intends to handle this work itself, their outputs can substitute for the consultant’s delivery of this work product.
Representation OptionsThe client is likely to have a standard job description that its organization uses. Depending on the scope of the initiative, this format may need to be supplemented with additional information. A textual document should summarize the highlights of the redesign and the implications for implementation.
