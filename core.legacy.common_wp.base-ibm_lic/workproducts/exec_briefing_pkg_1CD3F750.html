Artifact: Executive Briefing Package (BUS 321)
The Executive Briefing Package work product is an executive-level presentation of e-business concepts and information designed to educate executives on the trends, practices, and facts that will drive decision making during a project.
Domains: Business Strategy
Work Product Kinds: Record
Purpose

The primary purpose of this work product is to raise executive’s awareness and understanding of key issues and concepts that will be evaluated during the project. With a proper level of understanding, executive team members will be able to more quickly act upon findings of the engagement. Also, by increasing the early involvement, interaction, and open dialogue with an executive team, the project is likely to benefit from increased executive sponsorship throughout the remainder of the project.

The Executive Briefing Package is often presented as input to workshops, kickoff meetings and scenario planning activities.

Description
Main Description

The Executive Briefing Package should move beyond supplying facts, data and information, but rather refine these more explicit findings into tacit points of view upon which executives will ground future decisions.

Key Considerations

The Executive Briefing Package should be tailored depending upon its intended use.  For instance, if it is being used during a general kickoff meeting to educate team members about e-business, then its style, form and content should be structured appropriately around transferring knowledge about essential concepts and trends to the attendees.  A different application is where it is being used to set up follow-on workshops or working sessions such as Scenario Planning.  In this instance, its style, form and content should be structured to clearly present and communicate the insights conclusions that were previously gathered through interviews with key stakeholders, research, or other data gathering methods.

  • Ensure the style, approach and content aligns to the audience and its intended use
  • Ensure that current industry specific content (trends, business models, applications, etc.), which is relevant to the engagemens and/or focus areas being explored, is incorporated into the briefing package
  • Incorporate and address key “hot buttons” or “issues”

Follow IBM’s guidelines for preparing presentations and avoid including detail that can be more effectively provided upon request, addressed in a question-and-answer mode or distributed as supplemental material.  Less content is generally better when an interactive dialogue is needed or preferred.  Remember, Data Gathering is an iterative process defined by the limitations of time and the abundance of data. Avoid the trap of too much data, the temptation to “keep gathering data” and the seduction of analyzing too soon!

Tailoring
Impact of not havingSubsequent follow-on work can be delayed or impeded due to either lack of knowledgeable and engaged sponsorship or lack of a common understanding and agreement on the specific issues, challenges, focus and intended application of e-business.

Executives can be overwhelmed with data and information that they neither have the time or inclination to review and understand. This work product ensures that data and information are refined and presented in a concise manner for executives to read, understand and act upon in engagement activities, e.g., workshops.
Reasons for not needingThis work product is not needed when executive management is already well aware or well educated about the concepts related to the project.
Representation Options
