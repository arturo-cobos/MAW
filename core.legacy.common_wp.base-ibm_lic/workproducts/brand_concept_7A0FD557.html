Artifact: Brand Concept (BUS 106)
A Brand Concept is the set of requirements that translate a company’s vision, culture and business into a unique identity for their products and services in a competitive environment.
Domains: Marketing
Work Product Kinds: Concept
Purpose
A Brand Concept provides guidelines for the positioning of a company or one of its products or services in the marketplace. Its primary purpose is to provide a unified approach that will provide standards and consistencies across all media and establish the market position of the company, its products and services with its customers. This consistency reinforces the perceived value of the brand in the marketplace. A Brand Concept provides the design team with valuable input that will help them develop user interfaces consistent with the brand image.
Description
Main DescriptionA Brand Concept establishes a set of requirements for the unique identification of a company or product in the marketplace and identifies how the company would like its customers to feel when they do business with the company or buy a particular product or service.
 
Brand Concepts may also explore how Internet and e-business technologies can transform a company’s brand presence, products and services into new offerings and strategic market positions that create creative differentiation. The Brand Concept is a statement about the most important brand characteristics and attributes of a company.
Key Considerations

A company’s brand is its reputation in the marketplace.  Careful attention must be paid to portraying the brand as accurately as possible in a public environment.  If the application’s functionality, execution, and look and feel are inconsistent with the Brand Concept, the brand will be diluted, impacting the company’s reputation in the marketplace.

Top executives usually can verify their company’s Brand Concept.  For individual products and services, line managers will often be the brand experts.  The marketing organization within a company should also be able to offer expertise on the Brand Concepts for the overall company brand and/or any separately branded products and services.

When the Brand Concept is new, or in the process of changing, it is prudent to test the ideas with external sources who may be potential targets of the brand.  This testing is often conducted by market research organizations.  Brand Concepts may be tested with focus groups and through online surveys.  Special procedures apply to these activities.

Tailoring
Impact of not havingWithout a defined Brand Concept, the design team is handicapped to perform future design and development processes that meet industry standards relevant to the resulting user interface design and its consistency with the desired brand image.
Reasons for not needingThird party agency of choice has provided relevant brand documentation applicable to interactive creative application design.
Representation Options

Brand Concepts are documented as strategic requirements.  They include the identification of market position and market differentiation, competitive products and services, target audiences segmentation, and the emotional and cognitive experience the brand should represent.  Brand agencies typically use a combination of unique creative approaches that vary in their presentation and documentation to portray strategic messages, concept, or concept design and animatics and demographic or psychographic documentation to communicate Brand Concepts.
