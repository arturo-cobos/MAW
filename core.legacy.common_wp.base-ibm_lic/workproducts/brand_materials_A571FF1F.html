Artifact: Brand Materials (BUS 107)
Brand Materials are the artifacts and attributes of a company, product or service brand.
Domains: Marketing
Work Product Kinds: Realization
Purpose
Brand Materials are symbols that represent the identity of a company or product in the marketplace. The companyâ€™s symbols differentiate the company and its products and services. Successful Brand Materials are easily recognized and provoke an immediate cognitive association between a company and its offerings.
Description
Main DescriptionThe most common brand materials are the brand name and logo. These are usually represented in a styleguide that may also describe usage rights. Anything that can be trademarked is considered Brand Material: Company names, product names, service marks, logos, packaging designs, still images, audio jingles, key messages, commercial campaign slogans, photography, characters, animations, or other trademark media and multi-media assets.

Key Considerations

Collect Brand Material before beginning aesthetic design of an application to maintain consistency with the company's existing brand image.

Do not use production versions of Brand Materials (logos from websites, logos scanned from paper, etc.) as source materials for the development process.  The unavoidable inferior quality of these formats will make it difficult to create high quality work products.

Validation of current Brand Material is usually obtained from the Marketing or Corporate Communications organization within the company owning the brand.

New Brand Material can be tested with their intended audiences through focus groups and surveys.

Very successful Brand Materials are often integrated into the language and used by the general public to represent a whole class of products, for example, Coke and sodas, Kleenex and facial tissue, Band-Aid and bandages, and Lego and toy building blocks.  This popularity may serve to dilute the brand image, as other products in the same category may be perceived as being the same.  Some companies are very sensitive to this issue and have strict rules for the usage of their Brand Materials.  Incorrect usage of Brand Material is; at the very least, unprofessional, but occasionally, some companies consider Brand Material styleguide violations serious enough to be considered grounds for litigation or dismissal for employees and vendors.

Tailoring
Impact of not havingBrand Material is used to identify the company, service(s), or product(s) represented by an application. They serve to generate trust, instill confidence, and create loyalty in the products and services that are sold, executed, or represented by the application. Without Brand Materials, users will be less likely to recognize, value, and choose the application over competitive products in the marketplace.
Reasons for not needingThe audience for the application is not subject to any competitive marketplace pressures, or has no choice in the use of the application, for example, green-screen, text based applications intended only for internal use.
Representation Options
  • Brand names are text.
  • Brand logos are documented in analog formats or vector-based digital graphic formats such as Adobe Illustrator, Windows Metafiles, or TrueType Fonts.
  • Styleguides often provide detailed specifications for Brand Materials.  These specifications will include guidelines for usage of the Brand Material in relevant media.
