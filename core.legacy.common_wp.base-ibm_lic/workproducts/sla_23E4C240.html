Artifact: Service Level Agreement (SLA) (OPS 411)
The Service Level Agreement (SLA) defines the agreed to service levels or measurements (Availability and Performance objectives), by which the solution will be supported. These measurements are referred to as "Service Level Agreements" (SLA).
Domains: Maintenance
Work Product Kinds: Agreement
Purpose
The primary purpose of the Service Level Agreement (SLA) is to define the Performance and Availability measurements by which the solution will be supported in the IBM Infrastructure, agreed to by IBM and the customer.
Description
Brief Outline

The Service Level Agreement may include:

  • Network Support & Performance. How long should it take for an Internet or Intranet page to appear on your screen - how many seconds, at least 95% of the time?
  • Infrastructure Support – the level of support offerings, performance measurements and reporting, how will the system infrastructure be supported in the production environment, including disaster recovery if required.
  • Customer Service Center (Help desk) - When you need help, what's the availability of the help you need?  How long does it take you to get an answer after you've identified your problem?  If you must wait for a solution, how is it prioritized?  What level of satisfaction with the service provided could you expect?
  • What if you need Desk side/workstation support? When can you expect to get desk side support?  How fast?  Prioritization of customer problems is based on severity levels.
  • May also include; server availability, problem management response time, maintenance support level, disaster recovery, change management etc.
  • Sign off – sign-off by the service delivery team and customer representatives.
Key Considerations
The SLA requires sign-off by the delivery organization management team and the customer representatives, indicating agreement to support the solution in the production environment.

The delivery organization plays a significant role in defining the terms and conditions of the SLA and the service offerings available. For additional guidance, contact GS Service Delivery Center representative (Service Delivery Project Manager or DPE) supporting the customer account.

Tailoring
Impact of not havingNo agreement in place on performance and availability measurements, system support levels are not defined.
Reasons for not needingFor solutions that will not be deployed on the IBM Infrastructure, an SLA may not be required.
