Artifact: Conditions of Satisfaction (ENG 417)
This artifact captures the sponsor's expectations, priorities, and assessment of how well their expectations were met.
Domains: Relationship Management
Work Product Kinds: Measurement
Purpose

The Conditions of Satisfaction (COS) artifact is used in both sales and delivery to identify and manage the client's expectations across the life-cycle of an engagement.  Documenting and periodically reviewing the client's expectations across the life-cycle enables changes in priorities and new expectations to be easily identified and provides an objective criteria for the client to use when gauging IBM's success.

  • Captures client expectations during the sales phase of a project as solidify customer "buy-in"
  • Helps identify any client expectations surrounding engagement delivery, including any special expectation and assess IBM's performance against those expectations
  • Provides and objective criteria as defined by the client to assess satisfaction with IBM
  • Finally, COS gives you the opportunity to renegotiate expectations that are out of scope or cannot be met

Key Considerations

The initial Conditions of Satisfaction should be collected from the sponsor during the up-front sales process.  At the beginning of the delivery phase you should check with the sales team to find out what expectations were set during the sales phase.

Use this list of expectations to revalidate and refine the sponsor's expectations and priorities during the beginning of the delivery phase.  This will enable you to capture any changes in their expectations and priorities and ensure that you understand what is important to your sponsor.

Routine checkpoints with the sponsor during delivery and at the close of the engagement will keep the project team on track to a successful project.

Development Approach

  • Collect/verify sponsor expectations
  • Establish actions to meet expectations
  • Periodically review progress agains expectations
  • Capture sponsor's input regarding expectations
Tailoring
Impact of not having

Failure to capture and address the client's expectations and priorities leads to mis-understanding and mis-communication with the client and the IBM team.  This leads to:

  • Client dissatisfaction
  • Scope creep
  • Cost overrun
Reasons for not needing

If the project is very small or short in duration, this artifact may not be required.

With tightly coupled projects, a single artifact may be used if the multiple projects are performed for the same sponsor.  For single multi-year long term outsourcing or maintenance engagements, where long term relationships are developed and maintained, a similar process called Objective Setting is more appropriate.

Each IOT may have their own criteria.
