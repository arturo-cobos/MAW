Artifact: Pre-Qualification Vendor Analysis (BUS 061)
The Pre-Qualification Vendor Analysis work product captures valuable information provided from the vendors’ Request for Information (RFI) responses.
Domains: Selection
Work Product Kinds: Assessment
Purpose

The Pre-Qualification Vendor Analysis work product is the summarization of information from the RFI and/or research. This work product is used to assist the project team in evaluating a "long list" of potential vendors in order to identify an "interim list" of vendors that best meets the needs of the business.

The Pre-Qualification Vendor Analysis work product details the team’s findings and justifies elimination of vendors to the client, thus gaining "buy in" from the client.

The purpose of this work product is to provide a document that can be used in a structured presentation to the client executives to lead them through the selection process to get to a list of interim vendors. The document will also serve as a reference guide for the client to justify, to their organization, which vendors will be part of the detailed selection process.

Description
Main Description

The Pre-Qualification Vendor Analysis work product captures valuable information provided from the vendors’ Request for Information (RFI) responses. This information helps the team evaluate each vendor's viability as a software provider. This work product captures the following information about the vendors, where available:

  • General
  • Company Profile
  • Contact Information
  • Stability and Focus
  • Duration in Business
  • Installation Base
  • Financial Status
  • Industry Experience
  • Technology Overview
  • Functional Capabilities (high level)

The IBM consultant will use the vendor information to analyze each potential vendor’s ability to satisfy the initial future capabilities and attributes identified by the client. The Pre-Qualification Vendor Analysis work product assists in completing this task in order to eliminate vendors to reach an "interim list" of vendors.

Brief Outline

The Pre-Qualification Vendor Analysis work product provides a summary view of information gathered about the “long list” of vendors for comparison and elimination of vendors.  It captures data on the overall stability of the vendors and their products as well as their industry focus.  This work product overviews the vendors’ technology, functionality and provides general information about the vendors for review by the team.

This work product is normally developed in a series of spreadsheets, but may be created in a database or in word processing tables.

The Pre-Qualification Vendor Analysis work product should consist of the following work sheets or pages:

  • Title Page
  • General Information - This section captures company profile information such as name, locations, number of employees, contact data, web sites, etc.
  • Stability/Focus - This section details company history information such as number of years in business, number of installations, industry focus and client base.  It may also be used to capture financial performance and risk assessment information.
  • Technology Overview - This section details the general technology base of the product such as operating system, database(s), hardware requirements, etc.  It also looks at type of technology and interface provided by the vendor (i.e., Client/Server, GUI, etc.). It looks at the security standards and interfaces.
  • Functionality Overview - This section outlines the functional capabilities of the software being offered by the vendor against the required functional criteria.
  • Vendor Elimination - This section details the elimination of vendors from consideration, listing the reason for elimination.
  • Interim List - This section identifies the resulting “interim list” of vendors that will be considered going forward in the project.

After the analysis is complete, the information should be displayed in a presentation for the sponsor.  The main advantage of the work product is to take the sponsor through the process of the interim list selection; therefore, the presentation format seems to be the preferred approach to creating this interactive discussion session.

The main sections of the presentation portion of the work product include:

  • Cover Sheet
  • Table of Contents
  • Overview of Process and Sources
  • Screening Criteria appropriate for the target environment
  • Initial Long List of possible solution providers
  • Vendor Analysis Process
  • Elimination Criteria and Justification
  • Recommended Short List of Vendors
  • Next Steps

Key Considerations

The important outcome, however reached, is to gain the client’s agreement that the vendors on the short list are the correct vendors to work with through the detailed selection process.  The evaluation criteria for reaching an “interim list” of vendors should be clearly defined and agreed upon by the client.  It is necessary that the client agrees with the short list findings and that they consider the list complete.  This verification at this point will avoid having them second-guess the process and vendors consideration in the latter stages of the engagement.

This work product should provide the necessary detail to lead the client executive team through the process and the justification of selecting which vendors should be considered in detail for the client solution.  The document recommendations should be validated by the client for completeness to avoid the client coming back later and questioning why certain vendors were not considered.  The Summary worksheet should be validated with the client team lead to ensure agreement on the elimination criteria.

The Pre-Qualification Vendor Analysis should be validated through checking the vendor responses against the data captured in the template.  Research should be performed to validate financial data and the use of third party research should be included in the analysis where appropriate and available.  

Tailoring
Impact of not having

The Pre-Qualification Vendor Analysis provides validation of an "interim list" selection and reduces risk to the success of the project. Without the Pre-Qualification Vendor Analysis which details each vendors overall viability as a software provider, it is difficult to determine whether or not each vendor offers the general functionality, stability and technology to satisfy the client’s goals and objectives for their software selection project.

By eliminating the Pre-Qualification Vendor Analysis, you do not fully utilize valuable information gathered and increase your exposure on the project. Without the work product, the client may not understand why certain vendors were chosen or eliminated. Without this understanding of who and why they are on the interim list, time will be wasted as the client continues to question or second guess vendors.

Reasons for not needingThis work product would be optional when the client has provided the "interim list" of vendors and has contractually indicated that IBM did not assist in the selection of that list. In some cases, the client has already had other consulting groups perform this work, or the organization has outlined standards that need to be followed. In either case, if the client has an interim list of vendors that will only be considered, then the process can start with the validation of those vendors and this work product will not be used.
Representation OptionsThe format of this work product can vary tremendously.  The content emphasized in the presentation portion to the client is recommended as a subset of the research material to present.  The consultant should add any research information that is unique to the client, industry, or solution that would differentiate one solution vendor from another.
