Artifact: Process Assessment and Analysis (BUS 401)
The Process Assessment & Analysis work product documents the results of performing a process assessment on the either the current or future business processes/ sub-processes within the scope of the project.
Domains: Business Process
Work Product Kinds: Assessment
Purpose

This work product is produced to:

  • Select which enterprise, category, process group, processes and/or sub-processes within the scope of the project need to be analyzed further
  • Determine the status of each of the business processes / sub-processes within the project scope so that recommendations can be tailored accordingly
  • Identify potential problem areas
  • Identify potential improvement opportunities
  • Identify areas where the current processes / sub-processes are performing well and are candidates to keep in the future design
  • Identify whether the processes / sub-processes are critical to the business, industry standard or basic
  • Identify potential opportunities for process / sub-process harmonization across divisions, geographic locations, subsidiaries etc.
  • Identify potential opportunities to move responsibility for processes / sub-processes, e.g., to / from customers, suppliers, etc.
  • Identify potential opportunities for enabling technologies to automate the processes / sub-processes

Description
Main Description

The appropriate process assessment/analysis should be selected based on the nature of the engagement. These include:

  • Importance (ranking) / performance (rating) assessment
  • Maturity assessment to which degree existing processes have been implemented
  • Criticality classification assessment
  • Commonality (across multiple locations) assessment
  • Placement (in-house vs. outsource potential) assessment
  • Technology investment assessment
  • Characteristics (for potential technology enablement) assessment
  • How well are the client processes integrated with the their partners’ processes (e.g. suppliers)of the value net

Key Considerations

Key Points

  • If the scope of the project is driven by the client's business strategies/goals and/or customer imperatives, output of the Process Assessment work product should directly support the process selection with rationale such as process performance and process importance in achieving business goals and / or customer sat.
  • In a transformation project do not waste time at this stage in developing current process models / maps to a detailed level (identification of sub-processes (level 2) is sufficient to do an assessment, whereas level 3 (or 4) is required for analysis)
  • It is useful to get different views of importance / performance if you can, e.g. customers, suppliers, business partners, employees, etc.
  • Select the appropriate assessments based on client objectives / nature of the engagement, don't try and do them all!
  • The better you can prepare before the workshop, the more productive each workshop will be.
  • Use subject matter experts to minimize lost time and credibility.
  • Use the simple tools and techniques to help identify root causes; brainstorming, cause and effect (fishbone) diagrams, scatter diagrams, Pareto Analysis.

Approaches to business process redesign vary greatly. IBM’s business process design approach includes the following principles:

  • Best Practices is the task of providing measurement and comparison to best industry practices to ensure that operations remain cost efficient. Benchmarking may also involve the examination of similar processes outside the industry in which the organization currently competes. 
  • Operations assessment is a methodical process of analyzing how employee skill sets, business processes, and existing information systems function. The result is a comprehensive assessment of how effectively a company uses resources and how well its operating units perform.
  • Customer Alignment assesses the value of business activities as they pertain to customers to ensure that an organization is closely aligned with its customers’ specific needs.
  • End-To-End Solutions analyze an entire process to discover more effective and efficient ways of handling it. Examine business processes across all functional and organizational boundaries (including customers, suppliers, and business partners) to provide a holistic view and maximum flexibility in optimizing the process.
  • Eliminate Non-Value Added Activities by identifying and eliminating any activities that do not provide value to either internal or external customers.
  • Time Compression reduce process time and overlap activities whenever possible. Speeding up business cycles can reduce costs and increase customer satisfaction that translates into increased revenue.
  • Organize Around Outcomes organize and measure people and processes around goals and outcomes not as series of intermediate steps.
Tailoring
Impact of not having

If this work product is not produced, then:

  • There will be no basis for identifying where to focus energy on further analysis
  • There may be a tendency to try to "cover the waterfront" and map / analyze all the processes / sub-process in detail leading to wasted effort
  • There may be a tendency to address symptoms rather that genuine root causes
  • Individual processes will not have a known baseline performance for future measurement of performance improvement therefore it will be virtually impossible to directly quantify any cost reduction, cycle time improvement, or defect reduction achieved by the redesigned future process
  • It will not be possible to separate "potential" opportunities from "real" opportunities
  • It will not be possible to identify "quick" wins and prioritize other improvement ideas
  • Trade-offs between package function and process capabilities or capability gaps will be difficult
  • Opportunities for improvement e.g. harmonization, enabling technologies, etc. may be missed
  • There will be no basis for understanding which processes are critical for implementation of client’s business strategy
Reasons for not needing

There are very few instances when this work product is not required.

  • Possibly when a decision has been taken to install new technology, packages, or applications without needing to measure the resulting improvement in performance or to understand the causes of problems experienced with the current process
  • Importance/performance, maturity, criticality classification, commonality, placement, investment or characteristics assessments have already been performed and the proposal includes these results
  • The package or business problem is very limited in scope and the future state is totally defined by the functionality of the package

The more pertinent question is; to what level does the analysis need to be done?  The engagement scope and desired outcome determine the level of analysis required which will be reflected in the resulting work products.

Representation Options

The notation includes tables, matrices and other work product with supporting text for rationale, summary and recommendations.  Note: it is not necessary to produce all these tables, select those most appropriate for the engagement.  The following are shown in the linked example:

  1. Table showing overall summary of importance ranking and performance rating for each process in scope
  2. Table showing performance rating based on assessment criteria related to balanced scorecard
  3. Matrix showing importance ranking vs. performance rating for all the processes in scope
  4. Matrix showing customer view / internal view of performance
  5. Table showing high level cost assessment
  6. Table showing the maturity of IT processes
  7. Table showing the maturity of business processes
  8. Table showing the classification of each sub-process as critical, industry standard or basic
  9. Table showing which sub-processes / scenarios are performed in which divisions / locations
  10. Table showing the current placement of sub-processes.
  11. Matrix showing potential systems value to the process / adequacy of current systems
  12. Table showing whether each sub-process has the characteristics to make it a good candidate for enabling technologies
  13. Table showing problem areas, their classification, severity, impact and root causes.
  14. Summary of all improvement ideas, problem(s) fixed, extent problem(s) fixed, with preliminary benefit / cost qualification
  15. Process maps (activity level) comparing the sub-processes being performed at multiple locations
  16. Table showing recommendations / constraints based on process map comparisons
  17. Table showing recommendations / constraints based on placement analysis
  18. Process map showing the lowest level of Process Work Flow
  19. Table showing individual lowest Process Work Flow Data Flow Logic
