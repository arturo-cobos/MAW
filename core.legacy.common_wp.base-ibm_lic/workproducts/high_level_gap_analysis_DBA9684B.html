Artifact: High Level Gap Analysis (BUS 080)
This artifact identifies the high-level process gaps between the selected finalists for the software solution and the business process requirements.
Domains: Selection
Work Product Kinds: Assessment
Purpose
This work product provides the details needed in the development of the high-level strategy to plan the implementation of releases and to confirm the overall cost of implementation of the package. The High-Level Gap Analysis identifies and communicates the process gaps to the implementation team so that these requirements are addressed during the implementation of the package.

This work product also assists the implementation team in transitioning the client from selection to implementation.

Description
Main Description

This work product is organized by business process area and lists the process gaps with a brief description of the functional requirements desired by the business but not offered in the standard vendor software package. Functionality gaps that need to be addressed through the configuration of the system,  custom developed code, third-party software, or organizational and procedure changes are also identified and described.

Brief Outline

The details that support this work product are captured in matrix format using an document table or an spreadsheet.  Each identified process gap is organized by business or functional area defined with the primary processes.  The columns of the matrix include:

  • Business/Functional Area: This represents the business area or functional area of the enterprise.
  • Business Process: This lists the business process threads or scenarios.
  • Degree of Gap: This represents an estimate of the severity (high, medium or low), as an indication of the suitability of the package to its intended purpose. The extent of effort required to adapt the package to the target environment may be inferred, at a high level, from this scale.
  • Gap Description: This provides a description of the functional or process gap in the vendor's software solution. 
  • Comments: This section includes any general comments about the processes or the vendor's package.  Comments about how the gap can be addressed, along with a high level estimate of any effort required, are described here.  Comments may include:
    • Procedural or organizational change, and implications of a change
    • Special configuration of software
    • Software customization using the vendor toolset
    • Third-party software solution
    • Develop custom application interface

Process diagrams may be included with this work product optionally as supporting materials.  If detailed process models were developed to define the business processes, these models can be used to represent the gaps visually in the process requirements by highlighting or shading the process areas not supported by the vendor's product.

The typical business process models include:

  • Business Process Hierarchy
  • Process Maps
  • Process Flow Diagrams

Key Considerations

Review and validation of this work product must be closely facilitated.  Focus on the objective of the meetings must be maintained.  The client may wish to work at a much lower level of detail than is intended in this work product, and this must be avoided.   Also avoid the temptation to put the old processes into the new software solution by dismissing the software solutionâ€™s capabilities too quickly, and relying on custom programming to fill the gaps. 

All areas of process level gaps must be identified.  During the implementation phase, these areas will be addressed through activities such as:

  • Customizations of the software
  • Implementation of third-party software
  • Changes in procedures
Tailoring
Impact of not having

Not producing this work product can result in the implementation of a system that does not completely meet the business requirements or properly prepare the organization for the implementation. Also, organizational and procedure changes needed to adopt the processes of the new software package may go unidentified.

If this work product is not produced, the development of the implementation strategy for the solution may not be complete, overall estimated cost of implementation of the software solution may be misrepresented, and the transition into the implementation phase may be disjointed and cumbersome.

Reasons for not needing

The High-Level Gap Analysis is required to facilitate the implementation of the package into the target environment.  There will be few instances where this work product will not be produced.  Some circumstances to consider that may reduce or eliminate the development of this work product include:

  • The software package meets the business requirements defined by the client completely
  • The client plans to adopt their procedures to support the processes of the software solution, (organizational and procedural gaps must still be analyzed between the current client procedures and those defined by the software)
  • Configuration of the software is minimal
