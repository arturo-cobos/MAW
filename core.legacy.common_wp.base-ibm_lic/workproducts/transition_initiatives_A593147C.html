Artifact: Transition Initiatives (ORG 305)
The Transition Initiatives work product pulls together the various business and IT initiatives, projects, and transition issues defined in previous engagement activities.
Domains: Organization Change Management
Work Product Kinds: Requirement
Purpose
The Transition Initiatives work product is used to identify, define and group the initiatives needed to achieve a strategic intent, as well as to address the gaps that exist between the present and the desired business and IT environments. These initiatives provide the basis for making investments and a framework for sequencing, resourcing and deploying projects to implement the desired business and IT capabilities.

Description
Main Description

Various project activity inputs are combined into a single set of initiatives (each of which is made up of a number of identified projects), which will be input to subsequent Transition Strategy and Planning activities.   

At the strategic level, an initiative is defined in contextual terms, including its definition, audiences served, its specific scope and objectives, the benefits it will potentially deliver and its importance to the business and its investment requirements (both benefits and investments are ranges or magnitudes). Additional information to facilitate detail planning and follow-on development and implementation work is added to each initiative during subsequent transition strategy and transition planning activities.

Key Considerations
The terminology can get confusing: use what is comfortable for the client, but begin with a logical hierarchy in project definition and stick to it. For example,
  • Initiatives
    • Describe direction or intent
    • Typically used to change mindset or viewpoint
    • Will be tied to a change program
    • Groupings of projects
    • Deliver related or linked benefits
    • Often coupled to a "future vision" or similar motivator

Note: groupings of projects can sometimes be referred to as 'programs' - this distinction is not used in this technique paper.

  • Projects
    • Unit of work that can be described in an RFP
    • Activity can be managed by one "leader" or "supplier"
    • Has bounded scope, cost and time that are defined
    • Delivers articulated benefits to specified beneficiaries
Tailoring
Impact of not havingWithout this work product, the potential exists that there will not be a commonly defined, prioritized set of initiatives to target investment towards. In addition, follow-on transition strategy and planning activities may be high risk due to the lack of adequate descriptions and detail.
Reasons for not needingThis work product would not be required where the scope of an engagement specifically excludes initiative level identification and definition focus, such as when exploring a high level strategy.
Representation OptionsThis work product will be delivered as a combination of spreadsheets, documents, matrices and freelance presentations
