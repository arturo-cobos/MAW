Artifact: Facility Requirements (OPS 318)
The Facilities Requirements work product is a list of requirements grouped by category for the client's facility.
Domains: Facilities
Work Product Kinds: Requirement
Purpose
The Facilities Requirements helps ensure that the client's facility vision is turned into reality by providing a checklist of items to be contained in the new or modified facility. The requirements documentation provides a reference that eliminates the need to interpret what the client may want.

Description
Main Description

The requirements should cover all of the components in the facility including:

  • Hardware (based on the solution design plus any that are facility specific)
  • Software (based on the solution design plus any that are facility specific)
  • Equipment 
  • Facility cabling and wiring 
  • Security
  • Fire protection 
  • Physical space 
  • Power/UPS capacity 
  • Power/UPS backup and recovery 
  • Climate control/HVAC 
  • Flooring

Key Considerations

Many of the requirements for the IT infrastructure may have been gathered during IT infrastructure efforts.  Make sure to consult the IT Architects and IT Specialists working on the project so that you do not repeat their work.

Make sure that you give yourself enough time to complete the requirements definition.  This is the biggest pitfall in many projects.

Having softcopy drawings that depict various requirements is very helpful.  The client may not understand the various types of flooring or wiring available and may not be familiar with the terms.  Having a drawing that the client can look at facilitates comprehension.  The softcopy drawings also help if the information needs to be sent to various members of the project team, subcontractors or architectural firms that may be working on the project.

A person with an engineering background or a very strong facilities background must write the major portions of the requirements document.   In some instances, separate contractors may be used for greater in-depth technical, engineering portions of the work. 

Many of the sections relate to how the facility was originally built and the changes that must be made.  As-built drawings may have to be thoroughly reviewed in this effort.  The final report must be thoroughly reviewed with the clientâ€™s IT and facility staff.  The recommendation is to perform this review in person.  A conference call is not as effective.  Also, if an assessment was done of the existing facility, make sure you check the requirements against the problems identified during the assessment to make sure that you have everything covered. 


 

Tailoring
Impact of not havingWithout Facility Requirements, any new facility design or modifications could not be done according the client requirements. There would be no documentation outlining what the client wants and no way to check to make sure that any design incorporates the client's requirements.
Reasons for not needingIf changes to the facility are not within the scope of this engagement, then requirements will not be needed. If a new facility is to be designed or modifications made to the existing facility, requirements need to be gathered. Even if the client has determined its requirements, IBM should still review the document from the client and discuss with the client the details that are required to insure that the document meets all the IBM recommendations.
Representation OptionsThe requirements are typically a bulleted list of requirements grouped by category.  The requirements may also consist of a number of drawings for each category that help illustrate the requirement.  Facility design may not be familiar to the client and the drawings help the client understand the purpose and nature of the requirement.
