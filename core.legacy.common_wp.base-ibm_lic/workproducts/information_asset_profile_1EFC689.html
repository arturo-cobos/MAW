Purpose

Each Information Asset Profile is documented in an asset inventory, which is used as a "control book" for:

  • Highlighting which information requires protection,
  • Defining and documenting the level of security required for the business use of each information asset,
  • Defining information asset ownership, responsibility and primary usage,
  • Ensuring that the right types of control and mechanisms are implemented for identified critical business information.

The Information Asset Profile is used as a basic building block for defining an effective Information Security Program. The profile allows an organization to prioritize the tasks associated with the implementation of enterprise-wide information architecture based on business risk. This enables an information security program to be tailored to provide the right types of controls and mechanisms for the most critical business information.

The Information Asset Profile also provides a basis for business and technology managers to make informed decisions about the management of information within the organization, including security and recovery decisions for protecting critical information assets.

Description
Main Description

This work product includes an analysis of the business value of the information used by the key business areas and the security requirements for confidentiality, integrity, availability, and privacy.

Each Information Asset Profile is documented in an asset inventory, which contains a description, location, owner, users and usage, availability impacts, confidentiality impacts, integrity and privacy impacts.

Understanding the overall view of an organization's information assets and the protection each asset requires, is a key factor in ensuring business success.

Brief Outline

The Information Asset Profile work product is a textual document containing the following information for each identified asset:

  • Description of the asset
  • Owner (key business area manager with the most at stake related to this asset)
  • Location (information asset may be on a server, on the mainframe, in paper files, etc.)
  • Users and usage (principal category of users who create, modify, or need access to this asset)
  • Security requirements for the asset
  • Confidentiality requirements - determined by the business impact if the information gets into the wrong hands
  • Integrity requirements - determined by the impact to the business if the information was inadvertently, or maliciously altered
  • Availability requirements - determined by the impact to the business if the information was not available to authorized users.

Key Considerations
Risk management processes should drive security and privacy decisions. In the physical asset world, cost/benefit analysis is relatively simple because tangible value can be assigned to assets and environmental threats and vulnerabilities are well known and well understood by corporate risk managers. However, the same is not true of intangible information assets and business risks such as corporate image and customer confidence. This work product is designed to provide the client with business justification that typically cannot be quantified for making appropriate security and privacy decisions for the enterprise.
Tailoring
Impact of not havingWithout this work product organizations will not be effective in the management and securing of key information assets and appropriate budgets for these activities may not be available. There will be no sound basis for deciding which information assets are critical to the business and must be secured - this leads to an ineffective and inefficient information asset management and security program.
Reasons for not needingThe client already has a clear understanding of the information assets (hardcopy and softcopy) of the organization, who owns them and what their related security requirements are.
