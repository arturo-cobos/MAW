Artifact: Stakeholder Participation Management Plan (ORG 304)
The Stakeholder/Participation Management Plan work product identifies and sets out approaches for preparing and involving stakeholders during a project implementation.
Domains: Organization Change Management
Work Product Kinds: Plan
Purpose
The purpose of this work product is to increase the likelihood of success for a project or implementation by ensuring that the interests of affected key people or groups are considered and that they are actively involved in the change and encouraged to support the change.

Description
Main Description

This work product is based on a high-level participation approach and includes:

  • The identification and analysis of key stakeholder groups
  • Ongoing analysis of stakeholder issues and concerns
  • Development of approaches to involving stakeholder groups and individuals at key stages in the project
  • Design of "change management" interventions where appropriate
  • Close monitoring and evaluation of stakeholder views and involvement

Key Considerations
  • Sponsor support and involvement is critical to the effectiveness of this work product
  • Promote broad-based (large groups coming together) participation as much as possible
  • The quality of this work product can be enhanced by information collected from a number of specific exercises such as Role Mapping and Organization Network Analysis that require an Organization Change specialist.
  • The extent to which change is are being adopted ought to be one of the strongest influences on the timetable for implementation, for example if adoption is taking longer than anticipated, more activities may need to be planned.
  • Most often people err on the side of under-communicating. Remember that people like to know what is happening to them and their organization, so err on the side of over-involvement.

  • This work product is typically developed as part of an iterative process.

Tailoring
Impact of not havingThe normal reaction to change is resistance. This resistance can be disastrous to a change project if it is not identified and dealt with. The most efficient way to reduce resistance is to involve and inform people as much as possible. By not identifying key people and groups, identifying their issues, and developing a strategy to involve key stakeholders and groups, you risk tremendous resistance that could lead to a failed initiative.
Reasons for not needingThis work product may not be required in small organizations where control is centralized and there is strong support for the change or if the client has extensive experience with change and already has a defined strategy.
