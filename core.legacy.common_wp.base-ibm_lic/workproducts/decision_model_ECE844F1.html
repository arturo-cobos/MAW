Artifact: Decision Model (BUS 019)
The Decision Model is an analytical hierarchy of the criteria used in evaluating the alternative solutions to the business requirements.
Domains: Selection
Work Product Kinds: Model
Purpose
The purpose of the Decision Model is to define and document the decision and measurement criteria used in evaluating alternative solutions to the defined business requirements. The Decision Model provides a structure for summarizing the opinions and interpretations of the selection team, and represents the complex decision process in a simplified model. It is important to create this scoring type model early in the process so that interpretation of the vendor response results is consistent and objective.

Description
Main DescriptionThe Decision Model provides a single set of selection criteria for components, packages and suppliers to provide the functions and capabilities required by the business. It is essential that each criterion be carefully selected and defined so that no room for ambiguity is left when performing the actual evaluation and selection.

The allocation of weights and priorities can be a very emotionally charged process.  Assess the situation on your project, be prepared to deal with and resolve conflicts that arise during the working session, and strive to achieve consensus among the core team members.  It is important to devise a decision model where all membersâ€™ views can be represented.
Tailoring
Impact of not havingThe Decision Model is usually required, depending on the needs and complexity of the business area processes, and therefore is normally produced to assist in summarizing and interpreting the results from the solution selection decision making process.
 
By not producing the Decision Model:
  • The rationale for the decision may be harder to explain.
  • The analysis of the data gathered may be more difficult.
  • It may be difficult to achieve consensus within the core selection team on the best solution.
Reasons for not needingThe Decision Model may not be required when the decision will be made by a small group, with no need for broader communication of the decision or the analysis of the data gathered used in the solution evaluation process is very simple.
Representation Options

The Decision Model can be developed using a variety of techniques ranging from text to graphical notations.  The hierarchical decomposition of the decision model can be documented using documents, spreadsheets, diagrams or presentations. 

The choice of tool and technique used in documenting the decision model will depend greatly on the complexity of the model and the level of detail at which the sponsor chooses to evaluate the alternative solutions. 

Very complex decision models, and those defined with the intent to evaluate alternative solutions at the lowest process level, should use a graphical organization chart format to describe the evaluation criteria, weighting, and hierarchical relationships among the business processes.  For less complex decision models, or when decision criteria are defined at a summary level (i.e., business area), documenting the decision model as text in a document may be more appropriate.
