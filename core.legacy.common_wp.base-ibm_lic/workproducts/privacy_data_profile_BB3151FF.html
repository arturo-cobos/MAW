Artifact: Privacy Data Profile (OPS 313)
A Privacy Data Profile work product provides an overall view of an organization’s personal information assets in terms of the privacy protection they require.
Domains: Security & Privacy
Work Product Kinds: Inventory
Purpose

Understanding the overall view of an organization's information asset in terms of the protection required is important for business success. This work product may be used as a "control book" that highlights privacy-relevant information, who has ownership responsibility, and how and where the information is primarily used throughout the business. In some areas, most notably in europe, there are strict privacy laws that require this information to be collected and reported to regulatory agencies on a regular basis. Additional requirements of these laws mandate that "owners" have a right to verify their personal information by simply requesting a company to fully disclose its handling of the owner’s personal information. A Privacy Data Profile helps an organization to meet these and other privacy regulatory requirements. 

The Privacy Data Profile is a basic building block for an effective Privacy Program. The profile allows an organization to prioritize the tasks associated with the implementation of an enterprise-wide information privacy program based on business risk. This enables the program to be tailored to provide the right types of controls and mechanisms for the most critical information to the business. The controls involve any business or IT processes that collects or processes or stores any personal information (e.g. employees, customers, clients, suppliers, business partners, contractors, etc.)

The Privacy Data Profile is the basis for business and technology managers to make informed privacy decisions for protecting critical information assets.

Description
Main Description

Personal information in the Privacy Data Profile work product is defined as any information that can be used to uniquely identify an individual entity (person or business). The work product defines the characteristics of all the organization's collected personal information using categories (called privacy principles) from accepted WW privacy standards (e.g. in the EU Privacy Directive): Openness, Disclosure, Use Limitations, Verification, Security, Accountability, Collection Limitations, and Participation.

Brief Outline

The Privacy Data Profile work product is a textual document containing the following information for each identified asset.

  • description of the asset;
  • owner (key business area manager with the most at stake related to this asset);
  • custodian (user having temporary control of the information);
  • location (information asset may be on a server, on the mainframe, in paper files, etc.);
  • purposes for which the information is to be used;
  • original source of the information;
  • regulations that may apply to the information;
  • projected disposition; and
  • existing or potential need for adherence to the standards-based privacy principles.

Key Considerations

This work should only be performed by Methodology-trained Privacy professionals.  Additional resources available in the IT Security ICAP Database.

This artifact is validated against the client’s Information Asset Profile (see work product) for completeness and prevailing Privacy Laws and Regulations and Standards (such as the EU Privacy Directive).
Tailoring
Impact of not havingLack of a complete Privacy Data Profile will result in an ineffective and inefficient information privacy program and may result in legal action based on local laws.
Reasons for not needing

Client insists they know what their personal information assets (hardcopy and softcopy) are and what the related privacy requirements are for each asset.

Client indicates that security and privacy considerations are out of the engagement scope (e.g., when engagement is a proof of concept or rapid development of a prototype). In this case, consider adding the approved Security Disclaimer to the SOW (see References below).

On engagements with a very narrow focus (as opposed to an enterprise level), rather than including this work product, consider proposing a separate engagement to develop a privacy strategy for the client.
