Artifact: Business Continuity Asset Analysis (OPS 405)
The Business Continuity Asset Analysis work product provides an overall view of all the organization’s assets, in terms of the impact from their loss due to a business continuity threat.
Domains: Business Continuity
Work Product Kinds: Assessment
Purpose

Each asset is documented in an Asset Impact Profile, which is used as a "control book" for:

  • Highlighting which asset category or specific asset requires protection.
  • Defining and documenting the level of safeguard required for the business use of each asset.
  • Ensuring that the right types of control and mechanisms are implemented for identified critical business asset.

The Business Continuity Asset Analysis is used as a basic building block for defining an effective Business Continuity Risk Management Program. The profile allows an organization to prioritize the tasks associated with the implementation of enterprise-wide controls and safeguards based on business risk. This enables a Business Continuity Risk Management Program to be tailored to provide the right types of controls and safeguards for the most critical business assets.

Description
Main Description

The analysis provides information about where and how the assets are owned, managed, and used. This work product includes an analysis of the business value and the business’s determination of the degree of seriousness if the asset is not available for a period of time. This analysis determines the replacement cost, cost per hour of unavailability, and percentage of mission dependent on this asset, and other critical measures necessary to substantiate the installation and use of safeguards used by key business areas.   

Each Business Continuity Asset Analysis is documented in Asset Impact Profile, which contains a description of the asset category, listing of assets in that category, number of units, cost to replace and cost of loss per hour.   

Understanding the overall view of an organization's assets and the protection each asset requires, is a key factor in ensuring business continuity success.

Key Considerations
Risk management processes should drive security and business continuity decisions.  In the physical asset world, cost/benefit analysis is relatively simple because tangible value can be assigned to assets and environmental threats and vulnerabilities are well known and well understood by corporate risk managers.  This work product is designed to provide the client with business justification to provide adequate mitigation against their risk to exposure from threats.
Tailoring
Impact of not havingWithout this work product organizations will not be effective in the management and securing of key assets and appropriate budgets to mitigate risk of interruption for these activities may not be available. There will be no sound basis for deciding which assets are critical to the business and must be secured - this leads to an ineffective and inefficient Business Continuity Risk Management program.
Reasons for not needingThe client already has a clear understanding of the information assets (hardcopy and softcopy) of the organization, who owns them and what their related security requirements are. In such cases, the information can be provided directly to the consultant.
