Artifact: Interview or Survey Results (BUS 350)
This artifact documents the results of an administered survey or interview.
Domains: Business Strategy
Work Product Kinds: Measurement
Purpose
Depending on the content of a survey or interview, this artifact is used to determine some aspect of a client’s perception of the current (and possibly desired future) state of their business. The presentation of data underlying the perception can help both the consultant and the client to determine focus areas of concentration for the development of next steps.

Description
Main Description

The raw data available from the interviews or survey are collected, verified, sorted, and manipulated to most effectively and fairly present the information.

  • The form of the actual responses may be either quantitative or qualitative
  • Quantitative data may be either discrete, values on a continuum or a set of valid ranges
  • The data manipulation can be simple (e.g. a summary of counts by question, i.e. 10 favorable, 5 unfavorable) or as complex as the volume of valid, available data allows (e.g. average score, etc.)
  • A summary of the write-in questions (e.g. a listing of all the write in comments by question)
  • Statistical representation and manipulation, where valid, are useful forms of representing the results

This artifact is used to capture the project team member feedback for official metrics collection efforts. This is preceded by a facilitated team level lessons learned session.

This artifact is also used to capture the project sponsor feedback for official metrics collection efforts.

Brief Outline

This work product should produce a presentation and/or report, comprised of both text and graphics, which could contain any or all of the following contents:

  • A compilation of each respondent’s answer for each question
  • An average of the respondents’ scores for each question reflecting their measurement of current state capabilities
  • An average of the respondents’ scores for each question reflecting their measurement of desired future state capabilities
  • Any other valid statistical representation of the raw data which fairly and accurately reflect the information

Key Considerations

This Work Product can produce high value to the client and the engagement team.  The keys to success are:

  1. Start the data collection early in the engagement (as part of the kick-off or immediately following the kick-off). Use the information discovered to focus the assessment.
  2. Ensure that there is sufficient representation across the client organization.

Make sure that key individuals are available to participate. 

Share the compiled results with the project sponsor(s).  This will allow you to show some early progress and provide back up for any scope change discussions that need to occur as a result of the survey.

Ensure that individual participant responses remain anonymous if that is the agreement in place with the client. 

It is also very important to share the results with the IBM project team so everyone understands the results of the client’s self-assessment.
Tailoring
Impact of not having

The impact of not developing this work product may be:

  • The client’s perception of their current state may not be understood
  • The client’s perception of their desired future state may not be understood
  • The project team’s efforts may not be properly focused
  • The project team’s recommendations for improvement may not align with client’s existing perception
Reasons for not needing

Although this work product can deliver significant value, it may not be necessary in the following situations:

  • There are a relatively small number of participants and conclusions can be derived based on visual inspection of the responses
  • There are a relatively small number of questions and conclusions can be derived based on visual inspection of the responses
  • There has been an existing, on-going relationship with the client and a clear understanding of the current state and/or desired future state is understood from both an enterprise and individual departmental view
