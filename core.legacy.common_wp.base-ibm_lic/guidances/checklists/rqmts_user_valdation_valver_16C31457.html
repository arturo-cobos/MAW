Check Items
Number of participants surveyed is based on a sampling from each user group
The number of focus group participants from which the conclusions of the report are drawn is based on a minimum sample of 12 to 15 participants per user group.  Depending on the number of eventual end users, a more reliable and valid sample size would be closer to 30. The focus group should include people who have disabilities.
All comments from the focus group participants are documented
The comments are categorized on the basis of those that pertain to satisfaction, frequency of use, and whether they identify missing use cases.  These are typically documented and summarized in an appendix to the report.
Participants in the focus group were actual end users of the system
They should not include: managers, IT professionals, or business analysts unless they are the intended user.
End users involved in validating the requirements were not involved in generating the initial list of requirements
Accessibility of the survey itself did not interfere with validating the requirements by users with disabilities
Most, if not all requirements should not need to change if the use case is enabled for accessibility.  For example, a user who is blind and uses a screen reader should still be able to complete an order by filling out the forms and navigating the application and reading the results that are implied in the requirement.
Rationale for score weightings was provided before the data were collected and summarized
This is done for each of the measures, satisfaction, frequency of use, or other agreed upon measures.
