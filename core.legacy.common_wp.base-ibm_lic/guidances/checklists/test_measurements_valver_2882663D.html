Test Measurements are NOT the Test Log

(The Test Log work product describes the outcomes of a specific test execution, and compares the actual test results to the expected results from the test case.)  The Test Measurements are used to allow a project manager to decide on a project level if enough testing has been done; and on an enterprise level for Test Management to formally assess the effectiveness and capability of the testing process.


Measurements and metrics are correct and clearly presented

Accuracy in test planning depends on having the relevant measurements from prior projects on which to base new estimates of the required testing effort. 
Measurements meet the objectives of the Testing team and stakeholders involved in the project

The measurements should also meet the enterprise objectives of the Testing organization in terms of coverage, relevance, currency, breadth, level of detail, representation and focus.

Measurements follow the guidelines provided by the Testing and Quality organizations
Make sure that they do not deviate from these standards in terms of completeness and referential integrity, nor should there be any missing metrics, e.g. not measured, not known, not documented, etc.
