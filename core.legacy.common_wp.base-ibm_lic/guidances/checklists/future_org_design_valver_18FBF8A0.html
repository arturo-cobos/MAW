Check Items
The new organization structure, enablers, and behaviors need have been documented
A holistic view that shows the tight alignment of these three areas should be included. Articulate the rationales used and alternatives considered in order to support your recommendations and help the client understand them.
The inputs upon which the design is based are still up-to-date and have been reflected accurately
Doublecheck the designs with team members working in other domains to validate.
Questions and objections the client might raise have been considered
Anticipate especially the objections that might stem from a potential loss of organizational "turf." Organization redesign is an especially political and sensitive topic. Engaging leadership team members in the redesign and holding frequent interim reviews with sponsors can make this step go more smoothly. Be prepared during any review to have a high-level conversation about the effort that the client will need to put into communication and building commitment in order to implement a new organization design.
