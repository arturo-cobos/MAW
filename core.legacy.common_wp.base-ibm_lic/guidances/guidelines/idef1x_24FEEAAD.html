Main Description

The IDEF1X View Diagram is a graph where the nodes are the entities and the edges are the relationships.

An entity is represented as a box. Each entity is assigned a label, placed above the box. There may optionally be an identifying positive integer in the label, separated from the required descriptive, singular, noun phrase name by a "/". An entity can only appear once on a View Diagram, but may appear on several diagrams.

An attribute is represented as a descriptive, singular, noun phrase placed inside the associated entity box in a list. Primary key attributes are at the top of the list, separated from the others by a horizontal line.

The most common relationship in the IDEF1X notation is the "specific connection relationship". This is an occurrence of an association of an entity of cardinality 0 or 1 with an entity of cardinality 0, 1, or many. The former is called the "parent," the latter is called the "child". The relationship is depicted by a line between the involved entities, labeled by a verb phrase describing the meaning of the relationship. The verb phrase is read with the parent entity as the subject and the child as the object. The label can be made bi-directional by separating the two phrases with a "/" to support a reading from child to parent.

Cardinality is represented by dots at each end of a connection relationship. A "P" next to the dot indicates a cardinality of one or more. A "Z" next to the dot indicates a cardinality of zero or one. Single numbers or ranges next to the dot have their appropriate meanings.

The IDEF1X graphical notation is a rich notation. A complete language description can be found in the references.
