Main Description

Introduction
The purpose of the Static Testing Plan is to ensure that any defects in work products are found and removed, as close to the defect entry point as possible and as early in the software development lifecyle as possible. Early removal of defects increases project productivity by shortening testing cycles and reducing work. It also contributes significantly to improving the quality of project deliverables. This work product provides guidance on defect detection and is aimed at producing a “defect free” work product, before it is passed on to the next phase of the development process. Static testing may be applied to many different types of product deliverables such as requirements, specifications, design, code, prototypes as well as testware including test plans and scripts.

Scope
The scope of this document is focused on the quality of the Application Development and Testing work products relating to the xxx Project. The quality of the Project Management work products and the project deliverables is addressed through the Quality Management process maintained by the Project Control Office.

The specific work products to be tested are: 

  • Overall Project Documents 
    • Plans for each Phase 
    • Business Requirements Document 
    • Business Specifications Document 
    • Technical Specifications Document 
    • Code 
  • Test Work Products

    • Static Test Plan

    • Test Environment 
    • Test Specification

Objectives
The objectives are to:
       - increase quality and productivity,
       - minimize costs and cycle elapsed time,
       - facilitate project management.

These objectives are accomplished by:
       - providing a uniform approach for testing development and test material;
       - detecting and correcting the maximum number of defects injected in the work product at the earliest point possible within the development lifecycle;
       - verifying that the work product being inspected is an accurate translation and logical extension of the previous phase;
       - checking and certifying the completeness of work;
       - providing data for causal analysis.

Plan Activities
Each identified work product will pass through the process using the technique identified.
Stages of the Process
Planning
Confirm risk assessment and need for test
Confirm technique selected
Allocate resources
Schedule test

Preparation
Do any training on the technique as necessary
Review roles and responsibilities
Provide all participants with overview of process and material

Execution
Identify and record all defects, issues and suggestions relating to the work product and decide whether the material passes the test, or not, and what actions are required?

Rework Stage
Resolve all defects according to their severity rating
Update the work product to reflect the defect resolutions.

Follow-up
Ensure that all the defects have been satisfactorily resolved or deferred based on severity rating or management decision.
