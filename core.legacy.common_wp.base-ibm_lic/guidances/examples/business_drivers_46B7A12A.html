Main Description

Industry or External Business Driver: Powerful companies such as a major retailer are collaborating with customers, key suppliers and distributors to further drive costs from supply chain and are moving to demand-driven supply chains. - Through the use of technologies and process capabilities, sharing and using a variety of sales data, data mining, understanding supplier production schedules, and using sophisticated forecasting algorithms, all industry actors can minimize production and inventory costs and more reliably provide the right product at the right time at the right place.

Business Driver: we must transform our business from product centric to customer-centric - Through customer focus groups and other mechanisms to determine key customer needs and wants we will be able to better focus our activities and resources along the entire supply chain.

Internal or Architectural Driver: The architecture must provide enhanced connectivity among systems, and between both internal and external organizations. - Through the definition and use of standard interfaces specified in the architecture, connectivity between systems and organizations and people should become significantly more achievable. As new connectivity requirements or new communications technologies arise, the architecture will allow for a faster and more accurate assessment of feasibility and desirability.

Assumptions - sample

Some typical assumptions might include:

  • Supply Chain Management (SCM) and intercompany collaboration will continue for the foreseeable future.
  • Technologies are mature enough to better integrate the supply chain.
  • SCM and Customer Relationship Management (CRM) are continuing to converge to create demand driven supply chains "driving" the company.
  • Current strategic systems must be supported in the architecture.
  • Customers will participate in our intelligence gathering efforts.
  • The company focus will move from products to customers.

The Architecture will be based on proven, current generation technologies. The technologies which are currently implemented within the organization, will be built upon as appropriate and 'bleeding edge' technologies will be avoided whenever possible. Technologies must be compatible.
