Supply Chain Leading Practice

Procurement


Description
Process Outcomes
Enablers
Importance to Client

Vendor Managed inventory and UCSII.
Provide forecast, product specification and other information to vendor using EDI/UCSII and communicate forecast changes to vendor.
Open communication with vendors using EDI/UCSII.
Lower inventory cost due to just in time inventory. Lower cost of PO processing as vendor will manage inventory and POs will be processed by vendor.

Strategic alliance/relationship with vendor.
Mutual goals are identified and both parties work toward achieving these goals to reduce supply chain cost.
Evaluate goals and capabilities of both parties by using score carding techniques (category management, efficient replenishment, and efficient assortment).
Partnership will create sustainable competitive advantage due to right material at right place at right time. Cost reduction, improved level of quality, service and technology will be achieved.

Treat purchasing as strategic function and not a tactical function.
Trust, empowerment and teaming between purchasers and users. Purchasing managers should participate in strategic formulation and implementation.
Search in global economy for low cost high-quality suppliers. Electronic ordering based on shared forecast and on-line production schedules.
Flexibility and agility to compete in fast changing market conditions.

Encourage vendors to set-up their shops on site.
Trust, empowerment and teaming between manufacturer and vendor. Consignment agreements can be used to reduce assets and cycle time while increasing availability of critical items.
Establish long term partnership with vendor and let vendor do all the ordering for manufacturer. Consignment agreement if beneficial for both parties.
Cost reduction through just in time inventory and reduction in order processing cost. Faster new product development as vendor will act as in-house expert in the area of quality and availability of raw material.

Internet and Intranet for procurement function.
Use of Internet to post request for products, receiving bids and submit orders. Use of Intranet for on-line PO requests and approvals.
Internet and Intranet
Faster and simpler processes.

Create meaningful measures to gauge cost effectiveness. Create system to measure customer satisfaction. Create vendor performance metrics.
Measure price changes compared to last year and industry benchmarks. Ask vendors and internal customers to complete electronic survey to measure procurement effectiveness. Monitor vendor performance for quality, technology investment and capacity.
Measure to gauge performance of purchasing department and vendor over time (PO cycle time, on time receipt of material, delivery discrepancies).
Ongoing improvement in procurement performance to achieve higher productivity, cost saving and customer satisfaction. Vendor measures will improve cost and service.


Supply Chain Leading Practice

Distribution Resource/Replenishment Planning (DRP)


Description
Process Outcomes
Enablers
Importance to Client

Inventory forecasting at regional (DC) level, based on customer demand, rolled-up to national level. Forecasting changes should be driven by customer demand changes.
Create alliance with customers to get visibility into customer demand via their forecast, product plans, inventory positions and point-of-sales order velocity and incorporate them in regional DRP process.
Long term strategic alliance with major customers will optimize supply chain costs. Vendor managed inventory or Continuous Replenishment Program will motivate customers to share demand data with manufacturer.
Higher service levels will Increase sales and client can earn preferred manufacturer status with customers. Good relationship with customer can create efficiencies in other areas such as reduction in deductions and longer order lead-time.

Redesign processes to incorporate promotional activities, product profitability, transportation cost and distribution cost in DRP process.
Supply chain optimization can be achieved through smoothing demand (except seasonal products), declining product complexity, increasing forecasting accuracy, manufacturing flexibility, and material acquisition flexibility.
Reduce fixed-interval promotion (month-end push), create detailed regional forecasting using integrated systems, synchronize production according to customer demand, reduce lead times for material acquisition, prune unprofitable products, and install integrated systems.
Reduced storage cost and inventories, higher service level to customers (fewer outages), manufacturing cost reduction, higher profitability due to pruning of unprofitable products, higher productivity and employee satisfaction due to integrated systems.

Create cross-functional teams to measure performance of DRP.
Implementation of measures which are important to customers and to cross-functional areas (service level, storage cost as % of sales, distribution capacity utilization).
Willingness of cross- functional employees to participate in DRP process.
Cross-functional teams will provide valuable input to DRP process increasing accuracy. Higher service level to customer due to higher accuracy.

Integrated System to link demand with deployment and transaction systems (customer service).
DRP process will receive timely and accurate input from various functional areas for higher accuracy.
Integrated systems
Higher service level and lower out of stock due to synchronization of demand and deployment.

Description
Process Outcomes
Enablers

Integrated Systems.
Transportation, storage and handling needs will be calculated based on production schedule, material on hand and material in transition.
Implement integrated systems to improve processes for measuring material demand.
Integrated systems will eliminate manual/PC-based processes. Inbound logistics will be able to negotiate lowest landed rates because accurate information will be available when needed.

Electronic freight management.
EDI will be used to receive, approve and pay (EFT) freight bills. Also, use EDI for on-the spot quote and order entry.
EDI

Receive ASN from vendors
Schedule receiving and storage based on ASN.
EDI
Efficiencies in raw material storage, receiving, and cross docking.

Charge back to SKU/category based on resources consumed in area of transportation, in-house and outside storage and handling.
Charge back allocation system based on resource usage.
Integrated systems capable of calculating charges based on resource/activity usage.
Accurate cost of goods sold which will increase accuracy of SKU/category profitability.

Supply Chain Leading Practice

Accounts Payable


Description
Process Outcomes
Enablers
Importance to Client

Implementation of centralized accounts payable processing and issue credit cards for small purchases.
Central AP department will eliminate duplicate processes conducted at regional AP departments (duplicate vendor setup and maintenance, month end entries, reconciliation). Central AP department will be located in low labor cost area. Use of credit card for small purchases will eliminate purchase orders, invoices and checks.
Central AP office located in low labor cost area. Credit cards.
Cost saving due to elimination of duplicate processes and lower labor cost. Credit card will reduce processing costs of small item acquisitions.

Implement on-line electronic purchase order generation and approval system. Automatic payment for standard, recurring items. Eliminate manual check generation.
System for on-line/electronic purchase order (PO) requisition, PO generation and PO approval. Automatic payment will be made for standard, recurring items. Manual check generation will be eliminated.
Flexible system with effective internal network.
This process will increase PO processing speed and will allow users to track PO in process at all times. Cost reduction will be achieved through elimination of paper, elimination of paper work for recurring items and elimination of manual checks.

Create on-line expense processing system. Adopt two way match: payment linked to delivery and not to document.
On-line expense account processing will increase processing speed and employees will be reimbursed faster. Two-way match of PO will simplify process and increase speed of payment.
Technology Implementation and following changes will enable cost savings: payment linked to delivery, not document; electronic fund transfer for employee reimbursement.
Faster processing with lower cost due to simplified processes.

Physical Distribution


Description
Process Outcomes
Enablers
Importance to Client

Charge customers for "non-standard “ activities.
Efficient customers will get lower price and customers who consume non-standard activities will be charged based on consumption.
Menu Pricing or Cost-to-Serve
Cost saving through cost transfer to customers who consume non-standard activities resulting in increased supply chain costs. Over time, activities will be transferred to organization that can operate cost effectively, creating supply chain cost reduction.

Information integration of sales, DC and manufacturing.
All functional departments will function based on same facts/assumptions. Manufacturing will schedule production based on demand forecast and distribution center will plan resources based on manufacturing schedules. Demand and manufacturing changes will be communicated with accuracy and speed.
Integrated systems capable of pushing demand from sales to manufacturing to distribution.
Cost savings and employee satisfaction due to similar knowledge base across all functional areas. DC will be able to plan resources according to actual volume to create higher DC operating efficiencies.

Use of high level UCSII for all information exchange between all trading partners.
USCII will be used by customers and manufacturing for communicating following information: price, promotion, cases per tier and tiers per pallet, authorized product list.
Integrated UCSII capabilities between trading partners.
Increased accuracy will reduce, errors/returns and achieve cost saving. Employee satisfaction will increase due to higher accuracy.

Strategic alliance/ relationship with customers to create long term partnership
Identify mutual goals and work toward achieving these goals to reduce supply chain/efficient replenishment cost.
Score carding by both parties in area of efficient replenishment, open on-going communication through UCSII.
Improved relationship with customers will reduce distribution cost and improve level of quality and service.

Supply Chain Leading Practice

Outbound Transportation


Description
Process Outcomes
Enablers
Importance to Client

Enterprise wide load optimization.
Implementation of a central enterprise-wide load control center using intelligent technology for load building, route optimization, carrier assignments, dispatch operations and status checking.
Intelligent central enterprise-wide load control system, unified distribution network without divisional barriers.
Cost saving due to central load consolidation, route optimization, carrier assignment and payment authorization/processing. Shipment tracking and carrier performance will increase customer service levels.

Integrated planning.
Forecast based on distribution demand forecast. Transportation systems will generate “bill of loading” which will be used for freight payment. Integrated planning will evaluate transportation in terms of the trade-off between inventory, warehousing, forecast demands and actual capacities of the transportation network.
Integrated logistics systems, constant communication and coordination among shippers, carriers and customers to ensure the smooth flow of goods.
Transportation will be transformed from isolated function to highly integrated function. Complexity of transportation will be reduced and optimum solutions will be found. Cost saving will be achieved due to optimization. Customer demand of knowing delivery time within a five-minute window and knowing transportation costs will be satisfied.

Communicate delivery discrepancy information back to customer service before invoice is issued.
Invoice will be adjusted for delivery discrepancies before invoice is issued. Policy will be established stating time limit for reporting delivery discrepancies.
Implement system to communicate delivery discrepancies in timely manner. Implement policy, for customers, stating time for reporting delivery issues. (damage/short)
Number of deductions and processing cost of deductions will decline as delivery discrepancies will be reflected on invoice.

Build transportation logic in demand forecasting and inventory deployment.
Optimization of inventory, warehousing, demand forecasting and network capacity/cost.
Transportation logic built in demand and deployment systems.
Cost reduction due to optimization of inventory, warehousing, demand forecasting and network capacity/cost.

Electronic communication of dispatch operation.
Increased dispatch operation efficiencies due to elimination of dispatcher calling each carrier with the detail of each shipment for every shipment.
EDI
Load center efficiencies will increase due to elimination of manual information exchange.
