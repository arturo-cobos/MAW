Robert - Director of Field Operations

This example shows an example of one persona that maps to the Business Traveler user profile.

“Everything had best run like clockwork.” – Robert

Robert – Director of Field Operations
Company: Stewart Auto Glass
Citizen of Great Britain
45 years old
Degrees: BS Industrial Engineering
Personal concerns: being responsible and do everything with integrity
Corporate concerns: transforming Stewart Auto Glass into a premier auto industry supplier

Robert is responsible for all field operations, including glass installation at fourteen auto manufacturer’s plants as well as the over 350 nationwide auto-glass retail outlets owned by Stewart Auto Glass. Robert loves the highly active work environment and the demands of the job. He thrives on rapid-fire situation management, in which he demonstrates his quick analytic ability and decisive management style.

Robert travels frequently and is very experienced with airline, hotel, and other travel situations. He expects the same degree of precision, reliability, and integrity from the travel industry as he expects from himself and his own organization. He is a discerning and demanding business traveler. He prefers to make all arrangements in advance, from his office, but is also flexible and skilled at using all available channels to alter arrangements during a trip. His company’s travel policies are set by their CFO and all travel is closely scrutinized.

Robert is most happy when he has successfully solved a business problem that others stumbled on. In fact, he has become so confident in his abilities, he feels that his goal of becoming COO is well within his grasp. He is confident but not cocky, realizing that continuing to deliver results is what will win him his coveted position. His job requires the daily use of a laptop computer and he is a fairly proficient “one-finger” typist. Both Robert and his company are open to adoption of new approaches and technologies, especially if it will make their lives easier.

Robert’s personal goals:
A Sr. Executive position that commands prestige and respect
A demanding job with great monetary reward and satisfaction potential
A stable family situation that is supportive of his goals

Robert’s corporate goals:
Chief Operations Officer within the next 5 – 7 years
Help the company grow while still providing a “family-like” atmosphere
Reduce the install defect rate by 30% over the next three years
Cut warranty costs by 10% this year
Narrow the gap on competitor BIE Glass to less than 5 points this year

Annette - Communications Team Operator

This example shows another persona.


“Too many places to look…Minutes are valuable to us” - Annette


Annette has an Associates Degree in Computer Science from the local community college. She is a 33-year-old mother of two. Raising two boys has taught her to take everything in stride and not be surprised about anything that happens. She finds these traits to be useful in her job.

Annette is a member of a communicative team of operators who sometimes use a chat facility to communicate. She has a fair understanding of IT concepts, gleaned from her five years of experience. She’s been in her current job for four years. At her workspace, Annette watches two monitors displaying multiple tools. She only watches for red and black alerts, for which she usually opens trouble tickets quickly. She may use the command line or other device-specific tool to check out the resource or application generating the event before opening the ticket. If she needs additional information about the resource, she will request it from another one of her tools. If she has questions about procedures to follow, she can access the team’s run book, found on the Web. In the midst of all the alerts, Annette is always mindful of the possibility of missing an alert. Even though she doesn’t know the specifics, she is well aware that this kind of mistake affects the company. Overall, the procedures are simple: look at the screen, respond to each black and red event, look up the procedures, follow them, and move on to the next one.

Before the end of her day, Annette documents each ticket she opened in the shift log. This is the only major reporting she does. She has a high amount of interaction with Eddie, the administrator supporting her tools, as well as others outside her team. Overall, Annette trusts her tools.

Annette’s Experience Goals:

  • Avoid monotony on the job
  • Use as little effort as possible to work the problems
Annette’s End Goals
  • Insured service availability by fixing the important incidents according to their procedures
  • Pro-actively found negative trends to the business and followed proper procedures (e.g., change management) in order to prevent incidents from occurring or reoccurring.
Drove all of her above activities and communications from the perspective of how incidents impact the customer services of the company’s business processes.
