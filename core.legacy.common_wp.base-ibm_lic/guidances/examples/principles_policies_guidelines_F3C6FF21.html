Sample - Policy:

Individual Use

Individual accountability is required on all Company X computer systems. Access to Company X computer systems and networks must be provided through the use of individually assigned unique computer identifiers, known as user-IDs. Each individual who uses Company X computer resources must only access resources to which they are authorized by means of their user-ID. Associated with each user-ID is an authentication token, such as a password, which must be used to authenticate the person accessing the system or network. Passwords must be treated as confidential information, and must not be disclosed. All individuals are responsible for all activity performed under their user-ID. Sample - Principle Statement:

Information is a corporate asset and will be captured, stored and managed in a way that will allow appropriate levels of sharing across the enterprise.

Motivation:

  • Enables the identification of a customer's total relationships within the enterprise
  • Ensures consistent reporting to customers
  • Improves the relationship among business units and between business units and the corporate office
  • Increases the awareness of information importance by the Corporate Management Committee

Implication:

  • Valuable data must be identified and rules for its usage and sharing must be established
  • Regulatory issues with the sharing of data must be considered
  • Corporate standards for data sharing must be established and adhered to
  • Decisions on what information is a corporate asset vs. a business unit asset must be made
  • Upper management must be made aware of information importance
  • Funding is an issue, e.g. maintaining business unit customer data in Corporate Customer DB
  • Need to establish common methods and practices for data management

Note: The Enterprise Architecture Method has an associated Principles Database, which contains a wide range of different types of principle and will be made available to suitably trained consultants.


Sample - Guideline: Information should be regarded as a corporate asset, and where possible, stored and managed to allow sharing across the enterprise.
