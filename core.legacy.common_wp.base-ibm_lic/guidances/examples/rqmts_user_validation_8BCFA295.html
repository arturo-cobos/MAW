Main Description

The first two columns of the table identify the use case number and name. The next two columns provide mean (average) numerical ratings of frequency of use and satisfaction. These ratings are on a one- to seven-point scale with a score of “1” indicating infrequent use and a score of “7” indicating high frequency use for the Mean Rating of Frequency column. A score of “1” indicates high satisfaction and a score of “7” indicates low satisfaction in the Mean Rating of Satisfaction (for the current task) column.
The last column presents a composite of the frequency of use and satisfaction scores. In this case, both scores are equally weighted and summed to create the composite score. The use cases are then ranked in order, based on their composite scores.
In this example, EB004, “Add a new event,” had the highest composite score, while EB003, “Remove all registry information,” and was rated lowest.
