Financial Information - In general, corporate financial information is the proprietary information of the Owner of RCI. As such, authorization to this information must be strictly limited.
Financial information - May be comprised of items such as revenue, tax, compensation, and customer servicing data.
Unauthorized disclosure of financial information may effect corporate image and competitive advantage.
Competitors have high motivation to obtain financial information.
Confidentiality.
Need high-level authorization controls to isolate business actions between users. User access should be on a need to know basis with controls at field and file level. Financial data used for customer business should be restricted to transactions.







Resort Information - A resort is defined as the relationship of a legal entity with respect to a physical property.
Property - Physical structures of resorts including items such as location, units, and amenities. Property Information must be broadly available to affiliates and RCI personnel to satisfy customer requests.
Impacts are customer satisfaction, loss of productivity, financial loss.
Primary threats are system outages or incorrect entry or update of information. Europe (Errata) laws state that RCI is liable for supplying a customer with inaccurate information.
Availability, Audit and very high Integrity requirements in Europe.
As information is generally available to all users, an update history should be maintained. Some information must be kept from other properties.


Legal Entity - Proprietary information about a legal entity may be comprised of financial data, ownership, type of organization, officers, financial stability and other information about the general make-up of the legal entity.
Disclosure might result in legal liability, financial loss, and loss of corporate image.
The primary threat is loss of confidentiality between affiliates, or unauthorized disclosure of affiliate information to RCI competitors.
Confidentiality, Integrity, Audit and possibly Non-Repudiation.
Initial population of data in this category needs to be from an authorized source. Information must be verified as accurate and all updates tracked to individual IDs. This information is not generally available to vacation counselors.







Affiliation- An affiliate is generally defined as the relationship between the resort and RCI.
Affiliation information may include the nature of the contractual relationship with RCI, agreements on exchange contracts, number of properties held by the legal entity and other information that is proprietary to RCI and the specific legal entity.
Disclosure might result in loss of competitive advantage, legal liability, financial loss, and loss of corporate image.
The primary threat is loss of confidentiality between affiliates, or unauthorized disclosure of affiliate information to RCI competitors.
Confidentiality, Integrity, Audit and possibly Non-Repudiation.
Initial population of data in this category needs to be from an authorized source. Information must be verified as accurate and all updates tracked to individual IDs. Specific portions of this information should not be available to vacation counselors.
