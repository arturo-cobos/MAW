Task: Identify, raise and log defect (IMSP520)
Identify, validate and log the defect using the defect log.
Purpose
The purpose of this task is to identify, raise and log the defect(s).
Relationships
RolesPrimary Performer:
  • Project Manager
Additional Performers:
  • Originator
InputsMandatory:
  • Findings Log
  • Risk Management Plan
  • Test Log
  • Work Product Inspection Form
    		Optional:
    • None
    Outputs
    • Defect Log
    • Findings Log
      Main Description
      The Project Manager receives notification of a defect by any stakeholder associated with the project. The defect is validated by confirming that the required assets do not conform to the defined requirements or the intended purpose. Verify that the defect is not a duplicate then log the defect.
      Steps
      Identify and log the defect
      The Originator identifies and logs the defect using the defect log.
      Receive notification of a defect
      The Project Manager receives notification of a defect from any stakeholder associated with the project.

      Defects are identified during:

      • Inspections  
      • Testing
      • Deliverables Release  
        • Refer to the Deliverables Release and Acceptance Procedure (IMSP540) for information about release of deliverables. In this situation, sponsor findings have first been logged in the findings log. Some findings may identify nonconformities or defects which have to be fixed, others may fall into the category of controversial defects until an agreement has been reached on their status.
      • Expert evaluations, reviews, or other similar activities.
      Validate the defect
      Validate the defect by confirming that the required assets do not conform to the defined requirements or the intended purpose.
      • If the defect is valid, continue with the next step.
      • If the defect is NOT valid, communicate the status to the stakeholder who submitted the defect, update the defect log, as appropriate.
      • If a defect is identified and its validity is considered controversial, ambiguous or in dispute, resolve it as follows (or delegate the resolution to a resolution owner, as appropriate):
        • Discuss with the sponsor's representative to agree on an appropriate resolution to the matter. This may result in creation of a defect, change, or both.
        • Determine responsibility for necessary actions to resolve the matter.
        • Ensure that all parties, including suppliers, understand their responsibilities for resolving the matter.

      If a defect is found valid, but it is deemed that it will not be fixed due to some constraints, it can be deferred indefinitely in consultation with relevant stakeholders.

      Determine how the defect was introduced
      Determine if the defect was introduced as the result of:
      • An occurrence of a risk already identified in the project's risk management plan, which should be managed according to its documented mitigation strategy.
        • Refer to the Continuous Risk Management Procedure (IMSP600) for information about risk planning, monitoring and recording risk occurrences.
      • A change needed to amend the contractual obligations, baselined project plans, or project requirements.
        • Refer to the Change Management Procedure (IMSP610) for information about assessing and managing change.
      • An issue where the defect is an internal matter of concern for the project.
        • Refer to the Issue Management Procedure (IMSP620) for information about identifying and managing issues.

      If it is determined that the defect is the result of a risk occurrence, change or issue, perform the following:

      • Negotiate the closure of the defect.
      Determine if the defect is a duplicate
      Determine if the defect is a duplicate, by reviewing the current defect logt to identify any existing defects that encompass some or all of the new defect's symptoms.

      Duplicate defect occurrences are handled in accordance with the organization's policies and standards, including supporting the organization level measurement program. For example, how the defects and the duplicate defects are counted and reported, as part of the gathering of project data.

      Create or update the appropriate defect management documentation
      Create or update the defect log for the reported defect.

      If the organization or client has a standard set of defect codes, they should be used.
