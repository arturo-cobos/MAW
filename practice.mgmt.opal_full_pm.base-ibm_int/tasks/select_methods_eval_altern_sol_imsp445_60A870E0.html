Task: Select methods for evaluating alternative solutions (IMSP445)
This task selects the method used for evaluating the alternative solutions for the key decision.
Purpose
To select the method for evaluating the alternative solutions for the key decision.

      Main Description

      Select the method or methods for evaluating the alternative solutions against the established criteria.

      While many matters may need only one evaluation method in order to make a key decision, others may require multiple methods. For example, simulations may supplement a trade study to determine which design alternative best meets a given criterion.

      When selecting methods consider the purpose of the imminent evaluation (that is, why a Key Decision is required), as well as the availability of the information needed to support the chosen methods.

      Document the selected evaluation method or methods.

      Steps
      Select the method or methods for evaluating the alternative solutions

      Select the method or method for evaluating the alternative solutions against the established criteria.

      The level of detail of the selected method is to be commensurate with potential cost, schedule, performance, and risk impacts.

      Typical evaluation methods may be paper-based or hands-on and include:

      • Simulations
      • Trade studies
      • Cost studies
      • Business opportunity studies
      • Surveys
      • Extrapolations based on field experience and prototypes
      • User review and comment
      • Testing

      • Refer to historical information available in your organization for information relating to various evaluation methods.

      While many matters may need only one evaluation method in order to make a key decision, others may require multiple methods.  For example, simulations may supplement a trade study to determine which design alternative best meets a given criterion.

      When selecting methods, consider the purpose of the imminent evaluation (that is, why a Key Decision is required), as well as the availability of the information needed to support the chosen methods.  For example, the methods used for evaluating a technical solution when requirements are not well defined may be different from the methods used when the requirements are well defined.
