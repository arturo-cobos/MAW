Task: Analyze assigned duties and obtain approval (IMSP491)
This task will analyze assigned duties to verify and document that an appropriate separation of duties exists, ensure that appropriate actions are taken, documentation exists when there is a separation of duties conflict, and obtain approval on the separation of duties matrix and related artifacts.
Purpose

To ensure that an appropriate separation of duties exists and is documented for the project and, if it does not, ensures that the appropriate actions are taken and documentation exists.

      Main Description

      This task includes the following:

      • Use the separation of duties matrix to analyze the assigned duties and verify that appropriate Separation of Duties (SOD) exists.                                                                                             
        The IBM Framework of Internal Controls (FICs) Key Controls over Operations (KCOs) for Application Services mandates a clear Separation of Duties (SOD) is documented by each Project or Application, or each group of small support and maintenance Projects or Applications.               
        Refer to the 'Required Separation of Duties Table' below for information about the duties that should be separated (that is to be assigned to different staff members.)
      • If a separation of duties is not possible and a SOD conflict does exist, the following must be in place:
        • A secondary control
          Note: A secondary control may be that another Subject Matter Expert (SME) reviews the artifact for where the SOD conflict exists.
        • Artifacts to support the secondary control
          Note: In the example above the supporting artifacts will be the completed project quality plan to verify that the peer review has been planned and the work product inspection form to verify that the reviews have been completed.
        • A risk raised and managed as required
      • If an SOD conflict exists and there is no secondary control in place, any actions that are required to implement secondary controls must be transferred to the project status report.
      • Note: The completed separation of duties matrix is required to be reviewed, and revised if required, at least annually or whenever a resource change in the project occurs
