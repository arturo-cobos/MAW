Task: Prepare to begin formal evaluation process (IMSP445)
This task begins the formal evaluation process for making a formal key decision.
Purpose
To begin the formal key decision evaluation process.
Relationships
RolesPrimary Performer:
  • Project Manager
Additional Performers:
InputsMandatory:
  • Matter requiring key decision
  • Project Decision Structure
    		Optional:
    • None
    Outputs
    • Key Decision Record
      Main Description

      Review the matter that requires a key decision, and confirm the understanding of all available related information and that the matter is within the scope of the project.
       
      Identify and document the scope, objectives and requirements of the formal evaluation process.

      Identify and invite all relevant stakeholders to participate in the decision-making process.

      Steps
      Review the matter that requires a key decision

      Review the matter that requires a key decision, and confirms their understanding of all available related information and that the matter is within the scope of the project.

      If the matter is not within the scope of the project then refer the matter to the appropriate stakeholder.

      Identify & document scope,objectives & requirements of formal evaluation process

      Identify and document the scope, objectives and requirements of the formal evaluation process.

      Key decision record is used to document the scope, objectives and requirements, and all other results of this formal evaluation process.

      Identify &invite all relevant stakeholders to participate in the decision-making

      Identify and invite all relevant stakeholders to participate in the decision-making process.

      Review the project decision structure to help identify relevant stakeholders.

      By involving as many stakeholders as practical, the likelihood of identifying a wider range of alternatives is increased.  Also, input from stakeholders with diverse skills and backgrounds can help the team to identify and address assumptions, constraints, and biases. Relevant stakeholders may include, but are not limited to:

      • Client
      • End users
      • Project Team Members
      • Managers
