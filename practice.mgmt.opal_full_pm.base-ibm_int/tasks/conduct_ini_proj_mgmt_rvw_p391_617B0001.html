Task: Conduct initial project management review (IMSP391)
This task outlines the work to conduct a Initial Project Management Review (PMR), if required by Integrated Operating Team (IOT)/Growth Market Unit (GMU) rules.
Purpose
To ensure plans provide a complete, consistent, coherent, accurate, and acceptable approach, and to ensure agreement and commitment from stakeholders, reducing the risk of issues later in the project.

        Main Description

        Reference IGSDF - Using GBS Customer Relationship Management (CRM) on Engagements for links to your local GBS Customer Relationship Management (CRM) Playbook for business rules on whether a Project Management Review (PMR) needs to be conducted.

        The Initial Project Management Review (PMR) is primarily focused on the start up activities of a project.

        • a Project (Program) Management System is in place
        • the baseline for performance and profit has been established, and
        • issues from the latest Proposal Baseline Assessment are reviewed and addressed.

        The key focus items (7 keys) for this review may include:

        • Stakeholders are engaged and supportive and communication channels are in place:
          • Communications with the client Project Manager, Sponsor and Steering Committee
          • Stakeholder Management plan in place
          • If a multi-Brand/Geography Project
            • Reporting is being planned as required by the Master Agreement (may include specific reporting requirements from other brands/countries).
            • Lead Brand/Geography Client is satisfied with performance thus far in other brands/geographies.
        • Business Benefits have been defined and plan in place to capture or tasks have been defined to do so.
        • Work and Schedule is planned, documented, realistic and communicated to all interested parties:
          • Project management schedule
          • Other plans required per the nature of the project
          • Progress tracking and communication plan
          • For Business Transformation Outsourcing (BTO) and Application Management Services (AMS), status of the transition plan and the progress made toward establishing financial and performance baselines.
        • Team and Infrastructure is in place or planned and team members understand their roles and responsibilities:
          • Human resource and technical environment plans & status of their implementation
          • Project team organization defined and understood by all
          • Project team communications (formal and informal) understood
          • Kick off meeting/Initial Team briefing undertaken
          • Staff is in-place, per the project plan
        • Scope is fully documented, a signed contract exists and project participants are aware of the scope of the work:
          • Discussion of issues/concerns with Solution Design team
          • Change management procedures in place
        • Risks are identified and mitigation plans in place:
          • Risk Management plan, including output of GS RISK (if appropriate)
          • Quality plan that includes Project Management Reviews and other required technical reviews.
          • Follow-up on actions from prior Risk Management reviews
        • Delivery Organization Benefits tracking is in place:
          • Required Audit & Contract File Maintenance procedures
          • Financial tracking and reporting is in place
          • Billing mechanisms in place.
        Key Considerations

        For projects where there is an E2E Delivery Owner, there is a requirement to confirm this resource has participated in the review.  This should be noted in the QA Workbench GBS PMR record.

        For guidance on timing of Initial PMR/Project Launch activities please refer to your local Integrated Operating Team (IOT)/Growth Market Unit (GMU)/Growth Market Region (GMR)/Growth Market Team (GMT)/Country Business Rules.

        Some Integrated Operating Team (IOT)/Growth Market Unit (GMU)/Growth Market Region (GMR)/Growth Market Team (GMT)/Country Risk Managers may supplement the Project Launch and initial PMR by assisting the Partner/Associate Partner/Project Manager with startup operations. This may take the form of a kick-off meeting and coaching sessions instead of an actual review of the project.
