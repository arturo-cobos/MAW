Task: Analyze the defect and record action for defect resolution (IMSP520)
Analyze the defect documentation, determine the resolution and document the planned actions in the defect documentation.
Purpose

To analyze the defect documentation, determine the resolution and document actions steps.

Relationships
RolesPrimary Performer:
  • Resolution Owner
Additional Performers:
InputsMandatory:
  • Defect Log
  • Findings Log
    		Optional:
    • None
    Outputs
    • Corrective actions
    • Defect Log
    • Findings Log
      Main Description
      Analyze the defect documentation to ensure enough information to address the situation is available. Then confirm the diagnosis of the defect to ensure it was not caused by testing errors or omissions. Look for evidence that the defect impacts other areas of the organization, project or code and determine the actions required to resolve the defect.  Finally, document all planned actions and schedules in the appropriate defect documentation.
      Steps
      Analyze the defect log
      Analyze the defect log to ensure enough information to address the situation is available.

      If the information is insufficient, contact the Project Manager or Originator of the defect, as appropriate, for additional information.

      Confirm the diagnosis of the defect
      Confirm the diagnosis of the defect to ensure it was not caused by testing errors or omissions.  Attempt to recreate the defect, if necessary.
      Look for evidence that the defect impacts other areas of the organization
      Look for evidence that the defect impacts other areas of the organization, project or code.
      Determine the actions required to resolve the defect

      Determine the actions required to resolve the defect such as:

      • Design a fix that will accommodate all areas impacted
      • If the defect's impact is complex or it is expected to have significant impact on project scope or on other projects, then estimate the effort, skill and resources required to implement the fix

      If the time frame or the planned actions required to resolve the defect are unacceptable to one or more affected parties (such as the test team), then negotiation is required between the Resolution Owner, the Project Manager, the Stakeholder, and the any other affected parties to decide on an appropriate time frame and actions.

      Determine if it is not a defect
      If after this additional analysis, it is determined that this is not a defect, then perform the following:
      • Identify the reason (s) for rejection, such as working as designed
      • Update the defect log and findings log as appropriate
      • Close the defect
      • Notify the project manager and the originator of the decision to reject the defect
      • Exit this Defect Management Procedure
      Document all planned actions and schedules

      Document all planned actions and schedules in the appropriate section of the defect log.

      In the event that the Resolution Owner cannot implement all of the actions personally, the Resolution Owner assumes responsibility for enlisting others to implement the necessary actions. The Project Manager can assist with negotiating resources, coordinating schedules, and escalating conflicts.
