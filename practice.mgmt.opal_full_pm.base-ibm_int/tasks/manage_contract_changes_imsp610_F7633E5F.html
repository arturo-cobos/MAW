Task: Manage contract changes (IMSP610)
Raise the contract change by submitting the appropriate change management documentation, assess the solutions and obtain acceptance and schedule the change.
Purpose
To raise and manage contract changes for the change request.
Relationships
RolesPrimary Performer:
  • Project Office
Additional Performers:
  • Project Management Office (PMO)
InputsMandatory:
  • Change Log
  • Change Request
    		Optional:
    • Agreement
    • Project Decision Structure
      Outputs
      • Change Log
      • Change Request
        Main Description

        A contract change may be needed for the change.  This task will raise that contract change request by submitting the appropriate change management documentation to both the Client and the Organizations Program Office.  The change assessment is conducted and the change is either accepted or rejected.  The impacts of the solutions are assessed and the change is formally accepted and scheduled.

        For large amendments to the contract, a more formal process is needed (with appropriate estimating, risk management assessment, etc.) as for a new proposal (see Defining and Planning processes).

        Steps
        Raise a contract change request
        Raise a contract change request by submitting a contract change request to both the Client's and Organization's Program Management Offices (or appropriate parties) informing them that a contract change is being requested.
        Perform initial assessment
        Perform initial assessment, and reject or accept the proposed contract change for analysis.
        • Coordinate with the Program Executive (PE), the Contract Manager and the Client (and/or other appropriate parties) to ensure that the request is complete and acceptable.
        If a full assessment of the change is required, then a service request must be entered to perform the analysis:
        • Prepare the service request with the change request attached.
        • Close the change request
        • Exit this procedure
        If the proposed contract change is acceptable but will not enter the Service Request process, continue this procedure.
        Analyze impact and potential solutions.
        Analyze impact and potential solutions.
        • Schedule and hold a review meeting with all stakeholders or experts to ensure that the change is fully understood and that an impact assessment should proceed.
        • Update the change request to include any changes that result from the review and to reflect the new status.
        • Work with the client's and Organization's executives and other appropriate parties to assess the impact of the change, including:
          • Financial
          • Information Security
          • Industry Security Regulations
          • Local Privacy Laws
          • Contract Scope
          • Resources
          • Portfolio
          • Service Level Agreements (SLAs)
        • Client's Strategic Goals
        • Document all information gathered, detailing all impacts identified, and review the change request with the appropriate stakeholders and perform a reassessment of the change if required.
        • Draft the contract amendment, and distribute to the appropriate stakeholders for review.  This review must include approval of all revenue, resource, or other organization, project, or process changes that will be required to support the contract change.
        This step may repeat as necessary based on the results of the stakeholders' review of the draft.
        Obtain formal agreement and schedule change orders
        Obtain formal agreement and schedule change orders (approved change requests)
        • Receive feedback from the stakeholders and revise the draft agreement, as necessary.
        • Obtain formal commitment to the amendment from stakeholders identified in the project decision structure. The stakeholders approve the amendment with a dated signature.
        • Issue the contract with the amendment, and distribute to all appropriate parties.
        • Ensure that all adjunct plans associated with the amendment are fully implemented, or that a plan is in place for such implementations.  These adjunct plans may be diverse in nature, ranging from changes to a particular project to changes to the financial management of the contract.
        Close the contract change by updating the change management documentation
        Close the contract change by updating the change order, as appropriate, to reflect completion of the change.
