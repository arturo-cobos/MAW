Task: Identify deliverables quality requirements (IMSP391 and IMSP230)
This task will expand upon the quality requirements identified in the project management system summary produced while planning the project.
Purpose

This task will ensure all quality-related requirements are understood and that the associated quality activities are accounted for in the project plans. Identify changes to quality system, that were identified during the definition and planning activities during the proposal that might need changes following sales agreements.

        Main Description

        This task is based on the WWPMM work pattern S2: Implement project management system.

        Normally, the key features of the Project Quality Plan and of the project management system to be implemented at project startup have been defined when planning the project, particularly when these features were expected to have an impact on the project costs and commitments.

        However, these plans are typically only "outlined", being developed to a level which was sufficient to prepare the Agreement, but that is not sufficiently detailed to control project execution. It is now time to "expand" these plans to a level of detail relevant for project execution control, by doing the following:

        • Identify deliverables quality requirements: Review the sponsor Agreement, Deliverable Definitions and associated acceptance criteria, and the outline of the Project Quality Plan.
        • Determine the quality factors, quality criteria and quality contributors that will likely ensure meeting the acceptance criteria, and the procedures to ensure the effectiveness of the quality contributors.
        • As appropriate, get inputs from technical team members, assess that the estimates for quality related activities are reliable and that they are correctly reflected in the project Work Breakdown Structure and Project Management Schedule.
        • Expand the Project Quality Plan accordingly.
        • When the project organization contains several organizational units (POU) or subcontractors, ensure consistency of the overall project plan with the local quality plans that may exist at the POU or subcontractor level.
