        Main Description
        Determine what potential actions could be used to leverage any opportunities or to diminish any threats.

        For each healthy or unhealthy sign, evaluate the cost of potential actions and the time frame required and probable effectiveness of the actions.

        Categorize actions according to the following criteria:
        • can be handled by applying adjustments to current plans and resources,
        • can be delayed but must be taken in the near term,
        • must be taken immediately.


        For those actions which must be taken immediately, escalate the related issues and actions, as necessary.

        Document those issues where adjustment to current plans or resources will not be sufficient to achieve a successful project.

        Consider the side effects of the identified potential actions on all health dimensions. Balance all the pros and cons related to the implementation of these actions, and then rate health accordingly.

        Check the availability of potential action owners against their current assignments and priorities before launching actions.

        For those issues that are likely to severely affect project health in a recurrent way or over a long period of time, properly document a Risk Definition.

        For those issues that are likely to be resolved by applying adjustments to current resources and plans, verify that these adjustments do not effect key elements of the project baseline.

        Communicate any adjustments to the project team.
