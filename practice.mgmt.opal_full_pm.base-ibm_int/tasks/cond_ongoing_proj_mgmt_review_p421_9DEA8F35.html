Task: Conduct On-Going Project Management Review (P421)
This task outlines the work to conduct an on-going Project Management Review (PMR), if required by IOT/GMU rules.
Purpose
This task is performed to conduct an on-going Project Management Review to ensure all project requirements are being met and to evaluate the health of the project.
Relationships
RolesPrimary Performer:
  • Project Manager

        Main Description

        Reviews are conducted periodically during the project to ensure progress is being made on the Contract/Statement of Work and any issues resulting from previous Risk Management and/or PMR reviews have been resolved.

        The review is primarily focused on analyzing the project from the following perspectives:

        • Stakeholders are engaged, supportive and communication channels are in place
          • Client satisfaction is good with respect to project progress, quality of deliverables, skills and work habits of the project team members and communications (verbal and written).
          • Client expectations and conditions of satisfaction are being met (and managed).
          • Client is honoring their commitments including decision-making timeliness
          • If a multi-Brand/Geography Project
            • Reporting is taking place as required by the Master Agreement (may include specific reporting requirements from other brands/countries).
            • Lead Brand/Geography Client is satisfied with performance in other brands/geographies.
        • Business Benefits have been defined, are reasonable and metrics are in place to capture benefits realization
        • Work and Schedule is planned, realistic and communicated to all interested parties:
          • Sufficient progress has been made toward Contract/Statement of Work tasks with respect to the project plans.
          • Schedules are being met and managed via monitoring and reporting status, re-estimating and addressing the impact of any changes.
          • Issues are resolved in timeframes that do not jeopardize project schedules; specifically, an excessive number of critical issues are not deferred for subsequent project phases
          • Contract deliverables produced to-date are in alignment with any associated guidelines predefined for them and are of good quality in the eyes of the Client Project Manager.
          • Required client sign-offs are being obtained as materials are produced
        • Team and Infrastructure is in place:
          • Project team morale is good and team is high performing and effective.
          • Project is supported by an effective technical infrastructure.
          • Cross-team communications are effective.
          • Supplier performance is good (both internal and external to IBM suppliers)
          • If a multi-Brand/Geography Project
            • Reporting is taking place as required by the Master Agreement (may include specific reporting requirements from other brands/countries).
            • Processes are in place and being executed for communicating with the various Brand/IOT/GMU/GMR/GMT/Country Project Managers, including appropriate level of status reporting.
        • Scope is clearly defined, changes are managed and are reflected in up-to-date contract revisions:
          • Project changes, if any, are being controlled and properly accommodated.
          • Issues have been resolved with the participation of the Solution Design team if different from Project Team.
          • If a multi-Geography Project
            • National Language support commitments are being honored. (if applicable)
        • Risks are identified and mitigation plans in place:
          • Risk management plans are being implemented and tracked in accordance with the documented process.
          • Risks are reassessed at regular intervals
        • Delivery Organization Benefits tracking is in place:
          • Project Control Book is being properly maintained.
          • Profitability to-date is in alignment with the project financial plan.
          • Costs (i.e., labor hours, travel expenses, supplier expenses, etc.) are being properly tracked and are in alignment with the project plans.
          • Contract File is being properly maintained and is kept at an IBM location (per IBM Business Controls guidelines)
          • If a multi-Brand/Geography Project
            • DOU's with other brands/geographies are in place and being followed.
            • Intercompany Agreements (ICA's) are in place where necessary and financial transactions are taking place as planned/agreed.
            • There are no export control issues. (if applicable)

        On completing an on-going review, the Review Team will agree to the schedule for the next on-going review with the Partner/Associate Partner/Project Manager.  Subsequent reviews will be defined by Risk Management based upon the health of the project, business transaction type, and IOT/GMU/GMR/GMT/Country business rules. Maximum time between reviews is 12 months.
