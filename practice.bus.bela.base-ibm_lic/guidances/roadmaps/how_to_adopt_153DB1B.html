Getting Started

What are the Complimentary Approaches to Identification in SOMA?

Top down analysis in SOMA is called Domain Modeling and Decomposition (DMD) where business-driven techniques are employed to analyze the key business aspects to identify these five fundamental constructs. Domain analysis of the business environment is conducted. It examines following aspects of a business domain:

  • Functional areas representing a static partitioning of a business domain
  • Business processes (flows), or as some prefer, business use cases, representing dynamic behavior of the business
  • Information needed by the business to operate
  • Business rules and policies constraining the business domains, business processes and associated information
  • Variations in the business environment with respect to business processes, information, and rules

We have defined practices and techniques in SOMA to examine each of these aspects of the business domain.

SOMA Business Entity Lifecycle Analysis (SOMA BELA) focuses on business entity centric approach to business design in SOMA. It provides first class support for information in business process design, the identification of services, and the mapping of services to service components.

SOMA BELA would be a primary way to start the business design if you are focused on performing the business design based on business entities and their lifecycle in terms of business significant states. Before conducting SOMA BELA, it is recommended that you conduct Goal Service Modeling (SOMA Goal Service Modeling practice) to understand and establish the business goals and objectives and identify key entities and Information Analysis and Modeling - High Level (SOMA Information Management - High Level practice) to identify/refine list of key entities and understand the relationship among these key entities.
