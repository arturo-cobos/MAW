External Description

The Solution Plan Deliverable is a report that is provided to the customer’s major stakeholders outlining the key approaches and plans for the solution.

The purpose of this deliverable is to document the scope, approach and expectations that will form the basis for estimating the release phases (Realization, Implementation, and Deployment) of the next release of the solution.

The deliverable / report will contain the following content:

  • Release Plan - confirms the functionality intended for the current release
  • Deployment Plan - confirms the intended scope and approach for deployment of the current release
  • Operations and Support Plan – confirms the organizations responsible for operations, training, and other support responsibilities of the deployed solution.
  • Quality Plan – confirms the relative priorities of various attributes of quality that the project team will focus on.
  • Test Strategy and Plan – identifies the testing objectives and high level approach to follow
  • Readiness Assessment - confirm's the customers ability to manage and operate the solution
  • Project Schedule - describes the project's expected time frames and milestones
  • Risk Management Plan - describes project's approach to identifying and managing risk
  • Communication Plan - describes the project's approach to managing stakeholder communications
  • Data Migration and Quality Plans - identifies data sources and quality levels project is dependent upon
  • Education and Training Strategy and Plan - describes project's expected creation and/or delivery of training to support the new solution
  • Usability Design and Evaluation Plan - describes how the project will address usability issues

Some content in the Solution Plan will be further detailed in the Solution Management Strategy produced during Realization. The intent of this deliverable is to provide the basis scope of work to be included in the current release. A revised Solution Plan deliverable may be produced during the Deployment Phase to finalize details of the next release of the solution, prior to iterating through a subsequent set of Release phases.

Packaging Instructions

The final document will be delivered to the customer in a reproducible format.  The actual models may have originally been created using a diagramming tool, but should be included as a finished portion of the report for final delivery.  The final report may also be delivered in hard copy format printed in a single, bound document.

The artifact parts of this deliverable are the inputs to the various report topics or sections.  These artifacts may not all be fully elaborated (or completed in terms of both depth and breadth) at this time.  Some may be iterated and more fully developed in later phases of the engagement as the need for greater detail is identified. It is important that the deliverable accurately conveys the level of completeness for each artifact included.

The concept of completeness of an artifact instance depends largely on whether the artifact will be finished in this phase of the project, or iterated for additional work in later phases.  If the artifact is to be iterated later, it will only be partially complete at the end of this phase.  Regardless of whether each artifact instance is fully or partially completed at this time, the content of the artifacts should be fully included in the deliverable being produced in order to reflect the current level of known detail.
