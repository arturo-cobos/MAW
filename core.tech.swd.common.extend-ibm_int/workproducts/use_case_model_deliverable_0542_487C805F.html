External Description

The Requirements Model is a report that confirms the scope of a solution’s release, the functional requirements, and the non-functional (qualitative) requirements of the specific release of the envisioned solution.

The purpose of this deliverable is to finalize requirements relative to the scope of this specific release and to the extent necessary for the design of the solution’s application model and architecture model. Additionally, this deliverable establishes the set of well defined requirements for the solution’s various test plans, including the customer’s acceptance test plan.

The deliverable / report will contain the following content:

  • Solution Scope
  • Functional Requirements (scenarios required to support specific business functions or services that are within the scope of the solution)
  • Non-Functional Requirements (qualitative attributes of the proposed solution such as performance, availability, capacity, usability)
  • Requirements validation approach and results

The content in this deliverable forms the baseline for the development of the Application Model, Architecture Model, Release Design Specifications, and the Solution Plan deliverables.

The intent of this deliverable is to provide the requirements in sufficient detail to finalize the release design and identify the criteria needed for customer acceptance of the solution.

Packaging Instructions

The final document will be delivered to the customer in a reproducible format.  The actual models may have originally been created using a diagramming tool, but should be included as a finished portion of the report for final delivery.  The final report may also be delivered in hard copy format printed in a single, bound document.

The artifact parts of this deliverable are the inputs to the various report topics or sections.  These artifacts may not all be fully elaborated (or completed in terms of both depth and breadth) at this time.  Some may be iterated and more fully developed in later phases of the engagement as the need for greater detail is identified. It is important that the deliverable accurately conveys the level of completeness for each artifact included.

The concept of completeness of an artifact instance depends largely on whether the artifact will be finished in this phase of the project, or iterated for additional work in later phases.  If the artifact is to be iterated later, it will only be partially complete at the end of this phase.  Regardless of whether each artifact instance is fully or partially completed at this time, the content of the artifacts should be fully included in the deliverable being produced in order to reflect the current level of known detail.
