Deliverable: Current Client IT and Business Environments Report (DCD 278)
This deliverable overviews the IT and business domains to be included in the scope of a client’s project.
Purpose
The purpose of this deliverable is to:
  • Provide a common understanding of the existing technical infrastructure, principles, policies, standards, constraints, and anticipated changes that could affect the architecture of the proposed solution. It establishes a technical baseline for the development of the Architecture Model.
  • Gain an understanding of the client's business and organization as regards the proposed solution.
  • Document the business environment covered by the proposed solution and potentially affected by it.
  • Begin the articulation of business requirements that will need to be transformed into the technical application and architectural design of the solution.
  • Confirm the range of business activities potentially in scope and out of scope of the solution.
Main Description
This deliverable covers the following major topics:
 
Current IT Environment
 
This section documents the existing IT environment to which the solution is anticipated to be deployed. It covers the following topics as needed to meet an egagement’s specific requirements.
  • A reference to the existing IT Strategy governing the client’s technical operations
  • A collection and review of existing information security and privacy principles, policies and IT Standards to which the solution must adhere
  • A depiction and review of the client’s current IT environment and infrastructure including a profile of the network and identification of security functional components and security zones
  • A description of any known, planned or anticipated changes to IT environment that could affect the solution prior to deployment
 
Business Model from Solution Outline
 
This section overviews the business domain to be included in the scope of a client’s project. It defines aspects of the client’s business considered in-scope to the project as well as those considered out-of-scope to the project. It covers some or all of the the following topics as needed to meet an engagement’s specific requirements.
  • An overview of the engagement’s business objectives
  • An overview of the candidate business entities and their relationships that may be affected by the proposed solution
  • An overview of the client’s current organization, issues and goals
  • A definition of the client’s business areas and processes potentially affected by the proposed solution and considered in-scope to the project as well as those considered out-of-scope to the project
  • A definition of the clients business and IT drivers
  • A list of the clients information assets potentially affected by the proposed solution and considered in-scope to the project (as well as those considered out-of-scope to the project) along with there required levels of confidentiality, integrity, and availability
  • A definition of the clients risk appetite that outlines the value of identified assets, the known threats/threat agents and known vulnerabilities of those assets
  • A list of the business events that trigger the execution of the business processes in-scope to the project
  • A list of the client’s business rules and/or policies that are in use in current business activities and must be incorporated into the proposed solution’s design
  • A list of business terms used in the client’s business environment and classification relevant to the proposed solution
Deliverable Properties
External DescriptionThe objective of the information collection and documentation generation activities and tasks executed in association with this deliverable is to gain an understanding of the client’s existing business and I/T environment as regards the proposed solution. This is done via a set number of interviews with the identified project stakeholders. The number of interviews conducted is determined by the IBM project manager in conjunction with the client’s project manager. If interviews beyond this established number are needed, project estimates and schedules can be impacted and will be handled following Project Change Control procedures.
 
Based on the interviews conducted, IBM will work with the client to gather, review, and document the current IT and business environments to which the proposed solution will be deployed. The intent of this deliverable is to document the existing IT infrastructure and the existing operational business environment and processes not their redesign. It will include the following details pertaining to the potential scope of the proposed solution:
  • A description of the client’s current IT infrastructure and environment including a profile of the network and identification of security functional components and security zones
  • Review the customer’s current IT infrastructure and existing information security principles, policies, and IT standards to determine the impact they may have on the proposed solution
  • An overview of the project scope and the boundaries for those areas considered to be out of scope
  • A description of the client’s current business environment
  • An overview of the client’s current organization, issues and goals
  • An assessment of the proposed solution’s alignment with the client’s defined business strategic directions
  • A list of the clients business and IT drivers
  • A list of the clients information assets potentially affected by the proposed solution and considered in-scope to the project (as well as those considered out-of-scope to the project) along with there required levels of confidentiality, Integrity, and availability,
  • Documentation of the clients risk appetite that outlines the value of identified assets, the known threats/threat agents, known vulnerabilities of those assets, and proposed countermeasures taken in the solution
  • An overview of the client’s existing operational business processes along with those identified as candidates for automation in the proposed solution forming the basis for the solution’s key use cases
  • A listing of the regular business events that occur on a periodic schedule
  • Identification (and documentation) of the applicable business rules to be considered
  • Identification of business objects that receive, transmit, or transform information or that embody and execute defined customer’s business rules
  • A description of the current organization, if needed, to clarify the relationships of the relevant organizational units
  • A description of business terms used in defining and describing the solution
The contents of this report may be further detailed or clarified in subsequent project phases. The objective of this deliverable is to provide the breadth of details needed to understand the overall complexity of the proposed solution, produce reasonable estimates for the Macro Design phase, and planning information for the subsequent Release Phases.
