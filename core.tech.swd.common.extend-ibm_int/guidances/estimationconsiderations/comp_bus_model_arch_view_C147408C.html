Main Description
Creating CBM/A Models is very time consuming depending on the size and complexity of the business being modeled, and the availability of the various supporting work products. The minimal time required to construct a CBM/A without recognizable input work products is at least one month. It is not unusual for architecture models such as a CBM/A model to be constantly maintained and refined as part of the ongoing responsibilities of the enterprise’s Enterprise Architecture Office.
Significant factors that influence the CBM/A development effort include:
  • Business functional and operational complexity,
  • The number of components (typically in the range of 40 to 100) in the functional structure and…
  • …the number of operational resources (such as locations and business units). A more complex multi-national or multi-brand enterprise may require the creation of several inter-locking models, sharing components and or/operational resources.
  • Degree of distribution of component resources. A highly distributed or federated enterprise will require additional architectural effort.
  • Demanding eco-system requirements, such as extensive outsourcing or exploitation of partner businesses. Considerations such as complex legal or regulated environments, dynamic and changing markets, and levels of competency within the enterprise generally make the definition of a CBM/A’s physical views much more challenging.

All effort is by Enterprise Architects with significant business architecture expertise, and a sound grasp of the enterprise and its industry.
Effort distribution (%) by Phase:
Substantial rework takes place as the CBM/A is developed. This development is not performed at an even pace for all elements of the model, so different elements will be at different stages of development at the same time.
