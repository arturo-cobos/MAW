Checklist: Component Business Model Architecture View Validation and Verification
Relationships
Related Elements
  • Component Business Model (Architecture View)
Main Description

The CBM/A should be validated as a whole via formal reviews, generally at project milestones, and via informal means whenever the Business Architect requires. In all cases, the review is a peer-to-peer “technical” review by a fellow Business Architect, selected as a subject matter expert in the topic under review: it is vital to ensure the work product reflects the architectural view of the CBM, including a sense of robustness and accuracy of the components and the services they offer.

Additionally, an executive sponsor’s review on the business implications of the technical work should be sought, taking particular care to understand the strategic implications of the architectural view.

Reviews may be particularly useful:

  • After the initial development of the component specifications, highlighting the basic component responsibilities and services.
  • After gaining an outline understanding of the components’ relationships and their key interactions in support of critical (“architecturally significant”) business events.
  • After the detailed development of the CBM/A, prior to its completion and acceptance by the project and/or executive sponsor as representing the business’s required structure.
