Deliverable: Training Evaluation Summary (DCD 117)
The Training Evaluation Summary is based on data collected from conducting formative and summative training evaluations.
Purpose
Training material development, delivery, and effectiveness can be measured by conducting a training evaluation. Normally the evaluation strategy is divided into two components, the formative evaluation and the summative evaluation. During the Initial Training Strategy workshop, it is important to educate the client on the suggested evaluations, their purposes, and how conducting the evaluation will benefit the client.
 
The purpose of the document is to present the results of the evaluations to the client.
Main Description

The Training Evaluation Summary is based on data collected from conducting formative and summative training evaluations. The training evaluation strategy defines the type of evaluation and the method for performing evaluations. The Training Evaluation Summary is a compendium of information gathered from various evaluations gauging the effectiveness of training delivery, material, and course content.

Packaging Instructions

The Training Evaluation Summary content description assembles the defined work product outputs into a final presentation for client executives and key decision makers. The description provides a summary of key topics, outputs, findings and recommendations for the courseware. 
The format is a single document containing fifty to one hundred pages and a slide presentation. The document is either delivered as a printed and bound hardcopy (preferably printed in color) or in electronic files (.pdf, word processing, presentation slide formats) via CD-ROM, diskette and/or email.

The inputs used to develop the Courseware materials deliverable include the following:

ENG 343 Project Definition is used to understand the engagement objectives and how they affect the training solution.
ORG 153 User Support Specifications is used for knowing the configuration of the standard package, including the package extensions that have been developed, the reports and interfaces that have been developed, and how a combination of these will be used to support the client's business processes. The specifications define the boundaries between the different units of user documentation, as well as content, audience, purpose, standards to be used, and the language(s).
ORG 311 Education and Training Plan is used to identify activities and responsibilities related to the development, delivery, and evaluation of client education and training for the planned implementation.
ORG 312 Education and Training Strategy is used for identifying all staff affected by the implementing the new application, training requirements for each, sequence of training, delivery methods, and standards for success. 
ORG 310 Education and Training Evaluation provides the outline for the Training Evaluation summary.
