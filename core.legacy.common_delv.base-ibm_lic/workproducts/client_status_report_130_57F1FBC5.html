Deliverable: Client Status Report (DCD 130)
This report is created by aggregating information from sub-project/team and subcontractor status reports into one project status report.
Purpose
To provide the client with an accurate assessment of the current state of the project.

Main Description
The project manager creates the status report. The project manager follows the procedures and requirements as stated in the SOW for preparation of the report. Augmentation with graphics is helpful in showing progress or potential problems. Project status reports are often reviewed with IBM management prior to being delivered to the client, particularly if the project is not on schedule and/or over budget.
 
The status report is provided to the client on a scheduled basis, such as weekly or monthly, as well as, a final report at the end of the project. In general, the status report includes the following information: (1) Status that describes the state of the project vs. the project plans; (2) Progress that describes what the project has accomplished; and (3) Planned accomplishments for next period.
 
 
The type of information to cover in the report is summarized in the following table.
 
Project status summary
Overall Project Status A snapshot of the overall health of the project, aggregated from sub-project and subcontractor status reports. Projects often use letter grades such as A, B, C, or D or colors such as RED, YELLOW, or GREEN.
Project accomplishments
Achievements List of rollout sites/locations completed in this reporting period
Sites planned but not completed Includes explanation of why site is not complete, plus impact on overall project and corrective actions
Deliverables completed List of deliverables completed since last reporting period
Deliverables planned but not completed Includes explanation of why deliverable is delayed, impact on overall project, and corrective actions
Comments The impacts of any variances and the actions underway to remedy the situation
Planned accomplishments for next period
Achievements List of sites scheduled for completion by next reporting period
Deliverables complete List of deliverables scheduled for completion by next reporting period
Quality status
Project effectiveness Assessment of the quality contributors being used on the project. May be supported by measurements, such as number of compliance reviews or procedural changes.
Issues
List of new or outstanding issues Issues that if not resolved may impact schedule and quality, and recommended actions are described
Risks
List of new or changed risks Risks that are new, changed, or that occurred during reporting period, including recommended actions for dealing with the risk are described
Changes
New or updated changes Change request or Change order that are new, approved, rejected, or deferred in this reporting period, including summary of impact of change orders on project baselines are described
List of compliance incidents The status and impact of each compliance incident is described

Deliverable Properties
External Description

Status Report

Purpose: IBM will provide a Monthly Status Report as required during the project to describe the activities which took place during that period. Significant accomplishments, milestones, and problems will be described.

Delivery: One (1) hard copy will be delivered to the XYZ Co. Project Manager within five (5) working days following the reporting period.

Content: The report will consist of the following, as appropriate:

  • Activities performed during the reporting period
  • Activities planned for the next reporting period
  • Summary of hours (planned, expended, remaining)
  • Project change control activity
  • Problems, concerns, and recommendations
  • Other items of importance
Deliverable Parts
  • Action Log
  • Change Log
  • Issue Log
  • Milestone List
  • Project Management Schedule
  • Sub-Project Team Status Report
Packaging Instructions

The status report can be provided to the client in two formats:

  • In a document format that is delivered on a scheduled basis to the client. The document should be no more than 1-2 pages. Tables and charts can be included, if appropriate. A formal review of the report is at the request of the client. This format is the preferred format for a client status report.
  • In presentation format with charts and graphs. The presentation should be no more than 5 charts. The information in the presentation is presented and reviewed with the client on a formal scheduled basis.
