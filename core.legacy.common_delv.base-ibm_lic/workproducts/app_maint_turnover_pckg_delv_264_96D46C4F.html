Deliverable: Application Maintenance Turnover Package Deliverable (DCD 264)
This document is intended to provide guidance in the content and development of the package of documentation regarding a new or enhanced application known as the Application Maintenance Turnover Package.
Purpose

The purpose of this deliverable is to provide the IBM AME team with the project assets necessary to continue smooth maintenance of applications. The checklists included in the input section provide the application development team guidance on the type of assets to be turned over and the IBM AME team a criterion for acceptance of an application being turned over by the application development team. The criteria for acceptance are incorporated in checklists that enable the IBM AME team to assess the readiness of an application to be turned over to a maintenance environment.

Note: If the application development team and the maintenance team are both part of the same organization and, the established policy and process of turnover to the maintenance team, if they exist, take precedence over the stipulations provided by this DCD. If the organization does not have an established turnover preparation and delivery process, the guidelines provided in this document can be used to define the same. 

Main Description
The Application Maintenance Turnover Package provides the ongoing maintenance and enhancement team with a through knowledge of the purposes, functionality and processes of the application as a basis for future support. This DCD should be used in concert with the Application Maintenance Turnover Definition (APP xxx) which is designed to describe the scope of the Application Maintenance turnover process and the activities that it comprises. The turnover definition is intended to assist the practitioner in understanding and performing a structured turnover from the application development team to the maintenance team. These two documents, used together, represent the tactical and strategic views of the overall process.
 
This document consists of the following:
  • A maintenance asset checklist containing project assets and acceptance criteria as deemed necessary by the IBM application maintenance and enhancement (AME) team during an application turnover by the application development team.
  • A turnover assessment checklist containing the turnover readiness checks that are to be conducted by IBM AME team during an application turnover by the application development team.
Deliverable Properties
External DescriptionThis deliverable will enable the IBM AME team to assess the assets being turned over by an application development team and assist them in verifying the completeness of those assets. Guidance is provided in the form of Maintenance Assets Checklist and Turnover Assessment Checklist.
 
The turnover process is subjected to the guidelines and processes outlined by the application development team at the start of the engagement if the development team and AME team are part of the same organization and the application turnover is part of an ongoing engagement.
Deliverable Parts
  • Application Maintenance Turnover Definition
  • Configuration Management Plan
Packaging Instructions

The application development team will deliver the assets as per the Maintenance Assets Checklist and Turnover Assessment Checklist along with the completed checklists to IBM AME team in a reproducible format. The checklists along with the assets may also be delivered in hard copy format printed in a single, bound document to the IBM AME team.

Asset Checklist

The asset checklist is generic in nature and may be contained in a word document or a spreadsheet. Project assets and acceptance criteria may be added to or deleted from the checklist as deemed necessary by the AM Project Manager depending on specific turnover asset needs. The following work products may be referred to for adding additional assets/acceptance criteria: Increment Goals, Class Diagram, Interaction Diagram.

The concept of completeness of a work product/asset depends largely on whether the work product will be complete during development, or iterated for additional work later. If the work product is to be iterated later, it will only be partially complete at the end of the development phase. Regardless of whether each work product instance is fully or partially completed at this time, the content of the work product should be fully included in the deliverable being produced.

Turnover Assessment Checklist

The turnover checklist is generic in nature and may be contained in a word document or a spreadsheet. Items may be added to or deleted from the checklist as deemed necessary by the AME Project Manager depending on specific turnover needs.


Documentation
Does the application documentation include or reference:

  • requirement specifications 
  • program modules documentation 
  • all files, data definitions and data models? 
  • user documentation

Are the documents complete, correct, consistent and understandable?


Application environment

  • Is the infrastructure architecture and maintenance platform defined? 
  • If the maintenance team requires installation, do separate manuals or sections address each platform and installer? 
  • Is the maintenance platform completed and operational? 
  • Are the required technical skills needed to maintain the application identified? 
  • Are suitable support and test environments (including hardware and software) ready for hand over?


Application maintenance tools

  • Are all custom development tools to be used to maintain the application available? 
  • Is the Development Team required to supply configuration management tools? Are configuration management processes and procedures documented and operational? 
  • Are the supplied tools, processes and procedures capable of supporting the Maintenance and Enhancement teams?


Application ‘health’ status

  • Has the development team provided known defects and weaknesses in the application (together with details of their characteristics and locations)?
