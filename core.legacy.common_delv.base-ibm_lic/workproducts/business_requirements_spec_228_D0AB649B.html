Deliverable: Business Requirements Specification (DCD 228)
The Business Requirements Specification (BRS) contains all of the business requirements for a project. The BRS is used to create the project’s customer/client technical baseline.
Purpose
The Business Requirements Specification (BRS) is a deliverable used to baseline the business requirements for a project. The business requirements are normally created and documented by the customer and provided to the project team at the beginning of the project. In this case, the format of the business requirements may or may not match the BRS template provided below.
 
A Business Requirements Specification (BRS) includes the following:
  • A definition of the business requirements (both functional and nonfunctional)
  • The Business Scope and Objectives
  • The 'As-Is' Business Process Flows (if available for a legacy business process)
  • The 'As-Is' system landscape document (if available for a legacy business process)
 
If the customer requests the project team's support in creating the business requirements, then the following template should be used to create the customer requirements. If a Business Requirements Review (BRR) is being held for the project, then the business requirements will be reviewed at the BRR. If a BRR is not being held, then the business requirements will be reviewed at the System Requirements Review (SRR). If a BRR is held, then the SRR can either 1) re-review all of the business requirements to reinforce the customer baseline, or 2) only review the changes to the business requirements since the BRR. In either case, the business requirements traceability to the system requirements must be reviewed at the SRR.
 
When the Customer Project Manager and the IGS Project Manager approve and baseline the business requirements at either the BRR or the SRR, then the document and the requirements will be placed under change control.

Packaging InstructionsThis document should be compiled and included in the Project Control book. The business requirements may be documented as described in the Business Requirements Specification or they may be documented in other formats. There are many Requirements Management tools available (for example, Rational RequisitePro). The project’s requirements may be documented within one of these tools rather than using the format presented here. The Project Control book should describe the documentation method used by the project.
