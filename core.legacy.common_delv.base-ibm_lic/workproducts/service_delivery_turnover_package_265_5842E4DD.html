Deliverable: Service Delivery Turnover Package (DCD 265)
This document is intended to provide guidance in the development and content of the Service Delivery Turnover Package.
Purpose

The purpose of this document is to provide the SD team a guide to ascertain the readiness of application system for smooth takeover from the development/maintenance team, the considerations that influence the readiness of project assets and artifacts. This document serves to fulfill those checks.

If the application development/maintenance team and the SD team are both part of the same organization and an established turnover policy and process exists, then such policy and processes take precedence over the stipulations provided by this DCD. If the organization does not have an established turnover preparation and delivery process, the guidelines provided in this document can be used to define the same.

Main Description

This documentation provides the information necessary for creating an operations oriented turnover package to support the successful ongoing operation of the application in a production environment.

This DCD should be used in concert with the Service Delivery Turnover Definition (APP 404). The Service Delivery Turnover Definition provides insight into the scope and purpose of the service delivery turnover process and general content. The turnover definition is intended to assist the practitioner in understanding and performing a structured turnover from the application development/maintenance team to the service delivery team.

This document will consist of the following:

  • Turnover check lists of Project assets including hardware, software and project artifacts required for service delivery (SD) by IBM
  • Turnover acceptance checklists and acceptance criteria for the above assets, a turnover assessment checklist containing the turnover readiness checks that are to be conducted by IBM SD team
  • The document will address service delivery turnover for different engagement contexts like AMS (Application Management Services), ITS (Integrated Technology Services) and SO (Strategic Outsourcing)
Deliverable Properties
External DescriptionThe Service delivery turnover package will enable the Service delivery (SD) team to assess the project assets (Hardware, software and other project artifacts) being turned over by the development/maintenance team to SD team and verify the completeness and acceptability of those deliverables.
Deliverable Parts
  • Configuration Management Plan
  • Service Delivery Turnover Definition
Packaging Instructions

The development team will deliver the assets as per the Service Delivery Assets Checklist and Turnover Assessment Checklist along with the completed checklists to the SD team in a reproducible format. The checklists along with the assets may also be delivered in hard copy format printed in a single, bound document to the SD team.
The SD team is responsible for:

  •  Defining all project assets and acceptance criteria it deems necessary for initiating support activities.
  •  Creating service delivery assets and turnover assessment checklists and ensuring that all project assets and acceptance criteria defined are covered in the checklists.
  •  Validating and verifying that the project assets meet the acceptance criteria for a smooth turnover.

This deliverable comprises of the following work products: 

  •  Service Delivery Turnover Definition (APP 404): Describes the scope of turnover activities based on the service delivery engagement.
  •  Configuration Management Procedures (ENG 101): Describe details for identifying, organizing and controlling modifications to the configuration items.
  •  Checklists: Describe in general the asset list and acceptance criteria for application/ service turnover from development/maintenance team to SD team.

The same is discussed in the following section.
